🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Cradle of Both Buddhism AND Jainism

Buddhism in Bihar:

  • Bodh Gaya: the Buddha attained enlightenment (c. 528 BCE) under the Bodhi tree. Mahabodhi Temple = UNESCO World Heritage Site (2002). The most important Buddhist pilgrimage site in the world.
  • Rajgir: First Buddhist Council (c. 483 BCE, Saptaparni cave); Veluvana monastery (first Buddhist monastery, given by Bimbisara); Gridhakuta (Vulture Peak, where the Buddha preached the Lotus Sutra).
  • Vaishali: Second Buddhist Council (c. 383 BCE); the Buddha's last sermon before his death.
  • Pataliputra: Third Buddhist Council (c. 250 BCE, under Ashoka); the capital of the Mauryan Empire — where Ashoka ruled and promoted Buddhism.
  • Nalanda: the great Buddhist university (5th-12th century CE) — one of the world's first residential universities. Founded by Kumargupta I (c. 427 CE). Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE). UNESCO (2016).

Jainism in Bihar:

  • Vaishali (Kundagrama): birthplace of Mahavira (c. 599 BCE). Mahavira was born in the Jnatrika clan — a subordinate of the Licchavi confederacy of Vaishali.
  • Rajgir (Jrimbhakagrama): Mahavira attained Kevala Jnana (omniscience) near Rajgir — after 12 years of severe asceticism.
  • Pawapuri (Nalanda district): Mahavira attained Nirvana (death) at Pawapuri (c. 527 BCE). The Jal Mandir (a temple in a lotus pond) marks the cremation site — a major Jain pilgrimage destination.
  • First Jain Council: held at Pataliputra (Bihar) — c. 527 BCE, presided by Sthulabhadra. Compiled the 12 Angas (the core Jain texts) — in Ardhamagadhi Prakrit.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 100 (Mahajanapadas): Both Buddhism and Jainism originated in the Mahajanapada period (6th century BCE). The Buddha and Mahavira preached in Magadha, Kosala, and Vajji (all in Bihar/UP). The first three Buddhist Councils were held in Bihar.
  • Topic 102 (Mauryan Empire): Ashoka (268-232 BCE) promoted Buddhism — he convened the Third Buddhist Council (Pataliputra), sent Buddhist missionaries abroad, and built stupas (including the Great Stupa at Sanchi). Chandragupta Maurya embraced Jainism in his later years — he abdicated and became a Jain monk, fasting to death (Sallekhana) at Shravanabelagola (Karnataka).
📰 Current Relevance
  • Bodh Gaya: the most important Buddhist pilgrimage site in the world — the Mahabodhi Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (2002). Dalai Lama visits regularly. International Buddhist conferences held here.
  • Nalanda: UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016). A new Nalanda University has been established near the ruins (2014) — as an international university, reviving the ancient legacy.
  • Pawapuri: a major Jain pilgrimage site — the Jal Mandir attracts thousands of Jain pilgrims every year, especially during Mahavir Jayanti.
  • Buddhism globally: ~500 million Buddhists worldwide (mostly in China, Japan, SE Asia, Sri Lanka, Tibet). Buddhism spread from Bihar (via Ashoka's missionaries, 250 BCE) to become a world religion.
  • Jainism globally: ~4-5 million Jains (mostly in India — Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharashtra, Karnataka). Jainism's principle of ahimsa influenced Gandhi and the Indian freedom movement.

Q1. Gautama Buddha was born in which place (in modern Nepal)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Lumbini (Nepal, c. 563 BCE). UNESCO World Heritage Site (1997). Father: Suddhodana (Sakya king). Mother: Mahamaya. Personal name: Siddhartha; clan: Gautama.
Q2. Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment (Bodhi) under which tree and at which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bodhi tree (peepal) at Bodh Gaya (Bihar), c. 528 BCE, age 35. 49 days of meditation. Mahabodhi Temple = UNESCO (2002). Bihar = enlightenment.
Q3. Buddha delivered his first sermon (Dharmachakra Pravartana) at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Sarnath (Deer Park, near Varanasi, UP), c. 528 BCE. Taught Four Noble Truths + Eightfold Path to first 5 disciples. Dhamek Stupa marks the spot.
Q4. Buddha attained Mahaparinirvana (death/final liberation) at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Kushinagar (UP), c. 483 BCE, age 80. Last words: 'All conditioned things are impermanent.' Relics divided among 8 kingdoms → stupas.
Q5. The Four Noble Truths (Arya Satya) of Buddhism are:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Dukkha (suffering exists), Samudaya (cause = desire), Nirodha (cessation possible), Magga (path = Eightfold Path). Core teachings, first sermon at Sarnath.
Q6. The Eightfold Path (Astangika Magga) of Buddhism includes:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 8 steps: right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration. Grouped: wisdom (1-2), morality (3-5), meditation (6-8).
Q7. Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism, was born at:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Kundagrama (suburb of Vaishali, Bihar), c. 599 BCE. Father: Siddhartha (Jnatrika clan leader). Mother: Trishala (Licchavi princess). Personal name: Vardhamana. Bihar = birthplace of Mahavira.
Q8. Mahavira attained Nirvana (death) at which place (in Bihar)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Pawapuri (Nalanda district, Bihar), c. 527 BCE, age 72. Jal Mandir (temple in lotus pond) marks cremation site. Major Jain pilgrimage. After death: Jainism split into Digambara + Svetambara.
Q9. Buddhism and Jainism both emerged in which century BCE?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 6th century BCE. Both as a reaction against Vedic ritualism + caste system. Buddha (563-483 BCE) and Mahavira (599-527 BCE) = contemporaries. 'Sramana movement.'
Q10. The Tripitaka ('Three Baskets') is the sacred text of which religion?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Tripitaka = Buddhism. Three baskets: Sutta Pitaka (discourses), Vinaya Pitaka (monastic rules), Abhidhamma Pitaka (philosophy). In Pali. Compiled at First Council (483 BCE).
Q11. The sacred texts of Jainism are called:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Agamas (Jainism). 12 Angas + 12 Upangas + others. In Ardhamagadhi Prakrit. Svetambaras accept 45 Agamas; Digambaras believe original texts were lost.
Q12. The principle of 'Ahimsa' (non-violence) is most strictly followed in which religion?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Jainism = strictest ahimsa. Monks wear masks (avoid inhaling insects), sweep ground (avoid stepping), filter water (avoid microorganisms), avoid root vegetables. Buddhism allows meat (if not killed for the monk).
Q13. How many Tirthankaras are there in Jainism?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 24 Tirthankaras. First: Rishabhanatha (Adinatha). 23rd: Parshvanatha (c. 8th century BCE). 24th: Mahavira (c. 599-527 BCE). Not founders — they rediscover and teach Jainism in each cosmic cycle.
Q14. The Buddhist Sangha (monastic community) consisted of:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 4 groups: monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen. Open to all — regardless of caste, gender, social status. Revolutionary in caste-ridden Vedic society. Governed by Vinaya Pitaka.
Q15. The Five Precepts (Pancha Sila) of Buddhism are:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 5 precepts: no killing, no stealing, no sexual misconduct, no lying, no intoxication. For lay followers. Monks follow additional Vinaya rules (227 for monks, 311 for nuns).
Q16. Buddhism split into two main branches at the Fourth Buddhist Council (c. 72 CE). These are:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Hinayana (Theravada, 'Way of the Elders' — individual liberation, Pali, Sri Lanka/SE Asia) vs Mahayana ('Greater Vehicle' — Bodhisattva, Sanskrit, East Asia). Split at Fourth Council (Kashmir, c. 72 CE, Kanishka).
Q17. Jainism split into two main sects after Mahavira's death. These are:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Digambara (sky-clad, no clothes, South India) vs Svetambara (white-clad, white robes, West India). Digambaras: women cannot achieve moksha. Svetambaras: women can. Different Agamas.
Q18. The three jewels (Triratna) of Buddhism are:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Triratna: Buddha (teacher), Dharma (teaching), Sangha (community). 'I take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, Sangha.' Core declaration of Buddhist faith.
Q19. The three jewels (Triratna) of Jainism are:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Jain Triratna: Right faith (Samyak Darshana), Right knowledge (Samyak Jnana), Right conduct (Samyak Charitra). All three together = path to moksha.
Q20. The Five Vows (Pancha Mahavrata) of Jainism are:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — 5 vows: ahimsa (non-violence), satya (truth), asteya (non-stealing), brahmacharya (celibacy), aparigraha (non-possession). Stricter for monks (Mahavrata); less strict for lay (Anuvrata). Aparigraha uniquely Jain.
Q21. Bihar's connection with Buddhism includes which of the following sites?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bihar = cradle of Buddhism: Bodh Gaya (enlightenment), Rajgir (First Council), Vaishali (Second Council + last sermon), Pataliputra (Third Council + Ashoka), Nalanda (university). All first 3 Councils in Bihar!
Q22. Bihar's connection with Jainism includes which of the following sites?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar = cradle of Jainism: Vaishali/Kundagrama (Mahavira birth, c. 599 BCE), Pawapuri (Mahavira death, c. 527 BCE), Rajgir (Kevala Jnana + first Jain council, Pataliputra).
Q23. Nalanda University, the great Buddhist centre of learning, was located in:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Nalanda (Bihar, near Rajgir). Founded by Kumargupta I (c. 427 CE). 10,000 students, 2,000 teachers. Subjects: Buddhism, logic, medicine, astronomy. Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE). UNESCO (2016).
Q24. Which Buddhist council was held at Pataliputra (Bihar) under Ashoka?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Third Council: Pataliputra (Bihar, c. 250 BCE), Ashoka. Presided by Moggaliputta Tissa. Compiled Abhidhamma Pitaka. Sent Buddhist missionaries abroad — Buddhism became a world religion.
Q25. The Buddha and Mahavira were contemporaries. Which statement about them is correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Both preached in Magadha-Kosala (Bihar + UP). Contemporaries (Buddha 563-483 BCE, Mahavira 599-527 BCE). Both: rejected Vedas + caste; used vernacular; emphasised ahimsa; attracted merchants. 'Sramana movement' — reaction against Vedic Brahmanism.
