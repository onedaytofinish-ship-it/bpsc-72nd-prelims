Art and Literature — Architecture, Sculpture, Texts

📚 Core Content
EXAM TIP Ancient Indian Art & Literature (c. 322 BCE - 550 CE): Four art schools: Mauryan (polished stone pillars), Gandhara (Greco-Roman, Kushana), Mathura (indigenous, Kushana), Amaravati (Satavahana). Temple architecture: Nagara (North, curvilinear shikhara) + Dravida (South, pyramidal vimana). Key texts: Arthashastra (Kautilya), Ashtadhyayi (Panini), Shakuntala (Kalidasa), Aryabhatiya (Aryabhata), Charaka/Sushruta Samhita (medicine), Silappadikaram + Thirukkural (Tamil). Bihar: Ashokan pillars, Barabar Caves, Nalanda, Mahabodhi Temple.
Ajanta cave paintings (Maharashtra, UNESCO 1983) — the finest surviving examples of Indian mural art. Frescoes depicting Jataka tales (Buddha's previous births). Painted during the Vakataka-Gupta period (5th–6th c. CE). The Gupta Golden Age produced masterpieces in painting, sculpture, and architecture.
Sanchi Stupa (MP, UNESCO 1989) — originally built by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE), enlarged during the Shunga period. The gateway (torana) carvings depict Jataka tales and Buddhist symbols. Sanchi represents the stupa tradition of ancient Indian architecture. Bihar connections: Barabar Caves (oldest rock-cut caves, near Gaya) and Ashokan pillars at Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, and Rampurva (West Champaran).

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Centre of Ancient Indian Art and Architecture
  • Ashokan Pillars (Bihar): Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, and Rampurva (all in West Champaran district) — among the best-preserved Ashokan pillars, with lion, bull, and elephant capitals. Mauryan polished stone architecture.
  • Barabar Caves (near Gaya): the oldest rock-cut caves in India — donated by Ashoka to the Ajivika sect. Mauryan-era rock-cut architecture. Dasharatha (Ashoka's grandson) also donated at nearby Nagarjuna hills.
  • Nalanda University (Bihar): founded by Kumargupta I (c. 427 CE, Gupta period) — Buddhist monasteries, stupas, sculptures. One of the world's first residential universities. UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016). A centre of Buddhist art and architecture.
  • Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya, Bihar): originally built by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE, Mauryan), rebuilt during the Gupta period (c. 4th-5th c. CE). UNESCO World Heritage Site (2002). Bihar's most iconic monument — marking the site of the Buddha's enlightenment.
  • Pataliputra (Patna): the Mauryan and Gupta capital — described by Megasthenes as having a magnificent palace (Mauryan). Mauryan-era wooden palisade walls have been found in archaeological excavations.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 102 (Mauryan Empire): The Mauryan school of art — polished stone pillars, Barabar Caves — was Bihar's first great contribution to Indian art. The Ashokan pillars in Bihar (Lauriya, Rampurva) are among the finest examples.
  • Topic 104 (Gupta Empire): The Gupta 'Golden Age' produced the earliest Hindu temples (Nagara style), Kalidasa's literature, Aryabhata's mathematics, the Mahabodhi Temple (rebuilt), and Nalanda University — all centred in Bihar.
  • Topic 105 (Post-Gupta & Sangam): The Pallava and Chalukya temples (Mahabalipuram, Pattadakal) developed the Dravida style — parallel to the Nagara style of the north. Sangam literature (Silappadikaram, Thirukkural) is a separate literary tradition from Sanskrit.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Mahabodhi Temple (Bihar): UNESCO World Heritage Site (2002) — the most important Buddhist pilgrimage site in the world. Dalai Lama visits regularly. International Buddhist conferences held here.
  • Nalanda University (Bihar): UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016). A new Nalanda University was established (2014) — reviving the ancient legacy of Bihar as a centre of learning.
  • Sarnath Lion Capital: India's national emblem (adopted 26 January 1950). The Dhamma Chakra appears on the national flag. Ashoka's art = India's official symbols.
  • Ajanta + Ellora: UNESCO World Heritage Sites (1983). Major tourist attractions — showcasing ancient Indian rock-cut architecture and paintings.
  • Thirukkural: Thiruvalluvar's 1,330 couplets — translated into many languages. A statue of Thiruvalluvar was unveiled in Haridwar (2021). Tamil was declared a classical language (2004).

Q1. The Mauryan period is known for which architectural innovation?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mauryan period = polished stone pillars (Ashokan pillars). Single sandstone piece, mirror-polished, with animal capitals. Sarnath Lion Capital = national emblem. Barabar Caves (Bihar) also Mauryan. Beginning of Indian stone architecture.
Q2. The Sarnath Lion Capital, which is India's national emblem, was built during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sarnath Lion Capital (four lions) built by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE, Mauryan). India's national emblem (26 January 1950). Dhamma Chakra on the capital appears on the national flag.
Q3. The Barabar Caves (near Gaya, Bihar) — the oldest rock-cut caves in India — were donated by Ashoka to which sect?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Barabar Caves (near Gaya, Bihar) — oldest rock-cut caves in India. Donated by Ashoka to Ajivikas (Makkhali Gosala, believed in niyati/destiny). Dasharatha (grandson) also donated at Nagarjuna hills.
Q4. The Stupa architecture (Buddhist hemispherical domes) reached its peak at which site?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Great Stupa at Sanchi (MP). Originally built by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE), enlarged during Shunga period (2nd c. BCE). Four toranas (gateways) with sculptures. UNESCO (1989). Contains relics of the Buddha or his disciples.
Q5. The Gandhara School of Art (c. 1st-3rd century CE) is characterized by which feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gandhara School (1st-3rd c. CE, Kushana period) = Greco-Roman style. Hellenistic features: wavy hair, muscular body, Greek drapery, sandals. Blue-grey schist. Peshawar/Taxila (Pakistan/Afghanistan). First human Buddha images.
Q6. The Mathura School of Art (c. 1st-3rd century CE) is characterized by which feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mathura School (1st-3rd c. CE, Kushana period) = indigenous Indian. Usnisa (topknot), serene expression, transparent robes, red sandstone. Mathura (UP). Buddhist + Jain + Hindu images. Independent of Greco-Roman influence.
Q7. The Amravati (Amaravati) School of Art flourished in which region?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Amaravati School: lower Krishna valley (Andhra Pradesh, 2nd c. BCE - 3rd c. CE), under Satavahanas. White marble sculptures, Amaravati Stupa, limestone reliefs (Jataka tales). Now in British Museum + Chennai Government Museum.
Q8. The Nagara style of temple architecture is associated with which region?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Nagara = North Indian style. Curvilinear shikhara (tower) over garbhagriha (sanctum). Examples: Dashavatara (Deogarh, Gupta), Kandariya Mahadeo (Khajuraho), Lingaraj (Bhubaneswar), Sun Temple (Konark). Developed from Gupta period.
Q9. The Dravida style of temple architecture is associated with which region?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Dravida = South Indian style. Vimana (pyramidal tower), gopuram (gateway), enclosure walls, mandapas, temple tank. Examples: Brihadeshwara (Tanjore, Chola), Meenakshi (Madurai), Kailasanatha (Kanchipuram, Pallava). From Pallava period.
Q10. The earliest Hindu temples (flat-roofed, with a small shikhara) were built during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Earliest Hindu temples = Gupta period (4th-6th c. CE). Flat-roofed with small shikhara. Dashavatara Temple (Deogarh, UP) = classic early example (Vishnu, square shikhara). Template for all later Indian temple architecture.
Q11. The Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra) are famous for which type of art?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra, 29 caves) = Buddhist paintings (frescoes). Jataka tales (previous lives of the Buddha). Mostly Gupta period (4th-6th c. CE, caves 16-27), some earlier (2nd c. BCE). UNESCO (1983). Finest ancient Indian art.
Q12. The Ellora Caves (Maharashtra) are unique for which reason?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ellora Caves (Maharashtra, 34 caves, 6th-10th c. CE) = Hindu (14-29) + Buddhist (1-12) + Jain (30-34) caves. Kailasa Temple (cave 16, Hindu, Rashtrakuta Krishna I, c. 760 CE) = largest monolithic structure in the world. UNESCO (1983).
Q13. The Kailasa Temple at Ellora (cave 16) — the largest monolithic structure in the world — was built by which dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Kailasa Temple (Ellora cave 16) built by Rashtrakuta king Krishna I (c. 760 CE). Largest monolithic (single-rock) structure in the world — carved top-down from a single basalt cliff. Dedicated to Shiva. Peak of Indian rock-cut architecture.
Q14. Kalidasa's 'Abhijnanasakuntalam' (Shakuntala) is a work in which genre?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 'Abhijnanasakuntalam' (Shakuntala) = Sanskrit drama (play). Greatest Sanskrit play. King Dushyanta and Shakuntala. Translated by William Jones (1789) — sensation in Europe. Goethe admired it. Kalidasa = Navaratna of Chandragupta II (Gupta).
Q15. The 'Arthashastra' (treatise on statecraft) was written by which author?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Kautilya (Chanakya) wrote the Arthashastra (4th c. BCE, Mauryan). Statecraft, economics, military, law, administration. Describes Mauryan system (18 departments). Rediscovered 1909 by R. Shamasastry. Most important political treatise of ancient India.
Q16. Panini's 'Ashtadhyayi' is a foundational text in which field?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Panini's Ashtadhyayi (8 chapters, 3,959 sutras) = most systematic Sanskrit grammar ever written. One of the greatest works of linguistics in world history. Panini (5th-4th c. BCE, Gandhara). Codified Classical Sanskrit. Patanjali's Mahabhashya = commentary.
Q17. The 'Charaka Samhita' and 'Sushruta Samhita' are foundational texts in which field?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Charaka Samhita (internal medicine, Charaka 1st-2nd c. CE) + Sushruta Samhita (surgery, Sushruta 6th c. BCE — 'father of Indian surgery,' 120 instruments, rhinoplasty). Foundational texts of Ayurveda. Refined during Gupta period.
Q18. Aryabhata's 'Aryabhatiya' (c. 499 CE) is a foundational text in which field?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Aryabhatiya (499 CE, Gupta period) = mathematics + astronomy. Pi (~3.1416), Earth rotation, eclipses (shadows), place-value, sine table. Aryabhata was 23. India's first satellite named 'Aryabhata' (1975).
Q19. The 'Silappadikaram' (Tamil epic) was written by which author?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ilango Adigal (Chera prince, 2nd c. CE, Sangam). Silappadikaram = Kovalan and Kannagi story. One of five great Tamil epics. Celebrates Pattini (chaste wife) cult. Rich info about Sangam society.
Q20. The 'Thirukkural' (1,330 couplets) was written by which author?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Thiruvalluvar (1st-2nd c. CE, Sangam). 1,330 couplets in 133 chapters. Three books: Aram (Virtue), Porul (Wealth/Politics), Inbam (Love). 'Tamil Veda.' Translated into many languages.
Q21. The Iron Pillar of Delhi (Mehrauli) — famous for not rusting — was built during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Iron Pillar of Delhi (Mehrauli) = Gupta period (Chandragupta II, 4th c. CE). ~7m tall, ~6 tons. Not rusted in ~1600 years. Sanskrit inscription praises 'Chandra' (Chandragupta II). Originally at Udayagiri.
Q22. The Brihadeshwara Temple (Tanjore, Tamil Nadu) — a masterpiece of Dravida architecture — was built by which dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Brihadeshwara Temple (Tanjore, TN) = Chola king Rajaraja I (c. 1010 CE). Dravida architecture masterpiece: 66m vimana, massive Nandi, wall paintings. UNESCO (1987, 'Great Living Chola Temples'). Dedicated to Shiva. 80-ton granite capstone.
Q23. Bihar's contribution to ancient Indian art and architecture includes which of the following?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Bihar's contributions: Ashokan pillars (Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva), Barabar Caves (Gaya, oldest rock-cut India), Nalanda University (5th-12th c. CE, UNESCO 2016), Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya, UNESCO 2002). Centre of Buddhist art!
Q24. The 'Mahabodhi Temple' at Bodh Gaya (Bihar) — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — was originally built by whom and rebuilt during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya, Bihar): originally built by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE, Mauryan), rebuilt in current form during Gupta period (4th-5th c. CE). Site of Buddha's enlightenment. UNESCO (2002). Bihar's most iconic monument.
Q25. Which of the following is NOT one of the four main schools of ancient Indian art?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Four ancient schools: Mauryan (pillars), Gandhara (Greco-Roman), Mathura (indigenous), Amaravati (Satavahana). Mughal School is medieval (16th-18th c. CE) — NOT ancient. Ancient Indian art spans Mauryan (322 BCE) to Gupta (550 CE).
