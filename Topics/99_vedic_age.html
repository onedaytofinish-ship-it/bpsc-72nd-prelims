Vedic Age — Early vs Later Vedic, Society & Texts

Stone tools to iron tools — the transition from the Stone Age to the Iron Age (~1000 BCE) enabled the Vedic people to expand agriculture from Punjab to the Ganga valley. Iron ploughshares allowed clearing of forests; iron weapons changed warfare. This transition marked the shift from Early Vedic (pastoral) to Later Vedic (agricultural) society.

Rock paintings at Bhimbetka (MP) — prehistoric art that shows the cultural roots of ancient India before the Vedic period. The Vedic culture followed the IVC (~1750 BCE) and predated the rise of the Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE).

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Videha (Mithila): Centre of Upanishadic Philosophy
  • Videha (Mithila): a Later Vedic kingdom in modern north Bihar (Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi districts). Ruled by King Janaka — a philosopher-king who patronised the Upanishadic debates.
  • King Janaka: the most famous king of Videha — mentioned in the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad as hosting philosophical debates between Yajnavalkya and other scholars (including the woman philosopher Gargi).
  • Gargi Vachaknavi: a woman philosopher from Videha who debated with Yajnavalkya about the nature of Brahman. Her story shows that: (1) women could pursue philosophy in the Later Vedic period; (2) Bihar was a centre of philosophical learning.
  • Sita: Mithila was the birthplace of Sita (Ramayana) — daughter of King Janaka (a different Janaka, or the same, depending on tradition).
  • Later history: Videha was absorbed by the Vajji confederacy (a republic, capital: Vaishali) and then by Magadha — which became the most powerful Mahajanapada.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 98 (IVC): The decline of the IVC (~1750 BCE) was followed by the Vedic Age (~1500 BCE). Some scholars connect the IVC decline to the arrival of the Aryans (Indo-Aryans) — but this theory is now contested (Rakhigarhi DNA study, 2019).
  • Topic 100 (Mahajanapadas): The Later Vedic janapadas evolved into the 16 Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE). Magadha (Bihar) became the most powerful — with capitals at Rajgir and Pataliputra.
  • Topic 101 (Buddhism & Jainism): The Upanishadic concepts (karma, samsara, moksha) were developed further by Buddhism and Jainism (6th century BCE). Both Buddhism and Jainism originated in Bihar — in the same region (Mithila, Vaishali) that hosted the Upanishadic debates.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Vedic heritage: the Vedas are the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism — still recited orally (UNESCO Intangible Heritage, 2003). The oral tradition of Vedic chanting has been preserved for over 3,000 years.
  • Sanskrit: the Vedic language (Vedic Sanskrit) is the ancestor of Classical Sanskrit — which influenced all major North Indian languages (Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, etc.).
  • Yoga: the Upanishadic concept of meditation and self-realisation is the philosophical foundation of yoga — now practiced worldwide.
  • Caste system: the varna system (originating in the Purusha Sukta) evolved into the caste system — a persistent social issue in modern India.

Q1. The Vedic Age in India is generally dated to which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 1500-600 BCE. Divided into Early Vedic (1500-1000 BCE, Rig Veda) and Later Vedic (1000-600 BCE, other 3 Vedas). Followed IVC decline (~1750 BCE), preceded Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE).
Q2. The Vedic Age is divided into which two phases?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Early Vedic (Rig Veda, pastoral, Punjab) → Later Vedic (all 4 Vedas, agricultural, Ganga doab). Shift from tribe to territory; from pastoralism to agriculture.
Q3. Which is the oldest Veda?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rig Veda (oldest, 1500-1000 BCE). 1028 hymns in 10 mandalas. Oldest text in any Indo-European language. Only Veda composed in Early Vedic period.
Q4. How many Vedas are there in total?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Four Vedas: Rig (hymns), Sama (chants), Yajur (rituals), Atharva (magic). Each Veda has 4 parts: Samhita, Brahmana, Aranyaka, Upanishad.
Q5. The Rig Veda contains how many hymns (suktas) and books (mandalas)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 1028 hymns (suktas) in 10 books (mandalas). 10th mandala is latest (Purusha Sukta, varna origin). Indra = most hymns (250+). Preserved orally for 3000+ years.
Q6. In the Early Vedic period, the society was primarily:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Early Vedic: pastoral (cattle = wealth, gavishti = search for cows). Semi-nomadic in Punjab/Sapta Sindhava. Tribe (jana) = political unit. Agriculture secondary.
Q7. In the Later Vedic period, the society shifted to:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Later Vedic: agriculture in Ganga-Yamuna doab. Iron (~1000 BCE) enabled forest clearing. Settled villages/towns. Tribe → territory (janapada). Varna rigid. Sabha/Samiti declined.
Q8. The term 'Sapta Sindhava' in the Rig Veda refers to:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Sapta Sindhava = 'land of seven rivers' = Punjab. Seven rivers: Indus, Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej, Saraswati (now dried). Rig Veda calls Saraswati 'mother of rivers.'
Q9. Who was the most important god in the Rig Veda (with the most hymns dedicated to him)?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Indra (250+ hymns, ~25%): god of thunder, rain, war. Destroyer of Dasyus. Warrior god. Agni = 200 hymns (fire, mediator). Varuna = cosmic order (Rta). Soma = elixir.
Q10. The 'Purusha Sukta' in the Rig Veda (10th mandala) describes the origin of which system?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Purusha Sukta (10th mandala, latest): four varnas from cosmic man Purusha — mouth = Brahmin, arms = Kshatriya, thighs = Vaishya, feet = Shudra. Earliest textual reference to varna system.
Q11. In the Early Vedic period, the political unit was the:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Early Vedic: tribe (jana) led by chief (rajan). Assisted by assemblies: Sabha (council of elders) + Samiti (general assembly). Tribal democracy. Chief's power limited.
Q12. In the Later Vedic period, the political unit shifted from tribe (jana) to:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Tribe (jana) → territory (janapada, 'where the people place their feet'). Chief (rajan) → king (absolute). Assemblies declined. Led to the 16 Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE).
Q13. The Upanishads are part of which Vedic text?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Upanishads = Vedanta ('end of the Vedas'). 108 Upanishads (10-13 principal). Discuss: Brahman, Atman, Karma, Moksha, Samsara. Composed 800-500 BCE. Foundation of Hindu philosophy.
Q14. Arrange the four parts of each Veda in order: 1. Samhita 2. Brahmana 3. Aranyaka 4. Upanishad
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Samhita (hymns, oldest) → Brahmana (ritual commentaries) → Aranyaka (forest texts, philosophical) → Upanishad (philosophy, Vedanta, latest). Evolution from ritual to philosophy.
Q15. In the Early Vedic period, women's status was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Early Vedic: women had education, attended assemblies (Sabha/Samiti), women scholars (Lopamudra, Ghosha, Maitreyi, Gargi), no child marriage, widow remarriage. Later Vedic: status declined.
Q16. The famous woman scholar Gargi, who debated with the philosopher Yajnavalkya, is mentioned in which text?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Gargi Vachaknavi debated Yajnavalkya in the court of King Janaka of Videha (Mithila, Bihar). Recorded in Brihadaranyaka Upanishad. Shows Bihar was a centre of philosophical learning + women could pursue philosophy.
Q17. The term 'Aryan' in the Vedic context refers to:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — 'Arya' = 'noble/respectable' in Sanskrit — a linguistic/cultural term, not racial. Indo-European speakers who migrated ~1500 BCE. Brought Sanskrit and Vedic culture. Aryan migration theory debated (Rakhigarhi DNA, 2019).
Q18. In the Later Vedic period, which two kingdoms became the most powerful?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Kuru-Panchala (upper Ganga-Yamuna doab) = centre of Vedic culture. Videha (Mithila, Bihar) = centre of philosophy (King Janaka, Upanishadic debates). Brahmanas, Aranyakas, Upanishads composed here.
Q19. The 'Dasas' and 'Dasyus' mentioned in the Rig Veda refer to:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Dasas/Dasyus = non-Aryan people (possibly IVC remnants or indigenous tribes). Dark-skinned ('krishna tvacha'), non-Aryan speakers. Indra = 'destroyer of Dasyus.' 'Dasa' later = 'slave.'
Q20. Which metal was NOT known to the Early Vedic (Rig Vedic) people?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Iron was NOT known in Early Vedic. Introduced ~1000 BCE (Later Vedic, Atharva Veda: 'krishna ayas' = dark metal). Early Vedic: copper (ayas), gold (hiranya). Iron revolutionised agriculture + warfare.
Q21. The four ashramas (stages of life) in Vedic society were:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Four ashramas: Brahmacharya (student), Grihastha (householder), Vanaprastha (hermit), Sannyasa (renunciant). Formalised in Later Vedic/post-Vedic. Varna = social classes; Ashrama = life stages; Purushartha = life goals.
Q22. Bihar's connection with the Vedic Age was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Videha (Mithila, north Bihar): King Janaka hosted Upanishadic debates (Yajnavalkya vs Gargi). Brihadaranyaka Upanishad records these. Mithila = birthplace of Sita. Later absorbed by Vajji confederacy (Vaishali) → Magadha.
Q23. The 'Sabha' and 'Samiti' in the Early Vedic period were:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sabha = council of elders (smaller); Samiti = general assembly (all free members). Chief (rajan) assisted by both — power limited. Tribal democracy. Declined in Later Vedic. 'Samiti' → modern 'committee.'
Q24. The concept of 'Rta' in the Rig Veda refers to:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Rta = cosmic order — natural and moral law governing the universe. Upheld by gods (especially Varuna). Humans follow Rta through yajna (sacrifice). Precursor to Dharma in later Hinduism. Sophisticated concept of universal law.
Q25. Which of the following is NOT a correct match between the Veda and its content?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Incorrect match: 'Atharva Veda — hymns of praise' (that's the Rig Veda). Atharva Veda = spells, charms, medicine. Rig = hymns. Sama = chants. Yajur = rituals. Atharva = magic.
