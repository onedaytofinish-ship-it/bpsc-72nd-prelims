A. Causes of the Revolt of 1857 — Five Categories

BPSC asks "Which was NOT a cause?" or "Which was the immediate cause?" Know all five categories and the specific grievances within each.

CategorySpecific Grievances
1. PoliticalDoctrine of Lapse (Dalhousie): Jhansi, Satara, Nagpur annexed; Awadh annexed (1856, misgovernance); pension of Nana Saheb discontinued; Bahadur Shah Zafar told his heirs would not use the title "King"
2. EconomicLand revenue pressure (Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari over-assessment); loss of livelihood for artisans (deindustrialisation); ruin of traditional handicrafts; exploitation of peasants and zamindars
3. Social & ReligiousInterference in Indian customs; abolition of sati (Bentinck 1829) seen as interference; legalisation of Hindu widows' remarriage (1856); fear of forced Christian conversion; missionaries active
4. MilitaryIndian sepoys discriminated against in pay and promotion; foreign service allowance (batta) abolished; Enfield rifle greased cartridges (cow + pig fat); sepoys required to serve overseas (loss of caste)
5. ImmediateIntroduction of the Enfield rifle with greased cartridges (cow + pig fat); Mangal Pandey's revolt at Barrackpore (March 29, 1857); 85 sepoys court-martialled at Meerut (May 9, 1857)
🧠 Mnemonic — Causes "P-E-S-M-I":
Political (Doctrine of Lapse, annexations) → Economic (land revenue, deindustrialisation) → Social/Religious (interference in customs, conversion fears) → Military (sepoys discriminated, greased cartridges) → Immediate (greased cartridges, Mangal Pandey).
"PESMI: the five reasons why 1857 happened."
The Indian Rebellion of 1857 — also known as the First War of Indian Independence (Savarkar's term), the Sepoy Mutiny (colonial term), or the Great Rebellion. It began at Meerut on May 10, 1857.

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & the Revolt of 1857 — Kunwar Singh at Jagdishpur
  • Kunwar Singh (1777–1858): the 80-year-old zamindar of Jagdishpur (Ara, Bhojpur district, Bihar), was the leading figure of the 1857 Revolt in Bihar. Despite his advanced age, he led his forces with remarkable military skill and energy.
  • His campaigns: Kunwar Singh did not limit himself to Bihar — he fought across multiple provinces:
    • Bihar: captured Ara (Jagdishpur), fought at Danapur; engaged British forces near Patna
    • UP: relieved Azamgarh (a remarkable feat — he marched hundreds of miles to relieve a besieged garrison)
    • MP: fought in the Rewa/Bundelkhand region
    • Return to Bihar: he fought his way back to Jagdishpur, where he was mortally wounded in his final battle (April 1858)
  • His death (April 26, 1858): Kunwar Singh died at Jagdishpur after being mortally wounded in his last battle against British forces led by William Taylor. According to legend, he cut off his own wounded hand and offered it to the Ganga river (a traditional act of sacrifice) before dying. His younger brother, Amar Singh, continued the fight for some months after.
  • His legacy: Kunwar Singh is one of the most revered figures in Bihar's history. The Veer Kunwar Singh University (Ara) and Veer Kunwar Singh Bridge (over the Ganga at Ara) are named after him. His birthplace (Jagdishpur) is a pilgrimage site for Bihar's freedom movement. The Bihar government celebrates his birthday (November 22) as a state day of remembrance.
  • Other Bihar connections: the sepoys at Danapur (near Patna) also revolted on June 25, 1857 — the Bihar chapter of the revolt was not limited to Kunwar Singh. The revolt at Danapur led to the fall of the British garrison at Patna, temporarily freeing the city.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps
  • Leader ↔ place trap: Kunwar Singh → Jagdishpur (NOT Jhansi or Kanpur). Rani Lakshmibai → Jhansi (NOT Kanpur — Kanpur is Nana Saheb). Begum Hazrat Mahal → Lucknow (NOT Jhansi). This is the #1 trap in this topic.
  • Start date trap: Meerut = May 10, 1857 (the full-scale outbreak); Barrackpore = March 29, 1857 (Mangal Pandey, the precursor). Don't confuse the two dates — both are "start of 1857" in different senses.
  • Mangal Pandey fate trap: he was hanged on April 8, 1857 — BEFORE the main revolt (May 10, 1857). He was the precursor, not a participant in the main revolt.
  • Birsa Munda ≠ 1857 trap: the 70th BPSC asked about Birsa Munda's revolt — that was Chhotanagpur (1895–1900), NOT the 1857 Revolt. Don't confuse the two Bihar revolts.
  • Doctrine of Lapse introducer trap: Lord Dalhousie introduced the Doctrine of Lapse (NOT Lord Canning or Bentinck). Lord Canning was the Gov-Gen during the revolt; Dalhousie had left by 1856.
  • Bahadur Shah Zafar's fate: exiled to Rangoon (Burma), died 1862 (NOT executed — he was tried but exiled, not killed). This is a common trap.

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Revolt of 1857 started from which place on May 10, 1857?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The revolt began at Meerut on May 10, 1857, when sepoys of the 3rd Native Cavalry broke out after 85 comrades were court-martialled for refusing greased cartridges. Barrackpore (March 29) was the Mangal Pandey precursor.
Q2. Who was the first sepoy to revolt against the British, at Barrackpore on March 29, 1857?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Mangal Pandey of the 34th Native Infantry at Barrackpore attacked British officers on March 29, 1857 — the first act of open rebellion. He was hanged on April 8, 1857, before the main revolt.
Q3. The immediate cause of the Revolt of 1857 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The immediate cause was the Enfield rifle's greased cartridges (cow + pig fat). The deeper causes included the Doctrine of Lapse, economic exploitation, and political annexation. The cartridges were the trigger, not the root cause.
Q4. The Enfield rifle cartridges were greased with fat from which animals?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Cow fat (offensive to Hindus) and pig fat (offensive to Muslims). Sepoys had to bite the cartridge to open it, ingesting the forbidden fat. This religious grievance united Hindu and Muslim sepoys.
Q5. After capturing Delhi on May 11, 1857, the sepoys declared which Mughal Emperor as the symbolic leader?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bahadur Shah Zafar (last Mughal Emperor, 82 years old) was declared the symbolic head. He was a reluctant figurehead. After the revolt was suppressed, he was exiled to Rangoon (Burma), where he died in 1862.
Q6. Kunwar Singh, a key leader of the 1857 Revolt, was the zamindar of which place in Bihar?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Kunwar Singh was the 80-year-old zamindar of Jagdishpur (Ara, Bhojpur district, Bihar). He led the revolt in Bihar with remarkable military skill, fighting across Bihar, UP, and MP. He died on April 26, 1858.
Q7. Rani Lakshmibai led the revolt from which princely state?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rani Lakshmibai led the revolt from Jhansi. Her state was annexed by Dalhousie under the Doctrine of Lapse (1853). She died in battle at Gwalior on June 17, 1858, fighting dressed as a man.
Q8. Nana Saheb led the revolt from which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Nana Saheb led the revolt from Kanpur (Cawnpore). He was the adopted son of the last Peshwa Baji Rao II; the British refused to continue his pension after the Peshwa's death. His general, Tantia Tope, continued the fight.
Q9. Begum Hazrat Mahal led the revolt from which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Begum Hazrat Mahal led the revolt from Awadh (Lucknow). She was the wife of the deposed Nawab Wajid Ali Shah. She eventually escaped to Nepal, where she died in 1879.
Q10. The Doctrine of Lapse was introduced by which Governor-General?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lord Dalhousie (GG 1848–1856) introduced the Doctrine of Lapse: if a ruler died without a natural male heir, his kingdom would 'lapse' to the British. States annexed: Satara, Jhansi, Nagpur, and others. Awadh was annexed on grounds of misgovernance.
Q11. Which of the following states was NOT annexed under the Doctrine of Lapse?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Mysore was NOT annexed under the Doctrine of Lapse — it was restored to its ruler in 1799 after Tipu Sultan's defeat. Jhansi, Satara, and Nagpur were all annexed under the Doctrine of Lapse by Dalhousie.
Q12. Who was the Governor-General of India at the time of the Revolt of 1857?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lord Canning (GG 1856–1858, Viceroy 1858–1862) was the head during the revolt. He succeeded Dalhousie (whose annexationist policies created the conditions). Canning is known for the 'Clemency of Canning' and the Government of India Act 1858.
Q13. The Revolt of 1857 is also known by which of the following names?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The revolt is known by all three names: 'First War of Indian Independence' (Savarkar, nationalist), 'Sepoy Mutiny' (colonial), 'The Great Rebellion' (neutral). The 70th BPSC referred to it as the "First Indian War of Independence."
Q14. Match the 1857 leader with the place of revolt: Kunwar Singh, Rani Lakshmibai, Nana Saheb, Begum Hazrat Mahal.
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kunwar Singh → Jagdishpur (Bihar); Rani Lakshmibai → Jhansi; Nana Saheb → Kanpur; Begum Hazrat Mahal → Lucknow (Awadh). The Bihar connection: Kunwar Singh → Jagdishpur (Ara/Bhojpur district).
Q15. The Government of India Act 1858, which ended EIC rule and transferred power to the British Crown, was a direct consequence of:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Government of India Act 1858 (Queen's Proclamation, Nov 1, 1858) was a direct consequence of the 1857 Revolt. It ended EIC rule, transferred governance to the Crown, replaced Gov-Gen with Viceroy (Canning was first), and ended the Doctrine of Lapse.
Q16. Who was the last Mughal Emperor, who became the symbolic head of the 1857 Revolt and was exiled to Rangoon?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bahadur Shah Zafar (1837–1857) was the last Mughal Emperor. He was declared symbolic head of the revolt. After British recaptured Delhi (Sep 1857), he was tried, exiled to Rangoon (Burma), and died in 1862. He was also a noted Urdu poet.
Q17. Kunwar Singh died on which date, after being mortally wounded in battle?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kunwar Singh died on April 26, 1858, at Jagdishpur, mortally wounded in his final battle. June 17, 1858 is the death of Rani Lakshmibai at Gwalior. September 21, 1857 is the British recapture of Delhi.
Q18. The Queen's Proclamation of 1858 promised all of the following EXCEPT:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Queen's Proclamation promised religious neutrality, end of the Doctrine of Lapse, and equal opportunity in government service. It did NOT promise self-government — India remained a British colony for another 89 years.
Q19. The revolt of Birsa Munda took place in which region? (70th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Birsa Munda's revolt (1895–1900) took place in Chhotanagpur (now Jharkhand, then part of Bihar Presidency). He led the Munda tribal rebellion against British exploitation. Don't confuse with Champaran (Gandhi's 1917 satyagraha — a different Bihar revolt).
Q20. Which of the following was NOT a cause of the Revolt of 1857?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Partition of Bengal (1905) was NOT a cause of the 1857 Revolt — it happened 48 years later, under Lord Curzon. The causes of 1857 were: Doctrine of Lapse, economic exploitation, religious grievance over greased cartridges, and political grievances of deposed rulers.
Q21. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Mangal Pandey at Barrackpore 2. Sepoy revolt at Meerut 3. Capture of Delhi by sepoys 4. Bahadur Shah Zafar exiled to Rangoon
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Correct order: Mangal Pandey at Barrackpore (March 29, 1857) → Sepoy revolt at Meerut (May 10, 1857) → Capture of Delhi (May 11, 1857) → Bahadur Shah Zafar exiled to Rangoon (October 1858). The revolt unfolded over 18 months.
Q22. Consider the following about Kunwar Singh: 1. He was the zamindar of Jagdishpur (Bihar). 2. He was 80 years old during the revolt. 3. He fought only in Bihar and never outside the state. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is WRONG — Kunwar Singh did NOT fight only in Bihar; he also fought in UP (relieved Azamgarh) and MP before returning to Jagdishpur where he was mortally wounded.
Q23. Which of the following is NOT correctly matched?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Rani Lakshmibai is wrongly matched — she led the revolt from Jhansi, NOT Kanpur. Kanpur was the base of Nana Saheb. The other three are correctly matched.
Q24. After the Revolt of 1857, the British ended the rule of which entity in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — After the 1857 Revolt, the British government ended the East India Company's rule (which had governed India since 1765) and transferred power to the British Crown. The Government of India Act 1858 formalised this — Canning became the first Viceroy.
Q25. The Revolt of 1857 failed primarily because:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The revolt failed due to all three: (1) British had superior technology, artillery, and reinforcements (including loyal Sikh and Gurkha regiments). (2) The revolt was confined to north India — South India, Punjab, and most of Bengal stayed quiet. (3) The Indian leaders were regional and lacked a unified command or common political programme.
