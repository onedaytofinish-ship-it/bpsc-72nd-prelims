Mehrgarh (Balochistan, ~7000 BCE) — the Neolithic precursor to the Indus Valley Civilisation. Shows the transition from hunting-gathering to farming: wheat/barley cultivation, domestication of animals, mud-brick houses. The IVC emerged from this Neolithic base around 2500 BCE.
Indus Valley seals — the most iconic artefacts of the IVC. Made of steatite (soapstone), featuring animals (unicorn, bull, rhino) and the still-undeciphered Harappan script. Over 3,700 seals have been found. The Pashupati Seal (Mohenjodaro) depicts a yogic figure surrounded by animals.

B. Town Planning — Key Features PYQ PATTERN

  • Grid pattern: streets crossing at right angles; rectangular blocks
  • Citadel + Lower Town: citadel (higher, public buildings — Great Bath, granary) on a mud-brick platform; lower town (residential, shops, workshops)
  • Standardised bricks: ratio 4:2:1 (length:width:height); fired (burnt) bricks, not sun-dried
  • Drainage system: covered drains along streets; soakpots; waste water from houses connected to street drains — the most advanced drainage system in the ancient world
  • House design: houses had a courtyard, well, bathroom, and drains; single entrance from the street; built around the courtyard
  • Streets: main streets were 9m wide; narrower lanes branched off; streets divided the city into blocks

D. Economy & Trade

  • Agriculture: wheat, barley (staples); cotton (first in the world); sesame; peas; rice (Lothal); dates
  • Domestication: cattle, buffalo, sheep, goats, pigs, dogs, elephants, camels
  • Crafts: bronze tools (lost-wax); beads (carnelian — Chanhudaro); pottery (red ware with black designs); shell-working; weaving (cotton)
  • Trade: maritime trade with Mesopotamia (Sumer/Akkad) via the Arabian Sea (Lothal port). Mesopotamian texts mention 'Meluhha' (= IVC). IVC exports: carnelian beads, cotton, ivory, copper. IVC imports: cylinder seals.
  • Weights: standardised binary system (16, 8, 4, 2, 1); made of chert/cubical; show advanced mathematics

E. Decline of the IVC (c. 1900-1750 BCE)

  • Climate change: drying of the Saraswati/Ghaggar river; aridification; reduced monsoon
  • River shifts: the Indus and Ghaggar changed course; cities abandoned due to water scarcity
  • Aryan invasion theory: now contested — many scholars reject it; no evidence of violent destruction
  • Trade decline: collapse of trade with Mesopotamia (Sumer declined)
  • Ecological degradation: deforestation, overgrazing, soil erosion
  • Floods/earthquakes: some sites show evidence of flooding (Mohenjodaro)
  • Gradual decline: the IVC did not disappear suddenly — it went through a late Harappan phase (1900-1750 BCE) with smaller, rural settlements

Grid planning → Brick standardisation (4:2:1) → Drainage system → Script (undeciphered).

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — NOT part of the IVC
  • The IVC did NOT extend to Bihar. The civilisation was concentrated in the Indus Valley (Pakistan), Gujarat, Rajasthan, Haryana, and Punjab.
  • The easternmost IVC site is Alamgirpur (Uttar Pradesh, near Meerut) — about 500 km west of Bihar.
  • Bihar was not part of the urban IVC — it had its own Chalcolithic culture (Chirand) and later the Iron Age culture that led to the rise of the Mahajanapadas (Magadha, c. 600 BCE).
  • Bihar entered the historical period with the rise of the Mahajanapadas — particularly Magadha (capital: Rajgir → Pataliputra) — which became the most powerful kingdom in ancient India.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 97 (Prehistory): Mehrgarh (~7000 BCE) was the Neolithic precursor to the IVC. The IVC emerged from the Neolithic agricultural base. The Chalcolithic Age (~2500 BCE) overlapped with the early IVC.
  • Topic 99 (Vedic Age): The decline of the IVC (c. 1750 BCE) was followed by the Vedic Age (c. 1500 BCE). Some scholars connect the IVC decline to the arrival of the Aryans (Indo-Aryans) — but this theory is now contested.
📰 Current Relevance
  • UNESCO sites: Mohenjodaro (1980) and Dholavira (2021) are UNESCO World Heritage Sites — major tourist destinations.
  • Rakhigarhi DNA: the 2019 DNA study of Rakhigarhi skeletons showed no steppe (Aryan) ancestry — challenging the Aryan invasion theory and suggesting continuity between the IVC and modern South Asian populations.
  • Cotton legacy: the IVC was the first to cultivate cotton — India remains one of the world's largest cotton producers.

Q1. The Indus Valley Civilisation is also known as the:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Named after Harappa, the first site discovered (1921, Daya Ram Sahni). Bronze Age (2500-1750 BCE). First urban civilisation in India.
Q2. The Indus Valley site of Harappa was discovered in which year and by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Harappa (1921, Daya Ram Sahni); Mohenjodaro (1922, R.D. Banerjee); John Marshall announced the IVC in 1924. Cunningham first reported Harappa in 1853.
Q3. The Indus Valley site of Mohenjodaro was discovered in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Mohenjodaro ('Mound of the Dead') was discovered in 1922 by R.D. Banerjee, in Sindh (now Pakistan). Famous for Great Bath, Great Granary, Dancing Girl, Pashupati Seal. UNESCO (1980).
Q4. The Indus Valley Civilisation is generally dated to which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — 2500-1750 BCE (Bronze Age). Mature Harappan: 2500-1900 BCE; late Harappan (decline): 1900-1750 BCE. Contemporary of Egypt and Mesopotamia.
Q5. The most distinctive feature of Indus Valley town planning was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Grid pattern: streets crossing at right angles. Citadel + lower town. Standardised brick (4:2:1). Covered drainage. Most planned cities of the ancient world.
Q6. The Great Bath of Mohenjodaro was used for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Great Bath: 12m x 7m x 2.4m deep. Bitumen-sealed. Steps on two sides. Ritual bathing — precursor to Hindu ritual purification (snana). Earliest known public water tank.
Q7. The standardised brick ratio in the Indus Valley Civilisation was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Standard brick ratio 4:2:1 (length:width:height), ~28cm x 14cm x 7cm. Fired bricks (not sun-dried). Standardised across all IVC sites — shows central planning.
Q8. The Indus Valley script has been:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The IVC script has NOT been deciphered. Pictographic (400-600 signs). Written right to left. Appears on seals, tablets, pottery. Biggest obstacle to understanding the IVC.
Q9. The famous bronze 'Dancing Girl' statue was found at which IVC site?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Dancing Girl (bronze, 10.5 cm) found at Mohenjodaro (1926). Lost-wax technique. Now in National Museum, New Delhi. One of the most iconic IVC artefacts.
Q10. The famous 'Pashupati Seal' showing a figure seated in a yogic posture was found at which site?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Pashupati Seal (Mohenjodaro): yogic figure (padmasana) surrounded by animals (elephant, tiger, rhino, buffalo, deer). Three faces, horns. Proto-Shiva? Suggests yoga and Shiva worship may have IVC origins.
Q11. Which IVC site in Gujarat is known as a port city with a dockyard?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Lothal (Gujarat) — port city with the world's earliest dockyard (2200 BCE). Connected to the Sabarmati river for trade with Mesopotamia. Discovered 1954 by S.R. Rao.
Q12. Which IVC site is known for its sophisticated water management system with reservoirs?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Dholavira (Gujarat, Kutch): reservoirs (some 73m long) connected by channels. Three-part city. Large signboard. UNESCO World Heritage Site (2021).
Q13. Which IVC site is known for evidence of fire altars?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kalibangan (Rajasthan): fire altars (ritual fire worship); ploughed fields (earliest agriculture evidence); 'black bangles.' Discovered by Amlanand Ghosh (1951).
Q14. The IVC site in Haryana which is the largest IVC site in the Indian subcontinent is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Rakhigarhi (Haryana) is the largest IVC site (~350 hectares, larger than Mohenjodaro's 200 ha). DNA studies (2019) showed no steppe (Aryan) ancestry — challenging the Aryan invasion theory.
Q15. The decline of the Indus Valley Civilisation is attributed to which of the following?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Multiple factors: climate change (drying of Saraswati), river shifts, Aryan invasion (contested), trade decline, ecological degradation, floods. Most accepted: climate change + river shifts. Gradual decline (1900-1750 BCE).
Q16. The IVC people traded with which ancient civilisation?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Traded with Mesopotamia (Sumer/Akkad). Mesopotamian texts mention 'Meluhha' (= IVC). IVC exports: carnelian beads, cotton, ivory, copper. Via Arabian Sea (Lothal port). Dilmun (Bahrain), Magan (Oman) intermediaries.
Q17. The IVC people primarily cultivated which crops?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Wheat and barley (staples). Also: cotton (first in the world), sesame, peas, rice (Lothal), dates. Used ploughs (Kalibangan). Granary at Mohenjodaro stored surplus grain.
Q18. The IVC was the first to produce which commodity in the world?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — First to cultivate and produce cotton. Greeks called it 'sindon' (after Sindh) and 'karpass' (after Sanskrit karpasa). Cotton fragments found at Mohenjodaro. Spread to Mesopotamia, Egypt, Europe.
Q19. Which animal is most frequently depicted on IVC seals?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Unicorn (one-horned bull) = ~60% of all IVC seals. Other animals: elephant, tiger, rhino, buffalo, crocodile, deer. Pashupati Seal (yogic figure surrounded by animals) also famous.
Q20. The IVC site at Banawali (Haryana) is known for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Banawali (Haryana): evidence of both pre-Harappan and Harappan culture — showing continuity. Fortified town, citadel, barley + rice cultivation. Excavated by R.S. Bisht (1974).
Q21. The IVC site at Chanhudaro (Sindh) is known for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Chanhudaro (Sindh): bead-making factory (earliest in the world). Carnelian beads exported to Mesopotamia. Also: shell-working, pottery workshop. Unfortified (unusual). Excavated by Mackay (1931).
Q22. Which IVC site in Bihar shows evidence of late/post-Harappan culture?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The IVC did NOT extend to Bihar. Easternmost IVC site: Alamgirpur (UP, near Meerut). Bihar had its own Chalcolithic culture (Chirand) and later the Iron Age culture that led to the Mahajanapadas (Magadha).
Q23. The Indus Valley Civilisation's political organisation is believed to have been:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Decentralised, egalitarian — based on absence of palaces, royal burials, weapons, large temples. Governed by a council of merchants or priests. Speculative (script undeciphered).
Q24. The 'Priest-King' statue was found at which IVC site?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 'Priest-King' statue (soapstone, 17.5 cm) found at Mohenjodaro (1925). Fillet/band on head, trefoil robe. Religious leader? National Museum of Pakistan, Karachi.
Q25. Arrange the IVC sites from north to south: 1. Rakhigarhi 2. Kalibangan 3. Mohenjodaro 4. Lothal
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — North to south: Rakhigarhi (Haryana, ~29°N) → Kalibangan (Rajasthan, ~28°N) → Mohenjodaro (Sindh, ~27°N) → Lothal (Gujarat, ~22°N). IVC area: ~1.3 million sq km — largest of ancient civilisations.
