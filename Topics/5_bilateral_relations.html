A. India-UK CETA — In Force July 15, 2026 🔗

The Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA) is India's deepest trade pact with a Western economy. Negotiations launched January 2022, concluded after 14 rounds on 6 May 2025, signed in London on 24 July 2025 (PM Modi - PM Keir Starmer), and entered into force on 15 July 2026.

B. India-EU FTA — "Mother of All Deals" (Concluded Jan 27, 2026) 🔗

Concluded at the 16th India-EU Summit, Hyderabad House, New Delhi, on 27 January 2026, with Commission President Ursula von der Leyen and Council President Antonio Costa — both Republic Day 2026 chief guests. Dubbed the "mother of all deals", it covers ~2 billion people and ~25% of global GDP. Ratification is pending; expected in force around early 2027.

  • Goods: India cuts tariffs on ~96.6% of EU goods by value; the EU gives preferential access to 99.5% of Indian exports by value (90.7% immediate).
  • Autos & wine: Cars slashed 110% → 10%; wines 150% → 40%; machinery, chemicals and pharma mostly zero-duty.
  • Services & green: 144 services sub-sectors opened; €500 million EU support for India's green transition.
  • Protected: Agriculture excluded — beef, chicken, rice, sugar and dairy stay out. Investment Protection and Geographical Indications (GI) agreements are still pending.
  • Strategic add-on: The EU-India Security & Defence Partnership was signed the same day (27 Jan 2026).

C. India-US — Tariff Deal, COMPACT & iCET 🔗

After a turbulent 2025 in which US "reciprocal" tariffs on India rose to 50% (25% reciprocal + 25% penalty for Russian oil purchases), the 2 February 2026 interim framework (Trump-Modi call) cut the reciprocal tariff from 25% to 18% and removed the extra 25% Russian-oil penalty. India committed to stop Russian oil purchases and to buy $100-500 billion of US energy, aircraft and technology; the US gained sorghum and soybean access while India's dairy stayed protected. This sits inside the Bilateral Trade Agreement (BTA) talks and Mission 500 ($500 billion bilateral trade by 2030).

  • COMPACT (Feb 2025): Catalyzing Opportunities for Military Partnership, Accelerated Commerce & Technology — the umbrella framework launched during PM Modi's Washington visit.
  • iCET: Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technology — the NSA-led tech roadmap (launched 2023; 2nd annual meeting in New Delhi, June 2024) covering semiconductors, joint fabrication, and space.
  • Defence & Space: GE F414 engine co-production for Tejas Mk2; NISAR joint NASA-ISRO radar satellite; ISRO astronaut training at NASA for an ISS mission.

D. India-Pakistan — Pahalgam, IWT Abeyance & Operation Sindoor 🔗

The defining bilateral crisis of the cycle. On 22 April 2025, terrorists of The Resistance Front (TRF, an LeT proxy) killed 26 people at Pahalgam, J&K. India responded by holding the Indus Waters Treaty (IWT) in abeyance on 23 April 2025 — "terror and water cannot flow together" — and launching Operation Sindoor (night of 6/7 May to 10 May 2025).

  • Operation Sindoor: Strikes on 9 terror sites, including Bahawalpur (JeM HQ) and Muridke (LeT HQ) — India's deepest strikes inside Pakistan since 1971.
  • S-400 air defence: Starred in blunting Pakistan's retaliation, with a claimed 314 km kill — among the longest recorded air-defence engagements.
  • Ceasefire: Agreed on 10 May 2025 via the DGMO hotline.
  • Status as of mid-2026: IWT still in abeyance; Permanent Court of Arbitration supplemental award (15 May 2026) rejected by India as "null and void"; Track-2 rounds (London, Muscat, Bangkok, Doha, Colombo) through June 2026 but no formal dialogue.
  • Links frozen: Attari-Wagah border closed; Kartarpur corridor shut by Pakistan; Pakistan announced suspension of the Simla Agreement.
🧠 Mnemonic — "9-2-314": Op Sindoor hit 9 terror sites, including 2 terror HQs (Bahawalpur = JeM, Muridke = LeT), and the S-400 logged a 314 km kill claim. Date chain: Pahalgam 22 Apr → IWT abeyance 23 Apr → Sindoor 6/7-10 May 2025.

E. India-Russia — 23rd Annual Summit (Dec 4-5, 2025) 🔗

President Putin visited New Delhi on 4-5 December 2025 — his first India visit since 2021 — as the Strategic Partnership completed 25 years. This built on the 22nd Annual Summit (Moscow, July 2024), which had first set the $100 billion trade target for 2030.

  • Economics: $100 billion trade target by 2030 reaffirmed; Programme 2030 economic roadmap adopted; progress on an India-EAEU FTA; new consulates in Yekaterinburg and Kazan.
  • Connectivity: Push on the INSTC, the Chennai-Vladivostok maritime corridor, and the Northern Sea Route.
  • Defence: Su-30MKI upgrade worth ₹63,000 crore (84 jets); India requested 5 more S-400 squadrons plus ₹10,000 crore of missiles; Su-57 under evaluation.
  • Strategic: Russia reiterated backing for India's permanent UNSC seat. Watch the trade imbalance — India imports ~$63.8 billion (largely oil) vs exports of only ~$4.9 billion.

F. India-Japan — 16th Annual Summit (July 2, 2026) 🔗

PM Modi and Japan's PM Sanae Takaichi met in New Delhi on 2 July 2026, delivering the biggest package in years under the "Free and Open Indo-Pacific" (FOIP) vision.

  • Investment: $10 billion+ in new investment (~120 pacts, ~¥2 trillion); the ¥10 trillion decade target reaffirmed. Japan is India's 5th-largest investor (~$48 billion cumulative); bilateral trade crossed $27 billion (FY26).
  • Defence first: First-ever India-Japan defence co-development — the UNICORN naval radio antenna for Indian warships.
  • New areas: Joint statement on AI; Bio-Gas Initiative (1,000 plants); metals/energy security pact.
  • Legacy: JIMEX-24 maritime exercise was held at Yokosuka, Japan (70th BPSC PYQ); high naval interoperability continues.

G. India-Oman CEPA (Signed Dec 18, 2025) 🔗

India signed a Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement with Oman on 18 December 2025 — India's second major FTA in the Gulf after the UAE CEPA (2022). It deepens market access for goods and services and matters directly to the large Indian diaspora in Oman.

H. India-Australia — ECTA in Force, CECA Next 🔗

The Economic Cooperation and Trade Agreement (ECTA), signed April 2022, entered into force on 29 December 2022 (69th BPSC PYQ), eliminating tariffs on over 85% of trade lines. Talks to upgrade it into a comprehensive CECA continue — a stocktake was held in 2025, with agriculture and dairy remaining the sticking points.

I. India-China — Managed Thaw After Kazan 🔗

Against the backdrop of the Galwan clash (June 2020, 20 Indian soldiers), ties began thawing after disengagement at Depsang and Demchok and the Modi-Xi meeting at Kazan (October 2024) on the BRICS sidelines. In 2025: the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra resumed (June), Wang Yi visited Delhi for the 24th Special Representatives talks (August), PM Modi visited China for the SCO Tianjin summit (his first visit since 2018), and direct flights resumed (October).

L. India-Maldives Strategic Shift (2024) 🔗

Under President Mohamed Muizzu's "India Out" campaign, bilateral ties saw a strategic recalibration.

  • Troop Withdrawal: Indian military personnel operating aviation platforms (Dornier aircraft, helicopters) were replaced by civilian technical experts by mid-2024.
  • Development Aid: Despite political strains, India remained a key development partner, extending quotas for essential commodities.
📊 Types of Economic Integration Agreements

Lowest integration; tariff cuts on a limited positive list of goods
2. FTA (Free Trade Agreement) / TEPA
Eliminates tariffs on a wide range of goods (e.g., India-EFTA TEPA)
3. CEPA / CECA
Deepest integration; covers trade in goods, services, investment, and IP (e.g., India-UAE CEPA)

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar's Global Economic Footprint
  • Remittance Economy: Bilateral relations with Gulf countries (especially UAE, Saudi Arabia) directly impact Bihar. The state is one of the highest recipients of international remittances in India, which fuels household consumption in rural Bihar.
  • Gulf Diaspora & Oman CEPA: Bihar's large migrant workforce in the Gulf means the India-Oman CEPA (Dec 2025) and UAE CEPA directly affect Bihari households through jobs, wages, and safer migration/remittance channels.
  • Export of GI Tagged Items: Under bilateral trade pacts (like ECTA with Australia and CEPA with UAE), agricultural products of Bihar with GI tags (like Shahi Litchi, Jardalu Mango, and Katarni Rice) have seen rising export volumes.
  • Mithila Makhana & FTA Market Access: Mithila Makhana got its GI tag in 2022 — Bihar grows roughly 80-90% of the world's makhana — and Bihar's litchi is another GI star. New FTAs decide how far these reach premium markets: UK CETA is now in force, while the EU's GI agreement is still pending — a direct GI trade angle for Bihar.
  • Border-State Security Angle: Operation Sindoor (May 2025) and the IWT abeyance matter for eastern India too: Bihar shares an open ~726 km border with Nepal, so India-Pakistan escalation raises alert levels, troop movement, and border-management pressure across the eastern grid.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps
  • UK CETA In Force (Jul 15, 2026): Entered into force just yesterday — the freshest exam hook. Know the numbers cold: 99% of Indian exports duty-free; India 64% → 85%; Scotch 150 → 75 → 40; cars 100%+ → 10% under quota; $120bn target by 2030.
  • India-Japan Summit (Jul 2, 2026): PM Sanae Takaichi's first annual summit with PM Modi; UNICORN naval radio antenna = first-ever defence co-development; $10bn+ new investment.
  • EU FTA Ratification Track: Concluded Jan 27, 2026 ("mother of all deals") at Hyderabad House; ratification is pending with entry into force expected around early 2027. Do NOT write "in force" — a classic trap.
  • US Tariff Deal: The Feb 2, 2026 interim framework cut the reciprocal tariff from 25% to 18% and scrapped the 25% Russian-oil penalty; Mission 500 targets $500bn bilateral trade by 2030.
  • IWT Still in Abeyance: Since Apr 23, 2025, after the Pahalgam attack (Apr 22, 2025). Op Sindoor (6/7-10 May 2025) hit 9 terror sites incl. Bahawalpur (JeM HQ) and Muridke (LeT HQ); ceasefire via DGMO hotline on May 10, 2025.
  • EFTA Member States Trap: Ensure you do not include general EU states in EFTA. EFTA has only 4 members: Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein.
  • India-Maldives Transition: Pay attention to the shift of military personnel to civilian technical operators in mid-2024.
  • iCET Co-Chairs: It is led by the National Security Advisors (NSAs), not the Foreign Ministers or Trade Ministers.

c) CEPA — 3) Eliminates tariffs on a wide list of goods
