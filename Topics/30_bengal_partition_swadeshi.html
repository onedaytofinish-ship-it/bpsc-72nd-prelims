Swadeshi (Indian goods) → Boycott (of British goods, schools, courts) → National Education (Bengal National College, 1906).
"S-B-N: the three weapons of the Swadeshi Movement." Plus Vande Mataram as the anthem and Raksha Bandhan as the symbol of Hindu-Muslim unity.

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & the Bengal Partition / Swadeshi Movement
  • Bihar was part of the Bengal Presidency (1905): when Lord Curzon partitioned Bengal in 1905, Bihar was included in West Bengal (the Hindu-majority province, along with Orissa). Bihar was not a separate political entity yet — it was administered from Calcutta.
  • Bihar separated from Bengal in 1912: when the Bengal Partition was annulled in 1911, Bihar and Orissa were separated from Bengal as a new province in 1912 (capital Patna). This gave Bihar its separate political identity — a direct consequence of the Swadeshi Movement's success in forcing the annulment.
  • Limited Swadeshi impact in Bihar: the Swadeshi Movement had limited impact in Bihar compared to Bengal. Bihar lacked a strong textile industry to boycott, and its nationalist consciousness was less developed than Bengal's. The Bihar elite (mostly lawyers and landlords) were more aligned with the Moderate wing of the INC.
  • Bihar's nationalist press: the Searchlight (Patna, founded 1917) and the Hindustan Review (Sachchidananda Sinha) carried Swadeshi-era ideas to Bihar, but the mass mobilisation was weaker than in Bengal.
  • Champaran (1917) was Bihar's first mass nationalist movement: the Swadeshi Movement (1905) bypassed Bihar; Gandhi's Champaran Satyagraha (1917) was Bihar's first true mass movement — 12 years after the Swadeshi Movement.
  • Patna University (1917): the National Education movement (a Swadeshi plank) had limited impact in Bihar — Patna University was established in 1917, much later than Calcutta University's nationalist institutions.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps
  • Partition date trap: October 16, 1905 (NOT 1903 — that was the announcement; NOT 1911 — that was the annulment). Lord Curzon (NOT Minto — Minto was the 1909 Reforms Viceroy).
  • Vande Mataram source trap: Bankim Chandra Chatterjee's Anandamath (1882) — NOT Tagore's Gitanjali. Bankim died in 1894, before the Swadeshi Movement; his song was adopted by it posthumously.
  • Annulment year trap: 1911 (NOT 1909 — that was the Morley-Minto Reforms; NOT 1912 — that was Bihar's separation from Bengal). The annulment was announced at the Delhi Durbar (December 1911).
  • Muslim League founded 1906 (NOT 1905): December 30, 1906, at Dacca. The Swadeshi Movement (1905) preceded it; the League was partly a response to the Hindu-dominated Swadeshi Movement.
  • S-T-S strategy trap: "Struggle-Truce-Struggle" first came with the Swadeshi Movement (70th BPSC PYQ) — NOT with the Quit India Movement or Khilafat. The S-T-S pattern was first exhibited during 1905–1911.
  • Bande Mataram newspaper ≠ Vande Mataram song: the song was by Bankim Chandra (1882); the newspaper was by Aurobindo Ghosh (1906). Don't confuse the two.
  • Surat Split year trap: 1907 (NOT 1905 or 1906). 1905 = Bengal Partition; 1906 = four-fold programme (Calcutta session); 1907 = Surat Split.

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Partition of Bengal was carried out in which year and by which Viceroy?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Partition of Bengal was carried out on October 16, 1905, by Lord Curzon (Viceroy 1899–1905). The official reason was administrative efficiency (Bengal was too large), but the real motive was to divide the nationalist movement by separating Hindu-majority West Bengal from Muslim-majority East Bengal (including Assam). It triggered the Swadeshi Movement.
Q2. The Partition of Bengal was annulled in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Partition of Bengal was annulled (reversed) in 1911 by King-Emperor George V at the Delhi Durbar. The annulment was due to sustained Swadeshi resistance and the British desire to appease Bengali Muslims (who had been advantaged by the partition) was abandoned. In 1912, Bihar and Orissa were separated from Bengal as a new province (capital Patna).
Q3. The Swadeshi Movement was formally launched in response to which event?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Swadeshi Movement was launched in response to the Partition of Bengal (October 16, 1905). It was formally endorsed at the 1906 INC Calcutta session (presided by Dadabhai Naoroji) as part of the four-fold programme: Swaraj, Swadeshi, Boycott, National Education. It marked the rise of the Extremist wing (Lal-Bal-Pal) and the beginning of mass politics in India.
Q4. Who among the following was the chief architect of the Bengal Partition?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Lord Curzon (Viceroy 1899–1905) was the chief architect of the Bengal Partition. He announced it in 1903 (officially) and implemented it on October 16, 1905, just before he left India. His other controversial acts: the Calcutta Corporation Act (1899), the Indian Universities Act (1904), and the Partition of Bengal (1905). He is known for his administrative efficiency but also his arrogant imperial attitude.
Q5. The Swadeshi Movement's 'boycott' initially targeted which type of goods?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Swadeshi Movement's boycott initially targeted British textiles and manufactured goods (especially Manchester textiles). Indians were urged to use Indian-made (swadeshi) goods instead. The movement later expanded to boycott British schools, courts, and titles. It was both economic (hurting British commercial interests) and political (demonstrating Indian unity).
Q6. The slogan 'Bande Mataram' became a rallying cry of the Swadeshi Movement. It was taken from which literary work?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 'Bande Mataram' (Vande Mataram) was taken from Bankim Chandra Chatterjee's novel 'Anandamath' (1882). It became the rallying cry of the Swadeshi Movement after 1905. Aurobindo Ghosh started the newspaper 'Bande Mataram' (1906) which propagated the Swadeshi ideology. The song Vande Mataram was later adopted as the National Song of India (first stanza only, by constitutional assembly resolution, January 24, 1950).
Q7. Who started the newspaper 'Bande Mataram' in 1906, propagating the Swadeshi ideology?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sri Aurobindo Ghosh (1872–1950) started the newspaper 'Bande Mataram' in 1906. It was the most radical newspaper of the Swadeshi era, advocating total independence (Purna Swaraj) long before the INC formally demanded it. Aurobindo later turned to spirituality, founded the Sri Aurobindo Ashram in Pondicherry (1910), and developed 'Integral Yoga'.
Q8. The National Council of Education was established during the Swadeshi Movement in which year and where?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The National Council of Education was established in 1906 in Calcutta to promote national education as an alternative to British government schools. It set up the Bengal National College (with Aurobindo Ghosh as its first principal) and the Bengal Technical Institute. This was part of the four-fold programme of 1906 (Swaraj, Swadeshi, Boycott, National Education).
Q9. The political strategy 'Struggle-Truce-Struggle' (S-T-S) was first associated with which movement? (70th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The 'Struggle-Truce-Struggle' (S-T-S) strategy was first associated with the Swadeshi Movement (70th BPSC PYQ). The movement went through phases of active struggle (1905–1906), truce (when repression set in, 1907–1908), and renewed struggle. This pattern (struggle → truce → struggle) became the model for later nationalist movements, including Gandhi's Civil Disobedience Movement.
Q10. Who was the Bengali leader who led the Swadeshi Movement through the journal 'New India' and advocated 'Passive Resistance'?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bipin Chandra Pal (1858–1932), the 'Pal' of Lal-Bal-Pal, led the Swadeshi Movement through his journal 'New India' and advocated 'Passive Resistance' as a strategy. He was a fiery orator and writer. He visited England and America to spread the nationalist message. He was one of the three Extremist leaders (Lal-Bal-Pal) of the Swadeshi era.
Q11. The All India Muslim League was founded in which year, largely in response to the Hindu-Muslim tensions accentuated by the Bengal Partition?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The All India Muslim League was founded on December 30, 1906, at Dacca (Dhaka), with the Nawab of Dacca (Salimullah) as the moving spirit. It was formed partly in response to the Hindu-dominated Swadeshi Movement and partly due to British encouragement (Minto-Morley Reforms, separate electorates for Muslims in 1909). Its first President was Sir Aga Khan (III).
Q12. The Swadeshi Movement saw the use of which unique symbol of national unity during the protests?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — During the Swadeshi Movement, Rabindranath Tagore initiated the use of Raksha Bandhan (tying rakhis) as a symbol of Hindu-Muslim unity. Hindus tied rakhis on the wrists of Muslims, symbolising brotherhood against the partition. Tagore also led protest processions in Calcutta singing 'Bande Mataram'. This was a creative cultural innovation of the Swadeshi era.
Q13. Who among the following was NOT a leader of the Swadeshi Movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Lord Curzon (1859–1925) was NOT a leader of the Swadeshi Movement — he was the Viceroy who carried out the Bengal Partition (the trigger of the movement). Aurobindo Ghosh, Bipin Chandra Pal, and B.G. Tilak were all Extremist leaders of the Swadeshi Movement. Curzon was the target of the movement, not a participant in it.
Q14. The 1905 Bengal Partition created which two new provinces?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1905 Bengal Partition created two new provinces: (1) Eastern Bengal and Assam (capital Dacca, including Chittagong, Dacca, Rajshahi, and Assam) with a Muslim majority; (2) West Bengal (including Bihar and Orissa) with a Hindu majority. The division was designed to weaken the nationalist movement by dividing Bengali-speaking people on religious lines.
Q15. Who presided over the 1905 Banaras session of the INC, held during the early Swadeshi Movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — G.K. Gokhale presided over the 1905 Banaras (Varanasi) session of the INC — held in December 1905, two months after the Bengal Partition. This session was still Moderate-dominated; the Extremists' victory came at the 1906 Calcutta session (Naoroji's presidency, four-fold programme). Gokhale's Banaras presidency was a transitional moment between the Moderate and Extremist eras.
Q16. The 'Swadeshi' concept was first applied in which region during the anti-Partition agitation?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 'Swadeshi' concept was first applied in Bengal during the anti-Partition agitation (1905). Bengalis boycotted British goods and promoted Indian-made goods. The movement later spread to other regions (Punjab under Lajpat Rai, Maharashtra under Tilak, Madras under Subramania Iyer). Bengal was the epicentre because the partition directly affected Bengali identity.
Q17. Who composed the song 'Vande Mataram' which became the anthem of the Swadeshi Movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bankim Chandra Chatterjee (1838–1894) composed the song 'Vande Mataram' as part of his novel 'Anandamath' (1882). It became the anthem of the Swadeshi Movement after 1905. The first two stanzas were adopted as the National Song of India by the Constituent Assembly on January 24, 1950. Tagore composed 'Jana Gana Mana' (the National Anthem, 1911).
Q18. Consider the following about the Bengal Partition: 1. It was carried out by Lord Curzon in 1905. 2. It was annulled in 1911. 3. It led to the Swadeshi Movement. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct: (1) Lord Curzon carried out the Bengal Partition on October 16, 1905. (2) It was annulled in 1911 (Delhi Durbar, King-Emperor George V). (3) It led to the Swadeshi Movement (1905–1908), the first mass nationalist movement in India. The annulment was a major victory for the Swadeshi Movement, though it also reflected British divide-and-rule calculations.
Q19. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Bengal Partition 2. Swadeshi Movement launched 3. Muslim League founded 4. Partition annulled
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bengal Partition (October 16, 1905) → Swadeshi Movement launched (immediately after, late 1905) → Muslim League founded (December 30, 1906) → Partition annulled (1911, Delhi Durbar). The Swadeshi Movement was the direct response to the partition; the Muslim League was formed partly as a counter to the Hindu-dominated Swadeshi Movement; the annulment (1911) was the culmination of sustained Swadeshi resistance.
Q20. The Bengal National College was founded during the Swadeshi Movement. Who was its first principal?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sri Aurobindo Ghosh was the first principal of the Bengal National College, founded in 1906 as part of the National Council of Education (a swadeshi initiative). He served briefly before leaving to focus on political journalism (Bande Mataram newspaper). The College was part of the national education movement — an alternative to British government-run institutions.
Q21. The Swadeshi Movement was significant because it marked the first use of which political strategy in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Swadeshi Movement (1905) marked the first use of economic boycott of British goods as a political weapon in India. Indians boycotted British textiles and promoted swadeshi (Indian-made) goods. This was a novel strategy — previous Moderate-era tactics were petitions and prayers. The Swadeshi Movement also marked the beginning of mass politics (students, women, peasants) and the rise of the Extremists.
Q22. Bihar's connection with the Bengal Partition (1905) was that:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar was part of the Bengal Presidency in 1905. The Bengal Partition (1905) placed Bihar in West Bengal (the Hindu-majority province). When the partition was annulled in 1911, Bihar was separated from Bengal in 1912 as a new province (capital Patna). The Swadeshi Movement had limited impact in Bihar (compared to Bengal) — Bihar's first major nationalist movement was Champaran (1917, Gandhi).
Q23. Who among the following Bengali leaders joined the Swadeshi Movement but later returned to the Moderate fold after the Surat Split (1907)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Surendranath Banerjea (1848–1925) initially supported the Swadeshi Movement but was a Moderate at heart. After the 1907 Surat Split, he remained with the Moderates. He was known as 'Surrender Not' Banerjea. He founded the Indian Association (1876) and was a leading Bengali Moderate. He later accepted knighthood (1913) and became a minister in the Bengal government — distancing himself from the Extremists.
Q24. The Surat Split of 1907 was directly triggered by the disagreement over which issue?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Surat Split (1907) was triggered by the disagreement between Moderates (who wanted to return to constitutional methods) and Extremists (who wanted to extend the Swadeshi Movement's four-fold programme of Swaraj, Swadeshi, Boycott, and National Education). The Moderates, led by Pherozeshah Mehta and Gokhale, won control of the INC; the Extremists (Lal-Bal-Pal) were expelled. The split lasted until 1916 (Lucknow Pact).
Q25. The Indian Councils Act of 1909 (Morley-Minto Reforms) was a direct response to which movement's pressures?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Indian Councils Act of 1909 (Morley-Minto Reforms) was a direct response to the Swadeshi Movement's pressures and the Muslim League's demands. It introduced separate electorates for Muslims (the 'communal veto') and enlarged the Legislative Councils. John Morley (Secretary of State) and Lord Minto (Viceroy) were its architects. It was the first British acknowledgement of the need for political reform, but also institutionalised the communal divide.
