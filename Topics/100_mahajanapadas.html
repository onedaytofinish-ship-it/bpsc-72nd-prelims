The 16 Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE) emerged from the Later Vedic janapadas. Magadha (Bihar) became the most powerful — its capital Rajgir (surrounded by 5 hills) was naturally defended, and its later capital Pataliputra (at the confluence of Ganga, Son, and Punpun rivers) controlled the Ganga valley trade. 3 of the 16 Mahajanapadas were in Bihar: Magadha, Vajji (Vaishali), and Anga (Champa).

C. Factors for Magadha's Rise EXAM CORE

  • Fertile land: Ganga valley agriculture → surplus → taxes → large army
  • Iron deposits: Magadha controlled the iron-producing areas (Singhbhum/Gaya) → weapons
  • Elephants: access to elephant forests → war elephants (advantage in battle)
  • Ambitious kings: Bimbisara (expansion through conquest + marriages), Ajatashatru (conquered Vajji + Kosala), Mahapadma Nanda (conquered all Kshatriya kingdoms)
  • Strategic location: Rajgir (surrounded by 5 hills — natural defence) → Pataliputra (at the confluence of Ganga, Son, Punpun — trade + defence)
  • River trade: the Ganga enabled trade with the east (Bengal) and west (UP) — revenue from trade

Iron — key to Magadha's rise. Iron weapons gave Magadha's army a technological edge; iron ploughshares enabled intensive agriculture in the Ganga valley. Magadha controlled the iron-producing areas of Singhbhum and Gaya (both in Bihar). The Later Vedic Iron Age (~1000 BCE) enabled the second urbanisation that produced the Mahajanapadas.

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — The Heartland of the Mahajanapadas
  • Magadha (Rajgir/Pataliputra): the most powerful Mahajanapada — eventually conquered all others and became the nucleus of the Mauryan Empire. Capital: Rajgir (surrounded by 5 hills) → Pataliputra (confluence of Ganga, Son, Punpun). Key rulers: Bimbisara (founder Haryanka), Ajatashatru (conquered Vajji), Mahapadma Nanda (destroyer of Kshatriyas).
  • Vajji (Vaishali): a republic (gana-sangha) — confederacy of clans (Licchavi). One of the world's earliest republics. Birthplace of Mahavira (Jainism). Site of the Second Buddhist Council (383 BCE). Conquered by Ajatashatru after a 16-year war.
  • Anga (Champa, Bhagalpur): conquered by Bimbisara — the first major expansion of Magadha. Champa was a major port on the Ganga — controlling trade with the east.
  • Buddhist Councils: all four Buddhist councils were associated with Bihar (First at Rajgir, Second at Vaishali, Third at Pataliputra, Fourth related to Kashmir but Buddhism's heart was Bihar). Bihar was the cradle of Buddhism.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 99 (Vedic Age): The Later Vedic janapadas evolved into the 16 Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE). The Videha kingdom (Mithila, Bihar) was absorbed by the Vajji confederacy, which was then conquered by Magadha.
  • Topic 101 (Buddhism & Jainism): Both Buddhism and Jainism originated in the Mahajanapada period (6th century BCE). The Buddha and Mahavira were contemporaries — both preached in the Mahajanapada kingdoms (especially Magadha, Kosala, Vajji). The first three Buddhist councils were held in Bihar.
  • Topic 102 (Mauryan Empire): Chandragupta Maurya overthrew the last Nanda king (Dhana Nanda, 322 BCE) and founded the Mauryan Empire — the first pan-Indian empire. The Mauryan capital was Pataliputra (Bihar).
📰 Current Relevance
  • Rajgir: a major tourist and pilgrimage destination — site of the Saptaparni cave (First Buddhist Council), Gridhakuta (Vulture Peak, where the Buddha preached), and hot springs.
  • Vaishali: one of the world's earliest republics — a heritage site in Bihar, with the Ashokan pillar (Lion Capital) and the relic stupa of the Buddha.
  • Pataliputra (Patna): modern Patna — the capital of Bihar — was one of the largest cities in the ancient world. It was described by Megasthenes (the Greek ambassador, c. 300 BCE) in his 'Indica'.

Q1. How many Mahajanapadas existed in ancient India around 600 BCE?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 16 Mahajanapadas (~600 BCE). Listed in Buddhist text Anguttara Nikaya + Jain text Bhagavati Sutra. Most powerful: Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa, Avanti.
Q2. Which of the following was the most powerful Mahajanapada?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Magadha (Bihar) = most powerful. Capital: Rajgir → Pataliputra. Advantages: fertile land, iron, elephants, ambitious kings, strategic location. Eventually became the Mauryan Empire.
Q3. Magadha's first capital was:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Rajgir (Girivraja, 'surrounded by hills') = first capital. Surrounded by 5 hills = natural defence. Bimbisara's capital. Later shifted to Pataliputra (Ajatashatru/Udayin).
Q4. Who was the founder of the Haryanka dynasty of Magadha?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bimbisara (543-492 BCE) founded the Haryanka dynasty. Expanded through conquest (Anga) + marriages. Patron of Buddhism. Imprisoned and killed by son Ajatashatru.
Q5. Ajatashatru (c. 492-460 BCE) is known for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Ajatashatru: conquered Vaishali (16-year war, c. 485 BCE) + Kosala; sponsored First Buddhist Council (Rajgir, 483 BCE); shifted capital to Pataliputra. Killed his father Bimbisara.
Q6. The three dynasties that ruled Magadha before the Mauryas were:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Haryanka (543-412, Bimbisara/Ajatashatru) → Shishunaga (412-344) → Nanda (344-322, Mahapadma/Dhana). Nandas overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya (322 BCE).
Q7. Mahapadma Nanda, the founder of the Nanda dynasty, was known as:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mahapadma Nanda = 'destroyer of Kshatriyas.' Shudra background. First standing army (200,000 infantry, 20,000 cavalry, 3,000 elephants). Overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya (322 BCE).
Q8. Which Mahajanapada was a republic (gana-sangha) rather than a monarchy?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Vajji (Vaishali, Bihar) = republic (gana-sangha). Confederacy of clans (Licchavi). One of world's earliest republics. Birthplace of Mahavira. Conquered by Ajatashatru (c. 485 BCE).
Q9. Vaishali, the capital of the Vajji confederacy, is in which modern state?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Vaishali is in Bihar (Muzaffarpur/Vaishali district). Birthplace of Mahavira. Site of Second Buddhist Council (383 BCE). Buddha's last sermon. One of world's earliest republics.
Q10. The Vajji confederacy was conquered by which Magadhan king after a 16-year war?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Ajatashatru conquered Vaishali after a 16-year war (c. 485 BCE). Used minister Vassakara to sow dissension + mahashilakantaka (catapult) + war elephant. Made Magadha dominant in Bihar.
Q11. Which Mahajanapada was known for its cotton textiles and trade?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Kasi (Varanasi) = cotton textiles + trade. Banarsi silk legacy. Holiest city in Hinduism. Conquered by Kosala (King Prasenajit).
Q12. Kosala, one of the four powerful Mahajanapadas, had its capital at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Kosala capital: Sravasti (UP). King Prasenajit (Buddha's contemporary). Buddha spent time at Jetavana monastery. Conquered by Magadha (Ajatashatru).
Q13. The Second Buddhist Council was held at:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Second Council: Vaishali (c. 383 BCE), during Kalashoka (Shishunaga). Dispute over monastic rules (gold/silver). Schism: Sthaviras (Theravada) vs Mahasanghikas (Mahayana).
Q14. The First Buddhist Council was held at:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — First Council: Rajgir (Saptaparni cave, c. 483 BCE), sponsored by Ajatashatru. Presided by Mahakassapa. Compiled Sutta Pitaka (Ananda) + Vinaya Pitaka (Upali). Established Buddhist canon (Tripitaka).
Q15. Magadha's rise to power was due to which factors?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Multiple factors: fertile Ganga valley (surplus → taxes → army), iron (weapons, Singhbhum/Gaya), elephants (war), ambitious kings (Bimbisara, Ajatashatru, Mahapadma Nanda), strategic location (Rajgir hills, Pataliputra rivers).
Q16. The Anga Mahajanapada (capital: Champa, in modern Bihar) was conquered by which Magadhan king?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bimbisara conquered Anga (capital: Champa, Bhagalpur, Bihar). First major expansion of Magadha. Champa was a major Ganga port — controlling trade with the east.
Q17. Pataliputra (modern Patna) was founded as Magadha's capital by which king?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Ajatashatru founded Pataliputra (as a fort); Udayin made it the permanent capital. At confluence of Ganga, Son, Punpun. One of the largest cities in the ancient world (Megasthenes' Indica).
Q18. Arrange the Magadhan dynasties in chronological order: 1. Haryanka 2. Shishunaga 3. Nanda 4. Maurya
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Haryanka (543-412) → Shishunaga (412-344) → Nanda (344-322) → Maurya (322-185). HSNM mnemonic. Nandas overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya (322 BCE) with Chanakya.
Q19. Which Mahajanapada was located in modern Gujarat/Maharashtra?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Assaka (Asmaka) = southernmost Mahajanapada (Gujarat/Maharashtra). Capital: Pratishthana (Paithan, on Godavari). Only Mahajanapada south of Vindhyas. Conquered by Satavahanas.
Q20. The Shishunaga dynasty was founded by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Shishunaga (an amatya/official) overthrew the last Haryanka king (Nagadasaka). Conquered Avanti (Ujjain) — unifying north India under Magadha. Son Kalashoka: Second Buddhist Council (Vaishali, 383 BCE).
Q21. The Nanda dynasty was overthrown by:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Chandragupta Maurya (322 BCE) overthrew the last Nanda king (Dhana Nanda) with Chanakya (Kautilya, author of Arthashastra). Founded the Mauryan Empire — first pan-Indian empire.
Q22. Bihar's connection with the Mahajanapadas was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 3 Mahajanapadas in Bihar: Magadha (most powerful, Rajgir/Pataliputra), Vajji (republic, Vaishali, birthplace of Mahavira), Anga (Champa, Bhagalpur). Bihar = heartland of Mahajanapadas + Buddhism + Jainism.
Q23. Bimbisara was a patron of which religion?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Bimbisara = patron of Buddhism. Met the Buddha (c. 528 BCE), became follower. Gave Veluvana monastery (first Buddhist monastery, near Rajgir). Also patronised Jainism (Mahavira was contemporary).
Q24. The Vajji confederacy was praised by the Buddha for which of its practices?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Buddha praised Vajji's democratic system (Mahaparinibbana Sutta): 'seven conditions of welfare' — regular meetings, concord, traditions, respect for elders/women/shrines/Dharma. Ajatashatru undermined these before conquering.
Q25. Which Magadhan king is associated with the shift of capital from Rajgir to Pataliputra?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Udayin (c. 460-444 BCE, Shishunaga) formally shifted capital to Pataliputra. Ajatashatru founded it as a fort. Pataliputra at confluence of Ganga/Son/Punpun = trade + defence. Capital of Magadha/Mauryas/Guptas for 800+ years.
