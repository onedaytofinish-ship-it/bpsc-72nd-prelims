Gupta Empire — Golden Age — PATALIPUTRA
📂 Ancient History ⚖️ Weight: 0.7 🎯 Expected: 2–3 Qs 🔶 Bihar: Pataliputra (capital), Nalanda University, Gupta heartland
📚 Core Content
EXAM TIP Gupta Empire (c. 320-550 CE, ~230 years): Founded by Srigupta, real founder = Chandragupta I (Gupta Era 319-320 CE). Peak under Samudragupta (Napoleon of India) + Chandragupta II Vikramaditya (Navaratna, defeated Shakas 409 CE). Capital: Pataliputra (Bihar). 'Golden Age' — Kalidasa, Aryabhata, Ajanta paintings, Nalanda University (Kumargupta I, 427 CE). Decline: Hun invasions (5th-6th c.), Skandagupta fought them. Fa-Hien visited (405-411 CE).
Bodh Gaya (Bihar) — near the Gupta capital Pataliputra. The Guptas patronised Buddhist sites and built the original Mahabodhi Temple complex.
Gupta 'Golden Age' saw the emergence of early Hindu temple architecture — the template for all later Indian temples.

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Gupta Heartland and Golden Age Centre
  • Pataliputra (Patna): the Gupta capital — administrative and cultural centre of the 'Golden Age.' Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, and Chandragupta II all ruled from Pataliputra (Bihar). The Allahabad Pillar (originally at Prayag) records Samudragupta's conquests.
  • Nalanda University: founded by Kumargupta I (c. 427 CE) in Bihar — near Rajgir. It became the world's greatest centre of Buddhist learning (10,000 students, 2,000 teachers from China, Korea, Tibet, Central Asia). Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE). UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016).
  • Gupta origin: the Guptas likely originated from the Magadha region (Bihar) — they were initially a local family that rose to power. Pataliputra had been the capital of the Mauryas and Shungas before the Guptas.
  • Buddhist sites: the Guptas patronised Buddhist sites in Bihar — Bodh Gaya, Nalanda, Rajgir. Although Hindu (Vaishnavite), they were tolerant of Buddhism. Nalanda flourished under Gupta patronage.
  • Hiuen Tsang at Nalanda: the Chinese pilgrim Hiuen Tsang studied at Nalanda (Bihar) for 5 years (c. 630-645 CE) — during the post-Gupta period (Harsha's reign). His account is a crucial source for Nalanda and Bihar.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 103 (Post-Mauryan): After the Post-Mauryan fragmentation (Shungas, Kushanas, Satavahanas), the Guptas reunified north India from Pataliputra (c. 320 CE). The Guptas inherited the Mauryan capital and restored centralized rule.
  • Topic 101 (Buddhism & Jainism): The Guptas (Hindu/Vaishnavite) patronised Buddhism — founding Nalanda University (Bihar). Buddhist art flourished (Ajanta caves). Kalidasa wrote Buddhist-influenced literature. Hinduism was state religion but Buddhism was tolerated.
  • Topic 102 (Mauryan Empire): Both the Mauryas and Guptas ruled from Pataliputra (Bihar). The Mauryas (321-185 BCE) and Guptas (320-550 CE) were the two greatest empires of ancient India — both centred in Bihar.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Nalanda University: a new Nalanda University was established (2014) near the ancient ruins — as an international university, reviving the ancient legacy. UNESCO World Heritage Site (2016). A symbol of India's educational heritage.
  • Aryabhata's legacy: India's first satellite was named 'Aryabhata' (1975). Aryabhata's calculation of pi and his heliocentric insights are celebrated as foundational to Indian mathematics and astronomy.
  • Kalidasa's Shakuntala: translated into English by William Jones (1789) — it created a sensation in Europe. Goethe admired it. Kalidasa is often called the 'Shakespeare of India.'
  • Iron Pillar of Delhi: a testament to Gupta-era metallurgy — it has not rusted in ~1600 years. It is a major tourist attraction at Mehrauli (Delhi) and a subject of scientific research.
  • Ajanta Caves: UNESCO World Heritage Site (1983). The Gupta-era frescoes are considered some of the finest art of ancient India — inspiring artists and scholars worldwide.

Q1. Who founded the Gupta Empire and when?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Srigupta (c. 240-280 CE) was the founder. First Gupta king, ruled from Pataliputra (Bihar). Son Ghatotkacha succeeded. Gupta Era from Chandragupta I (319-320 CE).
Q2. Chandragupta I (c. 319-334 CE) is considered the real founder of the Gupta Empire. What was his key achievement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chandragupta I: started Gupta Era (319-320 CE), title 'Maharajadhiraja,' married Kumaradevi (Lichchhavi princess). Ruled from Pataliputra (Bihar).
Q3. Samudragupta (c. 335-380 CE), the greatest Gupta conqueror, is known by which title?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Samudragupta = 'Napoleon of India' (V.A. Smith). Allahabad Pillar (by Harisena) records conquests: Aryavarta (direct rule), Dakshinapatha (12 kings defeated, released), frontier kings (tribute). Also musician, poet.
Q4. The Allahabad Pillar Inscription, which records Samudragupta's conquests, was composed by which poet?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Harisena (court poet, minister, commander). Allahabad Pillar = 'Prayag Prasasti.' Records Samudragupta's conquests and his skills (music, poetry, scholarship).
Q5. Chandragupta II Vikramaditya (c. 380-415 CE) is famous for defeating which foreign tribe?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chandragupta II defeated Shakas (409 CE), killed Rudrasimha III, annexed Gujarat + Malwa. Title 'Sakari' + 'Vikramaditya.' Access to western ports (Broach, Kalyan, Sopara) and Roman trade.
Q6. The 'Navaratna' (Nine Gems) — a council of nine scholars — adorned whose court?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Navaratna (Nine Gems) in Chandragupta II's court. Famous: Kalidasa (poet), Varahamihira (astronomer), Dhanvantari (physician), Amarasimha (lexicographer), Shanku (architecture). Peak of Golden Age.
Q7. Kalidasa, the greatest Sanskrit poet and dramatist, lived during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kalidasa (Gupta period, 4th-5th c. CE). Navaratna of Chandragupta II. Works: Shakuntala, Meghaduta, Raghuvamsha, Kumarasambhava, Ritusamhara. Sanskrit. 'Shakespeare of India.'
Q8. The Gupta Empire's capital was at which city (in modern Bihar)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Pataliputra (Patna, Bihar) — continuing Mauryan and Shunga tradition. Chandragupta II also had Ujjain as second capital (after conquering Malwa). Centre of Golden Age.
Q9. Nalanda University, the great Buddhist centre of learning, was founded during the Gupta period by which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kumargupta I founded Nalanda (c. 427 CE, near Rajgir, Bihar). 10,000 students, 2,000 teachers from China, Korea, Tibet, Central Asia. Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE). UNESCO (2016).
Q10. Fa-Hien (Faxian), the Chinese Buddhist pilgrim, visited India during which Gupta king's reign?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Fa-Hien visited during Chandragupta II's reign (405-411 CE). 'Fo-kwo-ki' describes Gupta India: peaceful, prosperous, low taxes, no capital punishment. Visited Lumbini, Bodh Gaya, Sarnath, Kushinagar.
Q11. The Gupta period is called the 'Golden Age' of ancient India. Which of the following is a key reason?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Golden Age: literature (Kalidasa), science (Aryabhata), medicine (Sushruta, Charaka), art (Ajanta), architecture (temples), metallurgy (Iron Pillar), religion (Hinduism + Buddhism). Extraordinary achievements across all fields.
Q12. Aryabhata, the great mathematician and astronomer, lived during the Gupta period. Which of the following is his contribution?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Aryabhata (c. 476-550 CE), Aryabhatiya (499 CE): pi (~3.1416), Earth rotation, eclipses (shadows), place-value, sine table. Age 23 when wrote it. India's first satellite named 'Aryabhata' (1975).
Q13. The famous Ajanta Caves (Buddhist rock-cut caves with magnificent paintings) were mostly built during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ajanta Caves (Maharashtra, 29 caves) mostly Gupta period (4th-6th c. CE). Caves 16-27 have magnificent frescoes (Jataka tales). Some earlier (2nd c. BCE). UNESCO (1983).
Q14. The Iron Pillar of Delhi (Mehrauli) — famous for not rusting — was built during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Iron Pillar of Delhi (Mehrauli) — Gupta period (Chandragupta II). ~7m tall, ~6 tons. Not rusted in ~1600 years. Sanskrit inscription praises 'Chandra' (Chandragupta II). Originally at Udayagiri.
Q15. The Gupta Empire declined due to invasions by which foreign tribe?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Huns (White Huns / Hephthalites) from Central Asia (5th-6th c. CE). Skandagupta defeated them but treasury exhausted. Toramana and Mihirakula (c. 510-530 CE) finally broke Gupta power.
Q16. Skandagupta (c. 455-467 CE), the last great Gupta emperor, is known for which achievement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Skandagupta (455-467 CE) defeated Huns + restored Sudarshana Lake (Junagadh inscription). But wars exhausted treasury (gold coins lighter). Empire fragmented after him.
Q17. The Gupta Empire followed which religion as its state religion?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Guptas = Brahminical Hinduism (Vaishnavism). Chandragupta II = 'Paramabhagavata' (supreme devotee of Vishnu). But tolerant — patronised Buddhism (Nalanda), Jainism.
Q18. The Gupta administration was characterized by which feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gupta admin = decentralized. Feudatories (samantas), land grants (agraharas) to Brahmins/temples, provinces (bhuktis), districts (vishayas). This feudal structure eventually weakened the empire.
Q19. Hiuen Tsang (Xuanzang), the Chinese Buddhist pilgrim, visited India during which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Hiuen Tsang visited during Harsha's reign (c. 630-645 CE, post-Gupta). 'Si-yu-ki.' Studied at Nalanda (Bihar) for 5 years. Met Harsha. Crucial source for post-Gupta India.
Q20. The position of women during the Gupta period was characterized by which feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Women's status declined: no property rights (except stridhana), child marriage, sati began (first record: Eran, 510 CE), no education. Upper-class women (queens) were educated (Kumaradevi).
Q21. The caste system during the Gupta period was characterized by which feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Caste system proliferated: new sub-castes (jatis) emerged (varna-sankara, occupational groups, absorption of foreigners). Untouchability increased (chandalas). Number of castes multiplied.
Q22. Bihar's connection with the Gupta Empire is primarily through which fact?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar = Gupta heartland: (1) Pataliputra (Patna) was the Gupta capital. (2) Nalanda University founded by Kumargupta I (427 CE) in Bihar. (3) Guptas likely originated from Magadha (Bihar). Golden Age centred in Bihar!
Q23. The Guptas issued which type of coins primarily?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Guptas issued gold coins (dinaras) — largest gold coinage in ancient India. Portraits of kings (archer, tiger-slayer, lute-player) + titles. Silver (rupaka) after conquering Gujarat. Later coins became lighter (decline).
Q24. Which Gupta king is known as the 'musician king' — depicted playing the veena (lute) on his coins?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Samudragupta = 'musician king' (depicted playing veena on coins). Polymath: warrior (Napoleon of India), poet ('Kaviraja'), musician (veena), scholar. Allahabad inscription describes his talents.
Q25. The Gupta Era (starting c. 319-320 CE) is still used in India for which purpose?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gupta Era (319-320 CE) was historically significant for dating inscriptions, grants, and texts for ~300+ years. While Saka Era (78 CE) is the official National Calendar today, the Gupta Era was widely used in ancient India.
