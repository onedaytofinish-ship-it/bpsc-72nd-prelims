🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & the Civil Disobedience Movement
  • Salt Satyagraha at Patna: during the Civil Disobedience Movement (1930), salt law was violated on the banks of the Ganga at Patna. Bihar's salt satyagraha was part of the nationwide salt satyagraha movement that followed Gandhi's Dandi March.
  • No-tax campaigns in Bihar: Bihar's villages, particularly in the Tirhut and Champaran regions, had no-tax campaigns during the Civil Disobedience Movement. The Bihar Congress, led by Rajendra Prasad and Anugrah Narayan Sinha, organised these campaigns.
  • Tribal satyagrahas in Chhotanagpur: the tribal regions of Chhotanagpur (now Jharkhand, then part of Bihar) had satyagrahas against forest laws and land revenue. The Santhal and Munda tribal communities participated.
  • Rajendra Prasad's role: Dr. Rajendra Prasad was the key Bihar Congress leader during the Civil Disobedience Movement. He was arrested and imprisoned. After independence, he became the first President of India (1950-1962).
  • Anugrah Narayan Sinha: A.N. Sinha was another key Bihar leader. He was imprisoned during the movement. After independence, he became Bihar's first Deputy Chief Minister.
  • Bihar's limited impact (compared to Gujarat/Maharashtra): while Bihar participated, the Civil Disobedience Movement's strongest base was in Gujarat (Gandhi's home state) and Maharashtra. Bihar's contribution was significant but not as central as in the Champaran (1917) or Quit India (1942) movements.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps
  • GoI Act 1935 date trap: 1935 (NOT 1937 — that was when provincial elections were held under the Act; NOT 1932 — that was the Communal Award). The Act was passed in 1935 but came into force in 1937.
  • Three RTCs (NOT one or two): First (1930-31, INC boycotted), Second (1931, Gandhi attended), Third (1932, INC boycotted). Gandhi attended only the Second RTC.
  • Purna Swaraj was 1929 (NOT 1930): the Lahore session (December 1929) demanded Purna Swaraj; January 26, 1930 was the first Independence Day. The Dandi March (March 1930) followed.
  • Gandhi-Irwin Pact ≠ Poona Pact: Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 1931) was about suspending Civil Disobedience and attending RTC. Poona Pact (September 1932) was Gandhi + Ambedkar about Depressed Classes representation. Two different pacts.
  • Simon Commission (1927) ≠ Simon Commission report (1930): appointed 1927, arrived India 1928, report submitted 1930. The report led to the Round Table Conferences.
  • Nehru Report ≠ Nehru as PM: the Nehru Report (1928) was chaired by Motilal Nehru (Jawaharlal's father). Jawaharlal Nehru was a committee member. The Report was the first Indian constitution draft.
  • GoI Act 1935 did NOT introduce universal franchise: voting was limited to ~10% (property/education qualifications). Universal adult franchise came with the Indian Constitution (1950).

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Civil Disobedience Movement was launched by Gandhi with the Dandi Salt March in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Civil Disobedience Movement was launched on March 12, 1930, with the Dandi Salt March from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi (Gujarat coast), 240 miles. Gandhi reached Dandi on April 6, 1930, and broke the salt law by making salt from seawater. This symbolic act launched the Civil Disobedience Movement, which lasted until 1934.
Q2. The Purna Swaraj (complete independence) was demanded at which INC session, leading to the Civil Disobedience Movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Purna Swaraj (complete independence) was demanded at the 1929 Lahore session of the INC, presided over by Jawaharlal Nehru. January 26, 1930 was declared 'Independence Day.' This was a shift from dominion status to complete independence. The Civil Disobedience Movement (1930) followed. January 26 was later chosen as India's Republic Day (1950).
Q3. The Simon Commission was boycotted by Indians because it had no Indian member. In which year was it appointed?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Simon Commission was appointed in 1927 (arrived in India February 1928) to review the Government of India Act 1919 and recommend constitutional reforms. It was boycotted by all Indian parties because it had NO Indian member (7 British members). The slogan 'Simon Go Back' was raised. The Commission's report (1930) led to the Round Table Conferences.
Q4. How many Round Table Conferences were held, and in which years?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Three Round Table Conferences were held in London: (1) First (Nov 1930 - Jan 1931) — INC boycotted; (2) Second (Sep - Dec 1931) — Gandhi attended as the sole INC representative; (3) Third (Nov - Dec 1932) — INC boycotted. The conferences discussed constitutional reforms, leading to the Government of India Act 1935.
Q5. Which Round Table Conference was attended by Gandhi as the sole INC representative?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Gandhi attended the Second Round Table Conference (September - December 1931) as the sole INC representative. He was sent after the Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 1931). The conference was inconclusive — Gandhi's demand for a unified India was opposed by the Muslim League, the Ambedkar group, and the princes. Gandhi returned disappointed.
Q6. The Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931) led to which outcome?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 5, 1931) was signed between Gandhi and Lord Irwin (Viceroy). Gandhi agreed to suspend the Civil Disobedience Movement and participate in the Second Round Table Conference (London, September 1931). In return, the British agreed to release political prisoners and allow salt collection by coastal villagers.
Q7. The Nehru Report (1928) was a response to which event?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Nehru Report (1928) was a response to the Simon Commission's lack of Indian members. Drafted by a committee headed by Motilal Nehru (with Jawaharlal Nehru and Subhash Chandra Bose), it was the first Indian attempt to draft a constitution. It recommended dominion status, fundamental rights, and a unified electorate. The Muslim League rejected it (Jinnah's 14 points were a counter).
Q8. The Government of India Act 1935 was based on the recommendations of which commission/conference?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Government of India Act 1935 was based on the Simon Commission report (1930) and the three Round Table Conferences (1930-1932). It was the longest Act of the British Parliament (475 sections). Key features: provincial autonomy, diarchy at the centre (never implemented), the Reserve Bank of India, federal court, and the Joint Parliamentary Committee.
Q9. Which of the following was NOT a key feature of the Government of India Act 1935?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Government of India Act 1935 did NOT introduce universal adult franchise — voting was limited to about 10% of the population (based on property and education qualifications). Key features were: provincial autonomy, diarchy at the centre (never implemented), All-India Federation (never implemented), RBI (1935), Federal Court (1937). Universal adult franchise came only with the Indian Constitution (1950).
Q10. The Poona Pact (1932) was signed between Gandhi and whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Poona Pact (September 24, 1932) was signed between Gandhi (on hunger strike in Yerwada Jail) and B.R. Ambedkar. The British had announced the 'Communal Award' (August 1932) with separate electorates for the Depressed Classes. Gandhi opposed this and went on a fast unto death. Ambedkar relented — separate electorates were replaced with reserved seats for the Depressed Classes within the Hindu electorate.
Q11. The Communal Award (August 1932) was announced by which British Prime Minister?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Communal Award was announced by British Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald on August 16, 1932. It granted separate electorates to the Depressed Classes (Dalits) and other minorities. Gandhi opposed this for the Depressed Classes (he went on a fast unto death at Yerwada Jail), leading to the Poona Pact (September 1932) with Ambedkar.
Q12. Who was the Viceroy of India during the early Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-1931)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Lord Irwin (Viceroy 1926-1931) was the Viceroy during the early Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-1931). He signed the Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 1931). Lord Willingdon (1931-1936) succeeded him and handled the later phase of the movement. Lord Linlithgow (1936-1943) was Viceroy during WWII and the Quit India Movement.
Q13. The Civil Disobedience Movement was suspended after the Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931) and resumed in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Civil Disobedience Movement was suspended after the Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 1931) for Gandhi to attend the Second Round Table Conference (September 1931). The conference was inconclusive; Gandhi returned disappointed. The movement was resumed in January 1932 and continued until 1934 (formally withdrawn in April 1934).
Q14. The First Round Table Conference (November 1930 - January 1931) was boycotted by which party?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The First Round Table Conference (November 1930 - January 1931) was boycotted by the Indian National Congress — the Civil Disobedience Movement was on (Gandhi was in jail). The Muslim League, Hindu Mahasabha, the Depressed Classes (Ambedkar), and the princes attended. The conference was inaugurated by King George V.
Q15. Jinnah's 'Fourteen Points' (1929) were a response to which event?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Jinnah's 'Fourteen Points' (1929) were a response to the Nehru Report (1928), which had recommended a unified electorate (no separate electorates). Jinnah's 14 Points demanded: separate electorates for Muslims, one-third Muslim representation in legislatures, federal system with provincial autonomy, and other Muslim safeguards. The 14 Points became the basis of the Muslim League's future demands.
Q16. Consider the following about the Government of India Act 1935: 1. It granted provincial autonomy. 2. It introduced diarchy at the centre. 3. It introduced universal adult franchise. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is WRONG — the Act did NOT introduce universal adult franchise; voting was limited to about 10% of the population (based on property and education qualifications). The Act granted provincial autonomy (diarchy abolished in provinces) and introduced diarchy at the centre (which was never implemented).
Q17. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Simon Commission 2. Nehru Report 3. Civil Disobedience Movement 4. Government of India Act 1935
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Simon Commission (1927, arrived 1928) → Nehru Report (1928) → Civil Disobedience Movement (March 1930) → Government of India Act 1935. The Simon Commission triggered the Nehru Report (Indian counter); the Civil Disobedience Movement pressured the British; the Government of India Act 1935 was the constitutional outcome.
Q18. Who was the Chairman of the Simon Commission?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sir John Simon was the Chairman of the Simon Commission (officially the Indian Statutory Commission, appointed 1927, arrived in India February 1928). It had 7 British members and NO Indian member — which is why it was boycotted by all Indian parties. The slogan 'Simon Go Back' was raised at its arrival. Its report (1930) led to the Round Table Conferences.
Q19. The Karachi Resolution (1931) of the INC is famous for what?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Karachi Resolution (March 1931, at the Karachi INC session, presided by Vallabhbhai Patel) committed the INC to fundamental rights (freedom of speech, equality before law, protection of minorities) and an economic programme (land reforms, living wages, workers' rights). It was the first INC resolution to outline a vision for post-independence India.
Q20. Bihar's connection with the Civil Disobedience Movement was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar's connection with the Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-1934) was through salt satyagrahas (including at Patna, where salt law was violated on the banks of the Ganga) and no-tax campaigns in Bihar's villages. The Bihar Congress, led by Rajendra Prasad and Anugrah Narayan Sinha, organised these campaigns. Bihar also had tribal satyagrahas in the Chhotanagpur region.
Q21. Who presided over the 1931 Karachi session of the INC (where the fundamental rights resolution was passed)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Vallabhbhai Patel presided over the 1931 Karachi session of the INC. The session is famous for the Karachi Resolution — committing the INC to fundamental rights and an economic programme. Gandhi was released from jail (after the Gandhi-Irwin Pact, March 1931) to attend this session. The Karachi Resolution was the first INC document to outline a vision for post-independence India's governance.
Q22. The Government of India Act 1935 was described by Jawaharlal Nehru as:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Jawaharlal Nehru described the Government of India Act 1935 as 'a new charter of slavery' — because it gave limited provincial autonomy but retained British control at the centre. Gandhi called the Cripps Mission (1942) 'a post-dated cheque on a crashing bank' — a different quote for a different event. The 1935 Act was the longest British parliamentary Act.
Q23. The Government of India Act 1935 established which institution?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Government of India Act 1935 established BOTH the Reserve Bank of India (1935) and the Federal Court (1937). The RBI was established in April 1935 (based on the RBI Act 1934). The Federal Court was established in 1937 (under the 1935 Act) with jurisdiction over constitutional disputes — the predecessor of the Supreme Court of India (1950).
Q24. Who said 'Simon Go Back' during the Simon Commission protests?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The slogan 'Simon Go Back' was raised by Indian nationalists across all parties — the INC, Muslim League, and Hindu Mahasabha all boycotted the Simon Commission. The slogan was raised at the Commission's arrival in Bombay (February 3, 1928). Lala Lajpat Rai was lathi-charged during a protest in Lahore (October 1928) and died of his injuries (November 17, 1928).
Q25. The First Round Table Conference (1930) was inaugurated by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The First Round Table Conference (November 1930) was inaugurated by King George V in London. This was a rare royal opening for a constitutional conference. The conference was held at the House of Lords. The INC boycotted it (Civil Disobedience was on). Ambedkar attended as a representative of the Depressed Classes.
