🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Muzaffarpur Bomb Case (1908)
  • Muzaffarpur Bomb Case (April 30, 1908): Khudiram Bose and Prafulla Chaki threw a bomb at Muzaffarpur (Bihar) targeting Judge Kingsford (who was notorious for his harsh sentences against nationalists). The bomb hit the wrong carriage — killing Mrs. Kennedy and her daughter (wife and daughter of barrister Pringle Kennedy).
  • Khudiram Bose: arrested at Muzaffarpur railway station (he was only 18). Tried and hanged on August 11, 1908 — one of the youngest revolutionaries to be martyred.
  • Prafulla Chaki: shot himself to avoid arrest while fleeing from Muzaffarpur.
  • Significance: the Muzaffarpur bomb case is the Bihar-specific revolutionary event. It shocked the nation and made Khudiram Bose a martyr. The case is directly relevant to BPSC exams because of its Bihar connection.
  • Note: Bhagat Singh, Surya Sen, and most other revolutionaries were NOT from Bihar — Bihar's main revolutionary connection is through the Muzaffarpur case (Khudiram Bose). Bihar's main contribution to the freedom movement was through the Gandhian movements (Champaran 1917, Quit India 1942) rather than the revolutionary movement.
Surya Sen ("Masterda") — led the Chittagong Armoury Raid on April 18, 1930. He and 65 revolutionaries captured the armoury and declared Chittagong independent for four days. Surya Sen was arrested in 1933, tortured, and hanged on January 12, 1934.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
Cross-Topic Links:
  • Topic 32 (Gandhian Movements): The revolutionary movement was the alternative to Gandhian non-violence. While Gandhi led mass movements (Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India), the revolutionaries believed in armed struggle. Both approaches contributed to the freedom movement.
  • Topic 34 (Quit India 1942): The Quit India Movement saw a convergence of Gandhian and revolutionary approaches — underground activity (JP, Ram Manohar Lohia) borrowed tactics from the revolutionaries. Subhash Chandra Bose's INA was fighting alongside, creating pressure from multiple fronts.
  • Topic 28 (Socio-Religious Reforms): Aurobindo Ghosh, who was tried in the Alipore Bomb Case (1908), later became Sri Aurobindo — a philosopher and spiritual reformer. His journey from revolutionary to spiritual leader illustrates the intersection of the freedom movement and the reform movement.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Bhagat Singh's legacy: March 23 is observed as "Martyrs' Day" (Shaheed Diwas) across India — the anniversary of Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev's hanging (1931).
  • Chandrashekhar Azad Park: Alfred Park in Allahabad (Prayagraj) was renamed Chandrashekhar Azad Park — the site where he shot himself on February 27, 1931.
  • INA trials: the Red Fort INA trials (1945-1946) — of officers like Shah Nawaz Khan, Gurbaksh Singh Dhillon, and Prem Sahgal — generated massive public sympathy and contributed to the naval mutiny of 1946.
  • Bose's mystery: the circumstances of Subhash Chandra Bose's death (plane crash, August 18, 1945, Taiwan) remain a subject of debate and multiple commissions of inquiry. His legacy continues to inspire.
  • 'Jai Hind': Bose's slogan "Jai Hind" is now the official greeting of the Indian armed forces and appears on Indian currency and official communications.

Q1. Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, was hanged on March 23, 1931, for which incident?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were hanged on March 23, 1931, for the assassination of J.P. Saunders (Lahore Conspiracy Case). Saunders was killed in retaliation for the death of Lala Lajpat Rai.
Q2. Who founded the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) in 1928?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — HSRA was founded in 1928 at Ferozeshah Kotla (Delhi) by Bhagat Singh, Azad, and others. It evolved from the HRA (founded 1924 by Sanyal).
Q3. Who threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly on April 8, 1929, with the slogan 'Inquilab Zindabad'?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly (April 8, 1929) shouting "Inquilab Zindabad!" They courted arrest to use the trial as a platform.
Q4. Who was the leader of the Chittagong Armoury Raid (April 18, 1930)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Surya Sen ("Masterda") led the Chittagong Armoury Raid (April 18, 1930). He and 65 revolutionaries captured the armoury and declared Chittagong independent for four days.
Q5. The Kakori Conspiracy (1925) was associated with which revolutionary organization?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Kakori Conspiracy (August 9, 1925) was carried out by the HRA. Ram Prasad Bismil, Ashfaqulla Khan, and others looted a government train. 4 were hanged.
Q6. The slogan 'Inquilab Zindabad' (Long Live Revolution) was popularized by:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — "Inquilab Zindabad" was coined by Maulana Hasrat Mohani in 1921. It was later popularized by Bhagat Singh and the HSRA during the Assembly Bomb case (1929).
Q7. Who was the first President of the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Rash Behari Bose was the first President of the INA (founded 1942, Singapore). He handed over leadership to Subhash Chandra Bose in 1943.
Q8. Subhash Chandra Bose escaped from India in 1941 disguised as:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bose escaped from house arrest in Calcutta (January 17, 1941) disguised as a Pathan named "Ziauddin." He traveled through Peshawar, Kabul, and Germany before reaching Japan by submarine.
Q9. Which revolutionary organization was founded by Sachindra Nath Sanyal in 1924?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The HRA was founded in 1924 by Sanyal, Bismil, and others. It was renamed HSRA in 1928 by Bhagat Singh and Azad.
Q10. Who among the following was NOT associated with Azad Hind Fauz (INA)?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Bhagat Singh was hanged in 1931; the INA was formed in 1942. He was NOT associated with the INA. 71st BPSC Q83.
Q11. The Anushilan Samiti was a revolutionary organization based in which region?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Anushilan Samiti was based in Bengal (founded 1902). It carried out the Alipore Bomb Case (1908) and inspired later revolutionary groups.
Q12. The Alipore Bomb Case (1908) involved which revolutionary leaders?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Alipore Bomb Case (1908) involved Aurobindo Ghosh and Barindra Ghosh. Khudiram Bose was hanged. Aurobindo was acquitted and later became Sri Aurobindo.
Q13. Who was hanged on August 11, 1908, at the age of 18, becoming one of the youngest revolutionaries to be martyred?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Khudiram Bose was hanged on August 11, 1908, at age 18. He was involved in the Muzaffarpur bomb case (Bihar). One of the youngest martyrs.
Q14. Jatindra Nath Das died on September 13, 1929, after a 63-day hunger strike in Lahore Jail. What was the purpose of the hunger strike?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Jatindra Nath Das died after a 63-day hunger strike to protest against the inhuman treatment of political prisoners (British prisoners got better facilities than Indian political prisoners).
Q15. The Gadar Party was founded in 1913 in which country?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Gadar Party was founded in 1913 in San Francisco, USA, by Lala Hardayal and others. Its goal was armed rebellion against British rule.
Q16. The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q130) matched organizations with headquarters. Which of the following is the correct matching?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 71st BPSC Q130: Swadeshi Sevak Home → Vancouver; United India House → Seattle; Yugantar Ashram → San Francisco; INA → Rangoon. Code: I-d, II-c, III-a, IV-b.
Q17. Chandrashekhar Azad died on February 27, 1931, at which location, shooting himself with the last bullet?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Azad shot himself at Alfred Park, Allahabad (February 27, 1931). His pledge was to never be captured alive. The park was later renamed Chandrashekhar Azad Park.
Q18. Who was the first Muslim President of the Indian National Congress?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Badruddin Taiyabji was the first Muslim President of the INC (1887, Madras session). Maulana Azad was the youngest INC President (1923, age 35). 71st BPSC Q87.
Q19. Subhash Chandra Bose resigned from the presidency of the INC in 1939. Who succeeded him?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The 69th/70th/71st BPSC answer was (D) "More than one of the above" due to the question's framing. Bose resigned in April 1939; Rajendra Prasad succeeded him.
Q20. The famous slogan 'Give me blood, I shall give you freedom' was given by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Subhash Chandra Bose gave "Give me blood, I shall give you freedom" (1944, Burma, to INA soldiers). He also gave "Dilli Chalo" and "Jai Hind."
Q21. The 'Young India' newspaper was started by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 'Young India' was started by Gandhi (1919). Annie Besant started 'New India'; Tilak started 'Kesari'; Azad started 'Al Hilal.' 71st BPSC Q46.
Q22. Who raised the cry 'Divide and Quit' during India's independence movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — M.A. Jinnah raised "Divide and Quit" — a play on the "Quit India" slogan. He demanded the partition of India (Pakistan). 71st BPSC Q89.
Q23. Bihar's connection with the revolutionary movement was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar's connection is through the Muzaffarpur bomb case (1908). Khudiram Bose and Prafulla Chaki threw a bomb at Muzaffarpur (Bihar). Khudiram was hanged (age 18).
Q24. Who founded the Forward Bloc in 1939?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Subhash Chandra Bose founded the Forward Bloc in 1939 after resigning from the INC presidency. It advocated a more militant approach to the freedom struggle.
Q25. The revolutionary women who played a prominent role in the Chittagong Armoury Raid included:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Pritilata Waddedar and Kalpana Datta played prominent roles in the Chittagong Armoury Raid (1930). Pritilata led the Pahartali European Club attack (1932) and consumed cyanide. Kalpana Datta was arrested for manufacturing bombs.
