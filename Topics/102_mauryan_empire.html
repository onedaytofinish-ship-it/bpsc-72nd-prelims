Mauryan Empire — Chandragupta, Ashoka — PATALIPUTRA
📂 Ancient History ⚖️ Weight: 0.7 🎯 Expected: 2–3 Qs 🔶 Bihar: Pataliputra (capital), Ashokan pillars (Lauriya, Rampurva), Barabar Caves
📚 Core Content
EXAM TIP Mauryan Empire (321-185 BCE, ~137 years): Founded by Chandragupta Maurya (321 BCE, advised by Chanakya/Kautilya). Capital: Pataliputra (Bihar). Peak under Ashoka (268-232 BCE). Kalinga War (261 BCE, Odisha) → Ashoka embraced Buddhism. Third Buddhist Council (Pataliputra, 250 BCE). Missionaries to Sri Lanka (Mahendra + Sanghamitra). Decline after Ashoka; last king Brihadratha killed by Pushyamitra Shunga (185 BCE).
Lion Capital of Ashoka (Sarnath, c. 250 BCE) — four lions standing back-to-back, originally atop an Ashokan pillar. Adopted as the National Emblem of India (1950). The inverted lotus base, Dharma Chakra (wheel), and four animals (lion, elephant, bull, horse) are masterpieces of Mauryan art. Carved from polished Chunar sandstone.
Sanchi Stupa (Madhya Pradesh) — originally commissioned by Ashoka (c. 250 BCE), enlarged during the Shunga period. The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India. UNESCO World Heritage Site (1989). Ashoka's daughter Sanghamitra and son Mahendra carried Buddhism to Sri Lanka from Pataliputra (Bihar).

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Heartland of the Mauryan Empire
  • Pataliputra (Patna): the capital of the Mauryan Empire — founded by Udayin (Haryanka dynasty), but became the greatest city of ancient India under the Mauryas. Megasthenes described it as 9 miles × 1.5 miles, with a wooden wall (570 towers, 64 gates).
  • Ashokan Pillars in Bihar: Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, and Rampurva (all in West Champaran district). These are among the best-preserved Ashokan pillars — with lion, bull, and elephant capitals.
  • Barabar Caves (near Gaya): the oldest rock-cut caves in India — donated by Ashoka to the Ajivika sect (an ascetic sect). Later, Ashoka's grandson Dasharatha also donated caves at nearby Nagarjuna hills.
  • Third Buddhist Council (Pataliputra, 250 BCE): convened by Ashoka — compiled the Abhidhamma Pitaka and sent Buddhist missionaries abroad. Pataliputra was the centre from which Buddhism spread to the world.
  • Bodh Gaya: Ashoka visited Bodh Gaya (the site of the Buddha's enlightenment) and built the original Mahabodhi Temple (later rebuilt). His Rummindei Pillar Edict records his pilgrimage to Lumbini.
  • Rajgir + Vaishali: earlier Buddhist Councils were held here (First at Rajgir, Second at Vaishali) — and the Mauryas patronised these sites.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 100 (Mahajanapadas): The Mauryan Empire grew out of Magadha — the most powerful of the 16 Mahajanapadas. The Haryanka, Shishunaga, and Nanda dynasties preceded the Mauryas. Chandragupta Maurya overthrew the Nandas (321 BCE).
  • Topic 101 (Buddhism & Jainism): Ashoka promoted Buddhism — Third Council (Pataliputra), missionaries to Sri Lanka, stupas, pillars. Chandragupta embraced Jainism — died at Shravanabelagola. The Mauryas patronised both religions.
  • Topic 103 (Post-Mauryan): After the last Maurya (Brihadratha) was killed by Pushyamitra Shunga (185 BCE), the Shunga dynasty ruled. Foreign invasions: Indo-Greeks, Kushanas. Satavahanas in the Deccan.
📰 Current Relevance
  • National Emblem: the Sarnath Lion Capital (four lions) is India's national emblem (adopted 26 January 1950). The Dhamma Chakra (wheel) appears on the national flag. Ashoka's legacy = India's official symbols.
  • Ashoka Chakra: the 24-spoke wheel on India's national flag — derived from the Sarnath Lion Capital. Represents dhamma (righteousness) and the continuity of the nation.
  • Dhamma as governance: Ashoka's principles of religious tolerance, welfare, non-violence, and ethical governance are cited as foundational to India's constitutional values.
  • Buddhism as world religion: Ashoka's missionaries (sent from Pataliputra, Bihar) spread Buddhism to Sri Lanka, SE Asia, Central Asia — making it a world religion with ~500 million followers today.
  • Pataliputra (Patna): one of the longest-inhabited cities in the world. The Mauryan capital is now the capital of Bihar. Archaeological excavations have revealed Mauryan-period wooden palisade walls.

Q1. Who founded the Mauryan Empire?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Chandragupta Maurya founded the Mauryan Empire c. 321 BCE by overthrowing the Nanda dynasty with the help of Chanakya (Kautilya). Capital: Pataliputra (Bihar).
Q2. Who was the chief advisor and mentor of Chandragupta Maurya?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Chanakya (Kautilya/Vishnugupta) was Chandragupta's advisor. Wrote the Arthashastra (statecraft, economics, military). Teacher at Takshashila.
Q3. The Mauryan capital was located at which city (in modern Bihar)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Pataliputra (modern Patna, Bihar) — at confluence of Ganga + Son rivers. Megasthenes described it: 9 mi × 1.5 mi, wooden wall, 570 towers, 64 gates.
Q4. Megasthenes, the Greek ambassador to the Mauryan court, was sent by which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Seleucus Nicator sent Megasthenes to Chandragupta's court after the treaty (c. 303 BCE). His 'Indica' describes Pataliputra, Mauryan admin, Indian society.
Q5. The treaty between Chandragupta Maurya and Seleucus Nicator (c. 303 BCE) resulted in Chandragupta gaining which territories?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Chandragupta received Aria, Arachosia, Gedrosia (modern Afghanistan/Baluchistan) and gave 500 war elephants. Seleucus also sent Megasthenes. Empire extended to Afghanistan.
Q6. Who was the second Mauryan emperor, son of Chandragupta Maurya?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bindusara (c. 297-273 BCE). Extended empire south to Deccan. Greeks called him 'Amitrochates.' Father of Ashoka.
Q7. Ashoka the Great ruled from which to which year (approximately)?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Ashoka ruled c. 268-232 BCE (36 years). Third Mauryan emperor. Peak of empire — Afghanistan to Bengal, Himalayas to Karnataka.
Q8. The Kalinga War (c. 261 BCE) was fought by Ashoka in which modern state?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kalinga War (c. 261 BCE) in modern Odisha. ~100,000 killed, 150,000 deported. Ashoka renounced war, embraced Buddhism. Rock Edict XIII.
Q9. After the Kalinga War, Ashoka embraced Buddhism. Where did he go on Buddhist pilgrimage?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Ashoka went on dhamma-yatra to Lumbini. Rummindei Pillar Edict records his visit + tax exemption for Lumbini (because Buddha was born there).
Q10. Ashoka's inscriptions (edicts) were written in which scripts?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Brahmi (most of India), Kharoshthi (Northwest), Greek + Aramaic (Afghanistan). Language: Prakrit. Brahmi deciphered by James Prinsep (1837).
Q11. James Prinsep deciphered the Brahmi script in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — James Prinsep deciphered Brahmi in 1837. Identified 'Devanamapiya Piyadasi' with Ashoka. Unlocked Ashokan edicts and established Ashoka as historical.
Q12. How many Rock Edicts did Ashoka issue (the major Rock Edicts)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 14 major Rock Edicts + 2 Kalinga Edicts. ~40 sites across India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Nepal. Cover dhamma, non-violence, tolerance, welfare, Kalinga War (Edict XIII).
Q13. Ashoka's Pillar Edicts (7 major) are found at which locations?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Pillars at Delhi-Topra, Delhi-Meerut (transported by Firoz Shah Tughlaq), Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva (all Bihar), Sanchi, Sarnath, Lumbini, Rummindei. Several in Bihar!
Q14. Which Ashokan pillar is the national emblem of India?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Sarnath Lion Capital (four lions) = India's national emblem (adopted 26 January 1950). Dhamma Chakra on the capital appears on the national flag.
Q15. Ashoka convened the Third Buddhist Council at which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Third Council: Pataliputra (Bihar, c. 250 BCE), Ashoka. Presided by Moggaliputta Tissa. Compiled Abhidhamma Pitaka. Sent missionaries abroad — Buddhism became world religion.
Q16. Ashoka sent his son and daughter as Buddhist missionaries to which country?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mahendra (son) + Sanghamitra (daughter) to Sri Lanka. Sanghamitra carried a sapling of the Bodhi tree from Bodh Gaya (Bihar) to Anuradhapura. Theravada Buddhism established.
Q17. The Mauryan Empire was divided into how many provinces for administration?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 5 provinces: Uttarapatha (Taxila, North), Dakshinapatha (Suvarnagiri, South), Avantiratha (Ujjain, West), Prachyapatha (Tosali, East), Magadha (Pataliputra, central). Governed by Kumar/Aryaputra (royal prince).
Q18. The Arthashastra, a treatise on statecraft and economics, was written by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kautilya (Chanakya). Covers statecraft, economics, military, law, administration. Describes Mauryan admin (18 departments/tirthas). Rediscovered in 1909 by R. Shamasastry.
Q19. Megasthenes' work 'Indica' describes which Mauryan city in detail?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Megasthenes' 'Indica' describes Pataliputra: 9 mi × 1.5 mi, wooden wall (570 towers, 64 gates). Royal palace magnificent (Persian-influenced). City council of 30 (6 boards of 5).
Q20. Chandragupta Maurya in his later years embraced which religion and where did he die?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Chandragupta embraced Jainism (influenced by Bhadrabahu). Abdicated for son Bindusara, became monk, went to Shravanabelagola (Karnataka). Performed Sallekhana (ritual fasting) ~297 BCE.
Q21. The Mauryan Empire declined after Ashoka's death. The last Mauryan emperor was killed by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Last Maurya Brihadratha killed by general Pushyamitra Shunga (c. 185 BCE) → Shunga dynasty. Empire lasted ~137 years (321-185 BCE). Causes: weak successors, decentralization, Brahminical reaction.
Q22. Ashoka's Dhamma (Dharma) was a secular code of conduct. Which of the following is NOT a principle of Ashoka's Dhamma?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Animal sacrifice is NOT a principle — Ashoka PROHIBITED it. Dhamma = ahimsa, tolerance, respect for parents/teachers/Brahmins, welfare, moderation, truthfulness, compassion.
Q23. Which of the following is NOT a major Ashokan inscription site in Bihar?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Sanchi is in Madhya Pradesh, NOT Bihar. Bihar sites: Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva (all West Champaran), Bodh Gaya, Rajgir, Barabar caves.
Q24. The Barabar Caves (near Gaya, Bihar) were donated by Ashoka to which religious sect?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Barabar Caves (near Gaya, Bihar) — oldest rock-cut caves in India — donated to Ajivikas (founded by Makkhali Gosala). Ajivikas believed in niyati (destiny). Dasharatha (Ashoka's grandson) also donated at Nagarjuna hills.
Q25. Which Ashokan edict specifically mentions the Kalinga War and his remorse?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rock Edict XIII describes Kalinga War (261 BCE): '150,000 deported, 100,000 killed, many more died.' Ashoka expressed remorse, resolved to practice Dhamma-vijaya (moral conquest) instead of war.
