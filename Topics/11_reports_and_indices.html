B. GHI 2025 — Component Breakdown COMPONENT TRAP

The GHI combines four equally-weighted indicators. BPSC tests whether you know which is which — wasting and stunting are the most commonly swapped pair.

IndicatorWhat it MeasuresIndia 2025 Value
UndernourishmentShare of population with insufficient calorie intake12.0%
Child WastingLow weight-for-height (acute malnutrition)18.7%
Child StuntingLow height-for-age (chronic malnutrition)32.9%
Under-5 MortalityProbability of dying before age 52.8%
  • Severity bands: Low <5; Moderate 5–9.9; Serious 10–34.9 (India = 25.8); Alarming 35–49.9; Extremely Alarming ≥50.
  • Key warning: The publisher explicitly states that ranks and scores are comparable only within the same edition — country coverage, data sources and methodology change yearly.

C. GII 2025 — The Innovation Deep Dive RISING

  • Trend: India rose from 81st (2015) → 40th (2023) → 39th (2024) → 38th (2025) — a steady upward trajectory BPSC loves.
  • Income group: 1st among lower-middle-income economies (not high-income — India is not in that group).
  • Regional: 1st in Central and Southern Asia.
  • Output vs Input: Output rank 32 (better) vs input rank 52 (weaker) — India produces more innovation per unit of investment than expected.
  • GII pillars: Institutions, Human capital & research, Infrastructure, Market sophistication, Business sophistication, Knowledge & technology outputs, Creative outputs.

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar in National Reports — The Bridge to Topic 23
  • SDG India Index 2023-24: Bihar scored 57 — the lowest among all states, yet falls in the Performer band (50–64), not Aspirant. India's national score is 71; Kerala & Uttarakhand topped at 79.
  • Bihar's weakest SDGs: SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) = 24 and SDG 4 (Quality Education) = 32 — these drag down the composite. Nutrition is the largest MPI indicator contribution (27.95%).
  • National MPI (NFHS-5, 2019-21): Bihar's multidimensionally poor headcount = 33.76% (down from 51.89% in NFHS-4). Rural H = 36.95%; urban H = 16.67%. MPI value = 0.160.
  • NITI MPI 2024 progress estimate: Bihar's 2022-23 headcount = 26.59% (extrapolated, not directly observed); 377.09 lakh escaped multidimensional poverty 2013-14 to 2022-23.
  • Why this matters for Topic 11: BPSC can frame a Bihar-specific question from any national report — the SDG Index and MPI are the two highest-yield Bihar angles. Cross-link to Topic 23 for the full Bihar-in-reports deep dive.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps (2025–26 Cycle)
  • HDR 2025: India moved from 133 (2022 data) to 130 (2023 data); HDI reached 0.685. The report year (2025) ≠ the data year (2023) — BPSC can trap on either. Life expectancy 72.0 was the 71st PYQ answer.
  • GHI 2025: India 102/123, score 25.8. The 2024 edition had 127 countries (India 105/127) — don't mix editions. The publisher warns against cross-edition comparisons.
  • Gender Gap 2025: India 131/148. The number of economies rose from 147 (2024) to 148 (2025). WEF publishes this — not UN Women, not UNDP.
  • GII 2025: India 38/139, up from 39 (2024) and 40 (2023). Output rank 32 beats input rank 52 — a rare and memorable fact. WIPO publishes this — not WEF, not World Bank.
  • WHR 2026: India 116 (score 4.536). WHR 2025 was 118 — a 2-rank improvement. Published by Oxford + Gallup + UN SDSN — not WIPO, not UNDP, not WEF.
  • SDG India Index 2023-24: India 71; Kerala & Uttarakhand 79 (joint top); Bihar 57 (lowest). 16 SDGs ranked (SDG 17 excluded). Previous score was 66.
  • Edition-mixing is the #1 trap: Every report changes its country count, methodology, or data year between editions. Never compare a rank from one edition with a score from another — always cite the edition year.
  • Publisher-matching is the #2 trap: BPSC's favourite format: "Which is wrongly matched?" Know all six publishers cold: UNDP, Concern+Welthungerhilfe, WEF, WIPO, Oxford+Gallup+SDSN, NITI Aayog.
✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Human Development Report 2025 placed India at which rank? (71st BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — UNDP's HDR 2025 places India 130th of 193 countries, with HDI 0.685 based on 2023 data. Trap: 131/148 is the Global Gender Gap rank (WEF), 102/123 is GHI, 118/147 was WHR 2025 — all different reports.
Q2. India's HDI value in HDR 2025 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — India's HDI rose from 0.676 (2022 data) to 0.685 (2023 data), remaining in the medium-development category (0.550–0.699). 0.715 would place India in the high-development group, which it has not yet reached.
Q3. HDR 2025 reports India's life expectancy at birth (2023) as:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The UNDP figure is 72.0 years, up from 58.6 in 1990 — the exact fact asked in the 71st BPSC. 70 and 75 are common rounding traps; 67.2 is an older estimate.
Q4. The Human Development Report is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — UNDP publishes the HDR. UNESCO publishes education reports (e.g., GEM Report); UNICEF focuses on children; the World Bank produces World Development Indicators — a separate database.
Q5. HDI combines which three broad dimensions?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — HDI measures a long and healthy life (life expectancy), knowledge (expected and mean years of schooling), and a decent standard of living (GNI per capita). It does not capture inflation, gender, or governance directly.
Q6. India's Global Hunger Index 2025 rank and score were:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — GHI 2025 lists India 102 of 123 with a score of 25.8 (serious). 105/127 is the 2024 edition figure — the number of countries and methodology change yearly, so mixing editions is a trap. 130/193 is HDR; 131/148 is Gender Gap.
Q7. India's GHI 2025 hunger-severity category was:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — GHI severity bands: low (<5), moderate (5–9.9), serious (10–34.9), alarming (35–49.9), extremely alarming (≥50). India's 25.8 falls squarely in 'serious'. 'Alarming' would need ≥35; 'moderate' needs <10.
Q8. Which is NOT a component of the Global Hunger Index?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — GHI combines four indicators: undernourishment, child wasting, child stunting, and under-five mortality. Maternal mortality is not part of GHI — it belongs to MMR/MMIH reports.
Q9. The Global Hunger Index 2025 gave India a child wasting figure of:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — India's GHI 2025 components: wasting 18.7%, stunting 32.9%, undernourishment 12.0%, under-five mortality 2.8%. Wasting (low weight-for-height) and stunting (low height-for-age) are often swapped in traps — wasting is the acute, stunting the chronic condition.
Q10. A correct caution while comparing GHI results across years is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — GHI's publisher explicitly warns that revised data, methodology changes, and varying country coverage make cross-edition rank/score comparisons invalid. Each edition is self-contained — comparing 102/123 (2025) with 105/127 (2024) is meaningless.
Q11. The Global Gender Gap Report is released by: (69th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The World Economic Forum (WEF, Geneva) releases the Global Gender Gap Report — a direct 69th BPSC question. UN Women is a UN entity for gender equality but does not publish this index; UNDP publishes HDR; the World Bank produces separate development databases.
Q12. India's Global Gender Gap Report 2025 position was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — WEF ranked India 131st among 148 economies in 2025, with an overall parity score of 64.4%. 118/147 was the 2024 edition (147 countries); the number of economies rose to 148 in 2025. 38/139 is GII; 102/123 is GHI.
Q13. Match the report to the publisher: HDR, GII, SDG India Index.
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — HDR → UNDP; Global Innovation Index → WIPO; SDG India Index → NITI Aayog. WEF publishes the Gender Gap Report (not GII); the World Bank does not publish any of these three; MoSPI publishes PLFS.
Q14. The Global Innovation Index 2025 placed India at:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — WIPO's GII 2025 ranks India 38th of 139, improving from 39th (2024) and 40th (2023). The upward trajectory is a favourite BPSC angle. 48 is a trap from an older edition.
Q15. In GII 2025, India ranked first among:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — India was first among lower-middle-income economies and also first in the Central and Southern Asia region. It is not a high-income economy (that group is led by Switzerland, Singapore, the US). 'BRICS only' is a trap — the ranking is global, not BRICS-specific.
Q16. India's GII 2025 output rank was better than its input rank. The pair (output, input) is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — India's innovation output rank was 32 (better) and input rank was 52 (weaker) — a rare case where output outperforms input, meaning India extracts more innovation per unit of investment. 38 is the overall GII rank, not a sub-rank.
Q17. The World Happiness Report 2026 placed India at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — India ranks 116 in WHR 2026 (score 4.536). WHR 2025 had India at 118 — a 2-rank improvement. Report year matters: mixing 118 (2025) with 116 (2026) is the classic BPSC trap. 131 is the Gender Gap rank.
Q18. The World Happiness Report is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — WHR is produced by the Wellbeing Research Centre at the University of Oxford, in partnership with Gallup and the UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network (SDSN). WIPO publishes GII; WEF publishes Gender Gap; UNDP publishes HDR — all separate.
Q19. India's SDG India Index 2023-24 composite score was: (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — NITI Aayog reported India's composite score at 71, up from 66 in 2020-21. 57 is Bihar's score (lowest state); 79 is the top state score (Kerala & Uttarakhand). 66 is the previous edition — all four options are real numbers from the same index, making this a classic BPSC trap.
Q20. Which states jointly topped the SDG India Index 2023-24? (70th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Kerala and Uttarakhand jointly led with a score of 79. The 70th BPSC asked 'which state topped' with Kerala as the answer; the 2023-24 edition was the first to have a joint topper. Bihar was last at 57.
Q21. The SDG India Index 2023-24 assesses progress on how many SDGs?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — It assesses 16 SDGs; SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals) is not ranked — it is treated as a common goal across all states. The UN has 17 SDGs total, so '17' is the obvious trap. 15 is an older edition count.
Q22. The SDG India Index is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — NITI Aayog publishes the SDG India Index — India's own state-level SDG dashboard. UNDP publishes the global HDR; MoSPI publishes PLFS; WEF publishes Gender Gap. All four are distinct publishers with distinct indices.
Q23. The National Multidimensional Poverty Index in India is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — NITI Aayog publishes India's National MPI, using NFHS data. UNDP co-publishes the global MPI with OPHI (Oxford) — a separate product. RBI focuses on monetary policy; NABARD on rural credit. The national vs global MPI distinction is a BPSC trap.
Q24. Consider the following statements: 1. GII is published by WIPO. 2. Global Gender Gap Report is published by WEF. 3. HDR is published by UNDP. How many are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three publisher-report pairs are correct. These are high-frequency BPSC matching facts: WIPO→GII, WEF→Gender Gap, UNDP→HDR. Students often confuse one of the three, so BPSC bundles them into a statement question.
Q25. Which pair is WRONGLY matched?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — WIPO publishes GII (Global Innovation Index), not the World Happiness Report. WHR is produced by the Wellbeing Research Centre (Oxford), Gallup & UN SDSN. The other three pairs are all correctly matched.
