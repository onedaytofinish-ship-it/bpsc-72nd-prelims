A. Chhath Mahaparva & UNESCO — The BIGGEST Culture Story VERIFIED JUL 2026

Chhath is Bihar's defining festival — a four-day Vedic-era worship of the Sun God (Surya) and Chhathi Maiya, marked by strict fasting, holy bathing and arghya (offerings) at river ghats. The exam window has two stories: the 2025 festival dates and the UNESCO nomination status.

Chhath 2025 — DayRitualDateWhat happens
Day 1Nahay Khay25 Oct 2025Holy bath; devotees cook satvik prasad (kheer-bhaat) in new earthen/brass vessels
Day 2Kharna / Lohanda26 Oct 2025Day-long nirjala fast broken at dusk with kheer-roti prasad; then a 36-hour waterless fast begins
Day 3Sandhya Arghya27 Oct 2025Evening offering to the setting Sun at riverbanks/ponds — the iconic mass ghat image
Day 4Usha Arghya28 Oct 2025Dawn offering to the rising Sun; fast broken (paran) — festival concludes
  • UNESCO status — NOT YET INSCRIBED (the exam trap): India initiated a multinational nomination of "Chhath Mahaparva" for the 2026–27 cycle of UNESCO's Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH). Consultations were chaired by Culture Secretary Vivek Aggarwal at IGNCA on 15 Sep 2025 with the UAE, Suriname and the Netherlands; Mauritius, Fiji and Nepal joined later (Bhojpuri-diaspora link). As of 15 Jul 2026, Chhath is still in the nomination process — it is NOT on the list.
  • Contrast fact — Deepavali WAS inscribed: Deepavali (Diwali) entered the ICH Representative List at the 20th ICH Committee session, Red Fort, New Delhi, 8–13 Dec 2025 — the first such session hosted by India. It became India's 16th ICH element (tally 15 → 16). Do NOT swap Deepavali with Chhath.
  • Related UNESCO moves: India nominated Sarnath (Ancient Buddhist Site) for the World Heritage List, 2025–26 cycle (nominated, not inscribed); 'Maratha Military Landscapes of India' became India's 44th World Heritage Site in 2025.
Devotees offering arghya to the Sun during Chhath — the mass riverbank ritual that anchors Bihar's cultural identity. (Image: Wikimedia Commons)
🧠 Mnemonic — Chhath Days "Na-Kha-Sa-Usha"
Nahay Khay (bath + first prasad) → Kharna (kheer at dusk, 36-hr fast starts) → Sandhya Arghya (to the setting Sun) → Usha Arghya (to the rising Sun, paran). For 2025: 25-26-27-28 October — "Chhath = October's last week". Setting Sun first, rising Sun last: "S for Sunset-Day-3, U for Usha(sunrise)-Day-4".

C. GI Tags & Cultural Economy STATE THE FACT CLEARLY

  • THREE new Bihar GI tags in the window (granted January 2025): Bawan Buti Saree & Fabric (Nalanda), Patharkatti Stone Craft (Gaya) and Pidiya Painting (Bhojpur) — the last a Bhojpuri folk art. Mithila Makhana (2022) is no longer the latest; any option claiming "no new Bihar GI in 2025/2026" is a trap — mark it false.
  • Bihar's GI-tagged products (learn the full list): Madhubani paintings, Bhagalpur silk, Sujini (Sujani) embroidery, Sikki grass craft, Khatwa appliqué, Shahi litchi (Muzaffarpur), Katarni rice, Jardalu mango (Bhagalpur), Magahi paan (Magadh region), Mithila Makhana (2022) — plus the Jan-2025 trio: Bawan Buti Saree & Fabric (Nalanda), Patharkatti Stone Craft (Gaya), Pidiya Painting (Bhojpur).
  • Mithila Makhana in the news (Sep 2025): Union Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal inaugurated APEDA's first Bihar regional office at Patna on 11 Sep 2025 (Bihar Idea Festival) and flagged off a 7-MT trial consignment of GI-tagged Mithila Makhana to New Zealand, Canada and the USA.
  • National Makhana Board: announced in Union Budget 2025 (1 Feb 2025); launched by PM Modi from Purnia on 15–16 Sep 2025; HQ at Purnea (Seemanchal). Bihar produces ≈ 85–90% of India's makhana (Madhubani, Darbhanga, Sitamarhi, Saharsa, Katihar, Purnea, Supaul, Kishanganj, Araria belts).

🧠 Mnemonic — Bihar GI List "Ma-Bha-Su-Si-Kha + Sha-Ka-Ja-Ma-Ma"
Crafts: Madhubani paintings, Bhagalpur silk, Sujini, Sikki grass, Khatwa appliqué. Agri/food: Shahi litchi, Katarni rice, Jardalu mango, Magahi paan, Makhana (Mithila, 2022 = latest). Remember: "5 crafts + 5 foods = Bihar GI" — and nothing new since 2022.

D. Heritage Corridors & UNESCO Sites HOT STATIC+CURRENT MIX

  • UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Bihar = exactly TWO: Mahabodhi Temple Complex, Bodh Gaya (2002) and Nalanda Mahavihara / Archaeological Site of Nalanda (2016). Year-swap ("Mahabodhi 2016 / Nalanda 2002") is the classic trap.
  • Temple corridors: the Union Budget of July 2024 announced Vishnupad Temple (Gaya) and Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya) corridors on the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor model, plus a Rajgir–Nalanda tourism push. (In-window construction milestones are not confirmed — don't invent them.)
  • Buddhist circuit of Bihar: Bodh Gaya (Mahabodhi), Rajgir (Venuvana, Gridhrakuta), Nalanda (Mahavihara), Vaishali (relic stupa, Ashokan pillar), Kesaria (the tallest/largest stupa). Buddha Purnima = Vaishakh Purnima (12 May 2025; 1 May 2026).
  • Nalanda University (Rajgir): new campus dedicated by PM Modi on 19 Jun 2024 (background); in-window highlights: first Nalanda Literature Festival (Dec 2025), new Master's programmes and global MoUs (2025), visit of Bhutan's PM on 3 Sep 2025; VC = Prof. Sachin Chaturvedi.

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Why This Topic IS Bihar — The State-First Angle
  • Chhath = Bihar's cultural signature: the ghats of the Ganga in Patna and towns across the state draw lakhs of vratis; the Bhojpuri diaspora carries the festival to Mauritius, Fiji, Suriname and the Gulf — exactly why India's UNESCO nomination is multinational. The Patna administration creates makeshift ghats, and Indian Railways runs special trains every Kartik for the homecoming rush.
  • Fair economy: Sonepur (Saran) remains north India's biggest rural marketplace during Kartik; Gaya's Pitru Paksha sustains a specialised ritual economy around the Vishnupad Temple and the Falgu river; Sultanganj's Shravani Mela anchors Bhagalpur's pilgrim traffic toward Deoghar.
  • GI-driven farm exports: Mithila Makhana (GI 2022) now has an export pipeline — APEDA's first Bihar regional office (Patna, 11 Sep 2025) and the National Makhana Board (HQ Purnea, launched 15–16 Sep 2025) target value-addition and global markets for a crop where Bihar holds ≈85–90% of national production.
  • Heritage diplomacy stage: Rajgir hosted the first Nalanda Literature Festival (Dec 2025) and Nalanda University received Bhutan's PM (3 Sep 2025); Bodh Gaya's Mahabodhi Temple (WHS 2002) and Nalanda (WHS 2016) are the state's two UNESCO anchors, with Budget-2024 corridors planned on the Kashi Vishwanath model.
  • Governance hook: Bihar Diwas 2026 (22 Mar, theme 'Unnat Bihar, Ujjwal Bihar') was Nitish Kumar's last as CM — after 10 terms he moved to the Rajya Sabha, and Samrat Chaudhary (Bihar's first BJP CM) took oath on 15 Apr 2026 (cross-link Topic #17).

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. World famous Madhubani (Mithila) painting is found in which district of Bihar? (70th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The GI-tagged Mithila painting tradition takes its name from Madhubani district; the 70th BPSC asked this with exactly these four options. Darbhanga is the cultural capital of the Mithila region and the classic distractor — the painting's name gives the district away.
Q2. The first edition of the Nalanda Literature Festival (December 2025) was organised at: (71st BPSC venue pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1st Nalanda Literature Festival ran 21–25 Dec 2025 at the Rajgir Convention Centre, inaugurated by Governor Arif Mohammad Khan, with Shashi Tharoor and Sonal Mansingh among the speakers. The 71st BPSC used this exact "2025 event organised where?" format with Bihar towns as options.
Q3. In Chhath Mahaparva 2025, the Sandhya Arghya (evening offering to the setting Sun) was offered on:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sequence: Nahay Khay 25 Oct → Kharna/Lohanda 26 Oct → Sandhya Arghya 27 Oct → Usha Arghya 28 Oct 2025. Option (D) is the trap — 28 Oct is the dawn Usha Arghya to the rising Sun, not the evening Sandhya Arghya.
Q4. Consider the following statements about Chhath Mahaparva:
1. It is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and Chhathi Maiya.
2. It is a four-day festival that begins with Nahay Khay.
3. In 2025, the concluding Usha Arghya was offered on 28 October.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct. Chhath worships Surya and Chhathi Maiya across four days (Nahay Khay → Kharna → Sandhya Arghya → Usha Arghya); the 2025 Usha Arghya fell on 28 October, when the 36-hour fast is broken (paran).
Q5. As of 15 July 2026, the status of 'Chhath Mahaparva' on UNESCO's Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — India initiated a multinational nomination (IGNCA consultations of 15 Sep 2025, chaired by Culture Secretary Vivek Aggarwal) for the 2026–27 cycle; as of mid-July 2026 Chhath is still in process, NOT on the list. Option (A) is THE trap: Deepavali, not Chhath, was inscribed at the Dec 2025 Red Fort session.
Q6. The September 2025 consultations for the multinational UNESCO nomination of Chhath Mahaparva, chaired by the Culture Secretary at IGNCA, initially involved which group of countries besides India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 15 Sep 2025 IGNCA consultations (chaired by Culture Secretary Vivek Aggarwal) involved the UAE, Suriname and the Netherlands; Mauritius, Fiji and Nepal joined later. The common thread is the Bhojpuri diaspora that celebrates Chhath abroad — hence a multinational nomination.
Q7. In December 2025, 'Deepavali' was inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The 20th ICH Committee session (8–13 Dec 2025) was hosted by India at the Red Fort, New Delhi — the first time India hosted the session — and inscribed Deepavali as India's 16th ICH element. The ordinal (20th), the venue (Red Fort) and the host (India) are all examinable.
Q8. Consider the following statements:
1. Chhath was inscribed on UNESCO's ICH Representative List in December 2025.
2. Deepavali became India's 16th element on the ICH Representative List.
3. The December 2025 ICH Committee session was the first ever hosted by India.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Statement 1 is false: Chhath is only nominated (2026–27 multinational cycle), not inscribed. Statements 2 and 3 are correct: Deepavali took India's tally from 15 to 16 at the Red Fort session (Dec 2025), the first ICH session hosted by India.
Q9. The Sonepur Mela (Harihar Kshetra Mela) is held: (70th BPSC venue pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sonepur sits at the Ganga–Gandak sangam in Saran district (Harihar Kshetra, near Hajipur across the river from Patna). It begins around Kartik Purnima (early Nov–early Dec in 2025) and is billed as Asia's largest cattle fair. The Son–Arrah pairing (A) is the geography trap.
Q10. Consider the following statements about the Sonepur Mela:
1. It is traditionally described as Asia's largest cattle fair.
2. It is associated with the legend of Gajendra Moksha.
3. The sale of elephants at the fair has been banned since 2004 under the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct. The legend: Vishnu rescued the elephant-king Gajendra from a crocodile at this kshetra. Since the 2004 ban (Wildlife Protection Act, 1972), elephants may be displayed but not sold at the fair — a nuance BPSC can flip into a wrong statement.
Q11. The Pitru Paksha Mela, where lakhs perform pind daan for ancestors, is centred on the Vishnupad Temple at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The ~16-day Pitru Paksha fair runs at the Vishnupad Temple, Gaya (on the Falgu river); in 2025 Pitru Paksha fell on 7–21 September (Sarva Pitru Amavasya 21 Sep). Gaya is India's pre-eminent site for ancestral rites — a permanent fixture in BPSC's Bihar-culture bank.
Q12. During the Shravani Mela (Kanwar Yatra), kanwariyas carry Ganga water from Ajgaibinath Dham, Sultanganj (Bhagalpur) to:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Sawan kanwar route runs from Sultanganj to Baidyanath Dham, Deoghar (Jharkhand), about 105 km; Sawan 2025 ran 11 Jul – 9 Aug. Parli and Trimbakeshwar are Maharashtra jyotirlingas — pure distractors; Varanasi gets its kanwar from other Ganga points.
Q13. The Prakash Parv celebrations at Takht Sri Patna Sahib in January 2026 marked which-numbered birth anniversary of Guru Gobind Singh?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — January 2025 saw the 358th Prakash Parv; January 2026 (~5 Jan) was therefore the 359th. Takht Sri Patna Sahib is the Guru's birthplace (1666) and one of the five Takhts. Trap: naive subtraction (2026−1666 = 360) — the Panth's Prakash Parv numbering follows the traditional count, where 2025 was the 358th.
Q14. Bihar Diwas, celebrated on 22 March every year, commemorates:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bihar was separated from Bengal on 22 March 1912; Bihar Diwas 2026 was therefore the 114th edition. Jharkhand was carved out much later, on 15 November 2000 — option (A) is the standard date-swap trap.
Q15. The theme of Bihar Diwas 2026 (114th edition), inaugurated at Gandhi Maidan, Patna, was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The official theme of the 114th Bihar Diwas (22 Mar 2026, Gandhi Maidan, Patna) was 'Unnat Bihar, Ujjwal Bihar' (Developed Bihar, Bright Bihar), as reported by GKToday and Akashvani. It was widely noted as Nitish Kumar's last Bihar Day as CM before he moved to the Rajya Sabha (Samrat Chaudhary became CM on 15 Apr 2026). The earlier-circulated 'Pragatishil Bihar' formulation was not the official theme.

Rana & Co.; Drishti IAS, 15 Jun 2026). Option (B) is therefore wrong: Mithila Makhana (2022) is no longer the most recent Bihar GI. Options (C) and (D) are fabricated — Madhubani paintings retain GI status, and Bihar has several GI-tagged agri products (Shahi litchi, Katarni rice, Jardalu mango, Magahi paan, Mithila Makhana).

Q19. Consider the following statements about UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Bihar:

Q20. Which pairing of site ↔ UNESCO World Heritage inscription year is correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Mahabodhi = 2002, Nalanda = 2016. Option (A) is the classic year-swap trap. Memory hook: "Bodh Gaya got its tag in the early 2000s; Nalanda joined 14 years later."
