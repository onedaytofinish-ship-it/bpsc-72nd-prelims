C. Bharat Ratna & Bihar — Karpoori Thakur (2024) STATIC + RECENT

  • Karpoori Thakur (1924–1988) — former Chief Minister of Bihar (two terms in the 1970s), revered as "Jan Nayak", icon of social justice and backward-class empowerment; born at Pitaunjhia village, Samastipur district.
  • Bharat Ratna announced 23 January 2024 — the eve of his birth centenary — and conferred posthumously on 30 March 2024. The 2024 set had 5 recipients: Karpoori Thakur, L.K. Advani, P.V. Narasimha Rao (posth.), Chaudhary Charan Singh (posth.), M.S. Swaminathan (posth.).
  • Window alert: NO Bharat Ratna was announced in 2025 or in 2026 (up to 15 July 2026). Any option claiming a 2025/2026 Bharat Ratna winner is a trap.
  • Statics: Bharat Ratna instituted 1954; first recipients C. Rajagopalachari, S. Radhakrishnan, C.V. Raman; maximum 3 per year; no cash prize; cannot be used as a title (Article 18(1)); conferred by the President of India.

D. Sports Honours — Bihar's Khel Samman + National Sports Awards HOT

  • Khel Samman 2025 (Bihar state honour): CM Nitish Kumar felicitated 153 para athletes + 24 coaches at the Rajgir Sports Complex in October 2025 — cash awards plus government-job letters. Headline amounts: Shailesh Kumar ₹75 lakh, Gauri Kumari ₹10 lakh.
  • National Sports Awards 2024 (announced 2 Jan 2025, conferred 17 Jan 2025, Rashtrapati Bhavan; selection panel headed by Justice (Retd.) V. Ramasubramanian): Khel Ratna ×4 — D. Gukesh (chess), Harmanpreet Singh (hockey), Praveen Kumar (para-athletics), Manu Bhaker (shooting); Arjuna Award ×32 (17 of them para-athletes).
  • 2025 cycle: on 24 December 2025 the selection committee finalised Hardik Singh (hockey) as the sole Khel Ratna recommendation and 24 athletes for Arjuna (incl. Divya Deshmukh, Tejaswin Shankar, Mehuli Ghosh; Aarti Pal — first-ever Yogasan athlete; no cricketer). No Bihar athlete was confirmed in the headline names — do not invent one.
  • Statics: Khel Ratna instituted 1991-92, first winner Viswanathan Anand, renamed Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna in Aug 2021, ₹25 lakh; Arjuna Award 1961, ₹15 lakh; Dronacharya 1985, ₹15 lakh; Dhyan Chand Award (lifetime) 2002, ₹10 lakh; National Sports Day = 29 August (Dhyan Chand's birth anniversary).

E. Padma Rules — The Statics BPSC Smuggles In EVERGREEN

  • Padma awards instituted 1954; three classes — Vibhushan ("exceptional & distinguished service"), Bhushan ("distinguished service of a high order"), Shri ("distinguished service").
  • Announced every year on the eve of Republic Day; conferred by the President at Rashtrapati Bhavan (usually March-May, sometimes in phases — 2026 Phase-1 was 25 May 2026).
  • Padma Awards Committee is headed by the Cabinet Secretary; nominations are open to the public.
  • Government servants are not eligible — except doctors and scientists.
  • Order of precedence (civilian): Bharat Ratna > Padma Vibhushan > Padma Bhushan > Padma Shri.

🏛️ Why This Page Is Pure Bihar — The State-Specific Core
  • Bihar's Padma share: 7 of 139 Padma awards in 2025 (≈5%) and 3 of 131 in 2026 — Bihar punched well above its usual national-awards average in this window, making the state a near-certain BPSC question bank.
  • Chhath geet → national honour: Sharda Sinha carried Bihar's biggest festival onto every national stage; her Vibhushan is the first Padma Vibhushan for a Bihar folk artist in this window — pair it with Topic #22 (Chhath, festivals & culture).
  • Patna's institutions honoured: Mahavir Mandir Trust (Chairman Kishore Kunal, Padma Shri 2025 posth.) — the trust runs one of north India's largest temple-linked charity networks from Patna; the temple stands opposite Patna Junction.
  • Grassroots Bihar recognised: Bhim Singh Bhavesh's journalism for the Musahar community (Bhojpur) and Nirmala Devi's Sujni embroidery show the Padma "unsung heroes" tilt reaching Bihar's Mahadalit and artisan communities.
  • State honours pipeline: Khel Samman 2025 (Rajgir, Oct 2025) combined cash with government-job letters for para athletes — Bihar's own honour-and-employment model; pairs with Topic #19 (Bihar sports) and Topic #15 (state schemes).
  • Caveat for honesty: no official 2025-26 list of Bihar civil state honours (Bihar Gaurav-type awards) could be verified — if BPSC asks, the safe answers remain the Khel Samman and the Padma/Bharat Ratna names above.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps (2025–26 Cycle)
  • 25 Jan 2025 — Padma 2025: 139 awards; 7 from Bihar. Headliners: Sharda Sinha (Vibhushan, posth.) and Sushil Kumar Modi (Bhushan, posth.). Trap: calling Sharda Sinha's award "Padma Bhushan" — that was her 2018 honour (Shri 1991 → Bhushan 2018 → Vibhushan 2025).
  • 25 Jan 2026 — Padma 2026: 131 awards; 3 from Bihar, all Padma Shri (Bharat Singh Bharti, Bishwa Bandhu posth., Gopal Ji Trivedi); CM Nitish Kumar congratulated them the next day. Phase-1 conferment 25 May 2026 (66 recipients).
  • Bharat Ratna trap: none announced in 2025 or 2026 (up to 15 Jul 2026). Last set = 2024, five recipients including Bihar's Karpoori Thakur (announced 23 Jan 2024, his birth-centenary eve).
  • Oct 2025 — Khel Samman: 153 para athletes + 24 coaches at Rajgir Sports Complex; remember the pair Shailesh Kumar ₹75 lakh / Gauri Kumari ₹10 lakh — swapping the amounts is the obvious distractor.
  • National Sports Awards: 2024 cycle conferred 17 Jan 2025 (Khel Ratna ×4, Arjuna ×32); 2025 cycle recommendations finalised 24 Dec 2025 (Hardik Singh sole Khel Ratna pick; 24 for Arjuna; first Yogasan athlete Aarti Pal).
  • Verified-district caution: districts are confirmed only for Sharda Sinha (Patna/Supaul), Sushil Kumar Modi (Patna), Kishore Kunal (Patna), Bhim Singh Bhavesh (Bhojpur), Bharat Singh Bharti (Bhojpur region). Districts of Hemant Kumar, Nirmala Devi, Vijay Nityanand Surishwar Ji, Bishwa Bandhu and Gopal Ji Trivedi were not officially confirmed — treat any district-level MCQ option for them with suspicion.
✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. How many awardees from Bihar figured in the Padma Awards 2025 list announced on 25 January 2025?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Seven Bihar awardees in 2025: Sharda Sinha (Vibhushan), Sushil Kumar Modi (Bhushan), Kishore Kunal, Bhim Singh Bhavesh, Hemant Kumar, Nirmala Devi, Vijay Nityanand Surishwar Ji Maharaj (all Shri). 2026 had only three — don't carry that number back to 2025.
Q2. Dr. Sharda Sinha, the "Bihar Kokila", was honoured in the Padma Awards 2025 with:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — She received the Padma Vibhushan posthumously in 2025 (Art — folk music). Trap (B): Padma Bhushan was her 2018 honour; (A) Padma Shri was 1991. She never got the Bharat Ratna.
Q3. Which former Chief Minister of Bihar was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 2024, announced on the eve of his birth centenary?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Karpoori Thakur ("Jan Nayak", born Pitaunjhia village, Samastipur) got the Bharat Ratna announced 23 Jan 2024 and conferred 30 Mar 2024. Sri Krishna Sinha was Bihar's first CM but never a Bharat Ratna; Mandal and A.N. Sinha never held the office of CM in the framing used here and were not Bharat Ratna recipients.
Q4. Sushil Kumar Modi was awarded which Padma honour, and in which field, in 2025?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The former Bihar Deputy CM (Patna) received the Padma Bhushan posthumously in 2025 for Public Affairs. (C) describes Kishore Kunal's award; (A) describes Sharda Sinha's.
Q5. Consider the following statements about Kishore Kunal:
1. He was an Indian Police Service officer.
2. He chaired the Mahavir Mandir Trust, Patna.
3. He received the Padma Shri posthumously in 2025 for Civil Service.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct: the ex-IPS officer who headed Patna's Mahavir Mandir Trust was given the Padma Shri (Civil Service) posthumously in 2025. The trap options each drop one true statement.
Q6. Bhim Singh Bhavesh of Bhojpur received the Padma Shri 2025 for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — He is a journalist honoured for Social Work among the Musahar community (Bhojpur). (A) is Hemant Kumar's field, (D) is Nirmala Devi's craft — classic same-list swaps. (71st BPSC asked the profession of an award winner — same pattern.)
Q7. Which Bihar-linked awardee received the Padma Shri 2026 in the field of Science & Engineering?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gopal Ji Trivedi (Padma Shri 2026, Science & Engineering). Trap (B): Hemant Kumar is a 2025 awardee in Medicine; (C) and (D) are the other two 2026 names but in Art.
Q8. The correct order of precedence of India's top civilian honours is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bharat Ratna tops, then Vibhushan, Bhushan, Shri. (A) swaps Vibhushan and Bhushan — the single most common error; remember V-B-S = "Very Big Salute".
Q9. The Bharat Ratna, instituted in 1954, was first awarded to:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The first 1954 trio was Rajagopalachari, Radhakrishnan and C.V. Raman. Nehru (1955), Patel (1991) and Azad (1992) came later; the (D) trio spans 1990-92 — all plausible-sounding decoys.
Q10. The Padma Awards Committee, which recommends names for the awards, is headed by the:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Padma Awards Committee is constituted each year and headed by the Cabinet Secretary; the President approves and confers the awards. PM/Home Minister are tempting but wrong.
Q11. Consider the following statements:
1. Government servants are not eligible for Padma awards, except doctors and scientists.
2. A maximum of three Bharat Ratnas can be awarded in a year.
3. Bharat Ratna and Padma titles may be used as prefixes before a recipient's name under Article 18(1).
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is wrong: Article 18(1) bars titles, and the Supreme Court has held these honours cannot be used as prefixes/suffixes to names.
Q12. The Padma Awards 2025 (139 total) were split as:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 2025: 7 + 19 + 113 = 139. Trap (B) is the 2026 split (5 + 13 + 113 = 131) — same "113 Shri" tail in both years makes the years easy to confuse.
Q13. Consider the following statements about the Padma Awards 2026:
1. A total of 131 awards were announced on 25 January 2026.
2. Five Padma Vibhushan were awarded.
3. The Phase-1 conferment ceremony (66 recipients) was held at Rashtrapati Bhavan on 25 May 2026.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three hold: 131 = 5 Vibhushan + 13 Bhushan + 113 Shri, announced on the eve of the 77th Republic Day; President Murmu conferred the first phase on 25 May 2026. Multi-statement format mirrors the 71st BPSC style.
Q14. At the Khel Samman 2025, Chief Minister Nitish Kumar felicitated 153 para athletes and 24 coaches at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The October 2025 ceremony was held at the Rajgir Sports Complex (Nalanda district) — Bihar's new sports hub. Patliputra Sports Complex and Moin-ul-Haq Stadium are Patna venues used as decoys.
Q15. At Khel Samman 2025, para athlete Shailesh Kumar received a cash award of:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Shailesh Kumar received ₹75 lakh; the ₹10 lakh figure (A) belongs to Gauri Kumari at the same ceremony — the classic amount-swap trap. ₹25 lakh (B) is the Khel Ratna purse, a cross-award distractor.
Q16. Sharda Sinha's singing is most famously associated with which Bihar festival tradition?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The "Bihar Kokila" is synonymous with Chhath geet, played across the world during Chhath Puja. The other festivals are Bihar/Jharkhand traditions but not her signature — BPSC pairs her name only with Chhath.
Q17. Arrange Sharda Sinha's Padma honours in correct chronological order:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Shri 1991, Bhushan 2018, Vibhushan 2025 (posth.). (C) inverts Shri/Bhushan; (D) wrongly upgrades 2018 to Vibhushan and invents a Bharat Ratna; (B) tampers with the years.
Q18. Bharat Singh Bharti, honoured with the Padma Shri 2026, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — He is a 90-year-old folk artist of the Bhojpur region (Art). (C) is Nirmala Devi and (D) is Hemant Kumar — both Padma Shri 2025, so wrong year and wrong field.
Q19. The Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna Award carries a cash prize of:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Khel Ratna = ₹25 lakh (+ medallion + certificate). ₹15 lakh (A) is the Arjuna/Dronacharya purse and ₹10 lakh (C) the Dhyan Chand lifetime award — the standard amount-confusion set. Khel Ratna was instituted 1991-92 (first: Viswanathan Anand) and renamed for Dhyan Chand in Aug 2021.
Q20. How many sportspersons received the Arjuna Award for 2024 (conferred on 17 January 2025)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — 32 Arjuna awardees for 2024, of whom 17 were para-athletes. (A) is the number recommended for Arjuna 2025 (announced 24 Dec 2025) — a year-swap trap.
Q21. What is true of Sushil Kumar Modi? (71st BPSC "more than one" pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — (A), (B) and (C) are all true, so "More than one of the above" — the 71st BPSC used this exact construction when multiple bundled facts hold. Never pick the single true statement when several hold.
Q22. Match the Bihar Padma Shri 2025 awardees with their fields:
1. Nirmala Devi — Sujni embroidery (Art)
2. Hemant Kumar — Medicine (kidney-care awareness)
3. Vijay Nityanand Surishwar Ji Maharaj — Spiritualism (Jain leader)
Which pairs are correctly matched?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three pairs match the 2025 list. The distractor sets each drop a correct pair — BPSC's matching questions often hide "all correct" behind that pattern.
Q23. Consider the following statements about Karpoori Thakur:
1. His Bharat Ratna was announced on 23 January 2024, the eve of his birth centenary.
2. He is popularly remembered as "Jan Nayak".
3. He was the first Chief Minister of Bihar.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 1 and 2 are correct (born Pitaunjhia, Samastipur; CM twice in the 1970s). 3 is wrong: Bihar's first Chief Minister was Dr. Sri Krishna Sinha — the planted falsehood.
Q24. Which of these civilian honours did Sharda Sinha NOT receive in her lifetime or posthumously?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — She held all three Padma grades (1991, 2018, 2025) but never the Bharat Ratna. "NOT received" questions invert the usual reflex — read the stem twice.
Q25. Consider the following statements about the Bharat Ratna in the 2025-26 window:
1. No Bharat Ratna was announced in 2025.
2. No Bharat Ratna was announced in 2026 (up to 15 July 2026).
3. The most recent set was for 2024, with five recipients including Karpoori Thakur.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct: the 2024 set (Karpoori Thakur, Advani, P.V. Narasimha Rao, Charan Singh, Swaminathan) is the latest; any option naming a 2025/2026 Bharat Ratna winner is fabricated.
