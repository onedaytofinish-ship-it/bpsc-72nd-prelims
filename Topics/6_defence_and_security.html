A. Operation Sindoor — The Lead Story (Apr–May 2025) MAJOR

  • Pahalgam terror attack (Apr 22, 2025): Terrorists of The Resistance Front (TRF), a proxy of Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), killed 26 people (25 tourists and a local pony operator) at Baisaran meadow, Pahalgam (J&K) — the deadliest attack on civilians in India since 26/11.
  • Operation Sindoor launched (night of May 6–7, 2025): Precision strikes on 9 terror infrastructure sites in Pakistan and PoK — including Markaz Subhan Allah, Bahawalpur (JeM headquarters) and Markaz Taiba, Muridke (LeT headquarters). These were the deepest Indian strikes inside Pakistan since the 1971 war.
  • The 4-day conflict (May 7–10): Pakistan retaliated with drones and missiles, largely blunted by India's layered air defence — S-400 Triumf ("Sudarshan Chakra"), Akash, and legacy guns. India then struck Pakistani military infrastructure, including the Nur Khan air base (Rawalpindi) and other airfields.
  • S-400's star turn: India claimed an S-400 kill of a Pakistani AEW&C aircraft at ~314 km — among the longest recorded surface-to-air kills ever.
  • Ceasefire understanding (May 10, 2025): Reached after DGMO-level hotline talks — India consistently framed it as a bilateral military-to-military understanding, not third-party mediation.
  • Key lessons / reform push: Integrated air defence grids, counter-drone warfare, and a renewed push for jointness under the CDS and Integrated Theatre Commands; the armed forces received fresh Emergency Procurement (EP) powers post-Sindoor.
  • One year on (May 2026): Defence Minister Rajnath Singh released a commemorative volume "Operation Sindoor" documenting testimonies of 100 participating personnel, at a ceremony attended by the CDS and all three service chiefs. NEW

D. Recent Big-Ticket Developments (2025–26) NEW

  • Su-30MKI upgrade: CCS cleared the ~₹63,000 crore upgrade of 84 Su-30MKI fighters by HAL — new radar, avionics, electronic-warfare suite and indigenous weapons integration.
  • 5th-gen fighter race: Russia's Su-57 and the US F-35 are both under Indian evaluation even as the indigenous AMCA programme advances (CCS approval ~₹15,000 cr for AMCA development, 2024).
  • Emergency Procurement (EP) powers: Post-Op Sindoor, the forces fast-tracked emergency purchases of munitions, drones, counter-drone systems and air-defence missiles; the 2026-27 budget explicitly funds these EP commitments.
  • Defence Budget 2026-27: record ₹7.85 lakh crore (₹7,84,678 cr) — +15.19% over BE 2025-26 (₹6.81 lakh cr); ~2% of GDP; 14.67% of total central expenditure (highest of all ministries); capital outlay crosses ₹2.19 lakh cr; 75% of capital acquisition (₹1.39 lakh cr) reserved for domestic industry; DRDO gets ₹29,100 cr. First budget presented after Op Sindoor. VERIFIED
  • Theatre commands: Jointness accelerated after Sindoor — plan for Integrated Theatre Commands (western, northern and maritime) under CDS-led Department of Military Affairs is in advanced consultation.
  • Agnipath scheme (Jun 2022): recruits Agniveers for 4 years; up to 25% retained for regular service; exit package Seva Nidhi (~₹11.71 lakh, tax-free); no pension for the 4-year tenure.
  • Military Quantum Mission Policy Framework (Jan 22, 2026): released by CDS Gen Anil Chauhan — tri-service roadmap for quantum technologies, aligned with the National Quantum Mission. NEW

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Defence Footprint in Bihar
  • Officers Training Academy (OTA), Gaya: One of the Indian Army's two OTAs (the other is in Chennai); commissioned in 2011; trains Short Service Commission officers and Technical Entry Scheme (TES) cadets.
  • Bihar Regiment — Danapur, Patna: The regimental centre of the Bihar Regiment (raised 1941) is at Danapur Cantonment. Its battalions have served in 1971, Kargil (1999) and on the LAC — 16 Bihar's commanding officer Col. B. Santosh Babu was martyred leading his men in the Galwan clash (Jun 2020).
  • Danapur Cantonment: Among the oldest cantonments in India (established 1766) — a living link between Bihar and the Indian Army's institutional history.
  • Kargil Chowk, Patna: War memorial at the heart of Patna (near Gandhi Maidan) honouring Bihar's soldiers martyred in the 1999 Kargil War — a standard BPSC Bihar-GK landmark.
  • Soldiers & Agniveers from Bihar: Bihar is a traditional recruiting ground for the armed forces; Agnipath recruitment rallies draw massive participation across the state, and OTA Gaya keeps Bihar on the officer-training map.
  • Bihta air base: The IAF station at Bihta (Patna district) is being developed as Patna's second airport (civil enclave) — defence infrastructure doubling as Bihar's growth asset.

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 33 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Pahalgam terror attack of 2025, which triggered Operation Sindoor, took place on:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The attack occurred on April 22, 2025, at Baisaran meadow, Pahalgam (J&K); 26 people were killed. The Resistance Front (TRF), an LeT proxy, was responsible.
Q2. Consider the following statements about Operation Sindoor:
1. It was launched on the night of May 6–7, 2025.
2. It struck 9 terror infrastructure sites, including Bahawalpur (JeM HQ) and Muridke (LeT HQ).
3. A ceasefire understanding was reached on May 10, 2025, after DGMO-level talks.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three statements are correct. These were the deepest Indian strikes inside Pakistan since 1971, and the conflict ended with a bilateral DGMO-hotline understanding on May 10, 2025.
Q3. During Operation Sindoor, which Indian air-defence system was credited with a claimed ~314 km long-range kill — among the longest recorded surface-to-air engagements?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Russian-origin S-400 Triumf (Indian call-sign "Sudarshan Chakra") anchored the air-defence grid and was credited with a ~314 km kill of a Pakistani AEW&C aircraft. India ordered 5 squadrons in 2018 for $5.43 bn.
Q4. "Mission Divyastra", conducted in March 2024, refers to:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mission Divyastra (Mar 2024) was Agni-V's first flight with MIRV technology — a single missile carrying multiple warheads that can hit different targets.
Q5. The approximate range of the Agni-V ballistic missile is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Agni-V covers 5,000+ km (ICBM-class, canister-launched). Classic trap: it is NOT 10,000 km. Ladder: Agni-I 700, II 2,000, III 3,000, IV 4,000, V 5,000+.
Q6. Consider the following statements about the BrahMos missile:
1. It is a supersonic cruise missile.
2. It is produced by BrahMos Aerospace, an India–Russia joint venture.
3. Its first export order was signed with the Philippines.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — BrahMos flies at Mach ~2.8–3, is built by the India–Russia JV BrahMos Aerospace, and its first export contract (2022, ~$375 mn) was with the Philippines. Extended-range variants now reach 450–800 km.
Q7. Match the following systems with their descriptions:
a) Prithvi-II — 1) Beyond-visual-range air-to-air missile

c) Pinaka — 3) Multi-barrel rocket launcher

Q8. Consider the following statements about "Mission Shakti":
1. It was conducted on March 27, 2019.
2. It made India the fourth country to demonstrate anti-satellite (ASAT) capability.
3. The target satellite was destroyed in geostationary orbit.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statement 3 is wrong: the Microsat-R satellite was hit in LOW Earth orbit (~300 km altitude), not geostationary orbit. India became the 4th ASAT power after the US, Russia and China.
Q9. Which of the following statements are true about "Akashteer"?
1. Akashteer is the core of the Indian Army's Air Defence system.
2. It seamlessly integrates India's larger C4ISR network.
3. It reflects a strategic shift from passive defence to proactive retaliation.
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three statements were true in the 71st BPSC paper. Akashteer is the Army's automated air-defence control and reporting system, networked with C4ISR for sensor-to-shooter response.
Q10. Which of the following defence platforms are part of India's growing indigenous military ecosystem supporting the Make in India initiative?
(A) F-35 Lightning II (B) Main Battle Tank (MBT) Arjun (C) Advanced Towed Artillery Gun System (ATAGS)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Verbatim 71st BPSC question. Arjun AND ATAGS are indigenous, so "More than one of the above". The F-35 Lightning II is an American (Lockheed Martin) fighter — NOT indigenous.

✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 69th BPSC PYQ. The 12th DefExpo (Oct 2022, Gandhinagar) carried the theme "Path to Pride" and was the first edition reserved exclusively for Indian companies.
Q12. Where was the Japan-India bilateral Maritime Exercise (JIMEX-24) held?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Verbatim 70th BPSC (13 Dec) question. JIMEX-24 was hosted at Yokosuka, Japan. JIMEX pairs with Dharma Guardian (India–Japan army exercise).

✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Yudh Abhyas is the India–US army exercise; the 2025 (21st) edition was held at Fort Wainwright, Alaska. (Russia = Indra; UK = Ajeya Warrior; France = Shakti.)
Q17. The "Hand-in-Hand" joint exercise, suspended since 2020, is held between India and:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Hand-in-Hand is the India–China counter-terrorism exercise, last held in 2019 (Umroi, Meghalaya) and suspended after the 2020 Ladakh standoff.
Q18. "Desert Cyclone", whose first edition was held at Mahajan (Rajasthan) in January 2024, is a joint army exercise between India and:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Desert Cyclone is the India–UAE army exercise, launched in 2024 — reflecting deepening Gulf defence ties. (Oman = Al Najah; Egypt = Cyclone special-forces exercise.)
Q19. Consider the following statements about INS Vikrant:
1. It was commissioned on September 2, 2022.
2. It was built by Cochin Shipyard Limited.
3. It is India's first indigenously designed and built aircraft carrier.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All correct. INS Vikrant (IAC-1, ~45,000 tonnes) was commissioned at Kochi on Sep 2, 2022 — making India one of the few nations that can design and build its own aircraft carriers.
Q20. INS Arighaat, commissioned in August 2024, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — INS Arighaat (commissioned Aug 29, 2024, Visakhapatnam) is the second Arihant-class SSBN, strengthening the sea leg of India's nuclear triad. (P17A lead ship = INS Nilgiri; 6th Scorpene = INS Vaghsheer — both Jan 2025.)
Q21. INS Nilgiri, commissioned in January 2025, is the lead ship of:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — INS Nilgiri is the first of seven Project 17A stealth frigates, commissioned Jan 15, 2025, alongside INS Vaghsheer (6th Kalvari-class) and INS Surat (4th P15B destroyer) in the same ceremony.
Q22. INS Vaghsheer, commissioned in January 2025, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — INS Vaghsheer completes the six-boat Kalvari class built by Mazagon Dock (MDL) with France's Naval Group under Project 75. The follow-on Project 75I (6 AIP submarines) is pending.
Q23. The Tejas Mk2 light combat aircraft will be powered by which engine, under a deal involving ~80% Transfer of Technology to India?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The GE F414 deal (announced during PM Modi's 2023 US visit) includes ~80% ToT for co-production by HAL — powering Tejas Mk2 (and early AMCA). Tejas Mk1/Mk1A use the F404.
Q24. "Zorawar", developed by DRDO with Larsen & Toubro, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Zorawar is a ~25-tonne light tank (named after Gen Zorawar Singh) built for mountain warfare in Ladakh, where heavy MBTs like the Arjun/T-90 cannot operate effectively.
Q25. The CCS-cleared upgrade of 84 Su-30MKI fighters by HAL is valued at approximately:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The ~₹63,000 crore "Super Sukhoi" upgrade of 84 Su-30MKIs adds new radars, avionics, electronic-warfare suites and indigenous weapons (Astra, BrahMos-NG class integration).
Q26. In the Union Budget 2026-27, the total allocation for the Ministry of Defence is approximately:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — ₹7.85 lakh crore (₹7,84,678 cr) — a 15.19% jump over BE 2025-26 (₹6.81 lakh cr), ~2% of GDP and 14.67% of total central expenditure; the first budget after Operation Sindoor. Trap: ₹6.81 lakh cr was the PREVIOUS year.
Q27. India's annual defence production in FY 2024-25 reached a record:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ₹1,50,590 crore (+18% over FY24's ₹1.27 lakh cr; +90% over FY20's ₹79,071 cr). Don't confuse: ₹23,622 cr = defence EXPORTS FY25; ₹79,071 cr = FY20 production.
Q28. India's defence exports in FY 2024-25 stood at:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — ₹23,622 crore (+12% over ₹21,083 cr in FY24), with exports to 80+ countries. ₹50,000 crore is the TARGET for 2029, not a current figure.
Q29. Match the following appointments (as of mid-2026):
a) Chief of the Army Staff — 1) Admiral Dinesh K. Tripathi
b) Chief of the Naval Staff — 2) General Anil Chauhan
c) Chief of the Air Staff — 3) General Upendra Dwivedi
d) Chief of Defence Staff — 4) Air Chief Marshal A.P. Singh
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — COAS: Gen Upendra Dwivedi (Jun 2024); CNS: Adm Dinesh K. Tripathi (Apr 2024); CAS: ACM A.P. Singh (Sep 2024); CDS: Gen Anil Chauhan (since Sep 2022, 2nd CDS).
Q30. Who is the current Chairman of DRDO (as of mid-2026)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Dr Samir V. Kamat has headed DRDO since August 2022. (G. Satheesh Reddy = predecessor; S. Somanath = former ISRO chief; V.K. Saraswat = DRDO chief 2009–2013, now NITI Aayog member.)
Q31. Consider the following statements about the Agnipath scheme:
1. It recruits "Agniveers" for a four-year tenure.
2. Up to 25% of each batch is retained for regular service.
3. It was announced in June 2022.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All correct. Agniveers serve 4 years; up to 25% are retained; the rest exit with the tax-free Seva Nidhi package (~₹11.71 lakh) and no pension. Scheme announced June 2022.
Q32. The ET-LDHCM, tested by DRDO in November 2024, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Extended Trajectory–Long Duration Hypersonic Cruise Missile (range ~1,500 km, scramjet-powered) was tested on Nov 16, 2024, from Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Island, Odisha — putting India in the hypersonic club.
Q33. The Officers Training Academy (OTA) of the Indian Army located in Bihar is at:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — OTA Gaya (commissioned 2011) is one of the Army's two OTAs (the other at Chennai). Danapur hosts the Bihar Regimental Centre — a classic Bihar-GK confusion pair.
