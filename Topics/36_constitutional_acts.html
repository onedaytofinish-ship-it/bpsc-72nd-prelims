B. Government of India Act 1909 (Morley-Minto Reforms) PYQ PATTERN

  • Named after: Lord Morley (Secretary of State for India) and Lord Minto (Viceroy 1905-1910)
  • Key features: (1) separate electorates for Muslims (first time); (2) expanded Central and Provincial Legislative Councils; (3) non-official majority in provincial councils; (4) limited role for Indians in governance
  • Separate electorates: only Muslims could vote for Muslim candidates in Muslim constituencies — a British "divide and rule" strategy, demanded by the Muslim League (founded 1906)
  • Significance: first constitutional reform by the British; introduced communal representation which later became a major factor in partition
  • Indian reaction: Muslims (Muslim League) satisfied; INC disappointed (wanted more representation)

C. Government of India Act 1919 (Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms)

  • Named after: Edwin Montagu (Secretary of State 1917-1922) and Lord Chelmsford (Viceroy 1916-1921)
  • Background: Montagu Declaration (August 20, 1917) — "gradual development of self-governing institutions" in India
  • Key features:
  • Diarchy in provinces: provincial subjects divided into 'reserved' (police, justice, land revenue — administered by Governor's executive council) and 'transferred' (education, health, agriculture, local government — administered by Indian ministers responsible to legislature)
  • Bicameral legislature at centre: Council of State (Upper House, 60 members) + Legislative Assembly (Lower House, 145 members)
  • Direct elections: introduced for the first time (with limited franchise — about 10%)
  • Separate electorates: extended to Sikhs, Christians, Anglo-Indians, and Europeans
  • Budget separation: provincial budgets separated from central budget for the first time
  • Public Service Commission: established (1926) — for recruitment to civil services
  • Indian reaction: disappointed — the Rowlatt Act (1919) (detention without trial) undermined the reforms; led to the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920)

D. Government of India Act 1935 EXAM CORE

  • Based on: Simon Commission report (1930) + three Round Table Conferences (1930-32)
  • Longest British Act: 475 sections (longest Act of the British Parliament until then)
  • Key features:
  • Provincial autonomy: diarchy abolished in provinces; elected ministries responsible to provincial legislatures. 11 provinces got self-government
  • Diarchy at centre: reserved subjects (defence, foreign affairs) under Viceroy; transferred subjects under elected ministers. NEVER implemented (All-India Federation never came into being)
  • All-India Federation: British provinces + princely states. NEVER implemented (princes refused to join)
  • Three lists: Federal List (centre), Provincial List (provinces), Concurrent List (both — federal prevails). Adopted by Indian Constitution (1950) as Union, State, Concurrent
  • Federal Court: established 1937 (Delhi) — predecessor of Supreme Court (1950)
  • RBI: established April 1, 1935 (under RBI Act 1934)
  • Franchise: limited to about 10% (property + education qualifications). NOT universal adult franchise
  • 1937 elections: held under this Act; INC won 7 of 11 provinces; Muslim League won 2
  • Nehru's critique: "a new charter of slavery" — limited autonomy, British control at centre
Lord Linlithgow (Viceroy 1936-1943) — the longest-serving Viceroy of India. He oversaw the implementation of the Government of India Act 1935 (provincial part, 1937), the 1937 elections, the resignation of Congress ministries (October 1939, WWII), and the Quit India Movement (1942).

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — 1937 Elections & First Elected Ministry
  • 1937 provincial elections: held under the Government of India Act 1935. The INC won in Bihar and formed the provincial ministry.
  • Sri Krishna Singh: became the first Premier (later Chief Minister) of Bihar (1937-1939, then 1946-1961). Known as "Bihar Kesari."
  • Anugrah Narayan Sinha: became the Deputy Premier (later Deputy Chief Minister) of Bihar. He was the key finance and development minister.
  • Significance: Bihar's first experience of elected self-government. The Congress ministry implemented reforms in education, land revenue, and local government. The ministry resigned in October 1939 (WWII declared without consulting Indian leaders).
  • Return to power (1946): after elections in 1946, Sri Krishna Singh again became Premier — and continued as Chief Minister after independence (1947-1961), becoming the longest-serving CM of Bihar.

📰 Current Relevance
  • Constitutional legacy: the GoI Act 1935 is the most important precursor to the Indian Constitution (1950). Many features were adopted: three lists (Union, State, Concurrent), Federal Court (→ Supreme Court), RBI, bicameral legislature, and the office of the Governor.
  • Separate electorates debate: the system of separate electorates (introduced 1909, extended 1919, 1932) was abolished by the Indian Constitution (1950) — which established universal adult franchise with reserved seats for SCs/STs instead.
  • 1935 Act as constitutional framework: the GoI Act 1935 served as the constitutional framework for India until January 26, 1950 (when the Indian Constitution came into force). It was also the framework for Pakistan until 1956.
  • Provincial autonomy → federalism: the 1935 Act's provincial autonomy was the seed of Indian federalism. The Indian Constitution strengthened it with a strong centre (unlike the 1935 Act's weak centre — the All-India Federation never came into being).

Q1. The Government of India Act 1909 is also known as:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The GoI Act 1909 = Morley-Minto Reforms (Lord Morley, SoS + Lord Minto, Viceroy). Introduced separate electorates for Muslims for the first time.
Q2. Which Act introduced the system of 'separate electorates' for Muslims for the first time?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1909 Act introduced separate electorates for Muslims — a British "divide and rule" strategy demanded by the Muslim League (founded 1906).
Q3. The Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919) introduced which key feature in the provinces?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 1919 Act introduced Diarchy in provinces: reserved subjects (Governor's council) + transferred subjects (Indian ministers responsible to legislature).
Q4. Who made the historic declaration of August 1917 promising 'gradual development of self-governing institutions' in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Edwin Montagu (SoS) made the August 1917 declaration promising "gradual development of self-governing institutions." This led to the GoI Act 1919.
Q5. The Government of India Act 1935 was described by Jawaharlal Nehru as:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Nehru called the 1935 Act "a new charter of slavery." Gandhi called the Cripps Mission (1942) "a post-dated cheque on a crashing bank" — different quote.
Q6. Which Act granted 'provincial autonomy' and abolished diarchy in the provinces?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1935 Act granted provincial autonomy (abolished diarchy in provinces). Diarchy was instead introduced at the centre (never implemented).
Q7. The Government of India Act 1935 proposed an 'All-India Federation' comprising British provinces and princely states. Was it implemented?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The All-India Federation was NEVER implemented — the princely states refused to join. Only the provincial part came into force (1937).
Q8. Which institution was established by the Government of India Act 1935?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 1935 Act established the Federal Court (1937) — predecessor of the Supreme Court (1950). The RBI was established under the RBI Act 1934.
Q9. The Government of India Act 1919 introduced which feature at the centre?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The 1919 Act introduced a bicameral legislature: Council of State (Upper House) + Legislative Assembly (Lower House). Also introduced direct elections (limited franchise ~10%).
Q10. Arrange the constitutional reforms in chronological order: 1. Morley-Minto Reforms 2. Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms 3. Government of India Act 1935 4. Indian Councils Act 1861
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Indian Councils Act 1861 → Morley-Minto (1909) → Montagu-Chelmsford (1919) → GoI Act 1935.
Q11. Who was the Viceroy of India when the Morley-Minto Reforms (1909) were introduced?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lord Minto (Viceroy 1905-1910) + Lord Morley (SoS) = Morley-Minto Reforms (1909). Curzon was the previous Viceroy (1899-1905).
Q12. Who was the Viceroy of India when the Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919) were introduced?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Lord Chelmsford (Viceroy 1916-1921) + Edwin Montagu (SoS) = Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919).
Q13. Consider the following about the Government of India Act 1919: 1. It introduced diarchy in the provinces. 2. It introduced bicameral legislature at the centre. 3. It introduced universal adult franchise. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is WRONG — the 1919 Act did NOT introduce universal adult franchise; only ~10% could vote.
Q14. The Government of India Act 1935 divided powers into how many lists?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Three lists: Federal (centre), Provincial (provinces), Concurrent (both). This was adopted by the Indian Constitution (1950) as Union, State, Concurrent.
Q15. Under the Government of India Act 1935, what was the franchise (voting rights) percentage of the population?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — About 10% — based on property, education, and tax qualifications. NOT universal adult franchise. Universal franchise came with the Indian Constitution (1950).
Q16. The 1937 provincial elections were held under which Act?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 1937 elections were held under the GoI Act 1935. The INC won in 7 of 11 provinces. The Muslim League won only 2 — demonstrating limited mass support.
Q17. Which Act introduced the system of 'communal representation' (separate electorates) for the first time?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1909 Act introduced communal representation (separate electorates) for Muslims. The 1919 Act extended it to Sikhs, Christians, Anglo-Indians, and Europeans.
Q18. Who was the Secretary of State for India who made the August 1917 declaration?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Edwin Montagu (SoS 1917-1922) made the August 1917 declaration. He then drafted the Montagu-Chelmsford Report (1918) with Viceroy Chelmsford.
Q19. The Indian Councils Act 1861 is significant for which reason?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The 1861 Act introduced non-official (Indian) members into legislative councils for the first time — a limited but important first step.
Q20. The Reserve Bank of India was established in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The RBI was established on April 1, 1935, under the RBI Act 1934. The GoI Act 1935 provided the constitutional framework. Nationalised in 1949.
Q21. Which of the following was NOT a feature of the Government of India Act 1919?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Provincial autonomy was a feature of the 1935 Act, NOT the 1919 Act. The 1919 Act introduced diarchy (not full autonomy), bicameral legislature, and direct elections with limited franchise.
Q22. The Simon Commission (1927) was appointed to review which Act?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Simon Commission (1927) was appointed to review the GoI Act 1919. It was boycotted (no Indian member). Its report (1930) led to the Round Table Conferences and the GoI Act 1935.
Q23. Bihar's connection with the constitutional reforms was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Bihar's connection is through the 1937 elections (under GoI Act 1935). The INC won and formed the ministry — Sri Krishna Singh as first Premier, Anugrah Narayan Sinha as Deputy Premier.
Q24. Which constitutional reform introduced the principle of 'dyarchy' (diarchy) for the first time?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 1919 Act introduced dyarchy for the first time — in the provinces. The 1935 Act abolished diarchy in provinces and introduced it at the centre (never implemented).
Q25. The Indian Independence Act 1947 was based on which plan?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Indian Independence Act 1947 was based on the Mountbatten Plan (June 3 Plan). It partitioned India into two dominions — India and Pakistan — and granted independence on August 15, 1947.
