🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ This IS the Bihar Topic — Bihar-First Current Affairs
  • This is Bihar-first current affairs: the same NITI publications that generate national questions (cross-link Topic #11) supply Bihar-specific one-liners. Bihar's SDG 2 score (24) and SDG 4 score (32) identify the weakest goal areas — BPSC can ask either number.
  • Why Bihar is last in SDG Index: high multidimensional poverty (33.76%), low nutrition outcomes (largest MPI contributor at 27.95%), low educational attainment, and infrastructure gaps — all reflected in SDG 2 = 24 and SDG 4 = 32.
  • Rural-urban divide: Bihar's rural MPI headcount (36.95%) substantially exceeded urban (16.67%) in NFHS-5 — poverty is disproportionately rural. This is why Bihar's rural development schemes (Saat Nischay, cross-link Topic #15) matter.
  • Decline narrative: Bihar's MPI headcount fell from 51.89% (NFHS-4) to 33.76% (NFHS-5) — an 18.13 pp drop. NITI estimates a further decline to 26.59% by 2022-23 (extrapolated), with 377.09 lakh escaping poverty. This is the "improvement" angle BPSC can test.
  • Data hygiene rule: always carry the source period with the number — "33.76%, NITI National MPI, NFHS-5 (2019-21)" and "26.59%, NITI 2024 progress report (extrapolated estimate)". Never label an extrapolated figure as a direct survey result.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps (2025–26 Cycle)
  • SDG India Index 2023-24: Bihar 57 (lowest state, Performer); India 71; Kerala & Uttarakhand 79 (joint top). 16 SDGs ranked. This is the most recent edition — the next (2025-26) may not be published before the exam.
  • NITI MPI 2024 progress report: Bihar's 2022-23 H = 26.59% is explicitly extrapolated — NOT a direct NFHS-5 observation. 377.09 lakh escaped multidimensional poverty 2013-14 to 2022-23. This is the "estimate vs observation" trap.
  • NFHS-5 (2019-21) remains the latest directly observed data: Bihar H = 33.76%, A = 47.40%, MPI = 0.160. NFHS-6 has not been conducted. Census 2021 was postponed; Census 2027 with caste enumeration is planned (cross-link Topic #21).
  • Data hygiene — the #1 trap: a question can be numerically correct but wrong if it (a) assigns an extrapolated estimate to NFHS-5, (b) switches the SDG edition, (c) swaps SDG 2 and SDG 4 scores, (d) confuses rural and urban headcounts, or (e) attributes the national score (71) to Bihar.
  • National vs Global MPI — the #2 trap: NITI Aayog publishes India's National MPI. UNDP + OPHI publish the Global MPI. Both use the Alkire-Foster method but with different indicators and reference populations. Bihar's figures come from the National MPI.
  • Publisher trap: SDG India Index → NITI Aayog (NOT UNDP, NOT MoSPI). National MPI → NITI Aayog (NOT UNDP/OPHI). BPSC's "wrongly matched" format tests exactly this confusion.
✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Bihar's SDG India Index 2023-24 composite score was: (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — NITI Aayog gave Bihar 57, the lowest state score. 71 is India's national composite score — not Bihar's. 79 is the top state score (Kerala & Uttarakhand). 62 is a distractor. All four are real SDG Index numbers, making this a classic BPSC number-matching trap.
Q2. Bihar's overall category in the SDG India Index with a score of 57 is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — SDG Index bands: Aspirant (0–49), Performer (50–64), Front Runner (65–99), Achiever (100). Bihar's 57 falls in the Performer band (50–64). Trap: 'Aspirant' requires a score below 50, and 'Front Runner' requires 65+. Bihar is the lowest state but still a Performer, not an Aspirant.
Q3. Which states jointly topped the SDG India Index 2023-24? (70th BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Kerala and Uttarakhand jointly topped at 79. The 70th BPSC asked 'which state topped' (answer: Kerala). Bihar was last at 57. The 2023-24 edition was the first to produce a joint topper — a fresh fact BPSC can exploit.
Q4. Bihar's especially low SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) score in the 2023-24 Index was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar scored 24 on SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) — its weakest goal. 32 is Bihar's SDG 4 (Quality Education) score; 57 is Bihar's composite; 71 is India's national composite. BPSC swaps SDG numbers to create traps — memorise the pair: SDG 2 = 24, SDG 4 = 32.
Q5. Bihar's SDG 4 (Quality Education) score in the 2023-24 Index was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bihar's SDG 4 score was 32. The high-yield pair is SDG 2 = 24 and SDG 4 = 32 — do not interchange them. 24 is Zero Hunger; 32 is Quality Education. 57 is Bihar's composite; 79 is the top state score.
Q6. Bihar's multidimensionally poor headcount ratio in NFHS-5 (2019-21) was:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The directly observed NFHS-5 headcount was 33.76%. 51.89% is NFHS-4 (2015-16) — the previous survey round. 26.59% is a later extrapolated estimate (2022-23), not a survey observation. 16.67% is Bihar's urban headcount. All four are real Bihar MPI numbers from different periods — the ultimate BPSC trap.
Q7. Bihar's MPI headcount in NFHS-4 (2015-16) was:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Bihar's MPI headcount declined from 51.89% (NFHS-4, 2015-16) to 33.76% (NFHS-5, 2019-21) — a 18.13 percentage-point drop. 47.40% is the NFHS-5 intensity (A), not headcount (H). 33.76% is the NFHS-5 headcount.
Q8. The source period for the directly observed 33.76% Bihar MPI figure is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — NITI's National MPI Progress Review uses NFHS-5 (2019-21) for the 33.76% Bihar headcount — a direct household survey observation. NFHS-4 (2015-16) gives 51.89%. Census 2021 was never conducted (postponed). NSS 2011-12 is a different survey entirely.
Q9. Bihar's MPI intensity (A) in 2019-21 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Headcount H = 33.76%, intensity A = 47.40%, and MPI = H×A = 0.160. 51.01% is the NFHS-4 intensity (2015-16); 0.265 is the NFHS-4 MPI value. Intensity measures the average share of deprivations experienced by poor households.
Q10. Bihar's MPI value (H × A) in 2019-21 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bihar's 2019-21 MPI = H (33.76%) × A (47.40%) = 0.160. 0.265 is the NFHS-4 (2015-16) MPI value when headcount was 51.89% and intensity 51.01%. The MPI value fell by 40% between the two survey rounds.
Q11. Which Bihar area had the higher MPI headcount in NFHS-5 (2019-21)?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bihar's rural headcount was 36.95%, versus 16.67% urban — rural poverty was more than double urban. The question tests rural-urban reversal: students may swap the two figures. The state total (33.76%) is a weighted average, not a simple mean.
Q12. Bihar's urban MPI headcount in NFHS-5 (2019-21) was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Urban Bihar was 16.67%; rural Bihar was 36.95%; the state total was 33.76%. BPSC often swaps urban and rural figures — the urban number is always much lower because cities have better access to services and living standards.
Q13. The 2022-23 Bihar MPI headcount estimate of 26.59% should be described as:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — NITI's 2024 progress report explicitly labels the 26.59% figure as extrapolated from earlier survey data — it is not a direct household-survey observation. NFHS-6 has not been conducted yet; Census 2021 was postponed; PLFS measures employment, not multidimensional poverty.
Q14. Estimated people escaping multidimensional poverty in Bihar, 2013-14 to 2022-23, were:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — NITI estimates 377.09 lakh (3.77 crore) people in Bihar escaped multidimensional poverty over that interval — one of the largest declines among major states. 37.709 lakh is the same number divided by 10 (decimal trap). 26.59 lakh uses the headcount percentage; 51.89 lakh uses the NFHS-4 percentage.
Q15. In Bihar's 2019-21 MPI profile, the largest listed indicator contribution was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Nutrition contributed 27.95% — the largest single indicator contribution to Bihar's MPI. This is why Bihar's SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) score is so low (24). Electricity, drinking water and bank account deprivations have been addressed more aggressively by state schemes.
Q16. The National MPI is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — NITI Aayog publishes India's National MPI, using NFHS data. UNDP co-publishes the global MPI with OPHI (Oxford) — a separate product with different methodology. RBI focuses on monetary policy; state finance departments do not publish national indices.
Q17. Consider the following about Bihar MPI: 1. Headcount fell from 51.89% to 33.76%. 2. Intensity fell from 51.01% to 47.40%. Which statement(s) is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Both statements are correct for NFHS-4 (2015-16) versus NFHS-5 (2019-21). Headcount fell by 18.13 pp and intensity by 3.61 pp. BPSC's multi-statement format tests whether you know both H and A declined — not just headcount.
Q18. A score of 57 in the SDG India Index must NOT be confused with:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 57 is Bihar's NITI SDG composite score — a domestic index number, not an international rank. India's national score is 71 (a different number in the same index). Bihar does not appear in the global HDI table (HDI ranks countries, not states). All four seem plausible but only the international HDI table is a category error.
Q19. Which pair is correctly matched?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bihar SDG 2 = 24 (correct). The traps: SDG 4 = 32 (not 24); MPI 2019-21 = 33.76% (51.89% is NFHS-4); Bihar SDG composite = 57 (71 is India's national score). Only option A has the right pairing.
Q20. The SDG India Index is published by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — NITI Aayog publishes the SDG India Index, while UNDP publishes HDR and WEF publishes the Gender Gap Report. MoSPI publishes PLFS. All four are distinct publishers — BPSC's favourite 'wrongly matched' format tests exactly this confusion.
Q21. The SDG India Index 2023-24 tracks state progress against how many SDGs?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — It ranks progress on 16 SDGs; SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals) is excluded from scoring/ranking — it is treated as a shared national goal. The UN has 17 SDGs, so '17' is the trap. Millennium Development Goals were replaced by SDGs in 2015.
Q22. Bihar's MPI calculations combine deprivations across which dimensions?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — MPI is multidimensional: health (nutrition, child mortality), education (years of schooling, attendance), and living standards (cooking fuel, sanitation, water, electricity, housing, assets). It is not income-based — that is the key distinction from poverty-line measures.
Q23. Which is the correct order for Bihar MPI 2019-21: urban H, rural H, state H?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Urban H = 16.67% (lowest), rural H = 36.95% (highest), and overall Bihar H = 33.76% (weighted average, closer to rural because Bihar is predominantly rural). Option B swaps urban and rural; option C jumbles all three; option D uses figures from different periods.
Q24. Which statement about Bihar's reports profile is FALSE?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 26.59% figure is an extrapolated estimate from NITI's 2024 progress report — it is NOT directly observed NFHS-5 data. The directly observed NFHS-5 (2019-21) figure is 33.76%. The other three statements are all true: Bihar was lowest (57), in Performer band, and NFHS-5 H = 33.76%.
Q25. The best exam-safe description of Bihar's reports profile is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — BPSC traps mix editions, publishers and data years. Always bind a number to its report name, publisher, and reference period — e.g., 'Bihar MPI headcount 33.76%, NITI Aayog National MPI, NFHS-5 (2019-21)'. The other options are all common student errors that BPSC exploits.
