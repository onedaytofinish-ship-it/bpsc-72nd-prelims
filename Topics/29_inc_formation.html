B. The "Safety Valve Theory" PYQ PATTERN

The "Safety Valve Theory" was propounded by Lala Lajpat Rai in his book Youngman. It states that Hume, with the approval of Viceroy Dufferin, founded the INC as a "safety valve" — a controlled outlet for Indian discontent, preventing a violent revolution.

  • Proponents: Lala Lajpat Rai, R.P. Dutt, Bipan Chandra (with caveats).
  • Modern view (Bipan Chandra): the INC had genuine Indian roots; the "safety valve" interpretation is overstated. Hume helped organise it, but Indian nationalist leaders (Naoroji, Pherozeshah Mehta, Surendranath Banerjea) were already organising conferences.
  • Pre-Congres organisations: the Indian Association (Surendranath Banerjea, Calcutta, 1876); the Bombay Presidency Association (Pherozeshah Mehta, 1885); the Madras Mahajana Sabha (1884); the Poona Sarvajanik Sabha (1870). These provided the base for the INC.
Womesh Chunder Bonnerjee (1844–1906) — first President of the INC (1885 Bombay session). He was the first Indian elected to the British House of Commons (Finsbury Central, 1892).

🧠 Mnemonic — Moderates vs Extremists:
Moderates = "PPP" (Prayers-Petitions-Protests): Naoroji, Gokhale, Pherozeshah Mehta, Surendranath. Constitutional, gradual reform.
Extremists = "Lal-Bal-Pal" + Direct Action: Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Bipin Pal, Aurobindo. Swadeshi, Boycott, Swaraj.
"Moderates prayed; Extremists acted."

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & the Early INC
  • 1912 Bankipore (Patna) INC Session — Sachchidananda Sinha: the only INC session held in Bihar in the pre-Independence era. Sachchidananda Sinha (1871–1950) presided. He was a leading Bihar Congressman, lawyer, journalist (founded the Hindustan Review), and interim President of the Constituent Assembly in 1946 (before Rajendra Prasad's election). This is the highest-yield Bihar-specific fact for this topic.
  • Annie Besant visited Bihar in 1918 (71st BPSC PYQ): Annie Besant, the first woman President of the INC (1917) and founder of the Home Rule League (1916), visited Bihar in 1918 to strengthen the Home Rule movement. The 71st BPSC asked this directly. Her visit energised Bihar's nationalist circles.
  • Rajendra Prasad — Bihar's link to the later INC: Rajendra Prasad (from Bihar) presided over the 1934 Bombay INC session. He was a key Congressman from Bihar who went on to become the first President of India (1950–1962). His involvement begins after the Moderates-Extremists era but is Bihar's strongest link.
  • Bihar separated from Bengal (1912): Bihar was separated from the Bengal Presidency in 1912 (capital Patna) — the same year as the Bankipore INC session. The 1905 Bengal Partition (Curzon) had united Bengal-Bihar-Orissa; the 1911 annulment led to Bihar's separate identity.
  • Swadeshi in Bihar: the 1905 Swadeshi movement had limited impact in Bihar (compared to Bengal), but Bihar's nationalist press (the Searchlight of Patna, founded 1917) carried the INC's message. Champaran (1917, Gandhi) was Bihar's first mass nationalist movement.

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. The Indian National Congress was founded in which year and at which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Indian National Congress was founded on December 28, 1885, at Bombay (Gokuldas Tejpal Sanskrit College). The first session was presided over by Womesh Chunder Bonnerjee. 72 delegates attended. A.O. Hume, a retired British civil servant, was the key organiser (called the 'Father of the INC').
Q2. Who is known as the 'Father of the Indian National Congress'?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Allan Octavian Hume (1829–1912), a retired British civil servant, was the chief organiser and founder of the INC. He convened the first session in 1885 at Bombay. He is called the 'Father of the INC'. His aim was to provide a 'safety valve' for Indian discontent (per Lajpat Rai's interpretation).
Q3. Who presided over the first session of the INC in 1885?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Womesh Chunder Bonnerjee (1844–1906) presided over the first session of the INC at Bombay (December 28–31, 1885). He was the first Indian to contest and win a seat in the British Parliament (as a Liberal from Finsbury Central, 1892, though unseated in 1895). He also presided over the 1892 session.
Q4. Which theory states that A.O. Hume founded the INC as a 'safety valve' to let out Indian discontent?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 'Safety Valve Theory' was articulated by Lala Lajpat Rai (in his book 'Youngman'). It states that Hume (with the blessing of Viceroy Lord Dufferin) founded the INC as a 'safety valve' to channel Indian discontent through constitutional means, preventing violent revolution. Modern historians (Bipan Chandra) dispute this — the INC was genuinely Indian in origin.
Q5. The 'Drain of Wealth Theory' was propounded by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Dadabhai Naoroji (1825–1917), the 'Grand Old Man of India', propounded the 'Drain of Wealth Theory' in his book 'Poverty and Un-British Rule in India' (1901). He was the first Indian MP (British House of Commons, from Finsbury Central, 1892), and the only Congress President to be elected thrice (1886, 1893, 1906).
Q6. Which Viceroy was in office at the time of the INC's foundation in 1885?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lord Dufferin (Viceroy 1884–1888) was in office when the INC was founded in December 1885. He initially supported Hume's efforts (allegedly the 'safety valve') but later became critical of the Congress. Lord Ripon (1880–1884) preceded him and was popular among Indians (Ilbert Bill, local self-government).
Q7. Who among the following was NOT a Moderate leader of the INC?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Bal Gangadhar Tilak (1856–1920) was NOT a Moderate — he was the leader of the Extremists ('Lokmanya Tilak'). The Moderates (1885–1905) included Gokhale, Pherozeshah Mehta, Dadabhai Naoroji, R.C. Dutt, and Surendranath Banerjea. Tilak's slogan was 'Swaraj is my birthright, and I shall have it'.
Q8. The split between the Moderates and Extremists at the Surat Session happened in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Surat Split took place in 1907 at the Surat Session of the INC. The Moderates (led by Pherozeshah Mehta and Gokhale) and Extremists (led by Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Bipin Chandra Pal) clashed over the presidency and resolutions. The session was adjourned and the Congress split — Moderates retained control until 1916 (Lucknow Pact reunification).
Q9. Who was the President of the INC when the Surat Split (1907) occurred?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rash Behari Ghosh was elected President of the Surat Session (1907) — the Moderates' candidate, opposed by the Extremists who wanted Tilak's group. The session broke up in chaos. The split became formal at the Madras session (1908) where the Extremists were expelled.
Q10. The famous 'Lal-Bal-Pal' trio of Extremist leaders consisted of:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Lal-Bal-Pal = Lala Lajpat Rai (Punjab, 'Lal'), Bal Gangadhar Tilak (Maharashtra, 'Bal'), and Bipin Chandra Pal (Bengal, 'Pal'). They led the Extremist wing from 1905 (Bengal Partition) through the Swadeshi movement. A and C are the same — answer is (D).
Q11. Dadabhai Naoroji presided over the INC sessions of which years?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Dadabhai Naoroji was elected President of the INC three times — 1886 (Calcutta, 2nd session), 1893 (Lahore), and 1906 (Calcutta). The 1906 session (his presidency) is famous for the resolution on Swaraj, Swadeshi, Boycott, and National Education — the 'four-fold' programme. He is the only Congress President to be elected thrice.
Q12. The INC's 1906 Calcutta session (presided by Naoroji) declared the four-fold programme consisting of:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1906 Calcutta session (presided by Dadabhai Naoroji) adopted the four-fold programme: (1) Swaraj (self-government), (2) Swadeshi (use of Indian goods), (3) Boycott (of British goods), and (4) National Education. This was the Extremists' agenda, accepted by the Moderates. The demand for 'Swaraj' was formally made here for the first time.
Q13. Who among the following founded the 'Servants of India Society' and was a Moderate leader?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gopal Krishna Gokhale (1866–1915) was the leader of the Moderates. He founded the Servants of India Society (1905, Poona) and the Ranade Institute of Economics. He was a constitutionalist, opposed to Tilak's Extremist methods. He was Gandhi's political guru ('I have met no one like Gokhale').
Q14. Who said, 'Swaraj is my birthright, and I shall have it'?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Bal Gangadhar Tilak's famous slogan was 'Swaraj is my birthright, and I shall have it' (Swarajya maza janmasiddha hakka aahe ani to mi milavnarach). He was the 'Father of Indian Unrest' (per Valentine Chirol). He started the newspapers 'Kesari' (Marathi) and 'Mahratta' (English), and the Ganapati and Shivaji festivals for nationalist mobilisation.
Q15. The 'Safety Valve Theory' of Hume was propounded by which historian/leader?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lala Lajpat Rai propounded the 'Safety Valve Theory' in his book 'Youngman'. According to this theory, Hume, with the approval of Viceroy Dufferin, founded the INC as a 'safety valve' to release Indian discontent safely (preventing a violent revolution). Modern historians (Bipan Chandra) reject this — the INC had genuine Indian roots, not British creation.
Q16. The INC session of 1916 at Lucknow is famous for:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The 1916 Lucknow session achieved two historic things: (1) Reunification of Moderates and Extremists (split since 1907 Surat), with Ambika Charan Mazumdar presiding; (2) The Lucknow Pact between the INC and the Muslim League (Tilak and Jinnah were the architects) — an agreement on joint political demands and Muslim representation in councils. Annie Besant's Home Rule League influence was evident.
Q17. Which early Congress leader was known as the 'Lion of Bombay' and drafted the Bombay Presidency Association?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sir Pherozeshah Mehta (1845–1915) was the 'Lion of Bombay'. He founded the Bombay Presidency Association (1885) and was President of the INC in 1890 (Calcutta). He was a leading Moderate and an eminent lawyer (drafted the Bombay Municipal Act). His statue stands outside the Bombay Municipal Corporation HQ.
Q18. Which newspaper was started by Dadabhai Naoroji and later carried the Congress ideology?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Dadabhai Naoroji started 'Rast Goftar' (Truth Teller), a Gujarati newspaper, in 1851 in Bombay. It was an early nationalist voice. Tilak started 'Kesari' (Marathi) and 'Mahratta' (English) in 1881. Amrita Bazar Patrika was founded by the Ghosh brothers (1868, Bengal). The Hindu (Madras) was founded by G. Subramania Iyer in 1878.
Q19. Consider the following: 1. INC founded 1885 at Bombay. 2. First President W.C. Bonnerjee. 3. A.O. Hume was the chief organiser. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct: (1) INC was founded on December 28, 1885, at Bombay (Gokuldas Tejpal Sanskrit College). (2) Womesh Chunder Bonnerjee was the first President. (3) A.O. Hume, a retired British civil servant, was the chief organiser (the 'Father of the INC'). 72 delegates attended the first session.
Q20. Arrange in chronological order: 1. INC founded 2. Surat Split 3. Bengal Partition 4. Lucknow Pact
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — INC founded (1885) → Bengal Partition (1905) → Surat Split (1907) → Lucknow Pact (1916). The Bengal Partition triggered the Swadeshi movement and the rise of Extremism, which culminated in the Surat Split (1907). The Lucknow Pact (1916) reunited Moderates and Extremists and brought Congress-League unity.
Q21. The first woman President of the INC was: (71st BPSC PYQ reference — Annie Besant)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Annie Besant was the first woman President of the INC (1917, Calcutta session). She was a British-born Theosophist who led the Home Rule League. Sarojini Naidu (1925, Kanpur) was the first Indian woman President. Annie Besant predates her by 8 years. The 71st BPSC PYQ referenced her 1917 presidency and her 1918 Bihar visit.
Q22. Which Viceroy was famous for his benevolent reforms and was known as the 'Good Viceroy' before the INC's foundation?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Lord Ripon (Viceroy 1880–1884) was the 'Good Viceroy'. He introduced the Ilbert Bill (1883, allowing Indian judges to try Europeans — defeated by the white lobby), local self-government (1882, the Resolution on Local Self-Government), and the Factory Act (1881). His pro-Indian reforms inspired the early nationalists, and he left just a year before the INC was founded. Lord Lytton (1876–1880) was the opposite — Vernacular Press Act, Arms Act.
Q23. Who presided over the 1886 Calcutta session of the INC, where the early nationalist economic critique was formally articulated?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Dadabhai Naoroji presided over the 1886 Calcutta session (the 2nd INC session). He was the President — the session where the nationalist economic critique was first formally articulated. He would go on to be President again in 1893 (Lahore) and 1906 (Calcutta).
Q24. Bihar's connection with the early INC is through which leader who hosted the 1912 Bankipore (Patna) session?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Sachchidananda Sinha (1871–1950) presided over the 1912 Bankipore (Patna) session of the INC — the only INC session held in Bihar in the pre-Independence era. He was a leading Bihar Congressman, lawyer, and journalist (founded the 'Hindustan Review'). He was also the interim President of the Constituent Assembly in 1946 (before Rajendra Prasad's election).
Q25. The 1905 Bengal Partition led to the Swadeshi movement. The Swadeshi movement was formally endorsed at which INC session?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Swadeshi movement was formally endorsed at the 1906 Calcutta session of the INC (presided by Dadabhai Naoroji), which adopted the four-fold programme of Swaraj, Swadeshi, Boycott, and National Education. The Bengal Partition (1905, by Lord Curzon) triggered the movement; the Banaras session (1905, presided by Gokhale) was still Moderate-dominated. The Surat session (1907) saw the split over these very issues.
