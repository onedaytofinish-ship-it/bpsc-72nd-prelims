D. Economic Survey 2025-26 — The Numbers Behind the Budget 20th EDITION

  • GSDP growth 2024-25: 8.6% at constant (real) prices — ahead of India's 6.5%; 13.1% at current prices.
  • Per-capita GSDP 2024-25: ₹76,490 (+13% YoY) — barely one-third of the national average (₹2,34,859).
  • Per-capita GDP 2023-24: ₹68,624 — lowest among ALL Indian states (India ₹2,15,935). District spread: Patna highest ₹2,41,220, Begusarai ₹1,05,600; 11 districts below ₹50,000; lowest Sheohar ₹38,214, Araria ₹44,134.
  • Sector shares 2024-25: Agriculture 23% : Manufacturing 23% : Services 54%.
  • Outstanding debt: ₹3.74 lakh cr ≈ 37.7% of GSDP.
  • GSDP trajectory (budget documents): 2024-25 ₹9.92 lakh cr → 2025-26 RE ₹11.40 lakh cr → 2026-27 BE ₹13.09 lakh cr.

E. FY27 Flagship Announcements SPEECH HIGHLIGHTS

  • Saat Nischay-3 (2025–2030): the third resolve programme — double per-capita income, accelerated industrialisation, higher farmer income, better education/medical services, new planned cities, ease of living.
  • Jannayak Karpoori Thakur Kisan Samman Nidhi (NEW): ₹3,000/year per farmer as a state top-up over PM-KISAN's ₹6,000 (total ₹9,000/yr) — named after former CM Karpoori Thakur.
  • Industrial push: Integrated Manufacturing Cluster (IMC) Phase-2 at Gaya (Amritsar–Kolkata Industrial Corridor); defence corridor; pharma park; fintech city.
  • Transport: 5 new expressways; public buses to shift to CNG/electric; 2,000+ EV charging stations.
  • Education infra (Saat Nischay-3): a model school in every block + a degree college in every block.
  • Jobs pledge: NDA's headline promise of 1 crore jobs/employment opportunities, with special budget provisioning.
  • 16th Finance Commission (tabled 1 Feb 2026; Chair Arvind Panagariya): Bihar's tax-devolution share 9.95% for 2026-31 (down from 10.06%); Bihar grants 2026-31 — rural local bodies ₹51,923 cr, urban local bodies ₹9,169 cr, disaster management ₹13,615 cr; Patna eligible for a special wastewater-infra grant up to ₹5,000 cr.

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ What the Budget Reveals About Bihar's Fiscal DNA
  • Dependence on the Centre: in 2026-27 Bihar raises only ~26% of its revenue receipts itself; 74% comes from the Centre (≈55% tax devolution + ≈18% grants) — the single most-asked structural fact about Bihar's finances.
  • MMRY's fiscal shadow: the Mukhyamantri Mahila Rojgar Yojana's ₹10,000 initial grant reached 1.56 crore women — visible in the FY26 revised 'Mahila Sashaktikaran' spend of ₹21,050 cr, versus ₹9,052 cr budgeted for FY27 (PRS Budget 2026-27).
  • Free-power bill: the 125-units-free electricity scheme (effective 1 Aug 2025) was backed by ₹3,797 cr sanctioned to BSPHCL for FY 2025-26; FY27 carries a ₹15,702 cr power subsidy within the ₹18,649 cr energy budget.
  • Pension hike funded: the June 2025 hike of social-security pensions ₹400 → ₹1,100/month (1.16+ crore pensioners) sits inside the Social Welfare budget.
  • Patna's 16th FC windfall: Patna is eligible for a special wastewater-infrastructure grant up to ₹5,000 cr (2026-31); Bihar's local bodies get ₹51,923 cr (rural) + ₹9,169 cr (urban) + ₹13,615 cr (disaster management).
  • Development gap in one line: per-capita GSDP ₹76,490 vs India's ₹2,34,859 — the 'doubling per-capita income' goal of Saat Nischay-3 is written against this gap.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps (2025–26 Cycle)
  • 3 Feb 2026: FY 2026-27 budget of ₹3,47,589.76 cr presented by Bijendra Prasad Yadav — largest in state history. Trap: ₹3,24,925 cr is expenditure excluding debt repayment of ₹22,665 cr — don't pick it as "total outlay".
  • 1 Feb 2026: 16th Finance Commission report tabled — Bihar's devolution 9.95% (2026-31), down from 10.06%; the "increase/decrease" direction is a set-up trap.
  • Revenue SURPLUS ₹1,143 cr (0.1%) in FY27 — BPSC flips surplus↔deficit; fiscal deficit is 3% of GSDP (₹39,112 cr).
  • New scheme to name-drop: Jannayak Karpoori Thakur Kisan Samman Nidhi — ₹3,000/yr state top-up over PM-KISAN ₹6,000 (announced in the FY27 speech). Don't confuse with MMRY's ₹10,000 grant (that is for women, launched Sept 2025).
  • 3 Mar 2025: FY26 budget ₹3,16,895.02 cr by Samrat Chaudhary — the 70th BPSC asked the presenter question for 2024-25; the same question for 2025-26/2026-27 is highly probable.
  • Economic Survey 2025-26 (20th edition, tabled Feb 2026): 8.6% real growth vs India's 6.5%; nominal 13.1%; PCI ₹76,490 (2024-25); ₹68,624 (2023-24, lowest in India). Real-vs-nominal and Bihar-vs-India are the standard traps.
  • District extremes: Patna ₹2,41,220 (highest) vs Sheohar ₹38,214 (lowest); 11 districts below ₹50,000 — matching-type bait.
  • Statics that never age: state budget = Annual Financial Statement under Article 202; Finance Commission under Article 280; Bihar budget 2005-06 = ₹22,568 cr; education has been the top allocation year after year.
✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Who presented the Bihar Budget 2026-27 in the State Assembly? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Bijendra Prasad Yadav (JDU), Finance Minister in the post-election Nitish Kumar government, presented the FY 2026-27 budget on 3 Feb 2026. (B) is the trap: Samrat Chaudhary presented the 2024-25 and 2025-26 budgets — the 70th BPSC asked exactly this for 2024-25.
Q2. Which sector received the highest allocation in the Bihar Budget 2026-27? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Education got ₹68,216 cr = 19.63% of the FY27 budget, the highest of any sector. The 70th BPSC asked the same question for 2024-25 (answer: Education), and FY26 also topped with education (₹60,964.87 cr, 19.24%). Agriculture got only ₹8,463 cr (2.6%).
Q3. The total outlay of the Bihar Budget 2026-27 is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ₹3,47,589.76 crore, the largest budget in Bihar's history. (A) is FY 2025-26, (C) is FY 2024-25 BE, and (D) is the trap — ₹3,24,925 cr is FY27 expenditure EXCLUDING debt repayment of ₹22,665 cr, not the total outlay.
Q4. The Bihar Budget 2026-27 was presented on:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — 3 February 2026. (A) is the Union Budget / 16th Finance Commission tabling date (1 Feb 2026); the FY26 Bihar budget was presented on 3 March 2025 — BPSC mixes the two dates.
Q5. Consider the following statements about the Bihar Budget 2026-27:
1. It is the largest budget in Bihar's history.
2. The fiscal deficit is estimated at 3% of GSDP.
3. It estimates a revenue deficit of ₹1,143 crore.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct (outlay ₹3,47,589.76 cr; fiscal deficit ₹39,112 cr = 3% of GSDP). Statement 3 is the trap: FY27 estimates a revenue SURPLUS of ₹1,143 cr (0.1% of GSDP), not a revenue deficit.
Q6. As per the Bihar Economic Survey 2025-26, the contribution of agriculture to Bihar's GSDP (2024-25) is approximately: (70th BPSC theme)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 2024-25 sector split is Agriculture 23% : Manufacturing 23% : Services 54%. The 70th BPSC asked this exact theme ("contribution of agriculture in the GDP of Bihar") with a similar option spread.
Q7. Bihar's per-capita GSDP for 2024-25, per the Economic Survey 2025-26, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ₹76,490 (+13% YoY), roughly one-third of the national average (₹2,34,859). (A) ₹68,624 is the 2023-24 figure; (D) ₹2,41,220 is Patna district's per-capita income (highest); (C) ₹1,05,600 is Begusarai's.
Q8. Bihar's GSDP growth in 2024-25 at constant (real) prices was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 8.6% real growth, well above India's 6.5%. (C) 13.1% is the CURRENT-price (nominal) growth — the classic real-vs-nominal trap; (A) 6.5% is India's real growth.
Q9. Consider the following statements about Bihar's GSDP in 2024-25 (Economic Survey 2025-26):
1. It grew 13.1% at current prices.
2. It grew 8.6% at constant prices.
3. Bihar's real growth rate was lower than India's.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is false: Bihar's real growth (8.6%) was HIGHER than India's (6.5%) — the Survey's headline boast.
Q10. Consider the following statements about education in the Bihar Budget 2026-27:
1. Education received the highest sectoral allocation — ₹68,216 crore (19.63%).
2. School education accounts for ₹60,204 crore.
3. Higher education accounts for ₹8,012 crore.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct: ₹60,204 cr (school) + ₹8,012 cr (higher) = ₹68,216 cr, the largest sectoral allocation; PRS's wider Education+Sports+Arts club is ₹70,141 cr (21.7% of sectoral spend, vs the 14.5% all-state average).
Q11. The Jannayak Karpoori Thakur Kisan Samman Nidhi, announced in the Bihar Budget 2026-27, provides:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — It is a state top-up of ₹3,000/yr OVER the Centre's PM-KISAN ₹6,000 (total ₹9,000/yr), named after former CM Karpoori Thakur. Options (C) and (D) describe the Mukhyamantri Mahila Rojgar Yojana — the classic scheme-swap trap.
Q12. Match List-I (Sector, Bihar Budget 2026-27) with List-II (Allocation):
a) Rural Development — 1) ₹16,840 crore
b) Health & Family Welfare — 2) ₹30,387 crore
c) Police (Home) — 3) ₹20,230 crore
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Rural Development ₹30,387 cr (2nd largest; includes MGNREGS ₹3,192 cr); Health & FW ₹20,230 cr (6.3%; PM-ABHIM ₹1,495 cr); Police ₹16,840 cr (5.2%). Learn the descending order E-R-S-H-E-P to kill matching questions.
Q13. Saat Nischay-3, the resolve programme flagged in the Bihar Budget 2026-27, covers the period:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Saat Nischay-3 runs 2025-2030 (Part-1: 2015-20; Part-2: 2020-25). (D) 2026-31 is the 16th Finance Commission award period — a deliberate lookalike.
Q14. Consider the following statements about Bihar and the 16th Finance Commission (report tabled 1 Feb 2026):
1. Bihar's tax-devolution share is 9.95% for 2026-31.
2. Bihar's share has increased from 10.06%.
3. Bihar's share is the second highest among states, after Uttar Pradesh.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Statements 1 and 3 are correct (UP tops with 17.62%). Statement 2 is the trap: Bihar's share DECREASED from 10.06% to 9.95% — BPSC flips the direction of change.
Q15. Who presented the Bihar Budget 2025-26 (3 March 2025) in the State Assembly? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Samrat Chaudhary, then Dy CM & Finance Minister (BJP), presented the ₹3,16,895.02 cr FY26 budget. These are the 70th BPSC's own options (asked then for 2024-25 — same answer); Vijay Kumar Sinha is the other Dy CM, not the FM.
Q16. The education allocation in the Bihar Budget 2025-26 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ₹60,964.87 cr (19.24% of the FY26 budget), the highest sectoral allocation that year. (A) is the FY27 figure; (C) is FY26 health; (D) is the FY26 Saat Nischay-2 provision.
Q17. Which of the following was NOT announced in the Bihar Budget 2026-27 speech?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 125-units free-electricity scheme was announced on 17 Jul 2025 (cabinet approval 18 Jul 2025, effective 1 Aug 2025) — a pre-election decision, NOT an FY27 budget announcement (the budget only carries the ₹15,702 cr power subsidy that funds it). The other three are FY27 speech items.
Q18. Bihar's GSDP for 2026-27 (budget estimate, current prices) is projected at:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — ₹13.09 lakh cr, +15% over the 2025-26 revised estimate. The trajectory: 2024-25 ₹9.92 lakh cr → 2025-26 RE ₹11.40 lakh cr → 2026-27 BE ₹13.09 lakh cr. (D) ₹3.74 lakh cr is the total outstanding DEBT.
Q19. As per the Economic Survey 2025-26, Bihar's total outstanding debt (₹3.74 lakh crore) is approximately what share of GSDP?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 37.7% of GSDP. The distractors are the page's other percentages: 26% = Bihar's own-tax share of revenue receipts; 3% = FY27 fiscal deficit; 19.6% ≈ education's share of the FY27 budget.
Q20. Consider the following statements about the Bihar Economic Survey 2025-26:
1. It is the 20th edition of the Bihar Economic Survey.
2. Bihar's per-capita GDP in 2023-24 (₹68,624) was the lowest among all Indian states.
3. Sheohar has the highest per-capita income among Bihar's districts.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is inverted: Sheohar has the LOWEST district PCI (₹38,214); PATNA is the highest (₹2,41,220). Highest↔lowest swaps are a BPSC staple.
Q21. The 'Mahila Sashaktikaran' (women empowerment) allocation in the Bihar Budget 2026-27 is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ₹9,052 cr in FY27, within Social Welfare & Nutrition (₹24,710 cr). (A) ₹21,050 cr is the FY 2025-26 REVISED estimate — inflated by MMRY's ₹10,000 grants to 1.56 crore women; (D) is Samagra Shiksha.
Q22. The Integrated Manufacturing Cluster (IMC) Phase-2 under the Amritsar–Kolkata Industrial Corridor, announced in the Bihar Budget 2026-27, is located at:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Gaya. The FY27 industrial slate also includes a defence corridor, pharma park and fintech city — all part of the Saat Nischay-3 industrialisation plank.
Q23. The total capital outlay in the Bihar Budget 2026-27 is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — ₹39,377 cr total capital outlay. The traps are its neighbours: ₹22,665 cr is debt repayment; ₹5,034 cr is capital outlay on roads & bridges alone; ₹5,814 cr is capital outlay on irrigation & flood control.
Q24. The Annual Financial Statement (state budget) is laid before the State Legislature under which Article of the Constitution?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Article 202 mandates the Annual Financial Statement for states; Article 112 is the Union-Budget equivalent. Article 280 constitutes the Finance Commission; Article 368 governs constitutional amendment.
Q25. Bihar's budget in 2005-06 — the baseline from which the present budget has grown roughly 15 times — was approximately:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — ₹22,568 cr in 2005-06. From there to ₹3,47,589.76 cr (2026-27) is the ~15× expansion routinely cited in Bihar budget analyses — and a favourite "how many times" framing.
