🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Pala Dynasty: Buddhist Rulers of Bihar & Bengal
  • Pala dynasty (750-1161 CE): ruled Bihar, Bengal, and parts of Odisha for nearly 400 years. Buddhist rulers.
  • Founder: Gopala (c. 750 CE) — elected as king to end the Matsyanyaya (period of anarchy). First recorded democratic election in India.
  • Greatest rulers: Dharmapala (c. 770-810, founded Vikramashila University) and Devapala (c. 810-850, expanded the empire)
  • Vikramashila University: founded by Dharmapala in Bihar (near Bhagalpur). Major Buddhist centre — second only to Nalanda. Specialised in Tantric Buddhism. Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (c. 1200 CE).
  • Patronage of Buddhism: the Palas built Odantapuri University (Bihar) and patronised Nalanda. They sent Buddhist monks to Tibet (Atisha Dipankara).
  • Decline: the Palas declined in the 12th century and were succeeded by the Senas (Hindu dynasty).

Q1. The Chola Empire reached its greatest extent under which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rajaraja Chola I (985-1014 CE) expanded the empire to include South India, Sri Lanka, Maldives. Built Brihadisvara Temple (1010). Rajendra I further expanded to Bengal and Srivijaya.
Q2. The Brihadisvara Temple at Thanjavur was built by which Chola ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Brihadisvara Temple (completed 1010 CE) built by Rajaraja Chola I. UNESCO World Heritage Site (1987). 66m vimana. Finest Chola architecture in Dravida style.
Q3. Which Chola ruler conquered Bengal and took the title 'Gangaikonda Chola'?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Rajendra Chola I conquered Bengal (ganga-yamna campaign, ~1021-1023 CE), took title 'Gangaikonda Chola', founded Gangaikondacholapuram. Also sent naval expedition to Srivijaya.
Q4. The Chola administration is famous for which system of village governance?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ur (non-brahmin) and Sabha (brahmin) village assemblies. Members elected by lot (kudavolai system). Uttaramerur inscription (Parantaka I, 10th c.) details the process. Finest local self-government in ancient India.
Q5. The Rajputs are traditionally divided into how many main clans?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — 36 royal clans (36 Rajkul). Main: Chauhan, Solanki, Tomar, Pratihara, Rathore, Sisodia, Kachwaha, Chandela. Claim Suryavansha/Chandravansha/Agnikula descent.
Q6. Which Rajput dynasty ruled from Kanauj and was a major power in North India from the 8th-10th centuries?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Pratihara (Gurjara-Pratihara) dynasty ruled from Kanauj (8th-10th c.). Greatest ruler: Mihira Bhoja (836-885). Resisted Arab invasions — bulwark against Islamic expansion for 300+ years.
Q7. The famous Prithviraj Chauhan (who defeated Muhammad Ghori in the First Battle of Tarain, 1191) ruled from which city?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Prithviraj Chauhan (c. 1178-1192) ruled from Delhi (Lal Kot) and Ajmer. Won 1st Tarain (1191); lost 2nd Tarain (1192) — beginning of Muslim rule in North India. Court poet: Chand Bardai (Prithviraj Raso).
Q8. The Second Battle of Tarain (1192) was significant because:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Second Battle of Tarain (1192): Muhammad Ghori defeated and killed Prithviraj Chauhan. Marked the beginning of Muslim rule in North India — led to the Delhi Sultanate (1206 CE).
Q9. The Chola navy was the strongest in ancient India. Which ruler sent a naval expedition to Srivijaya (Sumatra)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Rajendra Chola I sent a naval expedition to Srivijaya (Sumatra/Malaya, ~1025 CE) to secure trade routes to China. Chola navy was strongest in ancient India — controlled Bay of Bengal, Maldives, parts of SE Asia.
Q10. The Uttaramerur inscription, which details the Chola system of village self-government, was issued during the reign of which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Uttaramerur inscription (Parantaka I, c. 919-922 CE, 10th c.) details the Chola village self-government. Describes kudavolai (election by lot), qualifications, committees. 'Magna Carta of village self-government.'
Q11. Which dynasty founded the Chola Empire in the 9th century?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Vijayalaya Chola (c. 850 CE) founded the Chola Empire by capturing Thanjavur. Feudatory of the Pallavas who declared independence. Chola Empire lasted 850-1279 CE (~400 years).
Q12. Arrange the Chola rulers in chronological order: 1. Vijayalaya 2. Rajaraja I 3. Rajendra I 4. Kulottunga
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Vijayalaya (850, founder) → Rajaraja I (985-1014) → Rajendra I (1014-1044, Bengal+Srivijaya) → Kulottunga (1070-1122, last great). Cholas overthrown by Pandyas (1279 CE).
Q13. The Rajputs claimed descent from which mythological lineages?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Three lineages: Suryavansha (Solar, e.g. Sisodias), Chandravansha (Lunar, e.g. Rathores), Agnikula (Fire — 4 clans: Pratihara, Chauhan, Solanki, Parmar, created from fire at Mount Abu).
Q14. The Chola empire was finally overthrown by which dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Pandyas overthrew the Cholas in 1279 CE (defeated Rajendra III). Pandyas and Hoysalas weakened the Cholas in the 12th-13th centuries. After Cholas, Pandyas dominated until Delhi Sultanate's invasion (1311 CE).
Q15. The Chola bronze sculptures (Nataraja, etc.) are considered masterpieces of Indian art. Which ruler's reign is most associated with them?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Chola bronzes (Nataraja, dancing Shiva) associated with Rajaraja I and Rajendra I (10th-11th c.). Made using lost-wax (cire perdue) technique. Masterpieces displayed in museums worldwide.
Q16. Which of the following is NOT a feature of Chola administration?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Iqta system was a Delhi Sultanate feature, NOT Chola. Cholas had Ur/Sabha, kudavolai, strong navy, well-organised bureaucracy, valanadu/nadu/kurram administration.
Q17. The Pratihara ruler Mihira Bhoja (c. 836-885 CE) is known for:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Mihira Bhoja (836-885) expanded the Pratihara empire to its greatest extent (Punjab to Bengal) and resisted Arab invasions. Known as 'Adivaraha' (from his coins). Bulwark against Islamic expansion for 300+ years.
Q18. The Cholas maintained close trade relations with which Southeast Asian empire?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Cholas traded with Srivijaya (Sumatra), Khmer (Cambodia), and Sailendra (Java). Chola navy controlled Bay of Bengal trade routes. Rajendra I sent naval expedition to Srivijaya (~1025 CE).
Q19. Bihar's connection with early medieval India was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Pala dynasty (750-1161 CE) — Buddhist rulers of Bihar and Bengal. Greatest: Dharmapala (founded Vikramashila) and Devapala. Palas patronised Buddhism — built Odantapuri and Vikramashila universities in Bihar.
Q20. The Pala dynasty of Bihar and Bengal was founded by:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Gopala (c. 750 CE) founded the Pala dynasty — elected as king to end the Matsyanyaya (anarchy). First recorded democratic election in India. Son Dharmapala founded Vikramashila University. Palas lasted 400 years (750-1161 CE).
Q21. Which university was founded by the Pala ruler Dharmapala in Bihar?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Vikramashila University was founded by Dharmapala (c. 770-810 CE) in Bihar (near Bhagalpur). Major Buddhist centre — second only to Nalanda. Specialised in Tantric Buddhism. Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (c. 1200 CE).
Q22. The Pallava dynasty, which preceded the Cholas in South India, had their capital at:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Pallava capital: Kanchipuram (Tamil Nadu, c. 4th-9th c.). Greatest ruler: Narasimhavarman I (defeated Chalukya Pulakeshin II). Rock-cut temples at Mahabalipuram (UNESCO site). Predecessors of the Cholas.
Q23. The famous Rajput fort of Chittorgarh (in Rajasthan) is associated with which dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Chittorgarh Fort is associated with the Sisodia dynasty of Mewar. Famous Sisodia rulers: Rana Kumbha (Vijay Stambh), Rana Sanga (fought Babur 1527), Maharana Pratap (fought Akbar). Three jauhars: 1303, 1535, 1568.
Q24. The Chola empire was divided into how many administrative units called 'valanadu' (provinces)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — 8-9 valanadu (provinces) → nadu (districts) → kurram (groups of villages) → ur/sabha (villages). Hierarchical: king → valanadu → nadu → kurram → village. Well-organised administration key to Chola success.
Q25. The Khajuraho temples (UNESCO World Heritage Site) were built by which Rajput dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Khajuraho temples (MP, UNESCO 1986) built by Chandela dynasty (Bundelkhand, 950-1050 CE). Famous for Nagara-style architecture and erotic sculptures. 25 surviving temples out of 85. Greatest ruler: Dhanga (950-1008).
