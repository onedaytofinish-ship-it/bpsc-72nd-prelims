🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Bakhtiyar Khalji & the Destruction of Nalanda
  • Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE): a Turkish general who conquered Bihar and Bengal during the early Delhi Sultanate period. He destroyed the famous Nalanda University and Vikramashila University (~1202 CE) — marking the end of Buddhist learning in India. The library of Nalanda burned for months.
  • Destruction of Nalanda: one of the most catastrophic events in Indian history — Nalanda was one of the world's first residential universities (5th-12th century). The destruction ended the Buddhist tradition of learning in Bihar. The Tibetan monk Dharmasvamin, who visited Nalanda in 1234, found it in ruins.
  • Khalji Dynasty of Bengal: after conquering Bihar and Bengal, Bakhtiyar Khalji established the independent Khalji Dynasty of Bengal (1202-1227) — with capital at Lakhnauti (Gaur). Bihar and Bengal remained under Muslim rule until 1947.
  • Sikandar Lodi: later (1490s), Sikandar Lodi conquered Bihar and brought it under the Lodi Dynasty's direct control.
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 137 (Early Medieval): The Second Battle of Tarain (1192) ended Rajput supremacy and led to the Delhi Sultanate (1206). The Pala dynasty of Bihar was overthrown by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200).
  • Topic 140 (Mughal Empire): The First Battle of Panipat (1526) ended the Delhi Sultanate (Ibrahim Lodi defeated by Babur) and began the Mughal Empire. Babur's artillery was the decisive factor.
  • Topic 142 (Bihar in Medieval): Sher Shah Suri (from Sasaram, Bihar) briefly interrupted the Mughal Empire (1540-1555) — he was an Afghan ruler who defeated Humayun and ruled from Delhi. Sher Shah's administrative reforms were adopted by Akbar.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Qutub Minar: UNESCO World Heritage Site (1993) — one of the most visited monuments in India. 73m (239 ft) tall, started by Qutbuddin Aibak, completed by Iltutmish.
  • Indo-Islamic architecture: the Delhi Sultanate introduced the Indo-Islamic architectural style — blending Islamic arches/domes with Indian carving techniques. Key monuments: Qutub Minar, Alai Darwaza, Begumpuri Mosque, Tughlaqabad Fort.
  • Hindustani language: the Delhi Sultanate period saw the development of Hindustani (Hindi-Urdu) — a blend of Sanskrit-based Prakrit and Persian/Arabic. Amir Khusrau (1253-1325) was a key figure in this development.
  • Destruction of Nalanda: the destruction of Nalanda University by Bakhtiyar Khalji remains a deeply emotional event — UNESCO declared the ruins of Nalanda a World Heritage Site (2016).

Q1. The Delhi Sultanate was founded in which year by which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — 1206 CE by Qutbuddin Aibak (former slave of Ghori). Slave/Mamluk Dynasty. Known as 'Lakh Baksh.' Started Qutub Minar. Ruled 1206-1210.
Q2. The Delhi Sultanate consisted of how many dynasties?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Five: Slave/Mamluk (1206-1290), Khalji (1290-1320), Tughlaq (1320-1414), Sayyid (1414-1451), Lodi (1451-1526). 320 years. Ended at Panipat (1526).
Q3. Who was the first ruler of the Delhi Sultanate to assume the title of 'Sultan'?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Iltutmish (1211-1236) was the first to assume 'Sultan.' Real consolidator: completed Qutub Minar, Iqta system, silver tanka, defeated Mongols.
Q4. The Qutub Minar in Delhi was started by Qutbuddin Aibak and completed by which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Started by Aibak (1199), completed by Iltutmish (1231-1232). 73m tall. UNESCO World Heritage Site (1993). Commemorates victory over Rajputs.
Q5. Razia Sultan (1236-1240 CE) was the first woman ruler of India. She belonged to which dynasty?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Razia Sultan (Slave Dynasty) — first woman ruler of India. Daughter of Iltutmish. Ruled 3.6 years. Killed by her own nobles (1240). Only woman ruler of the Delhi Sultanate.
Q6. Alauddin Khalji (1296-1316 CE) is famous for which military achievement?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Alauddin conquered most of India: Gujarat, Ranthambore, Chittorgarh (1303), Malwa, Deccan (via Malik Kafur, 1307-1313). Also defeated Mongols 6 times. Market reforms.
Q7. Who was the first Delhi Sultan to successfully penetrate the Deccan?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Alauddin's general Malik Kafur conquered Devagiri, Warangal, Dwarasamudra, Madurai (1307-1313). Deccan kingdoms became feudatories. First penetration of Deccan by Delhi Sultanate.
Q8. Muhammad bin Tughlaq (1325-1351 CE) is known for which failed administrative experiment?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Transfer of capital to Daulatabad (1327) — caused massive suffering; reversed. Also: token currency (failed), enhanced taxation (rebellion). 'Mad genius' of Indian history.
Q9. Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1351-1388 CE) is known for which constructive achievement?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Firoz Shah built canals (Yamuna canal), mosques (Begumpuri), cities (Firozabad, Jaunpur), hospitals (darul-shafas). Also: revenue reforms, translated Sanskrit works. Reinstated jaziya.
Q10. The Delhi Sultanate was brought to an end by which event?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — First Battle of Panipat (1526): Babur (Mughal) defeated Ibrahim Lodi (last Lodi Sultan). Babur's artillery was decisive. Beginning of Mughal Empire (1526-1857).
Q11. Arrange the five dynasties of the Delhi Sultanate in chronological order: 1. Slave 2. Khalji 3. Tughlaq 4. Sayyid 5. Lodi
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Slave (1206-1290) → Khalji (1290-1320) → Tughlaq (1320-1414) → Sayyid (1414-1451) → Lodi (1451-1526). 320 years. Mnemonic: S-K-T-S-L.
Q12. Who was the last ruler of the Lodi Dynasty (and the Delhi Sultanate)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Ibrahim Lodi (1517-1526) — last Lodi Sultan and last Delhi Sultan. Defeated and killed by Babur at Panipat (1526). Arrogant ruler who alienated Afghan nobles.
Q13. The Iqta system of the Delhi Sultanate was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Iqta = land revenue assignments to military commanders (iqtadars). Transferable (not hereditary). Introduced by Iltutmish; perfected by Alauddin Khalji. Backbone of Sultanate military-revenue system.
Q14. Who introduced the 'token currency' (copper coins valued as silver) in the Delhi Sultanate?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Muhammad bin Tughlaq introduced token currency (copper/bronze valued as silver). Failed due to forgery — people forged copper coins. Sultan had to withdraw and repay in silver. One of his famous failed experiments.
Q15. Alauddin Khalji's market reforms included:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Alauddin introduced strict price controls on grain, cloth, sugar, oil, meat. Fixed prices; inspectors; rationing; granaries; punishment for hoarding. Ensured army supply + controlled inflation.
Q16. The Mongol invasions of India during the Delhi Sultanate were successfully resisted by which ruler?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Alauddin defeated the Mongols 6 times — including the Battle of Amroha (1305). Built forts along the northwest frontier. The only Delhi Sultan to decisively defeat the Mongols.
Q17. The Slave Dynasty of the Delhi Sultanate included which famous ruler known as 'Lakh Baksh' (giver of lakhs)?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Qutbuddin Aibak was 'Lakh Baksh' (giver of lakhs) — generous in distributing wealth. First Sultan (1206-1210). Former slave of Ghori. Started Qutub Minar. Died in polo accident.
Q18. Balban (1266-1287 CE) is known for which theory of kingship?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Balban introduced 'divine right' — Sultan = God's shadow (zillullah). Introduced sijda (prostration) and paibos (kissing feet). Suppressed the Chihalgani (Group of Forty).
Q19. Bihar's connection with the Delhi Sultanate was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bakhtiyar Khalji conquered Bihar (~1200 CE), destroyed Nalanda and Vikramashila universities (~1202 CE). Library burned for months. Established Khalji Dynasty of Bengal. Bihar under Muslim rule until 1947.
Q20. The Sayyid Dynasty (1414-1451) claimed descent from which religious figure?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sayyids claimed descent from Prophet Muhammad (through Fatima and Ali). Founded by Khizr Khan (1414). Weak dynasty — controlled only Delhi. Lasted 37 years.
Q21. Timur (Tamerlane) sacked Delhi in which year, causing massive destruction?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Timur sacked Delhi in 1398 CE — looted for 3 days, killed thousands, destroyed buildings. Weakened the Tughlaq Dynasty — led to the rise of the Sayyid Dynasty (1414). Timur took treasure and artisans to Samarkand.
Q22. Who founded the city of Agra in 1504?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sikandar Lodi founded Agra (1504) — shifted capital from Delhi. Also conquered Bihar. Patron of learning (poet 'Gulruk'). Son Ibrahim Lodi was last Sultan (defeated at Panipat 1526).
Q23. Which Delhi Sultan shifted the capital from Delhi to Daulatabad (Devagiri)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Muhammad bin Tughlaq shifted capital to Daulatabad (1327) — to have a more central capital. Ordered entire population of Delhi to move. Caused massive suffering. Reversed after a few years. Famous failed experiment.
Q24. The Chihalgani (Group of Forty) was a powerful group of which type of people in the Delhi Sultanate?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Chihalgani = 40 Turkish slave nobles, originally slaves of Iltutmish. Became kingmakers — installed/deposed Sultans. Finally suppressed by Balban (1266-1287) who saw them as a threat.
Q25. Alauddin Khalji conquered Chittorgarh in 1303 CE. This event is associated with which legend?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Alauddin conquered Chittorgarh (1303). Associated with the legend of Padmini (recorded in Jayasi's Padmavat, 1540). Rajput women committed jauhar (self-immolation) to avoid capture. First of three jauhars at Chittorgarh.
