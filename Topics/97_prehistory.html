C. The Iron Age in India

  • Period: ~1,000 BCE (some say 1,200 BCE)
  • Impact: iron ploughshares revolutionised agriculture; iron weapons changed warfare
  • Associated with: Painted Grey Ware (PGW) culture; later Vedic period; rise of Mahajanapadas (600 BCE)
  • Key sites: Hastinapur, Atranjikhera, Ahichchhatra (UP)
  • Second urbanisation: after the decline of the IVC (~1750 BCE), the Iron Age saw the re-emergence of cities in the Ganga valley

Mehrgarh (Balochistan, ~7000 BCE) — the earliest Neolithic settlement in the Indian subcontinent. Shows the transition from hunting-gathering to farming: wheat/barley cultivation, domestication of cattle/sheep/goats, mud-brick houses, and pottery. Mehrgarh is a precursor to the Indus Valley Civilisation.
🧠 Mnemonic — P-M-N-C-I:
Paleolithic (hunting) → Mesolithic (microliths) → Neolithic (farming) → Chalcolithic (copper) → Iron Age.
"PMNCI: the five stages from stone to iron."
🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Chirand: The Neolithic Site on the Ganga
  • Chirand (Saran district, Bihar): the most important prehistoric site in Bihar. Located on the northern bank of the Ganga, near the confluence of the Ganga and the Ghaghra.
  • Period: continuous occupation from the Neolithic to the Chalcolithic period (~2,000 BCE onwards)
  • Key findings: (1) Neolithic stone tools (axes, celts); (2) Chalcolithic copper tools; (3) pottery (red ware, black-and-red ware); (4) bone tools (harpoons, needles); (5) evidence of rice cultivation and animal domestication; (6) burial sites
  • Significance: Chirand shows that the Ganga valley in Bihar was inhabited by farming communities from the Neolithic period. It is the earliest evidence of settled life in Bihar.
  • Other prehistoric sites in Bihar: Senuar (Neolithic, Rohtas district), Chechar-Kutubpur (Chalcolithic, Patna district), Maner (Chalcolithic)
🔗 Cross-Topic Connections
  • Topic 98 (Indus Valley Civilisation): The IVC (~2,500 BCE) emerged after the Neolithic period. Mehrgarh (~7,000 BCE) was the Neolithic precursor to the IVC. The IVC used bronze (copper + tin) — the Bronze Age. The Chalcolithic Age overlapped with the late IVC and continued after its decline.
  • Topic 99 (Vedic Age): The Iron Age (~1,000 BCE) is associated with the later Vedic period. Iron tools enabled the expansion of agriculture into the Ganga valley — leading to the rise of the Mahajanapadas.
📰 Current Relevance
  • Bhimbetka: UNESCO World Heritage Site (2003) — one of the most important prehistoric art sites in the world. Attracts researchers and tourists from around the world.
  • Mehrgarh: now in Pakistan (Balochistan) — but culturally part of the Indian subcontinent's prehistoric heritage. It shows the earliest evidence of farming in South Asia.
  • Neolithic Revolution: the transition from hunting-gathering to farming is still studied as the most important revolution in human history — it led to settled life, cities, civilisations, and eventually modern society.

Q1. The Paleolithic Age in India is also known as the:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Paleolithic = Old Stone Age (Greek 'palaios' = old, 'lithos' = stone). ~2 million - 10,000 BCE. Hunter-gatherers; rough stone tools; cave paintings (Bhimbetka).
Q2. Which site in India is famous for prehistoric rock paintings spanning the Paleolithic to Mesolithic periods?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bhimbetka (MP) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site with 750+ rock shelters and paintings spanning Paleolithic to Mesolithic. Depicts hunting, dancing, animals, daily life.
Q3. The Mesolithic Age in India is also known as the:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mesolithic = Middle Stone Age (Greek 'mesos' = middle). ~10,000-6,000 BCE. Microliths (small stone tools); climate change; diversified hunting.
Q4. The Neolithic Age in India began approximately in which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Neolithic Age began ~6,000 BCE in India. Key features: agriculture, settled villages, pottery, animal domestication, polished stone tools. The 'Neolithic Revolution.'
Q5. Which site is considered the earliest Neolithic settlement in the Indian subcontinent?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mehrgarh (~7000 BCE) is the earliest Neolithic settlement in the subcontinent. Wheat/barley farming, cattle/sheep/goat domestication, mud-brick houses. Precursor to the IVC. Discovered by J.F. Jarrige (1974).
Q6. Which period is known as the Chalcolithic Age?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chalcolithic = Copper-Stone Age (Greek 'chalkos' = copper). ~2,500-700 BCE. Copper + stone tools used together. Black-and-red ware pottery. Rural farming communities.
Q7. Arrange the Stone Ages in chronological order: 1. Paleolithic 2. Mesolithic 3. Neolithic 4. Chalcolithic
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Paleolithic (~2M-10,000 BCE) → Mesolithic (~10,000-6,000 BCE) → Neolithic (~6,000-2,500 BCE) → Chalcolithic (~2,500-700 BCE). From hunting-gathering to farming to metal tools.
Q8. The Indus Valley Civilisation is generally placed in which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The IVC (Harappan) is dated 2,500-1,750 BCE (Bronze Age). First urban civilisation in India. Discovered 1921 (Harappa, Daya Ram Sahni) and 1922 (Mohenjodaro, R.D. Banerjee).
Q9. Which of the following is NOT a feature of the Paleolithic Age?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Agriculture and settled villages are Neolithic features, NOT Paleolithic. Paleolithic = hunting-gathering, rough stone tools, cave paintings (Bhimbetka), nomadic lifestyle.
Q10. Which of the following is a key Neolithic site in Bihar?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chirand (Saran district, Bihar, on the Ganga) is the most important prehistoric site in Bihar. Stone + copper tools, pottery, rice cultivation, bone tools, burial sites. Continuous occupation from Neolithic to Chalcolithic.
Q11. The term 'microlith' refers to:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Microliths are small stone tools (1-5 cm) characteristic of the Mesolithic Age. Types: arrowheads, blades, points, lunates. Made from flint, chert, agate. Often hafted onto wood/bone handles as composite tools.
Q12. Which age is known for the beginning of agriculture in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Neolithic Age (~6,000 BCE) is known for the beginning of agriculture — the 'Neolithic Revolution.' First crops: wheat and barley (Mehrgarh ~7000 BCE). Rice cultivation began later in the Ganga valley.
Q13. Which prehistoric site in Kashmir is known for pit dwellings?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Burzahom (Kashmir, ~3000 BCE) is a Neolithic site known for pit dwellings — houses built in pits (for insulation against cold). Also: polished stone tools, bone tools, barley cultivation, dog burials.
Q14. Which metal was first used by humans in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Copper was the first metal used in India (Chalcolithic Age, ~2,500 BCE). Copper is found in a relatively pure state and is easy to work. Bronze (copper+tin) was used by the IVC. Iron came ~1,000 BCE.
Q15. The Iron Age in India began approximately in which period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Iron Age began ~1,000 BCE in India. Iron revolutionised agriculture (ploughshares) and warfare (weapons). Associated with PGW culture, later Vedic period, rise of Mahajanapadas, second urbanisation.
Q16. Which of the following correctly matches the site with its state?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bhimbetka is in Madhya Pradesh. Correct matches: Bhimbetka-MP, Mehrgarh-Balochistan, Burzahom-Kashmir, Chirand-Bihar.
Q17. The people of the Paleolithic Age survived primarily by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Paleolithic people survived by hunting wild animals and gathering wild fruits, roots, honey. They were nomadic — moving in search of food. Agriculture and domestication came in the Neolithic Age.
Q18. Which period is associated with the 'Neolithic Revolution'?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Neolithic Revolution (term by Gordon Childe) = transition from hunting-gathering to farming (~6,000 BCE in India). Most important revolution in human history — led to settled life, population growth, surplus food, urbanisation.
Q19. Which of the following was a feature of the Chalcolithic Age but NOT of the Neolithic Age?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Copper tools alongside stone tools = Chalcolithic feature (not Neolithic). Agriculture, domestication, and polished stone tools were Neolithic features that continued into the Chalcolithic Age.
Q20. Which archaeological site in Bihar shows continuous occupation from the Neolithic to the Chalcolithic period?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chirand (Saran district, Bihar, on the Ganga) shows continuous occupation from Neolithic to Chalcolithic. Stone + copper tools, pottery, rice cultivation, bone tools, burial sites. Most important prehistoric site in Bihar.
Q21. The rock paintings at Bhimbetka depict:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Bhimbetka paintings depict hunting scenes, dancing, animals (elephants, deer, tigers, boars), and daily life of prehistoric people. 750+ rock shelters, spanning Paleolithic to Mesolithic. UNESCO World Heritage Site (2003).
Q22. Who discovered the Indus Valley site of Harappa in 1921?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Daya Ram Sahni discovered Harappa (1921). R.D. Banerjee discovered Mohenjodaro (1922). John Marshall (DG, ASI) announced the IVC in 1924. IVC dated 2,500-1,750 BCE.
Q23. Which of the following statements about prehistoric Bihar is correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Chirand (Saran district, Bihar, on the Ganga) is an important Neolithic site. Stone tools, copper tools, pottery, rice cultivation, bone tools. Most important prehistoric site in Bihar. Other sites: Senuar, Chechar-Kutubpur.
Q24. The transition from the Chalcolithic Age to the Iron Age in India is associated with which pottery culture?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Painted Grey Ware (PGW) culture (~1,200-600 BCE) marks the transition from Chalcolithic to Iron Age. Grey pottery with painted designs. Associated with later Vedic period. Key sites: Hastinapur, Atranjikhera, Ahichchhatra.
Q25. The Black and Red Ware pottery is a characteristic of which age in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Black and Red Ware (BRW) is characteristic of the Chalcolithic Age (~2,500-700 BCE). Black interior, red exterior (inverted firing). Found at Ahar (Rajasthan), Malwa (MP), Jorwe (Maharashtra), Chirand (Bihar).
