B. The Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) PYQ PATTERN

  • Members: Lord Pethick-Lawrence (Secretary of State), Sir Stafford Cripps, A.V. Alexander
  • Key proposals: (1) united India with a weak centre (only defence, foreign affairs, communications); (2) provincial groupings (Group A: Hindu-majority, Group B: NW Muslim-majority, Group C: Bengal+Assam); (3) constituent assembly; (4) interim government
  • INC reaction: initially accepted; later disagreed over interpretations (especially the grouping clause)
  • Muslim League reaction: accepted, then withdrew acceptance (July 1946) and declared "Direct Action Day" (August 16, 1946)
  • Significance: the Cabinet Mission was the last attempt to keep India united. Its failure led directly to partition.

C. The Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947)

  • Announced: June 3, 1947, by Lord Mountbatten
  • Key features: (1) partition of India into two dominions — India and Pakistan; (2) transfer of power by August 15, 1947 (advanced from June 1948); (3) princely states free to join India, Pakistan, or remain independent; (4) Bengal and Punjab to be partitioned; (5) a boundary commission (Radcliffe) to demarcate the border
  • Accepted by: both the INC (Nehru, Patel) and the Muslim League (Jinnah)
  • Indian Independence Act (July 18, 1947): implemented the Mountbatten Plan; created two dominions; came into effect August 15, 1947

🔶 Bihar Connection
Bihar — Rajendra Prasad & Sachchidananda Sinha
  • Dr. Rajendra Prasad: the first President of the Constituent Assembly (December 11, 1946) and the first President of independent India (January 26, 1950 - May 13, 1962). He was from Bihar (Siwan district). He was a key Congress leader from Bihar — participated in Champaran (1917), Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, and Quit India movements.
  • Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha: the first temporary President of the Constituent Assembly (December 9, 1946). He was from Bihar (Arrah). He was a Congress leader and educationist — founder of the Patna University (1917).
  • Sri Krishna Singh: became the first Chief Minister of Bihar (1946-1961) — the longest-serving CM of Bihar. He had participated in the Quit India Movement (1942).
  • Anugrah Narayan Sinha: became the first Deputy Chief Minister of Bihar (1946-1957). He was the key finance and development minister.
  • Bihar during partition: Bihar was NOT affected by the Radcliffe Line — it remained part of India. Unlike Punjab and Bengal, Bihar had no Muslim-majority districts that could be claimed by Pakistan. Bihar's main contribution to independence was through its leaders (Rajendra Prasad, Sachchidananda Sinha) and its participation in the freedom movements.

📰 Current Relevance
  • August 15: celebrated as Independence Day across India. The Prime Minister addresses the nation from the Red Fort.
  • January 26: celebrated as Republic Day (Constitution came into force, 1950). Chosen because the INC declared Purna Swaraj on January 26, 1930.
  • November 26: celebrated as Constitution Day (Constitution adopted, 1949).
  • Partition legacy: the India-Pakistan border (Radcliffe Line) remains a flashpoint — Kashmir dispute, Indo-Pak wars (1947, 1965, 1971, Kargil 1999). The partition's communal violence (10+ million displaced, 1+ million killed) remains a traumatic memory.
  • Integration of princely states: Sardar Patel's integration is considered one of the greatest administrative achievements. His statue (Statue of Unity, 182m, Gujarat) is the world's tallest statue.

Q1. The Cabinet Mission Plan was proposed in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Cabinet Mission (Pethick-Lawrence, Cripps, Alexander) came in March 1946. Proposed a united India with weak centre + provincial groupings. INC and ML initially accepted; ML withdrew July 1946.
Q2. Who was the last Viceroy of British India and the first Governor-General of independent India?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Mountbatten: last Viceroy (Mar-Aug 1947) + first Governor-General of India (1947-48). C. Rajagopalachari was the first Indian Governor-General (1948-50).
Q3. The Mountbatten Plan (June 3 Plan) was announced on which date?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — June 3, 1947. Proposed partition into India + Pakistan; transfer of power by August 15, 1947 (advanced from June 1948). Accepted by both INC and ML.
Q4. The Indian Independence Act 1947 was passed by the British Parliament on which date?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — July 18, 1947 (royal assent). Came into effect August 15, 1947. Based on the Mountbatten Plan. Created two dominions: India and Pakistan.
Q5. India became independent on which date?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — August 15, 1947. Pakistan became independent on August 14 (one day earlier). January 26, 1950 = Republic Day (Constitution came into force).
Q6. Who gave the famous 'Tryst with Destiny' speech at midnight on August 14-15, 1947?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Nehru gave the "Tryst with Destiny" speech at the Constituent Assembly at midnight August 14-15, 1947. He became the first PM of independent India.
Q7. Who was the first Governor-General of Pakistan?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Jinnah was the first Governor-General of Pakistan (Aug 14, 1947 - Sep 11, 1948). "Quaid-e-Azam." Liaquat Ali Khan was the first PM of Pakistan.
Q8. Who was the first Indian Governor-General of independent India?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — C. Rajagopalachari (Rajaji) was the first Indian Governor-General (1948-50). Mountbatten was the first Governor-General but was British. After Rajaji, the office was replaced by the President (Rajendra Prasad, 1950).
Q9. The Radcliffe Line that partitioned India and Pakistan was drawn by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sir Cyril Radcliffe, a British lawyer who had never been to India, drew the boundary in 5 weeks. Announced August 17, 1947 (2 days AFTER independence) — causing massive confusion and violence.
Q10. Who raised the cry 'Divide and Quit' during India's independence movement?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Jinnah raised "Divide and Quit" — a play on the "Quit India" slogan. He demanded partition (Pakistan). 71st BPSC Q89.
Q11. The Lahore Resolution (1940), also known as the Pakistan Resolution, was passed by which party?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Lahore Resolution (March 23, 1940) was passed by the Muslim League at its Lahore session, presided by Jinnah. Demanded separate Muslim states. March 23 = Pakistan Day.
Q12. The Cabinet Mission Plan (1946) proposed which key feature?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Cabinet Mission proposed a united India with a weak centre (only defence, foreign affairs, communications) and provincial groupings (A, B, C). The last attempt to keep India united — it failed.
Q13. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Cabinet Mission Plan 2. Mountbatten Plan 3. Indian Independence Act 4. Lahore Resolution
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lahore Resolution (Mar 1940) → Cabinet Mission (Mar 1946) → Mountbatten Plan (Jun 3, 1947) → Indian Independence Act (Jul 18, 1947).
Q14. Who was the first Home Minister of independent India, responsible for the integration of princely states?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sardar Patel (Deputy PM + Home Minister, 1947-50) integrated 562 princely states into the Indian Union — using diplomacy, persuasion, and military action. "Iron Man of India." Assisted by V.P. Menon.
Q15. Which princely state was integrated into India through a military action (police action) in 1948?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Hyderabad was integrated through "Operation Polo" (Sept 13-18, 1948) after the Nizam refused to accede and his Razakars unleashed violence. Junagadh = plebiscite. Kashmir = Instrument of Accession (Oct 1947).
Q16. The accession of Jammu and Kashmir to India took place in which year and under what instrument?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Kashmir acceded to India on October 26, 1947, through the Instrument of Accession signed by Maharaja Hari Singh (after tribal raiders from Pakistan invaded). Led to the first India-Pakistan war (1947-48).
Q17. The Constituent Assembly of India was formed under which plan?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Constituent Assembly was formed under the Cabinet Mission Plan (1946). Elections July 1946; first meeting December 9, 1946. Drafted the Indian Constitution (1946-1949).
Q18. Who was the first President of the Constituent Assembly?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha was the first (temporary) President (Dec 9, 1946). Dr. Rajendra Prasad was elected permanent President (Dec 11, 1946). "First President" can be ambiguous — Sinha presided first.
Q19. Who presided over the first session of the Constituent Assembly?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha (from Arrah, Bihar) presided over the first session (Dec 9, 1946) as temporary president. Rajendra Prasad was elected permanent President on Dec 11.
Q20. Who was the first President of independent India?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Dr. Rajendra Prasad was the first President of India (Jan 26, 1950 - May 13, 1962). Served two terms. From Bihar (Siwan district). Before that, President of the Constituent Assembly.
Q21. The Indian Constitution came into force on which date?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — January 26, 1950 (Republic Day). Adopted Nov 26, 1949 (Constitution Day). Jan 26 chosen because INC declared Purna Swaraj on Jan 26, 1930 (Lahore session).
Q22. Who was the first Prime Minister of independent India?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Nehru was the first PM (Aug 15, 1947 - May 27, 1964). Served 17 years until his death. "Tryst with Destiny" speech. Architect of modern India (Non-Aligned Movement, Five-Year Plans).
Q23. Bihar's connection with independence and partition was through:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Bihar's connection: Dr. Rajendra Prasad — first President of the Constituent Assembly (1946) and first President of India (1950). From Bihar (Siwan). Also Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha (Arrah, Bihar) — first temporary president of the Constituent Assembly.
Q24. Subhash Chandra Bose resigned from the presidency of the INC in 1939. Who succeeded him?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The BPSC answer was (D) "More than one of the above." Bose resigned Apr 1939; Rajendra Prasad succeeded. Bose then formed the Forward Bloc (1939). 69th/70th/71st BPSC Q73.
Q25. Who was the last Viceroy of India before Mountbatten?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Lord Wavell (Viceroy 1943-1947) was the last Viceroy before Mountbatten. He attempted the Wavell Plan (1945) and Simla Conference (June-July 1945) — both failed. Replaced by Mountbatten in March 1947.
