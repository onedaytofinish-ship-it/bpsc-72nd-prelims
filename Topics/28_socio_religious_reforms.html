A. Two Streams of Reform — "Within" vs "Revivalist" EXAM CORE

The 19th-century reform movements took two broad approaches. BPSC asks you to identify which stream a reformer belongs to.

StreamApproachKey Reformers
1. Reformist (Within tradition)Reform from within — reinterpret scriptures rationally; accept Western rationalism; oppose superstition but stay in own faithRam Mohan Roy (Brahmo Samaj), Vidyasagar, Ranade (Prarthana Samaj), Sir Syed (Aligarh)
2. Revivalist (Back to roots)Return to "pure" original scriptures; reject later accretions; anti-Western; assertiveDayananda (Arya Samaj, "Back to the Vedas"), Vivekananda (Ramakrishna Mission), Theosophists
🧠 Mnemonic — Two Streams:
Reformist = R-R-V-S-R (Ram Mohan, Ranade, Vidyasagar, Sir Syed, Reform from within).
Revivalist = D-V-T (Dayananda, Vivekananda, Theosophists — "Back to roots").
"Reformists reinterpret; Revivalists return."
Raja Ram Mohan Roy (1772–1833) — "Father of Indian Renaissance"; founded Brahmo Samaj (1828); campaigned against Sati; died in Bristol, England (1833).

🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & Socio-Religious Reforms
  • Champaran Satyagraha (1917) — Gandhi's social reform in Bihar: Gandhi's Champaran movement was not just political — it was a social reform movement among Bihar's indigo farmers (the 'tinkathia' system). He set up schools, taught hygiene, and worked against untouchability in the Champaran villages. This is the Bihar-specific social reform angle most likely to be tested.
  • Bihar Bengal partition (1905) — communal angle: Lord Curzon partitioned Bengal in 1905, separating Eastern Bengal (with Bihar) from Western Bengal. Bihar was affected indirectly — Bihar was later separated from Bengal Presidency only in 1912. The partition fuelled Swadeshi and reform currents that touched Bihar's intelligentsia.
  • Swami Sahajanand Saraswati (Bihar's reformer): A sanyasi-reformer, he founded the Bihar Provincial Kisan Sabha (1929) and led the Kisan movement in Bihar. He worked for peasant reform and against zamindari exploitation in Bihar — a social reformer in the agricultural sphere. The 71st BPSC PYQ asked to match Champaran → JP Narayan; Azad Dasta → Shyam Narayan Singh; Individual Satyagraha → Sahajanand Saraswati; Kisan Sabha Bihar → (Sahajanand Saraswati). Bihar's homegrown reformer.
  • Education reform in Bihar: The 1854 Wood's Despatch led to the establishment of universities — Calcutta, Bombay, Madras (1857). Patna University was established much later (1917, separate from Bengal). Bihar's modern education lagged behind Bengal's reform milieu.
  • Sati in Bihar: Sati was practised in parts of Bihar (notably among zamindari families) until the 1829 abolition. The Patna/Gaya regions had occasional documented cases. Ram Mohan Roy's campaign and Bentinck's regulation applied equally to Bihar.

📰 Current Relevance & Potential Traps
  • Founder ↔ organisation ↔ book triplet: This is the #1 trap. Dayanand → Satyarth Prakash (NOT Ram Mohan Roy). Phule → Satyashodhak Samaj (NOT Arya Samaj). Ram Mohan Roy → Brahmo Samaj (NOT Arya Samaj). 71st BPSC asked exactly this.
  • Brahmo Samaj date trap: Founded 1828 (NOT 1829 or 1830). Renamed 1829. Ram Mohan Roy died in 1833 (in Bristol, England — NOT in India).
  • Sati abolition year trap: 1829 (NOT 1828, NOT 1856 — that's widow remarriage). Lord William Bentinck (NOT Dalhousie). Regulation XVII.
  • Vivekananda's founding date trap: Ramakrishna Mission founded 1897 (NOT 1893 — 1893 was the Chicago address). He went to Chicago in 1893, founded the Mission four years later.
  • Theosophical Society founder trap: Blavatsky and Olcott (NOT Annie Besant). Annie Besant joined later in 1889. Original society founded in New York 1875; Indian HQ Adyar 1886.
  • Prarthana Samaj founder trap: Atmaram Pandurang (1867) — NOT Ranade. Ranade was its most famous leader, but not the founder. Its inspiration was the Brahmo Samaj.
  • "Back to the Vedas" trap: Dayananda's slogan (NOT Vivekananda). Vivekananda's was Vedanta / "practical Vedanta." Dayananda was revivalist; Vivekananda was also revivalist but in a different way (universal Vedanta, not rejecting later texts).

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Who founded the Brahmo Samaj in 1828?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Raja Ram Mohan Roy founded the Brahmo Sabha in 1828 (renamed Brahmo Samaj in 1829). He is called the 'Father of Indian Renaissance'. He campaigned against sati, child marriage, and idolatry, and supported women's education and widow remarriage.
Q2. Which reformer's book is 'Satyarth Prakash'?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Satyarth Prakash (1875) was written by Swami Dayananda Saraswati, founder of the Arya Samaj. The 71st BPSC asked to match Dayanand → Satyarth Prakash. The book advocates 'Back to the Vedas' and critiques idolatry and caste.
Q3. The practice of Sati was abolished in which year and by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Sati was abolished in 1829 by Lord William Bentinck (GG 1828–1835), largely due to Raja Ram Mohan Roy's campaign. Regulation XVII of 1829 declared sati illegal. Bentinck is known for social reforms including sati abolition and English education (Macaulay's Minute 1835).
Q4. Who founded the Arya Samaj in 1875?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Swami Dayananda Saraswati founded the Arya Samaj in Bombay in 1875. His motto was 'Back to the Vedas' (krinvanto vishwam aryam). He opposed idolatry, caste system, child marriage, and untouchability. He wrote Satyarth Prakash.
Q5. The Hindu Widows' Remarriage Act was passed in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Hindu Widows' Remarriage Act was passed in 1856, drafted by Lord Dalhousie, due to the campaign of Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar. It legalised widow remarriage and declared the widow's first husband's lineage extinct. Vidyasagar also campaigned for women's education.
Q6. Who is associated with the Satyashodhak Samaj?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Jyotirao Phule founded the Satyashodhak Samaj in 1873 in Pune. Its aim was to liberate the lower castes (Shudras and Ati-Shudras) from Brahminical domination. The 71st BPSC asked to match Phule → Satyashodhak Samaj. Phule wrote 'Gulamgiri' (1871) and his wife Savitribai Phule opened the first girls' school in Pune (1848).
Q7. Swami Vivekananda founded which organization in 1897?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Swami Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897 (Belur Math, near Calcutta), named after his guru Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa. He represented Hinduism at the Parliament of Religions in Chicago (1893) and is famous for beginning his address with 'Sisters and Brothers of America'.
Q8. The Aligarh movement was started by whom?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Sir Syed Ahmad Khan started the Aligarh Movement after the 1857 Revolt, to promote modern (especially Western/scientific) education among Muslims. He founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College in Aligarh (1875) which became Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) in 1920. He was loyalist and advocated 'loyalty to the Crown'.
Q9. The MAO College at Aligarh was founded in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental (MAO) College was founded by Sir Syed Ahmad Khan in 1875 at Aligarh. It was modeled on Oxford/Cambridge. In 1920 it became Aligarh Muslim University (AMU). The 71st BPSC asked about the Principal of MAO College (Theodore Beck - an Anglo-Indian).
Q10. Who founded the Theosophical Society (with Indian headquarters at Adyar)?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Theosophical Society was founded in New York (1875) by Madame H.P. Blavatsky and Colonel H.S. Olcott. Its Indian headquarters were established at Adyar (Madras) in 1886. Annie Besant joined in 1889 and led it after Olcott's death; she also became Congress President (1917) and founded the Central Hindu College (Banaras, 1898).
Q11. Which reformer is known as the 'Father of Indian Renaissance'?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Raja Ram Mohan Roy (1772–1833) is called the 'Father of Indian Renaissance'. He founded the Brahmo Samaj (1828), campaigned against sati, advocated women's rights, modern education, and rationalism. He died in Bristol, England (1833). He was given the title 'Raja' by Akbar II (Mughal emperor).
Q12. Who founded the Prarthana Samaj in 1867?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Prarthana Samaj was founded in 1867 in Bombay by Dr. Atmaram Pandurang, inspired by the Brahmo Samaj. R.G. Bhandarkar and Mahadev Govind Ranade later led it. It worked for inter-dining, women's education, widow remarriage, and against child marriage and caste. Its Devkunj in Bombay was a reform hub.
Q13. The Sharda Act (Child Marriage Restraint Act) was passed in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Sharda Act (Child Marriage Restraint Act) was passed in 1929, drafted by Harbilas Sarda. It fixed the marriage age at 14 for girls and 18 for boys (later raised). It was the first law to restrain child marriage across all communities in British India. Passed during the Viceroyalty of Lord Irwin.
Q14. Which of the following is NOT correctly matched? (71st BPSC PYQ pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ram Mohan Roy is NOT matched with Arya Samaj - he founded the Brahmo Samaj (1828). Arya Samaj (1875) was founded by Dayananda Saraswati. The other three (Dayanand-Satyarth Prakash, Phule-Satyashodhak Samaj, Vivekananda-Ramakrishna Mission) are correctly matched. This is a direct 71st BPSC PYQ style question.
Q15. The Singh Sabha movement was associated with the reform of which community?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Singh Sabha Movement was a Sikh reform movement begun in 1873 in Amritsar (and Lahore, 1879). It aimed to restore Sikhism to its pure form, counter proselytising by Arya Samaj and Christian missionaries, and promote education. It led to the founding of the Khalsa College, Amritsar (1892), and later the Shiromani Gurdwara Prabandhak Committee (SGPC, 1925).
Q16. Who is known for the slogan 'Back to the Vedas'?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Swami Dayananda Saraswati's slogan was 'Back to the Vedas' - he believed the Vedas were infallible and contained all truth. He rejected later Hindu scriptures (Puranas), idol worship, caste by birth (supported caste by qualities), and child marriage. His Arya Samaj later started the Shuddhi (reconversion) movement.
Q17. Who founded the Depressed Classes Mission in 1906?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — V.R. Shinde founded the Depressed Classes Mission in Bombay in 1906. He was a social reformer influenced by the Prarthana Samaj. He worked for education and uplift of the 'depressed classes' (lower castes). Ambedkar later took forward the caste-reform movement more forcefully.
Q18. The Deoband movement (founded 1866) was a reform movement among:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Deoband movement was a Muslim reform movement begun at the Darul Uloom seminary in Deoband (UP, 1866) by Maulana Qasim Nanautvi. It was a conservative revivalist movement (against Western education, favoured traditional Islamic learning) - the counter to Aligarh. The Deoband seminary became a major centre of Islamic theology in India.
Q19. Who founded the Servants of India Society in 1905?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Gopal Krishna Gokhale founded the Servants of India Society in 1905 (Poona). It was the first secular organization for the social uplift of Indians, training workers for education and welfare work. Gokhale was Gandhi's political guru and mentor.
Q20. Consider the following about Raja Ram Mohan Roy: 1. Founded Brahmo Samaj. 2. Campaigned against Sati. 3. Wrote Satyarth Prakash. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct. Statement 3 is WRONG - Satyarth Prakash was written by Dayananda Saraswati (NOT Ram Mohan Roy). Ram Mohan Roy wrote 'Tuhfat-ul-Muwahhidin' (Gift to Monotheists) and the 'Precepts of Jesus'. He also founded the Vedanta College (1825).
Q21. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Brahmo Samaj 2. Arya Samaj 3. Ramakrishna Mission 4. Satyashodhak Samaj
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Brahmo Samaj (1828) → Satyashodhak Samaj (1873) → Arya Samaj (1875) → Ramakrishna Mission (1897). Ram Mohan Roy's was the first; Phule's Satyashodhak Samaj (1873) preceded Dayananda's Arya Samaj (1875) by 2 years. The Ramakrishna Mission (1897) was the last in this series.
Q22. Who founded the SNDP Yogam in 1903?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Sri Narayana Guru (1854–1928) founded the SNDP Yogam in 1903 in Kerala. He was a social reformer who worked for the Ezhava community and against caste. His famous message: 'One caste, one religion, one God for mankind.' He consecrated a Shiva temple at Aruvippuram (1888) - a revolutionary act by a lower-caste guru.
Q23. Which reformer founded the 'Tattwabodhini Sabha' in 1839?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Debendranath Tagore (father of Rabindranath Tagore) founded the Tattwabodhini Sabha in 1839 to propagate the Brahmo faith. It merged with the Brahmo Samaj in 1842. It published the 'Tattwabodhini Patrika' (1843), a major journal of the Bengal Renaissance.
Q24. Swami Vivekananda represented Hinduism at which famous event in 1893?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Vivekananda represented Hinduism at the Parliament of Religions in Chicago in 1893. He began his address with "Sisters and Brothers of America" - which won him a standing ovation. This event made Hinduism a world religion. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, four years later.
Q25. Bihar's own socio-religious reformer, who led the Kisan Sabha and worked for Bihar's peasants, was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Swami Sahajanand Saraswati was a sanyasi-reformer who founded the Bihar Provincial Kisan Sabha (1929) and led the Kisan movement in Bihar against zamindari exploitation. The 71st BPSC PYQ linked Sahajanand Saraswati with Bihar's Individual Satyagraha (1940). He was Bihar's homegrown social reformer in the agricultural sphere.
