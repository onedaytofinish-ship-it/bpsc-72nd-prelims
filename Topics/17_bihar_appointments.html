F. Historical Statics — The 1937–47 Premiership (PYQ Fuel) 71st BPSC

  • Government of India Act 1935 introduced provincial autonomy; Bihar's head of government was titled Premier.
  • Mohammad Yunus (Independent Party) — first Premier of Bihar, 1 Apr – 19 Jul 1937; led a minority ministry after Congress (the largest party) initially declined office.
  • Shri Krishna Sinha ("Bihar Kesari" / "Sri Babu") — Premier 20 Jul 1937 – 31 Oct 1939 (first Congress ministry; resigned with all Congress ministries over India being dragged into WWII without consultation); again Premier in 1946 and first CM of Bihar (1946–61). The 71st BPSC asked: "Who became CM of Bihar in March 1939?" → Shri Krishna Singh.
  • Dr. Anugraha Narayan Sinha ("Bihar Vibhuti") — Deputy Premier & Finance Minister (1937–39); later Bihar's first Deputy CM (1946–57); Rajendra Prasad (Bihar's son) became India's first President — never CM.
  • CM records: first woman CM Rabri Devi (1997); shortest-serving CM Satish Prasad Singh (5 days, 1968); President's Rule imposed 8 times; Nitish Kumar = longest-serving (10 oaths, ~20 yrs, till 14 Apr 2026); Samrat Chaudhary = 24th CM & first BJP CM (15 Apr 2026).
🧠 Mnemonic — Premiership ladder "Y-S-A: Yunus Started, Anugrah Assisted"
Yunus (Apr–Jul 1937, minority) → Shri Krishna Sinha (Jul 1937–Oct 1939, first Congress ministry) with Anugraha Narayan Sinha as Deputy Premier. Post-independence chain: SK Sinha → ... → Lalu (1990) → Rabri (1997, first woman) → Nitish (2005, 10 oaths) → Samrat (2026, 24th & first BJP).

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Who became Chief Minister of Bihar in March 1939? (71st BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Shri Krishna Singh ("Bihar Kesari") headed Bihar's first Congress ministry as Premier (20 Jul 1937 – 31 Oct 1939) under the Government of India Act 1935. Anugraha Narain Singh was his Deputy Premier — the trap — and Rajendra Prasad became India's first President, never CM of Bihar.
Q2. The first Premier of Bihar under the Government of India Act, 1935 was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Mohammad Yunus (Independent Party) led a minority ministry from 1 April to 19 July 1937, after Congress — the largest party — initially declined to form the government. Shri Krishna Sinha became Premier only on 20 July 1937, making (B) the trap.
Q3. In Shri Krishna Sinha's 1937 Bihar ministry, the Deputy Premier and Finance Minister was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Dr. Anugraha Narayan Sinha ("Bihar Vibhuti") was Deputy Premier & Finance Minister (1937–39) and later Bihar's first Deputy Chief Minister (1946–57). Rajendra Prasad never held office in the Bihar ministry.
Q4. The first woman Chief Minister of Bihar was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Rabri Devi became Bihar's first woman CM in 1997 after Lalu Prasad Yadav resigned in the fodder-scam case; she served three terms (1997–2005). The other names are present-day Bihar ministers — a recency trap.
Q5. The shortest-serving Chief Minister of Bihar, with a tenure of just five days (1968), was:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Satish Prasad Singh held office for only five days in 1968 — Bihar's shortest CM tenure. Contrast with Nitish Kumar, the longest-serving (10 oaths, ~20 years). Bhola Paswan Shastri also had short stints but not the shortest.
Q6. Who became the Chief Minister of Bihar in April 2026? (mirrors the 71st BPSC "March 1939" pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Samrat Chaudhary was sworn in on 15 April 2026 as the 24th CM and Bihar's first BJP CM, after Nitish Kumar resigned on 14 April 2026. Vijay Kumar Sinha was the outgoing Deputy CM and Nitin Nabin had moved to national BJP organisational duty — both are traps.
Q7. Samrat Chaudhary was administered the oath of office as Chief Minister:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Governor Hasnain swore in the new CM at Lok Bhavan (the renamed Raj Bhavan) on 15 Apr 2026. Trap (A): Arif Khan administered Nitish Kumar's 10th oath (20 Nov 2025) but had departed by March 2026. Trap (C): CJ Sahoo swore in the Governor, not the CM.
Q8. After the April 2026 transition, the two Deputy Chief Ministers of Bihar are:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Both new Dy CMs are from JD(U): Vijay Kumar Chaudhary (Education, Transport, Water Resources) and Bijendra Prasad Yadav (Finance, Energy, Planning). Trap (B) reproduces the Nov 2025 pairing — and "Vijay Kumar Sinha vs Vijay Kumar Chaudhary" is the deliberate name-swap trap.
Q9. In the Samrat Chaudhary ministry formed in April 2026, the Home Department is held by:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The CM kept 29 departments, including Home, Agriculture, Health and Tourism. The trap is the outdated 2025 arrangement in which Samrat Chaudhary held Home as Deputy CM — time-stamp the question before answering.

Q16. Match List-I (Constitutional Article) with List-II (Provision):
