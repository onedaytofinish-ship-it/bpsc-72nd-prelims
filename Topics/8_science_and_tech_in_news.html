B. Human Spaceflight — Shukla, Gaganyaan, BAS HOT

  • Shubhanshu Shukla (Group Captain) flew on Axiom-4 (June 2025) to the International Space Station — the first Indian on the ISS and the second Indian in space after Rakesh Sharma (1984, Soviet Soyuz T-11). He served as mission pilot; the flight was a precursor to Gaganyaan.
  • Gaganyaan astronauts: four IAF officer-astronauts announced February 2024 — Group Captains Prashanth Nair, Ajit Krishnan, Angad Pratap and Shubhanshu Shukla.
  • Gaganyaan status (mid-2026): human-rating of the LVM3 (HLVM3) complete; TV-D1 (Oct 2023) validated the Crew Escape System; first uncrewed mission G-1 with humanoid Vyommitra slated for 2026, followed by crewed flights.
  • Vyommitra: the half-humanoid female robot flying the uncrewed Gaganyaan tests — monitors module parameters and simulates crew functions.
  • Roadmap approved: Bharatiya Antariksh Station (BAS) by 2035; Indian on the Moon by 2040; Chandrayaan-4 lunar sample-return (~2027); Venus Orbiter Mission (Shukrayaan) ~2028; NGLV "Soorya" — the reusable next-gen launch vehicle.
🧠 Mnemonic — Docking Club "R-U-C-I"
Space-docking nations in order: Russia (1967) → USA → China (2011) → India (SpaDeX, Jan 16, 2025). And the human-spaceflight ladder: Sharma '84 (Soviet ride) → Shukla '25 (ISS) → Gaganyaan '26 → BAS '35 → Moon '40.

  • Launch port: Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC-SHAR), Sriharikota (Andhra Pradesh) — all 100+ Indian launches; a second spaceport is coming up at Kulasekarapattinam, Tamil Nadu (for SSLV).

  • NavIC / IRNSS: India's regional navigation system — 7 satellites, covering India and ~1,500 km beyond; NVS series is the 2nd generation (NVS-01 in 2023, NVS-02 in Jan 2025). GAGAN is the GPS-aided aviation augmentation system (with AAI).

D. AI & Emerging Tech — IndiaAI, Summit, Quantum MAJOR

  • IndiaAI Mission: approved March 2024 with an outlay of ₹10,371.92 crore over 5 years — 7 pillars (IndiaAI Compute Capacity, Innovation Centre, Datasets Platform, Application Development Initiative, FutureSkills, Startup Financing, Safe & Trusted AI). Compute pillar: 10,000+ GPU tender to build indigenous AI infrastructure.
  • India AI Impact Summit, New Delhi (Feb 16-20, 2026): the first major global AI summit hosted in the Global South; produced the New Delhi Declaration with ~88 endorsers (cross-link Topic #4). Sequence: Bletchley Park 2023 → Seoul 2024 → Paris 2025 (AI Action Summit — 71st BPSC PYQ) → New Delhi 2026.
  • Indigenous models: Sarvam AI (selected under IndiaAI to build India's foundational LLMs) and BharatGen Param2 (17B parameters) — the sovereign AI-model push; AIRAWAT (C-DAC Pune) is India's flagship AI supercomputer.
  • National Quantum Mission: approved April 2023, outlay ₹6,003.65 crore (2023-31) — quantum computing (50-1,000 qubits target), communication, sensing and materials; 4 thematic hubs.
  • Semiconductors: India Semiconductor Mission (₹76,000 crore incentive) — fabs/OSATs approved in Gujarat & Assam; Budget 2026-27 announced Semiconductor Mission 2.0 (cross-link Topic #7).

E. Biotech, Health & Research Misc NEW

  • BioE3 Policy (approved August 2024): Biotechnology for Economy, Environment and Employment — high-performance biomanufacturing; thematic verticals include bio-based chemicals, smart proteins, precision biotherapeutics, climate-resilient agriculture, carbon capture and marine/space research.
  • Genome India Project: completed sequencing of 10,000 genomes from diverse Indian populations; the dataset (released 2025) enables precision medicine and disease-gene discovery.
  • ANRF (Anusandhan National Research Foundation): established by the ANRF Act, 2023 — outlay ₹50,000 crore (2023-28); subsumes the Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB); apex body to seed, grow and fund university research.
  • mRNA vaccine tech: GEMCOVAC-19 (Gennova Biopharmaceuticals) — India's first mRNA COVID-19 vaccine (approved 2022), thermostable at 2-8°C.
  • U-WIN portal (MoHFW): digitises the Universal Immunization Programme on the CoWIN model — QR-based e-vaccination certificates, ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account) creation for parents/children, self-registration. ✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 34 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. What is true of the GSLV-F15 mission by ISRO? (71st BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Both (A) and (C) are true: GSLV-F15 (Jan 29, 2025) carried the 2nd-generation NavIC satellite NVS-02 and was the 100th launch from Sriharikota. (B) is the trap — it was the 100th, not the 101st.
Q2. ISRO's SpaDeX mission successfully demonstrated space docking on:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — SpaDeX was launched on December 30, 2024 aboard PSLV-C60; the two satellites (SDX01 Chaser and SDX02 Target) docked on January 16, 2025.
Q3. With the SpaDeX docking, India became which-numbered nation to demonstrate docking in space?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — India is the fourth nation to achieve space docking, after Russia (USSR), the United States and China.
Q4. Consider the following statements about NISAR:
1. It is a joint mission of NASA and ISRO.
2. It carries L-band and S-band synthetic aperture radars.
3. It was launched aboard PSLV-C60.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Statements 1 and 2 are correct (NASA provided the L-band radar, ISRO the S-band). Statement 3 is wrong: NISAR flew on GSLV-F16 (Jul 30, 2025); PSLV-C60 carried SpaDeX.
Q5. XPoSat, launched on January 1, 2024, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — XPoSat (PSLV-C58) is India's first X-ray polarimeter satellite — only the second such mission in the world after NASA's IXPE (2021). The solar observatory is Aditya-L1.
Q6. Consider the following statements about Aditya-L1:
1. It was launched by PSLV-C57 on September 2, 2023.
2. It sits in a halo orbit around the Sun-Earth Lagrange point 1, about 1.5 million km from Earth.
3. It entered its halo orbit in January 2024.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — All three are correct. Aditya-L1 is India's first solar observatory; its main payload VELC studies the solar corona. L1 gives an uninterrupted view of the Sun.
Q7. National Space Day, celebrated on August 23 every year, commemorates:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Chandrayaan-3's Vikram lander touched down near the lunar south pole on August 23, 2023; the day was declared National Space Day (first observed in 2024). The landing site is named Shiv Shakti Point.
Q8. Group Captain Shubhanshu Shukla's Axiom-4 mission (June 2025) is significant because he became:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Shukla, mission pilot of the private Axiom-4 flight, is the first Indian on the ISS and the second Indian in space after Rakesh Sharma (1984). The mission is a precursor to Gaganyaan.
Q9. The first Indian citizen to travel to space was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Squadron Leader Rakesh Sharma flew to the Salyut-7 station under the Soviet Interkosmos programme (April 1984). Chawla and Williams are American astronauts of Indian origin — a classic BPSC trap.
Q10. 'Vyommitra', recently in the news, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Vyommitra (from 'Vyoma' = space, 'Mitra' = friend) is the female-looking half-humanoid that monitors crew-module parameters on the uncrewed Gaganyaan flights, beginning with G-1 slated for 2026.
Q11. India's crewed Gaganyaan mission will be launched aboard:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The human-rated LVM3 (HLVM3) is the Gaganyaan launcher; its human-rating is complete and the TV-D1 flight (Oct 2023) validated the Crew Escape System.
Q12. How many astronaut-designates for the Gaganyaan programme were announced in February 2024?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Four IAF officers: Group Captains Prashanth Balakrishnan Nair, Ajit Krishnan, Angad Pratap and Shubhanshu Shukla (announced at VSSC, Thiruvananthapuram).
Q13. As per ISRO's 2025-26 roadmap, the first uncrewed Gaganyaan mission (G-1):
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — G-1 is the first uncrewed test flight, planned for 2026 with Vyommitra; it heads a seven-launch sprint announced in Parliament (Dec 2025). Crewed flights follow after uncrewed validation.
Q14. India targets setting up the Bharatiya Antariksh Station (BAS) by:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — BAS by 2035; an Indian on the Moon by 2040. Both targets were announced in 2023 and the approved programme architecture (Chandrayaan-4, VOM, NGLV) feeds into them.
Q15. Match List-I (Mission/Programme) with List-II (Description):
a) Chandrayaan-4 — 1) Venus mission, ~2028
b) Venus Orbiter Mission — 2) Lunar sample-return, ~2027
c) NGLV "Soorya" — 3) Approved reusable next-gen launch vehicle
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Chandrayaan-4 = lunar sample return (~2027); Venus Orbiter Mission (Shukrayaan) ~2028; NGLV Soorya = the approved ~30t-class reusable rocket. All three were approved together in September 2024.
Q16. What happened with PSLV-C62 in January 2026?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — PSLV-C62 (Jan 2026) saw a rare upper-stage anomaly; ISRO Chairman V. Narayanan announced corrective actions and the workhorse returned to flight in May 2026. NISAR flew on GSLV-F16; the 100th launch was GSLV-F15.
Q17. India's first industry-built PSLV is being produced by:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The HAL–L&T consortium is building five PSLV-XL rockets overseen by NSIL; the first industry-built PSLV flew by early 2026 — a milestone in space-sector privatisation.
Q18. Match List-I (Launch Vehicle) with List-II (Payload Class):
a) SSLV — 1) ~4,000 kg to GTO
b) PSLV — 2) ~500 kg to LEO
c) GSLV Mk-II — 3) ~1,750 kg to SSO
d) LVM3 — 4) ~2,250 kg to GTO
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The ladder: SSLV ~500 kg LEO; PSLV ~1,750 kg SSO; GSLV Mk-II ~2,250 kg GTO; LVM3 ~4,000 kg GTO (8-10 t LEO). Mnemonic: S-P-G-L, "500 to 8000".
Q19. The cryogenic upper stage (C25) of LVM3 is powered by which engine?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The CE-20 powers LVM3's C25 cryogenic stage; the smaller CE-7.5 powers GSLV Mk-II's CUS. Vikas is a liquid (non-cryogenic) engine used in PSLV/GSLV lower stages.
Q20. NavIC (IRNSS) is best described as:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — NavIC ("Navigation with Indian Constellation") has 7 satellites; the NVS series (NVS-01 in 2023, NVS-02 in Jan 2025 on the 100th-launch GSLV-F15) is its second generation. GPS (24 satellites) is the global American system.
Q21. Who is the Chairman of ISRO (as of mid-2026)?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — V. Narayanan took charge as ISRO Chairman and Secretary, Department of Space, on January 13, 2025, succeeding S. Somanath.
Q22. The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was established in:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — ISRO was formed on August 15, 1969 under Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, superseding INCOSPAR (1962). The Department of Space and the Space Commission were created in 1972.
Q23. Aryabhata (1975) holds which distinction?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Aryabhata, India's first satellite, was launched on April 19, 1975 by a Soviet Kosmos-3M rocket from Kapustin Yar. India's own first orbital launch came with SLV-3 in 1980.
Q24. The SLV-3 flight of July 1980 made India:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — SLV-3 (July 18, 1980) placed the Rohini RS-1 satellite in orbit, making India the sixth spacefaring nation. The project was led by Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.
Q25. Thumba (Kerala) is significant in India's space history because:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The first sounding rocket (an American Nike-Apache) lifted off from Thumba on November 21, 1963; the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station sits near the magnetic equator. The main launch port is SDSC-SHAR, Sriharikota; ISRO HQ is Bengaluru.
Q26. The IndiaAI Mission, approved in March 2024, has a total outlay of:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — ₹10,371.92 crore over five years, across 7 pillars — including compute capacity (10,000+ GPUs), an innovation centre for indigenous models, datasets, applications, skills, startup financing and safe-AI frameworks.
Q27. The India AI Impact Summit of February 16-20, 2026 was notable because it was:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — New Delhi hosted the summit (Bharat Mandapam), the first of the global AI summit series in the Global South, adopting the New Delhi Declaration with around 88 endorsers. Paris hosted the 2025 edition.
Q28. In which city was the AI Action Summit held in February 2025? (71st BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Paris hosted the AI Action Summit (Feb 2025), co-chaired by PM Modi. Sequence to remember: Bletchley Park 2023 → Seoul 2024 → Paris 2025 → New Delhi 2026.
Q29. Consider the following statements about India's indigenous AI push:
1. Sarvam AI was selected under the IndiaAI Mission to build foundational AI models.
2. BharatGen's Param2 is a 17-billion-parameter multimodal model.
3. AIRAWAT is an AI supercomputer installed at C-DAC, Pune.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct. Together they represent India's sovereign AI stack: models (Sarvam, BharatGen) + compute (AIRAWAT, IndiaAI GPU tender).
Q30. The National Quantum Mission entails an outlay of:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The NQM (2023-31) targets 50-1,000 qubit quantum computers, satellite-based quantum communication and quantum sensing, through four thematic hubs. (₹50,000 crore is the ANRF outlay — keep the two apart.)
Q31. The BioE3 Policy, approved in August 2024, is centred on:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — BioE3 = Biotechnology for Economy, Environment and Employment. Approved by the Union Cabinet in August 2024, it pushes biomanufacturing, bio-foundries and thematic verticals from smart proteins to precision biotherapeutics.
Q32. The Genome India Project is associated with:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Genome India Project (DBT-led, 20+ institutions) completed sequencing 10,000 genomes across India's diverse population groups; the dataset, released in 2025, anchors precision-medicine research.
Q33. Consider the following statements about the U-WIN portal of the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (71st BPSC pattern):
1. It digitises the Universal Immunization Programme on the CoWIN model.
2. It provides QR-based e-vaccination certificates.
3. It facilitates creation of Ayushman Bharat Health Accounts (ABHA) for parents and children.
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct features of U-WIN, which replicates CoWIN for routine immunisation (pregnant women and children), with self-registration, QR e-certificates and ABHA integration.
Q34. Consider the following statements about the Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF):

2. It has an outlay of ₹50,000 crore for 2023-28.
3. It subsumes the Science and Engineering Research Board (SERB).
Which of the statements given above are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — All three are correct. The ANRF Act, 2023 created India's apex research-funding body (₹50,000 crore, 2023-28), subsuming SERB, to seed and fund research across universities and colleges.
