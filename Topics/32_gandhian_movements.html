These three early satyagrahas (1917-1918) were Gandhi's "apprenticeship" in Indian politics — testing his South African methods in the Indian context. Champaran was the first (1917) and the most important for Bihar.

Mahatma Gandhi (1869–1948) — "Father of the Nation"; returned to India from South Africa in 1915; first satyagraha in India at Champaran (Bihar) in 1917; led the Non-Cooperation (1920), Civil Disobedience (1930), and Quit India (1942) movements.

🏛️ Bihar & the Gandhian Movements — The 71st PYQ Matching
  • Champaran Satyagraha (1917) → Dr. Rajendra Prasad (71st PYQ Q100): Gandhi's first satyagraha in India was at Champaran (Bihar) in 1917. The 71st BPSC PYQ matched Champaran with Dr. Rajendra Prasad — Bihar's most important Congressman and the first President of India (1950–1962). Rajendra Prasad was Gandhi's key Bihar associate at Champaran. Other Bihar associates: Anugrah Narayan Sinha, Braj Kishore Prasad, and Raj Kumar Shukla (the farmer who invited Gandhi).
  • Champaran Agrarian Inquiry Committee (71st PYQ Q82): the committee was appointed "in agreement between the Indian Government and Gandhiji." It had 4 members (including Gandhi). Its report led to the Champaran Agrarian Act (1918), abolishing the tinkathia system (forced indigo cultivation on 3/20 of land).
  • Azad Dasta → Shyam Narayan Singh (71st PYQ Q100): the Azad Dasta was an underground revolutionary group in Bihar during the Quit India Movement (1942). Shyam Narayan Singh led the Azad Dasta in the Hazaribagh region of Bihar. After the Congress leadership was arrested (August 9, 1942), underground groups like the Azad Dasta carried on the movement.
  • Individual Satyagraha in Bihar 1940 → Sahajanand Saraswati (71st PYQ Q100): Sahajanand Saraswati, Bihar's sanyasi-reformer who founded the Bihar Provincial Kisan Sabha (1929), led the Individual Satyagraha in Bihar in 1940 (during WWII). The Individual Satyagraha was Gandhi's response to the August Offer (1940) — carefully selected individuals offered satyagraha one at a time.
  • Kisan Sabha Bihar → Jayaprakash Narayan (71st PYQ Q100): Jayaprakash Narayan ("JP"), Bihar's most famous socialist leader, was associated with the Kisan Sabha (peasant movement) in Bihar. JP later led the Total Revolution (1974–75) against the Emergency. He was Bihar's homegrown revolutionary and political leader.
  • Champaran = Bihar's first mass nationalist movement: the Swadeshi Movement (1905) bypassed Bihar; Champaran (1917) was Bihar's first true mass nationalist movement — 12 years after the Swadeshi Movement. Gandhi's Champaran success established the Bihar Congress as a major force.

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Gandhi's first Satyagraha in India was at which place in 1917?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Gandhi's first Satyagraha in India was at Champaran (Bihar) in 1917, on behalf of indigo farmers protesting the 'tinkathia' system (forced indigo cultivation on 3/20 of land). Raj Kumar Shukla, a local farmer, invited Gandhi to Champaran. Gandhi was assisted by Rajendra Prasad, Anugrah Narayan Sinha, and Braj Kishore Prasad. The Champaran Agrarian Inquiry Committee was appointed by the Indian Government in agreement with Gandhi.
Q2. The Champaran Satyagraha (1917) was associated with which Bihar leader, as per the 71st BPSC PYQ matching?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q100) asked to match: Champaran Satyagraha → Dr. Rajendra Prasad (option a-4). Rajendra Prasad was Gandhi's key Bihar associate at Champaran. He later became the first President of India (1950-1962). The other 71st PYQ matches: Azad Dasta → Shyam Narayan Singh; Individual Satyagraha in Bihar 1940 → Sahajanand Saraswati; Kisan Sabha Bihar → Jayaprakash Narayan.
Q3. The Champaran Agrarian Inquiry Committee was appointed in agreement between which parties? (71st BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Champaran Agrarian Inquiry Committee was appointed in agreement between the Indian Government and Gandhiji (71st BPSC PYQ Q82). The committee was formed in 1917 after Gandhi's satyagraha. It had 4 members (including Gandhi) and investigated the indigo planters' exploitation. The committee's report led to the Champaran Agrarian Act (1918), which abolished the tinkathia system.
Q4. The Non-Cooperation Movement was launched by Gandhi in which year and against what?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Non-Cooperation Movement was launched in 1920 (September, by Gandhi at the Calcutta INC session). It was a response to the Jallianwala Bagh massacre (April 13, 1919), the Rowlatt Act (1919), and the Khilafat issue (the Ottoman Caliphate's abolition). The movement called for boycott of British schools, courts, councils, and titles. It was withdrawn in February 1922 after the Chauri Chaura incident.
Q5. The Non-Cooperation Movement was withdrawn by Gandhi after which incident?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Non-Cooperation Movement was withdrawn on February 12, 1922, after the Chauri Chaura incident (February 4, 1922) — where a mob set fire to a police station at Chauri Chaura (Gorakhpur district, UP), killing 22 policemen. Gandhi was shocked by the violence and called off the movement. He believed Indians were not yet ready for non-violent resistance. Gandhi was arrested in March 1922 and sentenced to 6 years imprisonment (released 1924).
Q6. The Civil Disobedience Movement began with the Dandi Salt March in which year?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Civil Disobedience Movement began on March 12, 1930, when Gandhi started the Dandi Salt March from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi (Gujarat coast), a distance of 240 miles. He reached Dandi on April 6, 1930, and broke the salt law by making salt from seawater. The Salt March was a symbolic act of civil disobedience against the British salt tax. The movement spread across India and lasted until 1934.
Q7. The Dandi Salt March covered a distance of approximately how many miles?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Dandi Salt March covered approximately 240 miles (384 km) from Sabarmati Ashram (Ahmedabad) to Dandi (Gujarat coast). Gandhi started on March 12, 1930, with 78 followers (from the Sabarmati Ashram) and reached Dandi on April 6, 1930 (24 days). Along the way, he held meetings at villages and the march grew. At Dandi, he broke the salt law by making salt from seawater — a symbolic act of civil disobedience against the British salt tax.
Q8. The Quit India Movement was launched in which year and with what slogan?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Quit India Movement was launched on August 8, 1942, at the Bombay session of the INC (Gowalia Tank Maidan, renamed August Kranti Maidan). Gandhi's slogan was 'Do or Die' (Karenge ya Marenge) — 'We shall either free India or die in the attempt.' The movement demanded immediate British withdrawal from India. The British arrested the entire Congress leadership the next morning (August 9, 1942), but the movement spread spontaneously across India.
Q9. Who said 'We shall either free India or die in the attempt'? (71st BPSC PYQ)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Mahatma Gandhi said 'We shall either free India or die in the attempt' at the launch of the Quit India Movement (August 8, 1942, Bombay). The slogan was 'Do or Die' (Karenge ya Marenge). This was the 71st BPSC PYQ. Gandhi's Quit India speech at Gowalia Tank Maidan (renamed August Kranti Maidan) was his final, most radical call for independence.
Q10. The 'Azad Dasta' was associated with which Bihar leader, as per the 71st BPSC PYQ matching?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q100) matched 'Azad Dasta' with Shyam Narayan Singh (option b-2). The Azad Dasta was an underground revolutionary group in Bihar during the Quit India Movement (1942). Shyam Narayan Singh led the Azad Dasta in the Hazaribagh region of Bihar. The group operated underground after the Congress leadership was arrested.
Q11. The 'Individual Satyagraha in Bihar 1940' was associated with which leader, as per the 71st BPSC PYQ?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q100) matched 'Individual Satyagraha in Bihar 1940' with Sahajanand Saraswati (option c-3). Sahajanand Saraswati was a sanyasi-reformer who founded the Bihar Provincial Kisan Sabha (1929). He led the Individual Satyagraha in Bihar in 1940 (during the WWII phase when the INC offered individual satyagrahis to the British). He was Bihar's homegrown peasant leader and reformer.
Q12. The 'Kisan Sabha Bihar' was associated with which leader, as per the 71st BPSC PYQ matching?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q100) matched 'Kisan Sabha Bihar' with Jayaprakash Narayan (option d-1). Jayaprakash Narayan ('JP'), Bihar's most famous socialist leader, was involved in the Kisan Sabha and peasant movements in Bihar. He later led the Total Revolution (1974-75) against the Emergency. JP was Bihar's homegrown revolutionary and political leader.
Q13. The Rowlatt Act was passed in which year and what did it provide?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Rowlatt Act (Anarchical and Revolutionary Crimes Act, 1919) allowed the British to detain suspects without trial for up to 2 years. It was based on the recommendations of the Sedition Committee (Rowlatt Committee, 1918). Gandhi launched the Rowlatt Satyagraha (March 1919) against this Act — his first all-India satyagraha. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre (April 13, 1919) occurred during the anti-Rowlatt protests in Punjab.
Q14. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre took place on which date and at which place?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Jallianwala Bagh massacre took place on April 13, 1919 (Baisakhi day), at Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar (Punjab). General Reginald Dyer ordered his troops to fire on a peaceful crowd of unarmed men, women, and children assembled for a meeting against the Rowlatt Act. Officially 379 were killed (unofficial estimates are much higher, up to 1,000). The massacre shocked India and was a major catalyst for the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920).
Q15. The Kheda Satyagraha (1918) was Gandhi's second Satyagraha in India. Where was it held?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Kheda Satyagraha (1918) was held in the Kheda district of Gujarat. It was Gandhi's second Satyagraha in India (after Champaran 1917). The peasants of Kheda were hit by famine and crop failure; they demanded relief from land revenue. Gandhi led the satyagraha with Vallabhbhai Patel and Indulal Yagnik. The British eventually suspended revenue collection. Kheda was a training ground for Gandhi's methods.
Q16. The Purna Swaraj (complete independence) was demanded at which INC session?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Purna Swaraj (complete independence) was demanded at the 1929 Lahore session of the INC, presided over by Jawaharlal Nehru. The INC declared January 26, 1930 as 'Independence Day' — celebrated across India. This was a shift from dominion status (the earlier goal) to complete independence. The Civil Disobedience Movement (1930) followed. January 26 was later chosen as India's Republic Day (1950) in honour of the 1930 Purna Swaraj declaration.
Q17. Consider the following about the 71st BPSC PYQ matching: 1. Champaran → Rajendra Prasad. 2. Azad Dasta → Shyam Narayan Singh. 3. Individual Satyagraha Bihar 1940 → Sahajanand Saraswati. 4. Kisan Sabha Bihar → Jayaprakash Narayan. Which is/are correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — All four are correct (71st BPSC PYQ Q100): Champaran → Rajendra Prasad (a-4), Azad Dasta → Shyam Narayan Singh (b-2), Individual Satyagraha Bihar 1940 → Sahajanand Saraswati (c-3), Kisan Sabha Bihar → Jayaprakash Narayan (d-1). The correct code was 4, 2, 3, 1 — all four matches are correct. This is the highest-yield Bihar-specific PYQ pattern for the Gandhian era.
Q18. Arrange in chronological order: 1. Champaran 2. Non-Cooperation 3. Dandi March 4. Quit India
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Champaran (1917) → Non-Cooperation (1920) → Dandi March (1930) → Quit India (1942). Champaran was Gandhi's first satyagraha in India; Non-Cooperation was his first all-India mass movement; the Dandi March launched the Civil Disobedience Movement; Quit India was his final mass movement before independence.
Q19. Who was the local farmer who invited Gandhi to Champaran in 1917?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Raj Kumar Shukla was the local Champaran farmer who invited Gandhi to Champaran in 1917. He met Gandhi at the Lucknow INC session (1916) and persuaded him to visit Champaran to see the indigo farmers' plight. Gandhi mentioned Shukla's persistence in his autobiography 'The Story of My Experiments with Truth'. The 71st BPSC PYQ (Q82) noted that the Champaran Agrarian Inquiry Committee was between the Indian Government and Gandhiji.
Q20. The Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931) was signed between Gandhi and Lord Irwin (Viceroy). What did it lead to?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — The Gandhi-Irwin Pact (March 5, 1931) was signed between Gandhi and Lord Irwin (Viceroy). Gandhi agreed to suspend the Civil Disobedience Movement and participate in the Second Round Table Conference (London, September 1931). In return, the British agreed to release political prisoners and allow salt collection by coastal villagers. The Second Round Table Conference (1931) was inconclusive — Gandhi returned disappointed. The Civil Disobedience Movement was resumed in January 1932.
Q21. Which of the following was NOT a phase of Gandhi's Satyagraha in India?
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — The Swadeshi Movement (1905) was NOT a phase of Gandhi's Satyagraha — it was launched before Gandhi's arrival in India (Gandhi was in South Africa until 1915). The Swadeshi Movement was led by Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Bipin Pal, and Aurobindo. Gandhi's Satyagrahas in India began with Champaran (1917), followed by Kheda (1918) and Ahmedabad Mill Strike (1918). The Dandi Salt March (1930) was a later phase.
Q22. The Khilafat Movement (1919-1924) was merged with which movement by Gandhi?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Khilafat Movement (1919-1924) was merged with the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920) by Gandhi. The Khilafat issue was the Ottoman Caliphate's abolition by the British after WWI. Indian Muslims (led by the Ali brothers — Muhammad Ali and Shaukat Ali) demanded the restoration of the Caliph. Gandhi merged the Khilafat with Non-Cooperation to achieve Hindu-Muslim unity. The Khilafat Movement ended when Turkey abolished the Caliphate itself (1924).
Q23. The Poona Pact (1932) was signed between Gandhi and whom, regarding reserved seats for the Depressed Classes?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The Poona Pact (September 24, 1932) was signed between Gandhi (on hunger strike in Yerwada Jail) and B.R. Ambedkar. The British had announced the 'Communal Award' (August 1932) with separate electorates for the Depressed Classes. Gandhi opposed this (he believed it would divide Hindu society) and went on a fast unto death. Ambedkar relented and signed the Poona Pact — replacing separate electorates with reserved seats for the Depressed Classes within the Hindu electorate.
Q24. The Ahmedabad Mill Strike (1918) was Gandhi's third Satyagraha. What was the issue?
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — The Ahmedabad Mill Strike (1918) was Gandhi's third Satyagraha in India. Textile mill workers in Ahmedabad demanded a 35% wage increase (the plague had caused inflation). The mill owners refused. Gandhi went on a hunger strike to pressure the mill owners. The strike ended with a compromise — a 35% wage increase. This was Gandhi's first fast (hunger strike) as a tool of satyagraha.
Q25. The August Offer (1940) was made by which Viceroy during WWII, promising dominion status after the war?
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — The August Offer was made by Lord Linlithgow (Viceroy 1936-1943) in August 1940, during WWII. It promised dominion status for India after the war and the expansion of the Viceroy's Executive Council. The INC rejected the offer (it demanded immediate self-government). Gandhi launched the Individual Satyagraha (1940-41) in response — Vinoba Bhave was the first Individual Satyagrahi; Nehru was the second.
