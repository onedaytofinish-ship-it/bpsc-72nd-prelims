🔶 Bihar Connection
🏛️ Bihar & Books/Obituaries — The Safe BPSC Angle
  • George Orwell was born in Bihar: Eric Arthur Blair — author of 1984 and Animal Farm — was born in Motihari (East Champaran) on 25 June 1903; his birthplace is a protected heritage site. BPSC's favourite "world author ↔ Bihar" one-liner.
  • Bihar-linked obituary edge cases (just outside window): Sharda Sinha — "Bihar Kokila", the voice of Chhath geet, Padma Bhushan 2018 — died 5 Nov 2024 (age 72); Sushil Kumar Modi — former Bihar Deputy CM — died 13 May 2024 (age 72).
  • 70th BPSC PYQ — banned book: Gharib Hindustan, critical of British policies, was banned in Bihar in 1930; author Mohd. Wali Hassan. Bihar's proscribed-literature history is directly examinable.
  • Satya Pal Malik (d. 1 Aug 2025): the former Governor's roster included Bihar (2017-18) alongside J&K, Goa and Meghalaya — a 2025-26 obituary with a Bihar hook.
  • Literary giants of Bihar: Rashtrakavi Ramdhari Singh Dinkar (Simaria, Begusarai — Rashmirathi, Kurukshetra, Urvashi); Phanishwar Nath Renu (Araria — Maila Anchal); Maithili poet Vidyapati ("Maithil Kokil", Madhubani); Nagarjun ("Baba Nagarjun", Mithila).
  • Khuda Bakhsh Oriental Public Library, Patna: founded 1891; declared an Institution of National Importance by an Act of Parliament (1969) — famed for Persian and Arabic manuscripts; the standard "Bihar library" static.
  • Writers-President: Dr. Rajendra Prasad (Ziradei, Siwan) wrote Atmakatha and India Divided; Jayaprakash Narayan's Prison Diary came out of the Emergency years.

  • Two aviation deaths — do not swap: Vijay Rupani (ex-Gujarat CM) died in the Air India AI171 crash, Ahmedabad, 12 Jun 2025; Ajit Pawar (Maharashtra DyCM) died in a Learjet 45 crash at Baramati airport, 28 Jan 2026. Crash ↔ person ↔ date is the testable triple.
  • Succession tracking: Pope Francis (d. 21 Apr 2025, 88; first Latin American & first Jesuit pope) → Leo XIV (Robert Prevost, first American pope); Khamenei (killed 28 Feb 2026) → son Mojtaba Khamenei. BPSC asks "who succeeded" as often as "who died".

  • Books with event hooks: The Emergency Diaries (released by Amit Shah on 25 Jun 2025, Samvidhan Hatya Diwas; compiled by BlueKraft, foreword by Deve Gowda — he is NOT the author/subject); Hope: The Autobiography (first by a reigning pope, Jan 2025); 107 Days (Kamala Harris, Sep 2025); The One (Shikhar Dhawan) pairs with Ashwin's I Have the Streets (70th BPSC PYQ).

✍️ MCQ Practice Set — 25 Questions (BPSC Level)
Q1. Who is the author of the autobiography "I Have the Streets: A Kutti Cricket Story"? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — A 70th BPSC verbatim question: off-spinner R. Ashwin co-wrote it with cricket journalist Sidharth Monga. Trap (A): Suresh Raina's memoir is Believe: What Life and Cricket Taught Me.
Q2. Who is the author of the famous book 'Gulamgiri'? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Jyotiba Phule wrote Gulamgiri (1873), dedicating it to American abolitionists. Trap (A): Ambedkar's signature work is Annihilation of Caste; (D) fails because the book has a single author.
Q3. In 1901, who published the book 'Poverty and Un-British Rule in India'? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Dadabhai Naoroji's 1901 classic laid out the drain-of-wealth thesis. Trap (A): R. C. Dutt wrote The Economic History of India — same theme, different book.
Q4. 'Nitisara', an early book of politics, was written by: (69th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Kamandaka authored the Nitisara. Trap (A): Kautilya wrote the Arthashastra — the default distractor for every ancient political-text question.
Q5. Which of the following statements regarding Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom is correct? (69th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Elizabeth II died on 8 September 2022 at Balmoral, aged 96, after 70 years on the throne — the longest-reigning British monarch, succeeded by Charles III. BPSC shuffles the reign-length/age pair; memorise 70 & 96.
Q6. 'Tarikh-i-Firuz Shahi' was written by: (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Tarikh-i-Firuz Shahi was written by Ziauddin Barani, the only qualifying choice among the listed options. Shams Siraj Afif also wrote a like-named work, but Afif is not among the options here (in the original 70th BPSC paper Afif was an option and Barani was absent — this option set has been edited), so "More than one of the above" is logically impossible. Minhaj Siraj = Tabaqat-i Nasiri; Yahya Sarhindi = Tarikh-i-Mubarak Shahi.
Q7. 'Futuhat-e-Alamgiri' was written by: (69th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Ishwar Das Nagar chronicled Aurangzeb's reign in Futuhat-e-Alamgiri. The traps are the Mughal shelf: Abul Fazl = Akbarnama, Lahori = Badshahnama, Khafi Khan = Muntakhab-ul Lubab.
Q8. Who was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2023? (70th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Jailed Iranian activist Narges Mohammadi won the 2023 Peace Prize. The others are year-swaps: Ressa (2021), Murad (2018), Abiy Ahmed (2019).
Q9. Yevgeny Prigozhin, whose death in an August 2023 plane crash was in news, was the chief of: (69th BPSC pattern)
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Prigozhin headed the Wagner mercenary group and died two months after his mutiny against Russia's defence establishment. Global obituaries ("who died + who were they") are a standing BPSC format — expect one from the 2025–26 window.
Q10. The first Jnanpith Award (1965) was given to:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Malayalam poet G. Sankara Kurup won the first Jnanpith (for Odakkuzhal); the award was instituted in 1961. Trap (C): Sumitranandan Pant was the first Hindi winner (1968), not the first overall.
Q11. The International Booker Prize 2025 was won by:
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — Heart Lamp (20 May 2025, Tate Modern) is the first Kannada work and first short-story collection to win. Trap (B): Tomb of Sand won in 2022; trap (D): Flesh won the Booker Prize 2025, not the International Booker.
Q12. The Booker Prize 2025 was won by:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — David Szalay won for Flesh (announced 10 Nov 2025, London; judges chaired by Roddy Doyle) — the first Hungarian-origin winner. Trap (A): Kiran Desai was only shortlisted (The Loneliness of Sonia and Sunny); Percival Everett won the Pulitzer Fiction 2025 (James).
Q13. The International Booker Prize 2026 was won by:
✅ Correct Answer: (C) — Taiwan Travelogue (announced 19 May 2026, Tate Modern) is the first Mandarin-language winner; the £50,000 is split between author and translator. (A) and (D) are earlier winners (2025, 2022); Han Kang's book was a 2025 shortlisted title.
Q14. The 59th Jnanpith Award (for 2024), announced in March 2025, was given to:
✅ Correct Answer: (D) — Hindi writer Vinod Kumar Shukla (Rajnandgaon) — the first Jnanpith laureate from Chhattisgarh and 12th Hindi winner (Naukar Ki Kameez). Traps: (A) and (B) shared the 58th (2023); (C) Vairamuthu won the 60th (2025).
Q15. Vairamuthu's 60th Jnanpith Award (for 2025), announced in March 2026, is significant because he is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Vairamuthu is the 3rd Tamil Jnanpith laureate and the first for Tamil poetry; his novel Kallikkaattu Ithihasam had won the Sahitya Akademi in 2003. Trap (A): the first Tamil winner was Akilan (1975).
Q16. For the year 2025, the Sahitya Akademi Award in English went to:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — Diplomat-author Navtej Sarna won for the novel Crimson Spring (announced 16 Mar 2026; ceremony 31 Mar 2026). Traps: Kire is the 2024 English winner; Mamta Kalia is the 2025 Hindi winner — the language-swap; Gagan Gill is the 2024 Hindi winner.
Q17. Which statement about Dr. Manmohan Singh is correct?
✅ Correct Answer: (A) — India's first Sikh PM (two terms, 2004–14), earlier RBI Governor and the Finance Minister of the 1991 reforms, died at AIIMS Delhi on 26 Dec 2024. It sits just before the Jan-2025 window — exactly the borderline death BPSC still asks (trap (B) shifts the year).

Q20. The book "The Emergency Diaries — Years That Forged a Leader", in news in 2025, is:
✅ Correct Answer: (B) — It documents PM Modi's role as a young RSS pracharak in the anti-Emergency movement and was released on the 50th anniversary of the Emergency. Trap (D): Deve Gowda wrote the foreword — he is neither author nor subject.
Q21. Consider the following statements about Hindi writer Vinod Kumar Shukla:

Q22. Consider the following statements about the International Booker Prize 2025:
