👵 C. Pension & Welfare Delivery Statics
  • Six pension schemes run by the Social Welfare Dept; the headline three: Mukhyamantri Vriddhajan Pension (old-age), Laxmibai Social Security Pension (widow), Bihar Nishakta Pension (disability). All paid by DBT; rate ₹400 → ₹1,100/month from June 2025; 1,16,04,685 pensioners; Dec 2025 payout ₹1,289.04 cr.
  • Free-power mechanics: benefit applies to energy charges up to 125 units/month for every domestic consumer; non-telescopic billing (cross 125 → all units bill at ₹5.52/unit); executed under Mukhyamantri Vidyut Upbhokta Sahayata Yojana with ₹3,797 cr to BSPHCL (FY 2025-26); rooftop solar planned on homes and public places over 3 years. Static backdrop: 100% village electrification (Oct 2018) and Saubhagya household connections (~2019).
  • JEEViKA (BRLPS): est. 2006, World Bank-supported; SHG federation model; ~1.4+ crore rural households mobilised; runs Satat Jeevikoparjan Yojana (ultra-poor graduation) and was the rural application channel for MMRY (urban channel: mmry.brlps.in with Urban Development Dept).
  • Decentralisation (Jun 2025): Mukhiyas empowered to approve works up to ₹15 lakh (Finance Commission grants) and MGNREGA works up to ₹10 lakh (earlier ₹5 lakh).

⚖️ E. Caste Survey → 65% Quota → Courts (The Legal Track)
📜 Timeline Every BPSC Aspirant Must Know
  • 2 Oct 2023: Bihar caste survey report released (Development Commissioner Vivek Singh) — EBC 36.01%, OBC 27.13%, SC 19.65%, ST 1.68%, General 15.52%; total 13.07 crore; 209 notified castes (112 EBC, 34 OBC, 23 SC, 32 ST). Bihar = first state since 1931 to enumerate all castes.
  • Nov 2023: Legislature raised quotas — BC 12→18%, EBC 18→25%, SC 16→20%, ST 1→2% = 65% (75% with 10% EWS).
  • 20 Jun 2024: Patna HC struck the 65% law down (breach of Indra Sawhney 50% ceiling; creamy layer point); SC (28–29 Jul 2024) declined to stay, agreed to hear appeal — pending as of Jul 2026; the 65% law remains inoperative.
  • Jul 2024: SC quashed Bihar's 2015 notification merging Tanti-Tantwa into the SC list (Article 341 — only Parliament can amend; 70th BPSC question).
  • Census 2027: will include caste enumeration (MHA notification 7 Jan 2026) — Phase-I houselisting Apr–Sep 2026; Phase-II (with caste) Feb 2027, reference date 1 Mar 2027; first caste count since 1931; budget ₹11,718.24 cr. Bihar's 2023 survey is the direct political ancestor of this decision.
🏛️ F. Bihar Polity & CM Records (Evergreen)
  • State basics: Bihar separated from Bengal 22 Mar 1912 (Bihar Diwas); Jharkhand carved out 15 Nov 2000; capital Patna; 38 districts, 9 divisions; area 94,163 km²; borders Nepal (N), UP (W), Jharkhand (S), West Bengal (E).
  • Legislature: 243 Vidhan Sabha + 75 Vidhan Parishad seats; 40 Lok Sabha + 16 Rajya Sabha seats; Raj Bhavan renamed "Lok Bhavan".
  • CM records: 1st CM Sri Krishna Sinha (1946–61); 1st woman CM Rabri Devi; shortest tenure Satish Prasad Singh (5 days); President's Rule imposed 8 times; Nitish Kumar = longest-serving (10 oaths, ~20 years, till 14 Apr 2026); Samrat Chaudhary = 24th CM & first BJP CM (15 Apr 2026).
  • Economic frame: GSDP 2024-25 ₹9.92 lakh cr → 2025-26 RE ₹11.40 lakh cr → 2026-27 BE ₹13.09 lakh cr; growth 8.6% (constant, 2024-25) vs India 6.5%; per-capita GSDP ₹76,490 (~1/3rd of national); sectors: agriculture 23% : manufacturing 23% : services 54%; outstanding debt ₹3.74 lakh cr (~37.7% of GSDP).
  • Budget anchors: 2025-26 outlay ₹3,16,895.02 cr (FM Samrat Chaudhary, 3 Mar 2025) → 2026-27 outlay ₹3,47,589.76 cr (FM Bijendra Prasad Yadav, 3 Feb 2026) — largest in state history; education the top head both years (₹60,964.87 cr → ₹68,216 cr); fiscal deficit 3% of GSDP; revenue surplus ₹1,143 cr (2026-27).
