Key Battles

  • First Battle of Tarain (1191): Prithviraj Chauhan defeated Muhammad Ghori. Prithviraj let Ghori go free — a chivalrous but fatal mistake.
  • Second Battle of Tarain (1192): Ghori returned with a larger army, defeated and killed Prithviraj. Marked the beginning of Muslim rule in North India. Led to the Delhi Sultanate (1206 CE).

Administration

  • Hierarchy: King → valanadu (8-9 provinces) → nadu (districts) → kurram (groups of villages) → ur/sabha (village)
  • Village assemblies: Ur (non-brahmin), Sabha (brahmin, also called Mahasabha)
  • Kudavolai: election by lot — names drawn from a pot. Qualifications: age 35+, land ownership, knowledge of Vedas. Disqualifications: criminal record, corruption.
  • Uttaramerur inscription (Parantaka I, 10th c.): 'Magna Carta of village self-government' — most detailed record of local democracy in ancient India.
  • Committees: each village had committees for: tanks (irrigation), gardens, justice, gold (treasury). Members served for limited terms (rotation).

Art and Architecture

  • Brihadisvara Temple (Thanjavur): built by Rajaraja I (1010). 66m vimana. UNESCO (1987). Dravida style.
  • Gangaikondacholapuram: built by Rajendra I. Another massive Shiva temple.
  • Chola bronzes: Nataraja (dancing Shiva) — made using lost-wax (cire perdue) technique. Masterpieces of Indian art.
  • Naval architecture: Chola navy was the strongest in ancient India — controlled Bay of Bengal, Maldives, parts of SE Asia.
🔶 Bihar Connection — Pala Dynasty Deep Dive
Pala Dynasty (750-1161 CE) — Buddhist Rulers of Bihar & Bengal
  • Founder: Gopala (c. 750 CE) — elected king to end Matsyanyaya (anarchy). First recorded democratic election in India.
  • Dharmapala (c. 770-810): expanded the empire; founded Vikramashila University (Bihar); patronised Buddhism.
  • Devapala (c. 810-850): further expansion; received Buddhist emissaries from Southeast Asia.
  • Universities: Vikramashila (founded by Dharmapala, near Bhagalpur), Odantapuri (Bihar). Both destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE).
  • Buddhist missionary work: Atisha Dipankara (Pala-era scholar) went to Tibet — spread Buddhism there.
  • Decline: weakened by the Senas (12th century); finally overthrown by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE).
  • Significance for BPSC: Pala dynasty is the Bihar-specific early medieval topic. Know: Gopala (founder), Dharmapala (Vikramashila), Devapala. Palas = Buddhist; Senas = Hindu.
🎯 Exam Predictions for 72nd BPSC
P1 Chola rulers (HIGH): Vijayalaya (founder 850) → Rajaraja I (Brihadisvara 1010) → Rajendra I (Bengal, Srivijaya, Gangaikonda Chola) → Kulottunga (last great). V-R-R-K sequence.
P2 Chola administration (HIGH): Ur/Sabha (village assemblies), kudavolai (election by lot), Uttaramerur inscription (Parantaka I). Valanadu → nadu → kurram → village. Finest local self-government.
P3 Rajput dynasties (MEDIUM): Pratihara (Kanauj, Mihira Bhoja), Chauhan (Ajmer/Delhi, Prithviraj), Sisodia (Chittorgarh), Chandela (Khajuraho). 36 clans; Suryavansha/Chandravansha/Agnikula.
P4 Second Battle of Tarain (MEDIUM): 1192 — Ghori defeated Prithviraj; beginning of Muslim rule in North India; led to Delhi Sultanate (1206).
P5 Bihar — Pala dynasty (MEDIUM): Gopala (founder 750, elected), Dharmapala (Vikramashila), Devapala. Buddhist rulers of Bihar/Bengal for 400 years. Vikramashila destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200).
P6 Chola navy + SE Asia (LOW): Strongest navy in ancient India. Rajendra I → Srivijaya (~1025). Trade with Srivijaya, Khmer, Sailendra. Controlled Bay of Bengal.
P7 UNESCO sites (LOW): Brihadisvara (1987, Rajaraja I), Khajuraho (1986, Chandela), Mahabalipuram (Pallava). Know which dynasty built each.
