Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) — Detailed Profile

1. Origins and Institutional Framework

The SCO was founded in **June 2001** in Shanghai by China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. It succeeded the "Shanghai Five" grouping formed in 1996.

⚠️ Common BPSC Exam Trap:
• The SCO **Secretariat** is located in **Beijing, China**.
• The Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure (**RATS**) is located in **Tashkent, Uzbekistan**.
• Both India and Pakistan became full members simultaneously at the **Astana Summit in June 2017**.

2. Accession of New Members (2023-2024)

  • 9th Member State: **Iran** officially joined as a full member in **July 2023** during the virtual summit hosted by India.
  • 10th Member State: **Belarus** was formally admitted as a full member at the **Astana Summit in July 2024** under Kazakhstan's chairmanship.

3. 24th SCO Summit (Astana, Kazakhstan — July 2024)

Astana Declaration: The declaration emphasized regional security, counter-terrorism, and trade cooperation. Belarus was officially welcomed, bringing the total membership to 10. India was represented by External Affairs Minister Dr. S. Jaishankar.

4. 25th SCO Summit (China — 2025)

Under China's chairmanship, the summit focused on economic connectivity, technology transfer, and joint security drills under the SCO RATS framework.
