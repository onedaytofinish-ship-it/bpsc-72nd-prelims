📖 Bihar — Nalanda in Post-Gupta Period
Bihar's Post-Gupta connection:
  • Nalanda University (Bihar): Hiuen Tsang studied at Nalanda for 5 years (630-645 CE) during Harsha's reign. Nalanda at its peak — world's greatest Buddhist university. Harsha patronised Nalanda.
  • Political shift: after the Guptas, political power shifted from Pataliputra (Bihar) to Kannauj (UP) under Harsha. Bihar was no longer the political capital — but Nalanda kept Bihar at the centre of Buddhist learning.
  • Harsha's patronage: Harsha donated land and resources to Nalanda (Bihar). Nalanda flourished under his support.
  • Hiuen Tsang's account: 'Si-yu-ki' provides detailed account of Nalanda (Bihar) — architecture, curriculum, teachers, students, daily life. Most comprehensive description of Nalanda that survives.

🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Harsha (HIGH): 606-647 CE. Kannauj. Defeated by Pulakeshin II (634 CE). Banabhatta (Harshacharita). Hiuen Tsang. Kanauj Assembly.
P2 Chalukyas + Pulakeshin II (HIGH): Badami. Defeated Harsha (634 CE, Aihole inscription, Ravikirti). Pattadakal (UNESCO 1987).
P3 Sangam + 3 kingdoms (MEDIUM): Chera (Senguttuvan), Chola (Karikala, Kallanai), Pandya (Nedunjeliyan). 'Muvendar.'
P4 Sangam literature (MEDIUM): Silappadikaram (Ilango Adigal), Thirukkural (Thiruvalluvar), Manimekalai (Sattanar). Aham (love) + Puram (war).
P5 Pallavas + Mahabalipuram (MEDIUM): Kanchipuram. Mahabalipuram (UNESCO 1984). Narasimhavarman I defeated Pulakeshin II (642 CE).
P6 Bihar = Nalanda (LOW): Hiuen Tsang studied at Nalanda (Bihar, 5 years, 630-645 CE). Political power shifted from Pataliputra to Kannauj.
P7 Sangam economy + society (LOW): Roman trade (Muziris, spices, pearls). No rigid caste (occupational groups). Women high status (Avvaiyar).
