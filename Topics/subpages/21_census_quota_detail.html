Census History, Caste-Count Timeline, Quota Jurisprudence & MPI Method — Statics That Anchor the Current Affairs

1. Census of India — The Static Spine

  • 1872: first census attempt under Viceroy Lord Mayo — non-synchronous (different regions counted in different years).
  • 1881: first synchronous all-India census (Lord Ripon) — the decennial cycle begins and has never been missed since (even 1941, WWII).
  • 1951: first census after Independence (8th in the series since 1872) — also the first with a National Register of Citizens-linked enumeration in Assam later.
  • 2021 census postponed (COVID-19) — the first-ever delay in the decennial cycle; it is now running as Census 2027.
  • Census 2027 = 16th census overall, 8th after Independence; conducted by the Registrar General & Census Commissioner of India under the Ministry of Home Affairs; legal basis = Census Act, 1948 (data confidential, cannot be used as evidence against individuals).
  • Two phases every time: Phase I = Houselisting & Housing (records housing conditions, amenities, assets); Phase II = Population Enumeration (demographic, social, economic data of every person).

  • Article 341/342: SC and ST lists are notified by the President; only Parliament can include or exclude communities — states cannot (the ground on which Bihar's 2015 Tanti-Tantwa notification was quashed in July 2024 — 70th BPSC PYQ).
  • 105th Amendment (2021): restored the states' power to identify OBCs (after the SC's Maratha ruling had read it down).

4. MPI Methodology — What the Index Actually Measures

  • Global MPI (OPHI + UNDP): 3 dimensions — Health, Education, Standard of Living; 10 indicators; a person deprived in ≥1/3 of weighted indicators is 'MPI poor'.
  • National MPI (NITI Aayog): same 3 dimensions but 12 indicators — adds bank account (financial inclusion) and maternal health-linked refinements; computed from NFHS microdata (NFHS-4 = 2015-16, NFHS-5 = 2019-21).
  • Two headline statistics: Headcount ratio (H) = % of people who are poor; Intensity (A) = average share of deprivations among the poor; MPI = H × A.
  • India's arc: 24.85% (2015-16) → 14.96% (2019-21) → ~11.3% (2022-23 update); 13.5 crore people exited poverty in the first interval.
  • Bihar's arc: ~51.9% → ~33.8% (largest absolute decline among states) → ~26.6% in the 2025 update — still the highest in India, ahead of Jharkhand (23.3%) and UP (17.4%).
  • Distinct from: UNDP's older Capability Poverty Measure (CPM) — child malnutrition, unattended births, FEMALE illiteracy (71st BPSC PYQ); and Tendulkar/Rangarajan consumption-poverty lines (Topic #86 territory).
🧠 Mnemonic — MPI dimensions "HES"
Health, Education, Standard of living — 3 dimensions, 12 national indicators, 1/3 deprivation cut-off. India MPI: "25 → 15 → 11"; Bihar: "52 → 34 → 27" (rounded).
