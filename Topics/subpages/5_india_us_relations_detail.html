Bilateral Treaties & Economic Partnerships (2024-2026)

Free Trade Agreements (FTAs) and strategic technology partnerships are highly valued by BPSC examiners. This subpage provides exhaustive, memorizable parameters for India's major recent bilateral treaties.

1. India-EFTA Trade & Economic Partnership Agreement (TEPA)

📅 Implementation Date: Signed on March 10, 2024; officially came into force on October 1, 2025.
  • EFTA Members: Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, and Liechtenstein (not part of the European Union).
  • The $100 Billion Target: EFTA countries have legally committed to facilitate $100 billion in Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) into India over 15 years. This is the first-ever Indian FTA to feature a binding investment target.
  • Job Creation Goal: The investment aims to directly generate 1 million jobs in India over 15 years.
  • Tariff concessions: EFTA reduces tariffs on 92.2% of its tariff lines (covering 99.6% of India's exports). India reduces tariffs on 82.7% of its tariff lines.
  • Sensitive Exclusions: India has completely excluded sensitive sectors like dairy, soya, coal, and several agricultural products to protect domestic farmers.

2. India-US Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technology (iCET)

Launched in January 2023, iCET is directed by the National Security Advisors (NSAs) of both countries to expand strategic technology partnerships and defense industrial cooperation.

  • 2nd iCET Annual Meeting (June 2024 - New Delhi): Co-chaired by India's NSA Ajit Doval and US NSA Jake Sullivan.
  • Key Pillars: Space exploration (including sending an ISRO astronaut to the International Space Station), semiconductor supply chains (establishing a semiconductor fab partnership), AI research, quantum computing, and co-development of jet engines (GE F414 engines in India).

3. India-UAE Strategic Agreements (Feb 2024)

India and UAE signed major bilateral treaties during PM Modi's visit to Abu Dhabi in February 2024.

  • Bilateral Investment Treaty (BIT): Aimed at promoting and protecting investments between the two nations, succeeding the 2013 Bilateral Investment Promotion and Protection Agreement (BIPPA).
  • Payment Interlinkage: Agreement to interlink India's UPI (Unified Payments Interface) with UAE's AANI payment system, and India's RuPay cards with UAE's JAYWAN cards.
  • Local Currency Settlement (LCS): Transitioning oil and general trade transactions directly into Rupee-Dirham payments, bypassing US Dollar conversion fees.

4. India-Australia Economic Cooperation & Trade Agreement (ECTA)

📅 BPSC PYQ Connection (69th BPSC): India signed the ECTA with **Australia** in April 2022 (entered into force Dec 2022). It eliminated tariffs on over 85% of Australian exports to India and over 96% of Indian exports to Australia.
  • CECA Negotiations: The Comprehensive Economic Cooperation Agreement (CECA) is currently being negotiated to expand the ECTA framework into services and digital trade.

5. India-Russia 22nd Annual Summit (July 2024)

📅 Key Highlight: Summit held in Moscow, July 2024.
  • Trade Target: India and Russia agreed to increase bilateral trade to $100 billion by 2030 (up from around $65 billion currently).
  • Energy Security: Long-term agreements for the steady supply of discounted Russian crude oil to India.
  • Nuclear Power: Continued Russian technical support for the Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant (KKNPP) in Tamil Nadu.
  • Defense: Focus on joint manufacturing under "Make in India," including spare parts for Russian-origin military platforms in India.

6. India-Maldives Strategic Shift (2024)

The bilateral relationship underwent recalibration following the election of President Mohamed Muizzu (who campaigned on an "India Out" platform).

  • Troop Withdrawal: By May 2024, India successfully replaced its military personnel stationed in the Maldives (who were operating two Dhruv helicopters and a Dornier aircraft for medical evacuations) with competent Indian civilian technical experts.
  • Development Assistance: India remains a crucial partner, renewing quotas for the export of essential commodities (rice, wheat, sugar, onions) to the Maldives for 2024-25 despite political tensions.

7. India's "Neighborhood First" Policy

A core pillar of Indian foreign policy focusing on peaceful relations and collaborative economic development with immediate neighbors.

  • Bangladesh: India's largest trade partner in South Asia. Focus on connectivity (Akhaura-Agartala rail link), energy pipelines, and transit agreements.
  • Nepal: Shares an open border (726 km with Bihar). Cooperation on hydropower projects and digital payment integration (UPI-NCHL).
  • Bhutan: India's steadfast ally; India heavily supports Bhutan's Five-Year Plans and develops massive run-of-the-river hydroelectric projects (e.g., Punatsangchhu, Mangdechhu).
