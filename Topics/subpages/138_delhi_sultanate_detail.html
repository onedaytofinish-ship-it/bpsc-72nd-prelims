🕌 Delhi Sultanate — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Five dynasties (1206-1526), key rulers, administrative systems, Bihar connection (Bakhtiyar Khalji), 7 exam predictions

📖 Five Dynasties Deep Dive

1. Slave/Mamluk Dynasty (1206-1290)

  • Founder: Qutbuddin Aibak (1206-1210) — former slave of Muhammad Ghori. 'Lakh Baksh.' Started Qutub Minar.
  • Consolidator: Iltutmish (1211-1236) — first 'Sultan.' Completed Qutub Minar. Iqta system. Silver tanka. Defeated Mongols.
  • Razia Sultan (1236-1240): first woman ruler of India. Daughter of Iltutmish. Killed by nobles.
  • Balban (1266-1287): divine right theory (Sultan = God's shadow). Suppressed Chihalgani. Sijda/paibos.

2. Khalji Dynasty (1290-1320)

  • Founder: Jalaluddin Khalji (1290-1296) — first Khalji Sultan.
  • Greatest: Alauddin Khalji (1296-1316) — conquered most of India + Deccan (via Malik Kafur). Defeated Mongols 6 times. Market reforms. Chittorgarh (1303, Padmini jauhar).

3. Tughlaq Dynasty (1320-1414)

  • Founder: Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (1320-1325). Built Tughlaqabad Fort.
  • Muhammad bin Tughlaq (1325-1351): 'Mad genius.' Failed experiments: Daulatabad (capital transfer), token currency, enhanced taxation. Scholar-king.
  • Firoz Shah Tughlaq (1351-1388): constructive ruler. Canals, mosques, cities, hospitals. Reinstated jaziya.
  • Timur's sack (1398): Timur sacked Delhi — massive destruction. Weakened the Tughlaq Dynasty.

4. Sayyid Dynasty (1414-1451)

  • Founder: Khizr Khan (1414-1421). Claimed descent from Prophet Muhammad.
  • Weak dynasty — controlled only Delhi. Lasted 37 years.

5. Lodi Dynasty (1451-1526)

  • Founder: Bahlul Lodi (1451-1489) — first Afghan dynasty.
  • Sikandar Lodi (1489-1517): founded Agra (1504). Conquered Bihar. Patron of learning.
  • Ibrahim Lodi (1517-1526): last Sultan. Defeated by Babur at Panipat (1526). End of Delhi Sultanate.
🧠 Mnemonic — S-K-T-S-L:
Slave (1206) → Khalji (1290) → Tughlaq (1320) → Sayyid (1414) → Lodi (1451).

🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
Bakhtiyar Khalji & the Destruction of Nalanda (~1200 CE):
  • Bakhtiyar Khalji — a Turkish general who conquered Bihar and Bengal during the early Delhi Sultanate period (~1200 CE).
  • Destroyed Nalanda University and Vikramashila University (~1202 CE) — marking the end of Buddhist learning in India. The library of Nalanda burned for months.
  • The destruction of Nalanda is one of the most catastrophic events in Indian history — Nalanda was one of the world's first residential universities (5th-12th century).
  • Established the independent Khalji Dynasty of Bengal (1202-1227) — capital at Lakhnauti (Gaur). Bihar and Bengal remained under Muslim rule until 1947.
  • Sikandar Lodi (1490s): later conquered Bihar and brought it under the Lodi Dynasty's direct control.
Significance for BPSC: Bihar's connection with the Delhi Sultanate is primarily through the destruction of Nalanda — a key event for BPSC's Bihar-specific questions. Also, Sher Shah Suri (from Sasaram, Bihar) later ruled from Delhi (1540-1555) — see Topic 142.
