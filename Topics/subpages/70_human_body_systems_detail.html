Topic 70 — Human Body Systems: Deep Dive

Digestive, circulatory, respiratory, nervous — organs, functions, Bihar medical connections

1. Digestive System — Complete Overview

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients that the body can absorb and use. The process involves: ingestion (eating) → digestion (breaking down) → absorption (taking in nutrients) → assimilation (using nutrients) → egestion (removing waste).

Key digestive enzymes: Salivary amylase (mouth, starch→maltose), Pepsin (stomach, protein→peptides, needs HCl), Trypsin (small intestine, protein→amino acids, from pancreas), Lipase (small intestine, fat→fatty acids+glycerol, from pancreas), Pancreatic amylase (small intestine, starch→maltose). Bile (liver/gallbladder) is NOT an enzyme — it emulsifies fats.

2. Circulatory System — Complete Overview

The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout the body. It consists of the heart, blood vessels, and blood (~5 litres in an adult).

Double Circulation

  • Pulmonary circulation: right ventricle → pulmonary artery → lungs (O₂ picked up, CO₂ released) → pulmonary vein → left atrium. Short loop (heart to lungs and back).
  • Systemic circulation: left ventricle → aorta → body (O₂ delivered, CO₂ picked up) → vena cava → right atrium. Long loop (heart to body and back).
  • Why double circulation? it ensures that blood passes through the lungs (to get oxygenated) before being pumped to the body — maintaining high oxygen pressure in the systemic circuit. Mammals and birds have double circulation (energy-intensive, warm-blooded).

3. Respiratory System — Complete Overview

The respiratory system exchanges gases: O₂ in (for cells to produce energy via respiration) and CO₂ out (waste product of cellular respiration).

Breathing Mechanism

  • Inhalation (inspiration): diaphragm contracts (flattens) + intercostal muscles contract (ribs move up/out) → chest cavity volume increases → pressure in lungs drops below atmospheric → air rushes IN through nose/mouth → trachea → bronchi → alveoli.
  • Exhalation (expiration): diaphragm relaxes (domes up) + intercostal muscles relax (ribs move down/in) → chest cavity volume decreases → pressure in lungs rises above atmospheric → air pushed OUT.
  • Gas exchange at alveoli: O₂ diffuses from alveolar air (high O₂) into blood capillaries (low O₂) → binds to haemoglobin. CO₂ diffuses from blood (high CO₂) into alveoli (low CO₂) → exhaled.

4. Nervous System — Complete Overview

The nervous system controls and coordinates all body activities — receiving stimuli, processing information, and sending responses. It is the body's communication network.

— Somatic (voluntary)Nerves to skeletal musclesConscious movement
