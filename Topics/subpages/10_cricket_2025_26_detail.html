🏆 ICC Men's Events — Champions Trophy 2025, WTC 2025, T20 WC 2026

1. ICC Champions Trophy 2025 — India's 3rd Crown 9 MAR 2025

  • Final (Dubai, 9 Mar 2025): India beat New Zealand by 4 wickets; captain Rohit Sharma scored 76 (Player of the Match).
  • Context: the tournament was hosted by Pakistan, but India played all its matches in Dubai under the hybrid model — a venue detail BPSC can twist ("final was in Lahore" = wrong).
  • India's CT titles: 2002 (shared with Sri Lanka), 2013 (d. England), 2025 — three in all.

2. WTC Final 2025 — South Africa Break the Jinx LORD'S

  • Scorecard (11–14 Jun 2025, Lord's): Australia 212 & 207; South Africa 138 & 283/4 — SA won by 5 wickets, chasing 282.
  • Aiden Markram 136 (Player of the Match); it was South Africa's first WTC title and a rare ICC trophy for the Proteas.
  • WTC roll of honour: 2021 New Zealand (d. India), 2023 Australia (d. India), 2025 South Africa (d. Australia). India lost TWO WTC finals — a fact-pattern BPSC can probe.

🏟️ Bilateral Tests & Retirements (2024-25 → 2025-26)

Border-Gavaskar Trophy 2024-25 — Australia 3-1

  • 22 Nov 2024 – 5 Jan 2025; Australia won the 5th Test at the SCG by 6 wickets (Scott Boland POTM) — India lost the BGT after a decade of holding it (wins in 2016-17 and 2023 at home; 2018-19 and 2020-21 away).
  • Fallout: the defeat ended India's WTC 2023-25 final hopes, clearing the way for the Australia v South Africa final at Lord's.

Anderson–Tendulkar Trophy 2025 (India tour of England) — Drawn 2-2 NEW NAME

  • The Pataudi Trophy was rebranded the Anderson–Tendulkar Trophy (Jun–Aug 2025) — honouring James Anderson and Sachin Tendulkar; note the name change for "firsts/renames" questions.
  • Edgbaston: India won by 336 runs — Shubman Gill amassed 430 runs in the match; Akash Deep took 10 wickets.
  • The Oval: India won by 6 runs — Mohammed Siraj 5-104; England fell short at 367 chasing 374.
  • Shubman Gill — top scorer + Player of the Series in his first series as Test captain.

💰 IPL 2025 & IPL 2026 — The RCB Double

IPL 2025 — RCB's Maiden Title (18th Season)

  • Final (Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad, 3 Jun 2025): Royal Challengers Bengaluru beat Punjab Kings by 6 runs — RCB's first IPL trophy after three lost finals (2009, 2011, 2016).
  • Awards: Orange Cap — Sai Sudharsan (GT), 759 runs; Purple Cap — Prasidh Krishna (GT); MVP — Suryakumar Yadav (MI).

IPL 2026 — RCB Go Back-to-Back VERIFIED

  • Final (Ahmedabad, 31 May 2026): GT 155/8 (Rasikh Salam 3/27) v RCB 161/5 — Virat Kohli 75* (POTM); RCB won by 5 wickets.
  • The Vaibhav Sooryavanshi sweep (RR, age 15): Orange Cap + MVP + Emerging Player + Super Striker + Most Sixes (72) — 776 runs at SR 237.30; first-ever MVP + Emerging double. Purple Cap: Kagiso Rabada (29 wickets).
🔶 Sooryavanshi = Bihar's Cricket Face (feeds Topic #19)
  • Born 27 Mar 2011, Tajpur, Samastipur district, Bihar.
  • Youngest-ever IPL signing — ₹1.1 crore by Rajasthan Royals at age 13 (2025 auction).
  • Youngest IPL debutant — 19 Apr 2025, aged 14 years 23 days.
  • Youngest T20 centurion — 101 off 38 balls v Gujarat Titans, 28 Apr 2025 (35-ball hundred = 2nd-fastest in IPL history).
  • U19 WC 2026: 175 off 80 in the final (highest in a U19 WC final) + Player of the Tournament (439 runs @ 62.71).
  • ₹50 lakh reward announced by the Bihar government (Feb 2026).
🌍 Women's World Cup 2025, U19 WC 2026, Asia Cup 2025

ICC Women's ODI World Cup 2025 — India's Maiden Title HISTORIC

  • Final (DY Patil Stadium, Navi Mumbai, 2 Nov 2025): IND 298/7 (Shafali Verma 87 + 2/36, POTM; Smriti Mandhana 45) beat SA 246 — margin 52 runs. Co-hosted by India + Sri Lanka (30 Sep – 2 Nov 2025).
  • Deepti Sharma — Player of the Tournament: 22 wickets including 5/39 in the final.
  • Laura Wolvaardt (SA): 101 in the final; 571 runs = single-edition tournament record.
  • Harmanpreet Kaur = 3rd Indian World-Cup-winning captain after Kapil Dev (1983) and M.S. Dhoni (2011) — and the first woman. She received the Padma Shri in 2026.

ICC U19 World Cup 2026 — Record 6th Title

  • Final (Harare Sports Club, Zimbabwe, 6 Feb 2026): IND 411/9 beat ENG 311 by 100 runs; captain Ayush Mhatre (53 in the final); Ambrish 3-56; England's Falconer made 115.
  • Vaibhav Sooryavanshi: 175 off 80 (15 fours + 15 sixes) — highest individual score in a U19 WC final; POTM + Player of the Tournament (439 runs @ 62.71).
  • India's U19 titles: 2000, 2008, 2012, 2018, 2022, 2026 — six, the most by any country.

Asia Cup 2025 (T20, UAE) — 9th Title

  • Final (Dubai, 28 Sep 2025): Pakistan 146 (Kuldeep Yadav 4 wickets) — India chased it with Tilak Varma 69* off 53, winning by 5 wickets.
  • India's 9th Asia Cup (ODI + T20 combined) — extend the count: 1984, 1988, 1990-91, 1995, 2010, 2016, 2018, 2023, 2025.
