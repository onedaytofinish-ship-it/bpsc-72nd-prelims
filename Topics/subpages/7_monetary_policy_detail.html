Monetary Policy — Deep Dive
🏦 RBI & the MPC 📅 Verified: July 2026
📐 Rate Corridor Mechanics (LAF Framework)
  • Operating target: the Weighted Average Call Rate (WACR) — the overnight unsecured interbank rate — is the explicit operating target of monetary policy. RBI steers WACR toward the repo rate using the corridor.
  • Corridor width: 50 bps, symmetric around the repo — SDF at Repo − 25 bps (5.00%) and MSF at Repo + 25 bps (5.50%). The corridor pins overnight money-market rates between the floor and ceiling.
  • SDF (floor, since April 2022): banks park surplus liquidity with RBI without giving collateral — this removed the collateral constraint of the old fixed reverse repo and made absorption capacity unlimited. Any repo change automatically reprices the SDF.
  • MSF (ceiling): emergency overnight borrowing for scheduled banks against SLR securities — banks may dip below their SLR requirement by up to 1% of NDTL under MSF. The penal spread discourages routine use.
  • Bank Rate = MSF rate: aligned since 2011; today its main operational role is the penal rate for CRR/SLR shortfalls, not refinance.
  • Liquidity management toolkit: LAF (daily, frictional needs) + OMO (durable liquidity — purchase injects, sale absorbs), Variable Rate Repo/Reverse Repo auctions (14-day main operation), MSS (sterilisation via dated securities/T-bills), and forex buy-sell swaps (Dec 2025: RBI ran OMO purchases and a 3-year $5 bn swap to keep surplus liquidity).
  • Current liquidity backdrop (mid-2026): average daily surplus ≈ ₹2.3 lakh crore — an accommodative liquidity stance matching the 125 bps of rate cuts.
🧠 Mnemonic — "Floor is Free, Ceiling Costs Collateral"
SDF (floor, 5.00%) needs no collateral — banks just deposit. MSF (ceiling, 5.50%) needs SLR collateral and is the last resort. The repo (5.25%) sits in between — RBI lends against G-secs at the policy rate.
👥 Monetary Policy Committee — Members & Tenure

Six members, decisions by majority, Governor's casting vote on ties, bi-monthly meetings (six a year), minutes published 14 days later, each member's vote on record. Quorum: 4.

🔁 Monetary Policy Transmission
  • What it means: the pass-through of repo changes to bank deposit/lending rates, credit growth, asset prices, the exchange rate — and finally output and inflation. India historically saw weak, lagged transmission; the 2025-26 cycle (125 bps) transmitted substantially to new loans.
  • External Benchmark regime (Oct 1, 2019): all new floating-rate personal/retail and MSME loans must be linked to an external benchmark — the policy repo rate, 3-month or 6-month T-bill yield, or any FBIL-published benchmark. Result: EBLR-linked home loans reprice within the quarterly reset cycle, while legacy MCLR loans lag by 50–90 bps.
  • Channels (exam one-liners): interest-rate channel (cost of funds), credit channel (bank lending volume), exchange-rate channel (rupee and import prices), asset-price/wealth channel (equities, bonds, housing).
  • Why cuts may not transmit fully: high credit-deposit ratio (~78%), sticky deposit rates as banks compete for deposits, NPAs and risk aversion, and administered small-savings rates setting a floor for deposits.
🏛️ Functions of the Reserve Bank of India
  • Monetary authority: formulates and implements monetary policy via the MPC (price stability with growth in mind).
  • Issuer of currency: sole authority to issue banknotes (except the ₹1 note/coins, issued by the Government of India but circulated through RBI); manages currency chests.
  • Banker to the Government: manages Union/State accounts, ways-and-means advances, and the public debt (G-sec auctions); this is an obligation under the RBI Act.
  • Bankers' bank & lender of last resort: holds banks' CRR, refinances through LAF/MSF, settles interbank payments (RTGS/NEFT run by RBI).
  • Regulator & supervisor of the financial system: banks, NBFCs, cooperative banks (Banking Regulation Act, 1949); prescribes capital norms (CRAR), conducts inspections; GNPA now at multi-decadal lows (~2.5%).
  • Manager of foreign exchange: FEMA 1999; manages ~$701 bn reserves, intervenes to curb rupee volatility.
  • Payment-system regulator: under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007 (UPI is operated by NPCI under RBI oversight).
  • Developmental & research role: financial inclusion (PMJDY, Kisan Rin Portal), priority-sector lending norms, CBDC (digital rupee e₹) pilots, data publications (WSS, Database on the Indian Economy).
