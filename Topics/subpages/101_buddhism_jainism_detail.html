📖 Bihar — Cradle of Both Religions
Buddhism in Bihar:
  • Bodh Gaya: Buddha's enlightenment (c. 528 BCE). Mahabodhi Temple UNESCO (2002). Most important Buddhist pilgrimage site.
  • Rajgir: First Buddhist Council (483 BCE, Saptaparni cave). Veluvana monastery (first). Gridhakuta (Vulture Peak).
  • Vaishali: Second Buddhist Council (383 BCE). Buddha's last sermon.
  • Pataliputra: Third Buddhist Council (250 BCE, Ashoka). Capital of Mauryan Empire.
  • Nalanda: Buddhist university (5th-12th c. CE). 10,000 students. UNESCO (2016). Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200).
Jainism in Bihar:
  • Vaishali (Kundagrama): Mahavira's birth (c. 599 BCE). Jnatrika clan.
  • Rajgir (Jrimbhakagrama): Mahavira's Kevala Jnana (omniscience, c. 557 BCE). After 12 years of asceticism.
  • Pawapuri (Nalanda district): Mahavira's Nirvana/death (c. 527 BCE). Jal Mandir = major Jain pilgrimage.
  • Pataliputra: First Jain Council (c. 527 BCE, Sthulabhadra). Compiled 12 Angas in Ardhamagadhi Prakrit.
🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Buddha's 4 sites (HIGH): Lumbini (birth, Nepal), Bodh Gaya (enlightenment, Bihar), Sarnath (first sermon, UP), Kushinagar (death, UP). LBSK.
P2 Bihar = cradle of both (HIGH): Buddhism: Bodh Gaya + Rajgir + Vaishali + Pataliputra + Nalanda. Jainism: Vaishali (birth) + Pawapuri (death). All first 3 Buddhist Councils in Bihar.
P3 Four Noble Truths + Eightfold Path (MEDIUM): Dukkha, Samudaya, Nirodha, Magga. 8 steps: right view/intention/speech/action/livelihood/effort/mindfulness/concentration.
P4 Jainism basics (MEDIUM): Mahavira = 24th Tirthankara. 5 vows: ahimsa, satya, asteya, brahmacharya, aparigraha. Digambara + Svetambara. Strictest ahimsa.
P5 Buddhist Councils (MEDIUM): 1st (Rajgir, 483, Ajatashatru), 2nd (Vaishali, 383, Kalashoka), 3rd (Pataliputra, 250, Ashoka), 4th (Kashmir, 72, Kanishka). First 3 in Bihar!
P6 Sects + texts (LOW): Buddhism: Hinayana/Mahayana, Tripitaka (Pali). Jainism: Digambara/Svetambara, Agamas (Ardhamagadhi).
P7 Nalanda + Pawapuri (LOW): Nalanda (Bihar, Buddhist university, UNESCO 2016). Pawapuri (Bihar, Mahavira death, Jal Mandir). Both in Bihar!
