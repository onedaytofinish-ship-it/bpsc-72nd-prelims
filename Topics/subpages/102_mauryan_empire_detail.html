🦁 Mauryan Empire — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Chandragupta, Bindusara, Ashoka, administration, edicts, Bihar connections (Pataliputra, Ashokan pillars, Barabar Caves), 7 exam predictions

📖 Bihar — Heartland of the Mauryan Empire
Pataliputra (Patna): The Mauryan capital — the greatest city of ancient India. Megasthenes described it (9 mi × 1.5 mi, wooden wall, 570 towers, 64 gates). Administrative centre of an empire from Afghanistan to Bengal.
  • Ashokan Pillars in Bihar: Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva (all West Champaran). Lion, bull, and elephant capitals. Among the best-preserved Ashokan pillars.
  • Barabar Caves (near Gaya): Oldest rock-cut caves in India. Donated by Ashoka to the Ajivikas (ascetic sect, Makkhali Gosala). Dasharatha (grandson) also donated at Nagarjuna hills.
  • Third Buddhist Council (Pataliputra, 250 BCE): Convened by Ashoka. Compiled Abhidhamma Pitaka. Sent missionaries — Buddhism became a world religion FROM Bihar.
  • Bodh Gaya: Ashoka visited and built the original Mahabodhi Temple. His Rummindei Pillar Edict records pilgrimage to Lumbini.
  • Rajgir + Vaishali: Earlier Buddhist Councils (1st at Rajgir, 2nd at Vaishali) — Mauryas patronised these sites.

🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Chandragupta + Chanakya (HIGH): Founder (321 BCE), Chanakya = Arthashastra. Defeated Seleucus (303 BCE). Megasthenes ambassador. Jainism, Shravanabelagola.
P2 Ashoka + Kalinga (HIGH): 268-232 BCE. Kalinga (261 BCE, Odisha) → Buddhism. Third Council (Pataliputra, 250 BCE). Mahendra + Sanghamitra to Sri Lanka. Sarnath = national emblem.
P3 Bihar = Mauryan heartland (HIGH): Pataliputra capital. Pillars: Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva. Barabar Caves (Ajivikas). Third Council at Pataliputra.
P4 Edicts (MEDIUM): 14 Rock + 7 Pillar. Brahmi/Kharoshthi/Greek/Aramaic. Prakrit. Prinsep 1837. Edict XIII = Kalinga.
P5 Administration (MEDIUM): 5 provinces. 18 departments. City council 30. Bhaga (1/6 tax). Arthashastra.
P6 Decline (LOW): After Ashoka (232 BCE). Brihadratha killed by Pushyamitra Shunga (185 BCE).
P7 Barabar + Ajivikas (LOW): Oldest rock-cut caves (Gaya, Bihar). Donated to Ajivikas (Makkhali Gosala, niyati/destiny).
