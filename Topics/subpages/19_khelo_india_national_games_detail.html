🏟️ A. The Khelo India Family — Scheme Statics
  • Khelo India Scheme: revamped and launched in 2018 by the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports (merging earlier schemes such as the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Abhiyan) — objectives span mass participation, talent identification and sports infrastructure.
  • Khelo India Youth Games (KIYG): annual since 2018 (1st edition: New Delhi) — under-17/under-21 multi-sport nationals; the flagship pipeline for Olympic talent.
  • Khelo India Winter Games: snow/ice disciplines, held in Gulmarg (and later Ladakh legs) since 2020.
  • Khelo India Para Games: 1st edition 2023 (New Delhi).
  • Khelo India University Games (KIUG): 1st edition 2020 (Bhubaneswar, Odisha).
  • TOPS (Target Olympic Podium Scheme): the companion elite-funding scheme — identifies and funds medal-probable athletes for the Olympics.

🏅 C. National Games — History & Recent Editions
  • Origin: began as the Indian Olympic Games in 1924 (Lahore); renamed the National Games from the 1940 edition — India's own Olympic-style multi-sport meet.
  • Raja Bhalindra Singh Trophy: awarded to the overall champion (state/unit with most golds). Services (SSCB) are the most frequent winners — including 2025 (5th time, 121 medals / 68 gold).
