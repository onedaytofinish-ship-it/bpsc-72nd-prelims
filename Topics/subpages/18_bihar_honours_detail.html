🏅 Core Concepts — How India's Civilian Honours Work

A. Bharat Ratna — the jewel at the top

  • Instituted 1954 (same year as the Padma awards); awarded for "exceptional service towards advancement of art, literature and science, and in recognition of public service of the highest order".
  • First recipients (1954): C. Rajagopalachari, Dr. S. Radhakrishnan, Dr. C.V. Raman.
  • Cap: maximum 3 awards in a year; carries no cash — a sanad (certificate) signed by the President and a peepal-leaf-shaped medallion.
  • Not a title: under Article 18(1) of the Constitution it cannot be prefixed or suffixed to the recipient's name.
  • Latest set (2024, 5 recipients): Karpoori Thakur (posth., Bihar), L.K. Advani, P.V. Narasimha Rao (posth.), Chaudhary Charan Singh (posth.), M.S. Swaminathan (posth.) — conferred 30 Mar 2024. None announced in 2025 or 2026 (up to 15 Jul 2026).

  • Cycle: announced on the eve of Republic Day (25 Jan); conferred by the President at Rashtrapati Bhavan — usually March-May, sometimes in phases (2026 Phase-1: 25 May 2026, 66 recipients).
  • Selection: Padma Awards Committee, headed by the Cabinet Secretary; nominations are open to the public (including self-nomination).
  • Eligibility bar: government servants (including those of PSUs) are not eligible — except doctors and scientists.
  • 2026 texture: 19 women, 16 posthumous, 6 foreigners/NRI/PIO/OCI, 45 "unsung heroes", 2 duo cases (each counted as one award).
  • Precedence (civilian): Bharat Ratna > Padma Vibhushan > Padma Bhushan > Padma Shri.

📜 Roll of Honour 2025-26 — Mini-Bios (from verified reports)

Padma 2025 — the seven

  • Sharda Sinha (Padma Vibhushan, posth., Art): born 1952 at Hulas village, Supaul district; based in Patna; the voice of Chhath geet and Bihari folk music; Padma Shri 1991, Padma Bhushan 2018; died 2024; "Bihar Kokila".
  • Sushil Kumar Modi (Padma Bhushan, posth., Public Affairs): former Deputy Chief Minister of Bihar; Patna-based BJP leader; among the state's most prominent political figures of the 2005-2020 era.
  • Kishore Kunal (Padma Shri, posth., Civil Service): ex-IPS officer who, as Chairman of the Mahavir Mandir Trust, Patna, built one of north India's largest temple-linked charity networks.
  • Bhim Singh Bhavesh (Padma Shri, Social Work): Bhojpur-based journalist who spent decades reporting on and organising the Musahar community — among Bihar's most marginalised Mahadalit groups.
  • Hemant Kumar (Padma Shri, Medicine): honoured for kidney-disease treatment and awareness work (district not officially confirmed).
  • Nirmala Devi (Padma Shri, Art): master artisan of Sujni embroidery — Bihar's traditional quilted-embroidery craft (district not officially confirmed).
  • Vijay Nityanand Surishwar Ji Maharaj (Padma Shri, Spiritualism): Jain spiritual leader (district not officially confirmed).

Padma 2026 — the three (all Padma Shri)

  • Bharat Singh Bharti (Art — folk): 90-year-old folk artist from the Bhojpur region — the classic "unsung hero" pick.
  • Bishwa Bandhu (Art, posth.): cultural figure known as "Vishwabandhu" (district not officially confirmed).
  • Gopal Ji Trivedi (Science & Engineering): honoured for scientific/engineering contributions (district not officially confirmed).

Bihar state honour in the window

  • Khel Samman 2025 (Oct 2025, Rajgir Sports Complex): CM Nitish Kumar felicitated 153 para athletes + 24 coaches — cash awards plus government-job letters; headline pair: Shailesh Kumar ₹75 lakh, Gauri Kumari ₹10 lakh. Rajgir (Nalanda) is Bihar's flagship new sports hub — pair with Topic #19.

📰 Current Relevance — What Changed in the Window
  • Bharat Ratna vacuum: 2025 and 2026 (up to 15 Jul) saw no Bharat Ratna announcement — the 2024 set of five is the freshest; Bihar's Karpoori Thakur is therefore the "latest Bharat Ratna from Bihar" answer for this exam.
  • Padma momentum: Bihar went 7 names (2025) → 3 names (2026); 2025 was the bigger haul with two top-tier honours (Vibhushan + Bhushan), both posthumous.
  • Ceremony currency: Padma 2026 conferment began only on 25 May 2026 (Phase-1, 66 recipients) — late enough that "who was conferred when" is fair game for the 72nd prelims.
  • Honesty flag: districts for Hemant Kumar, Nirmala Devi, Vijay Nityanand Surishwar Ji Maharaj (2025) and Bishwa Bandhu, Gopal Ji Trivedi (2026) could not be verified from official/reliable sources; this page deliberately avoids assigning them districts.
