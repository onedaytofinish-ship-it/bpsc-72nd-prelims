UNFCCC Conference of the Parties (COP) — Climate Summits

The Conference of the Parties (COP) is the supreme decision-making body of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), established in **1992** at the Rio Earth Summit. COP meets annually to assess progress in dealing with climate change.

1. COP28 (Dubai, United Arab Emirates — December 2023)

The UAE Consensus: Historic agreement representing the first Global Stocktake under the Paris Agreement.

Key Outcomes:

  • Transitioning Away from Fossil Fuels: First time in COP history that countries explicitly agreed to transition away from fossil fuels in energy systems, in a just, orderly, and equitable manner, accelerating action in this critical decade.
  • Loss and Damage Fund: Formally operationalized on Day 1, with initial funding pledges of over $700 million to support developing nations facing climate disasters.
  • Tripling Renewable Energy: Commitment to triple global renewable energy capacity and double the rate of energy efficiency improvements by 2030.

2. COP29 (Baku, Azerbaijan — November 2024)

The Finance COP: Centered on establishing the New Collective Quantified Goal (NCQG) on climate finance to replace the $100 billion annual target.

Key Outcomes:

  • New Climate Finance Target: Hard-fought negotiations concluded on a new goal of raising at least $300 billion annually by 2035 from developed nations to assist developing countries.
  • Article 6 Rules: Finalization of rules for international carbon market frameworks, allowing countries to trade carbon credits to meet their Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

3. COP30 (Belém, Brazil — November 2025)

The NDCs COP: Focused on the submission of updated, highly ambitious Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs 3.0) for the 2035 targets.

Key Outcomes:

  • Amazon Preservation Action Plan: Major international funding agreements finalized specifically for preserving the Amazon biome.
  • Global Decarbonization Milestones: Review of global emission trajectories to align policies with the 1.5°C Paris Agreement limit.

4. COP31 (Antalya, Turkey / Australia — November 2026)

The Regional Implementation COP: Features a unique co-presidency model where Turkey acts as the host venue and Australia directs the policy negotiations, focusing on small-island developing states (SIDS) in the Pacific.
