Key Events — Phase 1

  • Alipore Bomb Case (May 1908): revolutionaries of the Anushilan Samiti attempted to assassinate Judge Kingsford (Calcutta). The bomb mistakenly killed two European women. Aurobindo Ghosh was tried but acquitted; Barindra Ghosh was sentenced to death (later commuted to transportation for life). Khudiram Bose was hanged (August 11, 1908, age 18).
  • Muzaffarpur Bomb Case (April 30, 1908): Khudiram Bose and Prafulla Chaki threw a bomb at Muzaffarpur (Bihar) targeting Judge Kingsford (who had been transferred from Calcutta). The wrong carriage was hit. Khudiram was arrested and hanged; Chaki shot himself. This is the Bihar-specific revolutionary event.
  • Delhi-Lahore Conspiracy (1912): Rash Behari Bose threw a bomb at Viceroy Hardinge in Delhi (December 23, 1912) during his coronation durbar procession. Hardinge was injured but survived. Rash Behari Bose escaped to Japan.
🔥 Phase 2 & 3: HRA → HSRA (1924-1931)

Hindustan Republican Association (HRA, 1924)

  • Founded: 1924 by Sachindra Nath Sanyal, Ram Prasad Bismil, and others.
  • Manifesto: "The Revolutionary" — outlined the goal of an independent republican India through armed revolution.
  • Kakori Conspiracy (August 9, 1925): Ram Prasad Bismil, Ashfaqulla Khan, Chandrashekhar Azad, and others held up a train at Kakori (near Lucknow, UP) and looted the government treasury. 4 were hanged (Bismil, Ashfaqulla, Roshan Singh, Rajendra Lahiri).

Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA, 1928)

  • Founded: 1928 at Ferozeshah Kotla (Delhi) by Bhagat Singh, Chandrashekhar Azad, and others. It evolved from the HRA — the name change reflected the socialist orientation (inspired by the Russian Revolution).
  • Saunders Assassination (December 17, 1928): Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Azad assassinated J.P. Saunders (Assistant Superintendent of Police, Lahore) in retaliation for the death of Lala Lajpat Rai (who died of injuries from a police lathi-charge during anti-Simon Commission protests, October 1928).
  • Assembly Bomb Case (April 8, 1929): Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly (Delhi), shouting "Inquilab Zindabad!" The bombs were not meant to kill — they were deliberate and theatrical ("to make the deaf hear"). They courted arrest to use the trial as a platform for revolutionary propaganda.
  • Jatindra Nath Das's hunger strike: Jatindra Nath Das died on September 13, 1929, after a 63-day hunger strike in Lahore Jail, protesting against the inhuman treatment of political prisoners. His death generated nationwide protests.
  • Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, Sukhdev hanged: March 23, 1931, in Lahore Jail (for the Saunders assassination). Bhagat Singh was 23 years old.
  • Azad's death: February 27, 1931, at Alfred Park, Allahabad. Surrounded by police, he shot himself with the last bullet (his pledge: never to be captured alive).
⚔️ Phase 4: Chittagong & INA (1930-1945)

Chittagong Armoury Raid (April 18, 1930)

  • Leader: Surya Sen ("Masterda") — a schoolteacher in Chittagong, Bengal.
  • Action: 65 revolutionaries captured the armoury, police armoury, and telephone/telegraph offices. They declared Chittagong independent for four days. The revolutionaries retreated to the Jalalabad hills, where a battle with the British left 12 revolutionaries dead.
  • Surya Sen's fate: arrested in 1933 (betrayed by a associate), tortured, and hanged on January 12, 1934.
  • Women revolutionaries: Pritilata Waddedar (led the Pahartali European Club attack, September 1932, consumed cyanide, age 21) and Kalpana Datta (arrested for manufacturing bombs).

Azad Hind Fauj (INA, 1942-1945)

  • Formation: 1942, Singapore. First President: Rash Behari Bose. Initially formed by Captain Mohan Singh (from Indian POWs captured by Japan in WWII).
  • Subhash Chandra Bose: took over the INA in 1943. He escaped from India on January 17, 1941 (disguised as Pathan "Ziauddin"), traveled through Peshawar, Kabul, and Germany (where he formed the Free India Centre and the Indian Legion), before reaching Japan by submarine. He arrived in Singapore in July 1943.
  • INA's war: the INA fought alongside the Japanese army against the British in Burma and India (1944). The INA's most famous battles were at Arakan, Imphal, and Kohima (1944). The INA was defeated when Japan surrendered (August 1945).
  • Bose's slogans: "Give me blood, I shall give you freedom" (1944, Burma); "Dilli Chalo" (On to Delhi); "Jai Hind."
  • Bose's death: August 18, 1945, in a plane crash at Taipei (Taiwan). The circumstances remain debated — multiple commissions of inquiry have investigated.
  • INA trials (1945-1946): the Red Fort trials of INA officers (Shah Nawaz Khan, Gurbaksh Singh Dhillon, Prem Sahgal) generated massive public sympathy and contributed to the naval mutiny of 1946.
🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
Muzaffarpur Bomb Case (April 30, 1908):
  • The Muzaffarpur bomb case is the Bihar-specific revolutionary event. Khudiram Bose and Prafulla Chaki threw a bomb at Muzaffarpur (Bihar) targeting Judge Kingsford (who had been transferred from Calcutta to Muzaffarpur).
  • The bomb hit the wrong carriage — killing Mrs. Kennedy and her daughter (wife and daughter of barrister Pringle Kennedy).
  • Khudiram Bose was arrested at Muzaffarpur railway station (he was 18 years old). He was tried and hanged on August 11, 1908 — one of the youngest revolutionaries to be martyred.
  • Prafulla Chaki shot himself to avoid arrest while fleeing from Muzaffarpur.
Bihar's Limited Revolutionary Role:
  • Bihar's main contribution to the freedom movement was through the Gandhian movements (Champaran 1917, Quit India 1942) rather than the revolutionary movement.
  • The revolutionary movement was primarily based in Bengal (Anushilan, Jugantar, Surya Sen), Punjab (Bhagat Singh, HSRA), and North India (Kakori, HRA).
  • Bihar's one revolutionary event — the Muzaffarpur bomb case — is important for BPSC exams because of its Bihar connection.
