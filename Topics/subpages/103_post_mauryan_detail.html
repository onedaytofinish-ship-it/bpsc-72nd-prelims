🏛️ Post-Mauryan Period — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Shungas, Indo-Greeks, Shakas, Kushanas, Satavahanas, art schools, Bihar connections, 7 exam predictions

📖 Bihar — Shunga Capital
Bihar's Post-Mauryan connection:
  • Pataliputra (Patna): the Shungas continued to rule from Pataliputra (Bihar) — the former Mauryan capital. Pushyamitra Shunga (c. 185 BCE) ruled from here.
  • Brahminical revival: Pushyamitra, a Brahmin, revived Vedic traditions (Ashvamedha) in the heartland of Buddhism (Bihar) — a cultural shift from Ashoka's Buddhism.
  • Buddhism continued: Bharhut Stupa (Shunga period) shows Buddhist art still patronised. Nalanda University would be founded later (5th c. CE, Gupta period) in Bihar.
  • After Shungas: Bihar came under the Guptas (c. 320 CE) — who made Pataliputra their capital, beginning the 'Golden Age.' Kushanas did NOT directly rule Bihar.

🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Shungas + Pushyamitra (HIGH): 185 BCE. Pataliputra. Brahminical revival. Ashvamedha. Bharhut Stupa. Patanjali.
P2 Kanishka + Kushanas (HIGH): 78 CE. Saka Era (National Calendar). Fourth Council (Kashmir). Gandhara + Mathura art. Silk Route.
P3 Indo-Greeks + Menander (MEDIUM): Milinda. Milinda Panha. First gold coins with portraits. Bilingual legends.
P4 Satavahanas (MEDIUM): Deccan. Gautamiputra Satakarni. Matronymics. Lead/potin coins. Both Buddhist + Hindu.
P5 Shakas + Rudradaman (MEDIUM): Gujarat + Malwa. Junagadh inscription (150 CE, first long Sanskrit). Sudarshana Lake.
P6 Gandhara vs Mathura art (LOW): Gandhara (Greco-Roman, schist, Buddhist only). Mathura (indigenous, red sandstone, all three religions).
P7 Bihar = Shunga capital (LOW): Shungas ruled from Pataliputra. Brahminical revival in Buddhist heartland. After Shungas → Guptas (320 CE).
