🏅 Core Concepts — How India's Civilian Honours Work
  • Instituted 1954: the Padma awards (and the Bharat Ratna) were created in 1954 and are announced every year on the eve of Republic Day (25 January) by the Ministry of Home Affairs.
  • Three classes, in descending order: Padma Vibhushan ("exceptional and distinguished service") → Padma Bhushan ("distinguished service of a high order") → Padma Shri ("distinguished service"). Civilian order overall: Bharat Ratna > Padma Vibhushan > Padma Bhushan > Padma Shri.
  • Who decides: nominations (open to the public, online) are screened by the Padma Awards Committee, headed by the Cabinet Secretary; the Home Secretary and 4-6 eminent persons sit on it; recommendations go to the PM and then the President.
  • Eligibility: any person without distinction of race, occupation, position or sex; government servants are NOT eligible — except doctors and scientists; posthumous awards are permitted.
  • Conferment: by the President of India at Rashtrapati Bhavan, typically March–May, often in two or more phases. The award is a medallion + certificate (Sanad) — no cash, and it cannot be used as a title (Article 18(1)).
  • Bharat Ratna statics: instituted 1954; first recipients C. Rajagopalachari, S. Radhakrishnan, C.V. Raman; statutory cap of 3 per year (2024's five was exceptional); none announced in 2025 or 2026 (up to 15 Jul 2026); last set (2024) included Bihar's Karpoori Thakur (posth.).
BPSC ANGLE 70th BPSC asked a straight "recipient of Bharat Ratna for 20.." one-liner. The exam's favourite statics here: instituted year (1954), max-3-per-year cap, first recipients, and the doctors-and-scientists exception to the no-government-servants rule.

🧠 Mnemonic — 2026 Vibhushan "D-K-N-P-V"
Dharmendra (Art, posth.) • K.T. Thomas (Public Affairs) • N. Rajam (Art) • P. Narayanan (Lit & Edu) • V.S. Achuthanandhan (Public Affairs, posth.) — "2 Art + 2 Public Affairs + 1 Lit", and 3 of 5 from Kerala. 2025's seven ran "1-1-3-1-1" by field (Medicine / Public Affairs / Art×3 / Lit / Trade).
🧠 Mnemonic — Totals "139 ↔ 131, Shri stays 113"
2025 = 139 (7-19-113); 2026 = 131 (5-13-113). Vibhushan fell 7→5, Bhushan fell 19→13, Shri constant at 113. If an option pairs 131 with 2025 or 139 with 2026, reject it instantly.
  • 25 Jan ritual: both lists came on R-Day eve — a date BPSC attaches to "Padma Awards" statement questions (NOT 26 Jan itself).
  • Ceremony drift: 2026 Phase-1 was 25 May 2026 (66 recipients) — later than the usual March–April window; a fresh "what happened in 2026" hook.
  • Static traps to rehearse: Padma ≠ title (Art. 18(1)); no cash component; govt servants barred except doctors & scientists; committee headed by Cabinet Secretary (not Home Secretary); Bharat Ratna cap 3/year (2024's 5 exceptional).
