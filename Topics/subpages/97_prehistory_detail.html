🗿 Prehistory — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Paleolithic → Mesolithic → Neolithic → Chalcolithic → Iron Age. Key sites, Bihar connection (Chirand), 7 exam predictions

📖 The Stone Ages — Deep Dive

1. Paleolithic Age (Old Stone Age, ~2M - 10,000 BCE)

  • Meaning: 'Paleolithic' = Old Stone Age (Greek: palaios = old, lithos = stone)
  • Duration: ~2 million years ago to ~10,000 BCE — the longest period of human history
  • Subdivisions: Lower Paleolithic (hand axes, cleavers), Middle Paleolithic (flakes, scrapers), Upper Paleolithic (blades, bone tools)
  • Lifestyle: nomadic hunter-gatherers; no agriculture; no domestication; lived in caves and rock shelters
  • Tools: rough stone — hand axes, cleavers, flakes, choppers (made of quartzite, flint, chert)
  • Art: cave paintings and rock art (Bhimbetka, MP)
  • Key sites: Bhimbetka (MP), Attirampakkam (Tamil Nadu), Narmada valley (Homo erectus fossil, Hathnora)

2. Mesolithic Age (Middle Stone Age, ~10,000 - 6,000 BCE)

  • Meaning: 'Mesolithic' = Middle Stone Age (Greek: mesos = middle)
  • Duration: ~10,000 BCE to ~6,000 BCE — a transitional period
  • Climate: end of the last Ice Age; warmer climate; more diverse vegetation and fauna
  • Tools: microliths — small stone tools (1-5 cm): arrowheads, blades, points, lunates, triangles. Made from flint, chert, agate. Often hafted onto wood/bone handles as composite tools.
  • Lifestyle: hunter-gatherers but more diversified; beginning of animal domestication (dogs, goats); semi-nomadic
  • Key sites: Bagor (Rajasthan), Adamgarh (MP), Bhimbetka (MP — later paintings), Teri (Tamil Nadu)

3. Neolithic Age (New Stone Age, ~6,000 - 2,500 BCE)

  • Meaning: 'Neolithic' = New Stone Age (Greek: neos = new)
  • Duration: ~6,000 BCE to ~2,500 BCE
  • Key features: (1) Agriculture — wheat, barley, rice; (2) Domestication of animals (cattle, sheep, goats); (3) Settled village life; (4) Polished stone tools (axes, celts); (5) Pottery (hand-made → wheel-made); (6) Burial of the dead
  • Neolithic Revolution: the transition from hunting-gathering to farming — the most important revolution in human history (term by Gordon Childe). Led to: settled life, population growth, surplus food, specialisation, urbanisation.
  • Key sites: Mehrgarh (Balochistan, ~7000 BCE — earliest Neolithic), Burzahom (Kashmir, ~3000 BCE — pit dwellings), Chirand (Bihar, ~2000 BCE), Belan valley (UP)

4. Chalcolithic Age (Copper-Stone Age, ~2,500 - 700 BCE)

  • Meaning: 'Chalcolithic' = Copper-Stone Age (Greek: chalkos = copper, lithos = stone)
  • Duration: ~2,500 BCE to ~700 BCE
  • Key features: (1) Copper tools (axes, chisels, bangles) alongside stone tools; (2) Rural farming communities; (3) Black-and-Red Ware (BRW) pottery — black interior, red exterior; (4) Cultivation of cereals (rice, barley, millets); (5) Burial practices
  • Limitations: copper is soft — cannot be used for heavy tools; stone tools remained in use; no writing; rural communities (not urban — the urban centres were the IVC)
  • Key sites: Ahar (Rajasthan), Malwa (MP), Jorwe (Maharashtra), Chirand (Bihar), Inamgaon (Maharashtra)

5. Iron Age (~1,000 BCE onwards)

  • Period: ~1,000 BCE (some say 1,200 BCE)
  • Impact: iron ploughshares revolutionised agriculture; iron weapons changed warfare; second urbanisation (after IVC decline)
  • Associated with: Painted Grey Ware (PGW) culture; later Vedic period; rise of Mahajanapadas (600 BCE)
  • Key sites: Hastinapur, Atranjikhera, Ahichchhatra (UP)
🧠 Mnemonic — P-M-N-C-I:
Paleolithic (hunting) → Mesolithic (microliths) → Neolithic (farming) → Chalcolithic (copper) → Iron Age.
"PMNCI: the five stages from stone to iron."
🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
Chirand — The Neolithic Site on the Ganga:
  • Location: Saran district, Bihar, on the northern bank of the Ganga, near the confluence of the Ganga and the Ghaghra.
  • Period: continuous occupation from the Neolithic to the Chalcolithic period (~2,000 BCE onwards).
  • Key findings: (1) Neolithic stone tools (axes, celts); (2) Chalcolithic copper tools; (3) pottery (red ware, black-and-red ware); (4) bone tools (harpoons, needles); (5) evidence of rice cultivation and animal domestication; (6) burial sites.
  • Significance: the most important prehistoric site in Bihar. Shows that the Ganga valley in Bihar was inhabited by farming communities from the Neolithic period. Earliest evidence of settled life in Bihar.
Other prehistoric sites in Bihar:
  • Senuar: Neolithic site in Rohtas district, Bihar.
  • Chechar-Kutubpur: Chalcolithic site in Patna district, Bihar.
  • Maner: Chalcolithic site near Patna, Bihar.
Significance for BPSC: Bihar's prehistoric heritage is significant but less well-known than its historical heritage (Buddhist, Mauryan, Gupta). Chirand is the key Bihar prehistoric site for BPSC exams.
🎯 Exam Predictions for 72nd BPSC
P1 Stone Age chronology (HIGH): Paleolithic → Mesolithic → Neolithic → Chalcolithic → Iron Age. Know periods, key features, tools for each. PMNCI mnemonic.
P2 Key sites (MEDIUM): Bhimbetka (MP, rock paintings, UNESCO), Mehrgarh (Balochistan, earliest Neolithic ~7000 BCE), Burzahom (Kashmir, pit dwellings), Chirand (Bihar, Neolithic-Chalcolithic).
P3 Bihar — Chirand (MEDIUM): Saran district, on the Ganga. Most important prehistoric site in Bihar. Stone + copper tools, pottery, rice cultivation, bone tools. Continuous occupation from Neolithic to Chalcolithic.
P4 Neolithic Revolution (LOW): Transition from hunting-gathering to farming (~6,000 BCE). Term by Gordon Childe. Mehrgarh = earliest evidence. Led to settled life, population growth, urbanisation.
P5 Iron Age + PGW (LOW): Iron Age ~1,000 BCE. PGW marks the transition. Later Vedic period. Second urbanisation. Sites: Hastinapur, Atranjikhera.
P6 Bhimbetka (LOW): UNESCO World Heritage Site (2003). 750+ rock shelters. Paintings depict hunting, dancing, animals, daily life. Spanning Paleolithic to Mesolithic.
P7 First metal + pottery (LOW): Copper = first metal (Chalcolithic). BRW = Chalcolithic pottery. Bronze = IVC. Iron ~1,000 BCE. PGW = Iron Age. NBPW = Mauryan.
