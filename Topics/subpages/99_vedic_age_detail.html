🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
Videha (Mithila) — Centre of Upanishadic Philosophy
  • Videha (Mithila): a Later Vedic kingdom in modern north Bihar (Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi). Ruled by King Janaka — philosopher-king.
  • King Janaka hosted Upanishadic debates — Yajnavalkya vs Gargi (Brihadaranyaka Upanishad). Gargi asked about Brahman. Shows Bihar was a centre of philosophical learning.
  • Mithila = birthplace of Sita (Ramayana). Daughter of King Janaka.
  • Later: Videha absorbed by Vajji confederacy (republic, capital Vaishali) → then by Magadha (most powerful Mahajanapada).
🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Four Vedas (HIGH): Rig (oldest, 1028 hymns, 10 mandalas), Sama (chants), Yajur (rituals), Atharva (magic, latest). 4 parts: Samhita → Brahmana → Aranyaka → Upanishad.
P2 Early vs Later Vedic (HIGH): Early: pastoral, Punjab, Rig Veda, tribe, Sabha/Samiti, women high. Later: agricultural, Ganga doab, 4 Vedas, janapada, king absolute, varna rigid, women declined.
P3 Gods + Rta (MEDIUM): Indra (250+ hymns, most), Agni (fire), Varuna (Rta/cosmic order), Soma (elixir). Later: Prajapati (creator). Rta → Dharma.
P4 Bihar — Videha (MEDIUM): King Janaka of Mithila (north Bihar). Upanishadic debates (Yajnavalkya vs Gargi). Brihadaranyaka Upanishad. Mithila = Sita's birthplace. Later → Vajji → Magadha.
P5 Purusha Sukta + varna (LOW): Rig Veda 10th mandala: varnas from Purusha — mouth=Brahmin, arms=Kshatriya, thighs=Vaishya, feet=Shudra. Earliest varna reference.
P6 Iron Age (LOW): Iron NOT known in Early Vedic. ~1000 BCE (Later Vedic). Revolutionised agriculture + warfare. Enabled Ganga valley expansion.
P7 Upanishads (LOW): Vedanta. 108 Upanishads. Brahman, Atman, Karma, Moksha, Samsara. 800-500 BCE. Foundation of Hindu philosophy + Buddhism/Jainism.
