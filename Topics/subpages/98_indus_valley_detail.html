🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
Bihar — NOT part of the IVC
  • The IVC did NOT extend to Bihar. The civilisation was concentrated in the Indus Valley (Pakistan), Gujarat, Rajasthan, Haryana, and Punjab.
  • The easternmost IVC site is Alamgirpur (Uttar Pradesh, near Meerut) — about 500 km west of Bihar.
  • Bihar was not part of the urban IVC — it had its own Chalcolithic culture (Chirand) and later the Iron Age culture that led to the rise of the Mahajanapadas (Magadha, c. 600 BCE).
  • Bihar entered the historical period with the rise of the Mahajanapadas — particularly Magadha (capital: Rajgir → Pataliputra) — which became the most powerful kingdom in ancient India.
  • For BPSC: remember that Bihar was NOT part of the IVC. This is a potential exam trick question.
🎯 Exam Predictions for 72nd BPSC
P1 Discovery + dates (HIGH): Harappa (1921, Sahni), Mohenjodaro (1922, Banerjee), John Marshall (announced 1924). Period: 2500-1750 BCE. Know who, when, where.
P2 Town planning (HIGH): Grid pattern; citadel + lower town; brick ratio 4:2:1; drainage system. GBDS mnemonic. Most planned cities of the ancient world.
P3 Key artefacts (MEDIUM): Dancing Girl (Mohenjodaro, bronze, lost-wax), Pashupati Seal (yogic figure, proto-Shiva?), Priest-King, Unicorn Seal (60%), Great Bath (ritual bathing).
P4 Key sites (MEDIUM): Lothal (port, dockyard), Kalibangan (fire altars, ploughed fields), Dholavira (reservoirs, UNESCO 2021), Rakhigarhi (largest, DNA), Chanhudaro (bead factory).
P5 Decline (LOW): Multiple factors — climate change (Saraswati drying), river shifts, Aryan invasion (contested), trade decline. Gradual decline (1900-1750 BCE). Most accepted: climate change + river shifts.
P6 Trade + cotton (LOW): Traded with Mesopotamia ('Meluhha'). IVC = first to produce cotton ('sindon'). Lothal port. Carnelian beads (Chanhudaro).
P7 Bihar + script (LOW): Bihar NOT part of IVC (easternmost = Alamgirpur, UP). Script undeciphered (pictographic, 400-600 signs, right to left).
