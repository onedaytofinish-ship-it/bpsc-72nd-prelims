EXAM TRAP "Plassey led directly to the Diwani" — FALSE. Plassey gave the EIC influence; Buxar gave the Diwani. The Diwani was granted by Shah Alam II in 1765 after the Treaty of Allahabad, which was signed after Buxar (1764), NOT after Plassey (1757). BPSC tests this distinction in statement-based questions.
📜 The Treaty of Allahabad (1765) — Full Terms
SignatoryWhat they gaveWhat they received
Shah Alam II (Mughal Emperor)Diwani of Bengal-Bihar-Orissa to EICKara and Allahabad districts; ₹26 lakh/year tribute from EIC
Shuja-ud-Daulah (Awadh)₹50 lakh war indemnity; ceded Banaras to EICRetained Awadh as a buffer state; EIC alliance
Mir Jafar (Bengal)₹50 lakh to EIC; retained only NizamatReinstated as Nawab (figurehead under Dual System)
  • Key provision: the Diwani gave the EIC the right to collect revenue (land revenue, customs, excise) and administer civil justice in Bengal, Bihar, and Orissa.
  • What the Nawab retained: Nizamat — criminal justice, police, and military (funded by a fixed allowance from the EIC).
  • Annual tribute: the EIC agreed to pay ₹26 lakh per year to Shah Alam II as the price for the Diwani — a symbolic acknowledgment of Mughal sovereignty.
  • Buffer state: Awadh was kept as a buffer between EIC Bengal and the Marathas/Rohillas — a strategic decision that shaped North Indian politics for decades.
⚖️ The Dual System of Government (1765–1772)
PowerHeld byWhat it means
Diwani (revenue + civil justice)EICCollect all taxes, administer civil courts, manage land revenue
Nizamat (criminal justice + police)Nawab (puppet)Maintain law and order, run criminal courts — but funded by EIC allowance
  • The problem: the EIC had the revenue (money) but no responsibility for governance; the Nawab had the responsibility (law and order) but no revenue to fund it. Result: administrative collapse.
  • The Bengal Famine of 1770: killed approximately 1 crore people (~1/3 of Bengal's population). The EIC continued collecting revenue at full rates even during the famine. Warren Hastings reported that revenue collection actually increased in 1771 compared to 1768.
  • Abolition: Warren Hastings abolished the Dual System in 1772 and assumed direct control of both Diwani and Nizamat. The EIC was now the de jure ruler of Bengal.
🟠 Bihar under the Dual System: Bihar (as part of the Bengal subah) was under EIC Diwani from 1765. The EIC's revenue collection in Bihar focused on land revenue, saltpetre, opium, and indigo. The Patna Opium Factory (1780) was later established on this revenue foundation — Bihar's opium became the EIC's most profitable commodity, exported to China. The Munger arsenal (Mir Qasim's former military base) was repurposed by the EIC as a military depot.
🔶 Bihar Connections — Deep Dive
1. Buxar — the battlefield is in Bihar:
Buxar is a town in Bhojpur district, Bihar, on the south bank of the Ganga. The Battle of Buxar (22 October 1764) was fought here. Today, Buxar is a district HQ and a railway station on the Howrah-Delhi main line. The battlefield is near the village of Dadar, about 5 km from Buxar town.
2. Munger (Monghyr) — Mir Qasim's fortified capital:
Mir Qasim shifted his capital from Murshidabad to Munger in 1762. He built a modern arsenal, manufactured firearms, and trained soldiers in European methods. Munger's location on the Ganga, with natural defenses (hills on one side, river on the other), made it a strategic fortress. After Buxar, the EIC took over the Munger arsenal and used it as a military depot — it later became the munitions manufacturing centre of Bihar and remains an important industrial town.
3. Patna Massacre (1763):
Before Buxar, during the English-Mir Qasim conflict, Mir Qasim's forces killed approximately 150 English and Indian prisoners at Patna in 1763 — the "Patna Massacre." This inflamed English sentiment and was one of the direct causes of the military confrontation that culminated at Buxar. Walter Reinhardt (known as "Sombre"), a European mercenary in Mir Qasim's service, was allegedly involved in this massacre.
4. Diwani included Bihar:
The Diwani of 1765 covered Bengal, Bihar, and Orissa. This meant the EIC controlled the revenue of Bihar — including the lucrative saltpetre, opium, and indigo trade. The Patna Opium Factory (1780), the Munger arsenal, and the Bhagalpur silk trade all grew on the foundation of EIC revenue control established by the Diwani.
🎯 Exam Predictions for 72nd BPSC
PREDICTION 1 Treaty of Allahabad after Buxar (HIGH): "The Treaty of Allahabad (1765) was signed after which battle?" → Buxar (1764). This is a direct 69th BPSC PYQ — the highest-probability question from this topic. Know the chain: Buxar → Allahabad → Diwani.
PREDICTION 2 Buxar combatants (HIGH): "The Battle of Buxar was fought between the English and the combined forces of —" → Mir Qasim (Bengal) + Shuja-ud-Daulah (Awadh) + Shah Alam II (Mughal Emperor). The trap: omitting one of the three or including Siraj-ud-Daulah (who was already dead since 1757).
PREDICTION 3 Bihar angle — Buxar and Munger (MEDIUM): "The Battle of Buxar was fought in which present-day Indian state?" → Bihar (Bhojpur district). "Mir Qasim's capital was at which Bihar town?" → Munger (Monghyr). Given the 71st BPSC's Bihar-heavy trend, this is exam-critical.
PREDICTION 4 Chronological ordering (MEDIUM): "Arrange: 1. Plassey 2. Buxar 3. Allahabad Treaty 4. Diwani" → 1 (1757), 2 (1764), 3 (1765), 4 (1765). The tricky part: Allahabad Treaty and Diwani are both 1765, but the treaty came first (it was the legal instrument that enabled the Diwani grant).
PREDICTION 5 Statement-based — Plassey vs Buxar (MEDIUM): "Consider: 1. Plassey was fought in 1757. 2. Mir Jafar betrayed Siraj at Plassey. 3. Plassey led directly to the Diwani." Answer: 1 and 2 only (statement 3 is FALSE — Buxar led to the Diwani, not Plassey).
PREDICTION 6 "Not correctly matched" (LOW): Likely trap: "Diwani grant — by Warren Hastings" (WRONG — by Shah Alam II). Or: "Plassey — Hector Munro" (WRONG — Clive led at Plassey, Munro at Buxar).
PREDICTION 7 Dual System / Famine 1770 (LOW): "The Dual System of Government was introduced by?" → Robert Clive (1765). "Who abolished it?" → Warren Hastings (1772). "The Bengal Famine of 1770 was caused by?" → the Dual System's revenue exploitation.
