🕌 2. Medieval Chronicles — The Same-Title Trap Zone
70th BPSC PYQ 'Tarikh-i-Firuz Shahi' was written by — options: Minhaj Siraj / Isami / Yahya Sarhindi / Shams Siraj Afif ✓. Lesson: TWO books carry this exact title — one by Ziauddin Barani (the famous one) and one by Shams Siraj Afif. If Barani is missing from the options, Afif is the key; if both appear, "More than one of the above" is the honest answer.

🏆 5. Literary Award Statics

Jnanpith Award — firsts that get asked

  • Instituted 1961 (Bharatiya Jnanpith, Sahu Jain family); first awarded 1965 — G. Sankara Kurup (Malayalam, Odakkuzhal).
  • Prize: ₹11 lakh + bronze Vagdevi (Saraswati) statuette + citation; only living Indian citizens — never posthumous.
  • First woman: Ashapurna Devi (Bengali, 1976); first Hindi: Sumitranandan Pant (1968); first English: Amitav Ghosh (2018).
  • 58th (2023): Gulzar (Urdu) + Jagadguru Rambhadracharya (Sanskrit) → 59th (2024): Vinod Kumar Shukla (Hindi) → 60th (2025): Vairamuthu (Tamil).

Booker & India

  • Booker est. 1969, £50,000. Indian winners: Salman Rushdie (1981, Midnight's Children), Arundhati Roy (1997, The God of Small Things), Kiran Desai (2006, The Inheritance of Loss), Aravind Adiga (2008, The White Tiger).
  • International Booker (single-book format since 2016): 2022 — Geetanjali Shree, Tomb of Sand, tr. Daisy Rockwell (first Hindi/Indian winner) → 2025 — Banu Mushtaq (Heart Lamp) → 2026 — Yáng Shuāng-zǐ (Taiwan Travelogue).
  • Nobel Literature: first Indian & first Asian laureate = Rabindranath Tagore, 1913 (Gitanjali); 2025 = László Krasznahorkai (Hungary).
  • World Book and Copyright Day = 23 April (UNESCO; death anniversary of Shakespeare and Cervantes, 1616).
