Peter Mitchell (Nobel 1978) proposed chemiosmotic hypothesis — applies to BOTH chloroplasts and mitochondria.
⚗️ Dark Reaction Pathways Deep Dive

Calvin Cycle (C₃) — Step by Step

PhaseStepDetail
1. CarboxylationRuBisCO catalyses CO₂ + RuBP (5C)→ unstable 6C intermediate → splits into 2× 3-PGA (3-phosphoglycerate, 3C). First stable product = 3-PGA.
2. Reduction3-PGA → 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate → G3PUses ATP (phosphorylation) + NADPH (reduction). 6 turns of cycle fix 6 CO₂ → 12 G3P; 2 G3P exit to make 1 glucose, 10 regenerate RuBP.
3. RegenerationG3P → RuBPComplex sugar shuffling; uses 3 ATP per CO₂. Maintains the cycle.
  • Energy budget (per glucose): 18 ATP + 12 NADPH consumed (i.e., 3 ATP + 2 NADPH per CO₂ fixed). The ATP & NADPH come from the light reaction.
  • RuBisCO affinity: Km for CO₂ is high (low affinity) — hence slowest enzyme. Also accepts O₂ → photorespiration. C₄ plants boost CO₂ at RuBisCO to suppress this.

C₄ Pathway (Hatch-Slack) — Detailed

StepCell TypeReaction
1. Initial CO₂ fixationMesophyll cellPEP carboxylase (PEPC): PEP (3C) + CO₂ → oxaloacetate (4C). PEPC has high CO₂ affinity, no oxygenase activity.
2. TransportMesophyll → bundle sheathOxaloacetate → malate (or aspartate); transported through plasmodesmata.
3. DecarboxylationBundle sheath cell (Kranz)Malate → pyruvate + CO₂ (released). CO₂ enters Calvin cycle (RuBisCO). Bundle sheath chloroplasts are agranal (less PS-II, less O₂).
4. RegenerationMesophyllPyruvate → PEP (uses 2 ATP, via pyruvate-PI-dikinase). This "CO₂ pump" costs 2 ATP per CO₂ — the price of suppressing photorespiration.
  • Kranz anatomy: wreath-like arrangement — mesophyll cells surround vascular bundle, then a ring of large bundle-sheath cells with large chloroplasts. Characteristic of C₄ grasses (maize, sugarcane, sorghum).
  • C₄ energy budget: 12 ATP extra (per glucose) vs C₃ = 30 ATP total. But no photorespiration loss → net gain at high temperature & light.

CAM Pathway — Detailed

TimeStomataProcess
NightOPENCO₂ enters → PEPC fixes to oxaloacetate → malic acid → stored in vacuole (tissue becomes acidic at night)
DayCLOSEDMalic acid decarboxylated → CO₂ released internally → Calvin cycle (with light). No new CO₂ enters (stomata closed) → minimal transpiration.
  • Key difference C₄ vs CAM: C₄ = SPATIAL separation (mesophyll vs bundle sheath, simultaneously). CAM = TEMPORAL separation (night vs day, in same cell). Both use PEPC + Calvin cycle.
  • Water-use efficiency: CAM > C₄ > C₃. Productivity: C₄ > C₃ > CAM (CAM is slowest, hence desert succulents grow slowly).
🔋 Respiration Deep Dive

Glycolysis (EMP) — Key Steps

  • Phase 1 — Energy investment: Glucose → glucose-6-P → fructose-6-P → fructose-1,6-bisP (uses 2 ATP). Split into DHAP + G3P (3C each).
  • Phase 2 — Energy payoff: G3P → 1,3-BPG → 3-PGA (2 ATP each, ×2 = 4 ATP) → 2-PGA → PEP → pyruvate (2 NADH). Net: 2 ATP + 2 NADH + 2 pyruvate.
  • Regulation: Phosphofructokinase (PFK) is the rate-limiting enzyme; inhibited by ATP, stimulated by AMP.

Phloem Translocation — Pressure Flow Details

  • Source: mature leaf (photosynthesis) or storage organ (during germination — tuber becomes source). Sucrose synthesised → loaded into sieve tubes via apoplast/symplast (companion cells provide ATP for active loading against gradient).
  • Sink: growing roots, fruits, seeds, young leaves, storage organs. Sucrose unloaded (used for respiration or stored as starch).
  • Flow: high turgor at source → low turgor at sink → bulk flow of phloem sap (sucrose + amino acids + hormones). Bidirectional (different sieve tubes can flow in different directions).
  • Evidence: aphid stylet technique — aphid pierces sieve tube, exuding sap collected proves sucrose transport & flow rates (~1 m/hr, far faster than diffusion).
🌱 Plant Growth & Hormones Deep Dive

Plant Growth Phases

  • Three phases of growth (logistic curve): (1) Lag phase (slow), (2) Log/exponential phase (fast), (3) Senescence/stationary phase. Growth rate plotted → sigmoid curve.
  • Phases of cellular growth: Cell division (meristem, mitosis) → Cell elongation (enlargement, vacuole expansion — auxin-driven) → Cell maturation/differentiation.
  • Primary growth: apical meristems (height). Secondary growth: lateral meristems — vascular cambium (secondary xylem/phloem) + cork cambium (periderm/bark) → girth (dicots only; monocots lack secondary growth).

• P680=PS-II, P700=PS-I (numbers match)
• C₃ = Cool Climate (Rice, Wheat, Potato, Soybean); C₄ = Hot Hardy (Maize, Sugarcane, Sorghum, Pearl millet)
• "All Good Crops Eat Apples" — Auxin, Gibberellin, Cytokinin, Ethylene, ABA
