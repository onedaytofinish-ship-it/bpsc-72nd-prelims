Budget Concepts, Fiscal Vocabulary & Bihar Statics
📖 A. The Budget in the Constitution — Core Concepts
  • Article 202: the Annual Financial Statement (AFS) — the "budget" — must be laid before the State Legislature every financial year. The Union equivalent is Article 112.
  • Article 203: estimates charged on the Consolidated Fund of the State are NOT voted (e.g., Governor's emoluments, High Court judges' salaries, debt charges); other expenditure is voted as Demands for Grants.
  • Article 204 / 205 / 206: Appropriation Bill; supplementary/additional/excess grants; votes on account, votes of credit and exceptional grants.
  • Article 266: Consolidated Fund of the State (all revenues + loans) and Public Account (provident funds, small savings etc.). Contingency Fund of the State — Article 267(2).
  • Article 280: Finance Commission every 5th year — recommends tax devolution between Centre and states and among states.
  • Financial year: 1 April → 31 March. The state budget speech follows the Economic Survey tabling.

⚖️ C. Finance Commissions & Bihar's Share
  • 14th FC (2015-20, Chair Y.V. Reddy): raised states' share of the divisible pool from 32% to 42% — the big structural jump that swelled every state budget including Bihar's.
  • 15th FC (2020-21 interim + 2021-26 final, Chair N.K. Singh): kept 41% (1% cut for J&K reorganisation); Bihar's share ≈ 10.06%.
  • 16th FC (award 2026-31; report tabled 1 Feb 2026; Chair Arvind Panagariya): Bihar 9.95% — down from 10.06% but still the 2nd highest after Uttar Pradesh (17.62%). Bihar grants 2026-31: rural local bodies ₹51,923 cr; urban local bodies ₹9,169 cr; disaster management ₹13,615 cr; Patna wastewater-infra grant up to ₹5,000 cr.
  • Exam trap: "increased to 10.06%" is wrong — 10.06% was the OLD (15th FC) share; the change is a decrease.

🗳️ E. Bihar Budget Presenters & Milestones
  • 2024-25 & 2025-26 budgets: presented by Samrat Chaudhary (BJP) as Dy CM & Finance Minister — the 70th BPSC asked the 2024-25 presenter question verbatim.
  • 2026-27 budget (3 Feb 2026): presented by Bijendra Prasad Yadav (JDU) — FM of the post-election Nitish Kumar government sworn in on 20 Nov 2025.
  • Budget-size milestones: 2005-06 ₹22,568 cr → 2024-25 BE ₹2,78,725.72 cr → 2025-26 ₹3,16,895.02 cr → 2026-27 ₹3,47,589.76 cr (largest ever; ~15× in two decades).
  • Constancy worth memorising: education has been the single largest sectoral allocation in successive Bihar budgets (FY26 ₹60,964.87 cr = 19.24%; FY27 ₹68,216 cr = 19.63%).
  • First budget after the Apr 2026 transition: Samrat Chaudhary became Bihar's first BJP CM on 15 Apr 2026 — AFTER the FY27 budget was presented, so the FY27 budget belongs to the Nitish-led government. Date-order traps are likely.
🧠 Mnemonic — Article ladder "112 Union, 202 State, 280 FC"
Budget Articles: 112 = Union AFS; 202 = State AFS; 203 = charged-vs-voted; 266 = Consolidated Fund; 267 = Contingency Fund; 280 = Finance Commission. Deficit trio: Revenue (recurring gap), Fiscal (borrowing), Primary (fiscal minus interest) — "RFP: Really Fiscal Problems".
