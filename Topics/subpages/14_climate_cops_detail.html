Climate COPs — COP28 → COP30 Decisions Comparison (plus COP31/32)

The Conference of the Parties (COP) is the supreme decision-making body of the UNFCCC (United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change), adopted at the 1992 Rio Earth Summit, in force since 1994, with 198 parties. COP meets annually; its two landmark legal instruments are the Kyoto Protocol (1997, COP3) and the Paris Agreement (adopted 12 Dec 2015, COP21).

2. What Came Before — The Ladder to Dubai

  • COP21 Paris 2015: Paris Agreement adopted 12 Dec 2015 — hold warming "well below 2 °C", pursue 1.5 °C; NDCs every 5 years; net-zero balance in the second half of the century. India's own net-zero year: 2070.
  • COP26 Glasgow 2021: Glasgow Climate Pact — "phase-down of unabated coal"; India announced the Panchamrit: 500 GW non-fossil capacity by 2030, 50% energy from renewables by 2030, 1 bn-tonne CO₂ reduction by 2030, 45% emissions-intensity cut by 2030, net-zero 2070.
  • COP27 Sharm el-Sheikh 2022: historic agreement to set up the Loss & Damage Fund (operationalised a year later at Dubai).

3. What Comes Next — COP31 & COP32

  • COP31 (Nov 2026): Antalya, Türkiye — the venue host; Australia serves as "President of Negotiations" under a unique compromise, with a pre-COP leaders' event in the Pacific (Australia had bid to host with Pacific island nations).
  • COP32 (2027): Ethiopia — the first Least Developed Country (LDC) to host a climate COP.
