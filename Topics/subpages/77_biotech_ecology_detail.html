StepProcessKey Enzyme/ToolDetails
1. IsolationExtract target gene & vector DNARestriction enzymesCut DNA at specific palindromic sites (e.g., EcoRI: GAATTC). Produces sticky ends or blunt ends
2. LigationInsert gene into vectorDNA ligaseJoins sticky ends of gene & vector → recombinant DNA (rDNA). Vector = plasmid, phage, BAC
3. TransformationInsert rDNA into host cellHeat shock / electroporationHost = usually E. coli. Only some cells take up the vector
4. SelectionIdentify transformed cellsSelectable markers (antibiotic resistance)Cells with plasmid survive antibiotic (e.g., ampicillin). Blue-white screening with lacZ gene

PCR — Detailed Mechanism

StepTemperatureProcess
Denaturation94–95°CDouble-stranded DNA separates into single strands (H-bonds break)
Annealing55–65°CPrimers bind (anneal) to complementary sequences flanking the target region
Extension72°CTaq polymerase extends primers, synthesizing new DNA strands (5'→3')
  • Each cycle doubles the DNA → after 30 cycles, ~1 billion copies (2³⁰).
  • Taq polymerase: from Thermus aquaticus (Yellowstone hot spring bacterium). Thermostable — survives 95°C denaturation. Replaces ordinary polymerase that would denature.
  • RT-PCR (Reverse Transcription PCR): uses reverse transcriptase to convert RNA → cDNA first, then amplify. Used for RNA virus detection (COVID-19, HIV viral load).

🌿 Ecosystem Energetics Deep Dive

Productivity Concepts

TermDefinitionFormula/Note
Gross Primary Productivity (GPP)Total energy fixed by photosynthesisTotal photosynthesis in an ecosystem
Net Primary Productivity (NPP)Energy remaining after plant respirationNPP = GPP − R (respiration). Available to consumers
Secondary ProductivityEnergy stored at consumer levelsLess than NPP (10% law applies)
Standing CropBiomass present at a given timeMeasured as dry weight per unit area
TurnoverRate of replacementHigh turnover = low standing biomass but high productivity (phytoplankton)
  • Most productive ecosystems: tropical rainforest (NPP ~2,200 g/m²/yr), estuaries, coral reefs, algal beds. Least productive: desert, tundra, open ocean (~125 g/m²/yr).
  • Why are oceans low productivity despite covering 71% of Earth? Low nutrient availability (limiting N, P, Fe in open ocean); light penetration limited to photic zone (~200 m).

🔄 Biogeochemical Cycles Deep Dive

Nitrogen Cycle — All Steps & Organisms

StepProcessOrganism(s)Conversion
1. N₂ FixationConvert atmospheric N₂ to ammonia (NH₃)Rhizobium (symbiotic, legume nodules), Azotobacter (free-living soil), Nostoc/Anabaena (cyanobacteria), Frankia (alders)N₂ → NH₃/NH₄⁺
2. NitrificationOxidize ammonia to nitrite then nitrateNitrosomonas (NH₃→NO₂⁻), Nitrobacter (NO₂⁻→NO₃⁻)NH₃ → NO₂⁻ → NO₃⁻
3. AssimilationPlants absorb nitrate/ammoniumPlants (root uptake)NO₃⁻/NH₄⁺ → organic N (amino acids)
4. AmmonificationDecompose organic N to ammoniaDecomposer bacteria & fungiOrganic N → NH₃
5. DenitrificationReturn nitrate to atmospheric N₂Pseudomonas denitrificans, ThiobacillusNO₃⁻ → N₂ (back to atmosphere)
  • Legumes & Rhizobium: symbiotic N₂ fixation in root nodules. Leghemoglobin regulates O₂ (nitrogenase enzyme is O₂-sensitive). Crop rotation with legumes (pulses) naturally fertilizes soil — relevant to Bihar's pulse cultivation.
  • Industrial Haber-Bosch process: N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃ (high pressure, Fe catalyst) — artificial fixation for fertilizers. Fritz Haber, Nobel 1918. This doubled global food production but also caused N pollution (eutrophication).

Bihar Ecology & Biotech — Detailed Connections
  • NRC Makhana, Darbhanga: tissue culture propagation, variety development (Makhana-1), disease resistance. Mithila Makhana GI tag (2022). Makhana cultivation in ponds = wetland agro-ecology.
  • Vikramshila Dolphin Sanctuary (Bhagalpur): 50 km Ganga stretch. Dolphin count surveys by KMVV (Kosi-Vikramshila). Echolocation studies. Threats: water pollution, dams, fishing nets.
  • Valmiki Tiger Reserve (West Champaran): Terai Arc Landscape. Tiger monitoring via camera traps. Prey base: chital, sambar, wild boar. Cross-border connectivity with Chitwan NP (Nepal).
  • Kanwar Lake (Begusarai): oxbow lake, ~6,000 ha. Migratory birds (Siberian crane historically, Northern Pintail, Common Teal). Threatened by encroachment & agriculture.
  • RPCAU Pusa (Samastipur): research on rice (medicinal 'Nivara', black rice), makhana, biofertilizers (Rhizobium for pulses), IPM. ICAR Research Complex, Patna — eastern region agriculture.
  • Arsenic in groundwater: Bhojpur, Buxar, Patna, Ara — Gangetic alluvium arsenic release (reducing conditions, biogeochemical process). Health impact: skin lesions, cancer. Mitigation: dug wells, arsenic removal plants.
  • Kosi floods & ecosystem: Kosi fan shifts course historically. Embankments alter sediment deposition, fish migration. Flood-prone north Bihar districts: Supaul, Saharsa, Madhepura, Khagaria.
