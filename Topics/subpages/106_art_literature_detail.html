🎨 Art and Literature — Master Detail

Complete analysis: art schools, temple architecture, key texts, Bihar connections, 7 exam predictions

📖 Bihar — Centre of Ancient Indian Art
Bihar's art heritage:
  • Ashokan Pillars (Bihar): Lauriya-Araraj, Lauriya-Nandangarh, Rampurva (West Champaran). Polished stone, animal capitals. Mauryan.
  • Barabar Caves (near Gaya): Oldest rock-cut caves in India. Donated by Ashoka to Ajivikas. Mauryan.
  • Nalanda University (Bihar): Buddhist monasteries, stupas, sculptures. Gupta-founded (5th c. CE). UNESCO (2016).
  • Mahabodhi Temple (Bodh Gaya): Originally Ashoka (250 BCE), rebuilt Gupta (4th-5th c. CE). UNESCO (2002). Bihar's most iconic monument.
  • Pataliputra: Mauryan + Gupta capital. Megasthenes described magnificent palace. Mauryan wooden palisade walls found in excavations.

🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Gandhara vs Mathura (HIGH): Gandhara (Greco-Roman, schist, Buddhist only). Mathura (indigenous, red sandstone, all 3 religions). Both Kushana period.
P2 Nagara vs Dravida (HIGH): Nagara (North, curvilinear shikhara, Gupta). Dravida (South, pyramidal vimana + gopuram, Pallava).
P3 Bihar art heritage (HIGH): Ashokan pillars, Barabar Caves (Ajivikas), Nalanda (UNESCO 2016), Mahabodhi Temple (UNESCO 2002).
P4 Key texts (MEDIUM): Arthashastra (Kautilya), Ashtadhyayi (Panini), Shakuntala (Kalidasa), Aryabhatiya (Aryabhata), Charaka/Sushruta, Silappadikaram + Thirukkural.
P5 Caves (MEDIUM): Barabar (Bihar, oldest, Ajivikas). Ajanta (Buddhist frescoes, Gupta). Ellora (Hindu+Buddhist+Jain, Kailasa Temple = largest monolithic).
P6 Stupa architecture (LOW): Sanchi (MP, Ashoka origin, Shunga enlargement, UNESCO 1989). Amaravati (Andhra, Satavahana, white marble).
P7 Iron Pillar + metallurgy (LOW): Gupta (Chandragupta II), rust-free ~1600 years. Testament to Gupta metallurgy.
