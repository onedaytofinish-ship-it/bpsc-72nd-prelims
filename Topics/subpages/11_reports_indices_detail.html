📊 Reports & Indices — Master Detail

Complete report-by-report breakdown: methodology, indicators, edition history, India figures, Bihar figures, and traps

📖 Core Concepts — What Is an Index?
  • Index: a composite score derived from multiple indicators, designed to rank countries/states on a specific dimension (development, hunger, innovation, gender parity, happiness, sustainability).
  • Why BPSC tests indices: they are one-mark factual questions — "who publishes X", "what is India's rank in Y", "which is wrongly matched". Low reasoning, high recall. 11 PYQs mapped from 69th–71st.
  • The golden rule: always learn a report as a bundle — publisher + edition year + data year + India rank/value + severity band (if any). Missing any element is a trap.
  • Edition trap: indices change country coverage, methodology, and data sources between editions. Never compare a rank from one edition with a score from another.
  • Publisher trap: many indices have similar-sounding names but different publishers (Global MPI by UNDP+OPHI vs National MPI by NITI Aayog; HDR by UNDP vs WHR by Oxford+Gallup+SDSN).

GGGR Sub-Indices (4 dimensions)

  • Economic participation and opportunity: labour force participation, wage equality, earned income
  • Educational attainment: literacy, enrolment in primary/secondary/tertiary
  • Health and survival: sex ratio at birth, healthy life expectancy
  • Political empowerment: women in parliament, ministerial positions, heads of state
69th PYQ "The Global Gender Gap Report is released by —" Answer: World Economic Forum. NOT UN Women, NOT UNDP, NOT World Bank.

WHR Methodology

  • Based on Gallup World Poll responses (Cantril life ladder: 0 = worst possible life, 10 = best possible)
  • Six key variables: GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom, generosity, corruption perception
  • Averages 3 most recent years of data
TRAP Publisher confusion: WHR is NOT published by WIPO, UNDP, or WEF. It is the Wellbeing Research Centre (Oxford) + Gallup + UN SDSN. BPSC's "wrongly matched" format tests this.
