🇮🇳 Independence and Partition — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Cabinet Mission, Mountbatten Plan, Indian Independence Act, Radcliffe Line, integration of princely states, Bihar connections, 7 exam predictions

📖 The Road to Independence (1945-1947)

Step 1: Wavell Plan & Simla Conference (1945)

  • Lord Wavell (Viceroy 1943-47) proposed an executive council with Indian members (excluding INC and ML leaders but including other representatives).
  • Simla Conference (June-July 1945) — failed because the Muslim League insisted on nominating all Muslim members. Jinnah argued that only the League represented Indian Muslims.

Step 2: INA Trials & Naval Mutiny (1945-46)

  • The Red Fort INA trials (1945-46) of officers (Shah Nawaz Khan, Gurbaksh Singh Dhillon, Prem Sahgal) generated massive public sympathy.
  • The naval mutiny (February 1946) — ratings of the Royal Indian Navy mutinied in Bombay. Supported by the INC and the Communist Party. The British realized they could no longer rely on the Indian armed forces.
  • These events convinced the British that they had to leave India soon.

Step 3: Cabinet Mission (March 1946)

  • Three-member mission: Lord Pethick-Lawrence (SoS), Sir Stafford Cripps, A.V. Alexander.
  • Proposed a united India with: (1) a weak centre (only defence, foreign affairs, communications); (2) provincial groupings (A: Hindu-majority, B: NW Muslim-majority, C: Bengal+Assam); (3) a constituent assembly; (4) an interim government.
  • INC initially accepted; ML accepted then withdrew (July 1946) and declared "Direct Action Day" (August 16, 1946).
  • Direct Action Day: massive communal violence in Calcutta ("Great Calcutta Killing") — 4,000+ killed. Violence spread to Noakhali, Bihar, and other areas.

Step 4: Interim Government (September 1946)

  • Nehru as Vice President; Liaquat Ali Khan (ML) as Finance Member.
  • A coalition of INC and ML but dysfunctional — the ML members refused to cooperate.
🇮🇳 Mountbatten Plan & Partition

Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947)

  • Lord Mountbatten (last Viceroy, March-August 1947) proposed partition after the Cabinet Mission's failure and escalating communal violence.
  • Key features: (1) partition into India and Pakistan; (2) transfer of power by August 15, 1947 (advanced from June 1948); (3) princely states free to join India, Pakistan, or remain independent; (4) Bengal and Punjab to be partitioned; (5) a boundary commission (Radcliffe) to demarcate the border.
  • Accepted by both INC (Nehru, Patel) and ML (Jinnah). Gandhi was opposed to partition but could not prevent it.

Indian Independence Act (July 18, 1947)

  • Passed by the British Parliament on July 18, 1947 (received royal assent same day).
  • Came into effect on August 15, 1947.
  • Created two independent dominions: India (Hindu-majority) and Pakistan (Muslim-majority, split into East and West Pakistan).
  • Each dominion got a Governor-General (India: Mountbatten; Pakistan: Jinnah).
  • Princely states were free to accede to either dominion or remain independent.

Radcliffe Line (August 17, 1947)

  • Sir Cyril Radcliffe, a British lawyer who had never been to India, was given 5 weeks to demarcate the boundary between India and Pakistan in Bengal and Punjab.
  • The boundary was announced on August 17, 1947 — two days AFTER independence — causing massive confusion. Millions of people did not know which country they were in.
  • Partition violence: 10-12 million people displaced; 1-2 million killed in communal violence. The largest mass migration in human history.
  • Gandhi was in Calcutta on August 15, 1947 — fasting to stop communal violence. He did not celebrate independence.

Sardar Patel integrated 562 princely states into the Indian Union — using diplomacy, persuasion, and military action. He offered each ruler a "privy purse" (annual payment) and allowed them to retain their titles. Assisted by V.P. Menon (Secretary, States Ministry). Known as the "Iron Man of India." The Statue of Unity (182m, Gujarat, 2018) is the world's tallest statue — dedicated to Patel.

🔶 Bihar Connection — Deep Dive
1. Dr. Rajendra Prasad:
  • First President of the Constituent Assembly (December 11, 1946) and first President of independent India (January 26, 1950 - May 13, 1962).
  • From Bihar (Siwan district, Ziradei village). Participated in Champaran (1917), Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India.
  • Served two terms as President (1950-1957, 1957-1962) — the only President to serve two full terms.
2. Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha:
  • First temporary President of the Constituent Assembly (December 9, 1946). From Arrah, Bihar.
  • Congress leader, educationist, and journalist. Founder of Patna University (1917). Wrote "Some Eminent Indian Contemporaries."
3. Sri Krishna Singh:
  • First Chief Minister of Bihar (1946-1961) — the longest-serving CM. "Bihar Kesari."
  • Participated in Quit India (1942). Led Bihar through the first 15 years of independence.
4. Anugrah Narayan Sinha:
  • First Deputy Chief Minister of Bihar (1946-1957). Key finance and development minister.
5. Bihar during partition:
  • Bihar was NOT affected by the Radcliffe Line — it remained part of India. Unlike Punjab and Bengal, Bihar had no Muslim-majority districts.
  • Bihar's main contribution to independence was through its leaders (Rajendra Prasad, Sachchidananda Sinha) and its participation in the freedom movements.
