👑 Gupta Empire — Master Detail

Complete analysis: Gupta rulers, Golden Age achievements, Bihar connections (Pataliputra, Nalanda), 7 exam predictions

📖 Bihar — Gupta Heartland
Bihar = Gupta heartland:
  • Pataliputra (Patna): Gupta capital — administrative and cultural centre of the 'Golden Age.' Srigupta, Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, Chandragupta II all ruled from here.
  • Nalanda University: founded by Kumargupta I (c. 427 CE) in Bihar. 10,000 students, 2,000 teachers. UNESCO (2016). Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE).
  • Gupta origin: likely from the Magadha region (Bihar). Rose from a local family to imperial power. Pataliputra had been the capital of the Mauryas and Shungas before.
  • Buddhist sites: Guptas patronised Bodh Gaya, Nalanda, Rajgir. Hindu (Vaishnavite) but tolerant of Buddhism.
  • Hiuen Tsang at Nalanda: studied 5 years at Nalanda (Bihar, c. 630-645 CE). 'Si-yu-ki' is crucial source for Nalanda and Bihar.

🎯 Exam Predictions
P1 Samudragupta + Chandragupta II (HIGH): Napoleon of India, Allahabad Pillar (Harisena), musician. Vikramaditya, Navaratna (Kalidasa), defeated Shakas (409 CE), Iron Pillar.
P2 Bihar = Gupta heartland (HIGH): Pataliputra capital. Nalanda (Kumargupta I, 427 CE). Guptas from Magadha. Golden Age centred in Bihar.
P3 Golden Age achievements (HIGH): Kalidasa, Aryabhata, Ajanta, Iron Pillar, Nalanda. Navaratna.
P4 Fa-Hien + Hiuen Tsang (MEDIUM): Fa-Hien (Chandragupta II, 405-411 CE). Hiuen Tsang (Harsha, 630-645 CE, Nalanda).
P5 Decline + Huns (MEDIUM): Huns invaded. Skandagupta defeated them. Toramana + Mihirakula (510-530 CE) broke Gupta power.
P6 Society + caste (LOW): Women's status declined (sati at Eran, 510 CE). Caste proliferated. Feudal admin (samantas, land grants).
P7 Nalanda University (LOW): Kumargupta I (427 CE, Bihar). 10,000 students. Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (~1200 CE). UNESCO (2016).
