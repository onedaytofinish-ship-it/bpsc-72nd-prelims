Whittaker's Five Criteria for Classification

  • 1. Cell structure: Prokaryotic (no nucleus/organelles — Monera) vs Eukaryotic (Protista, Fungi, Plantae, Animalia)
  • 2. Body organisation: Unicellular (Monera, Protista) vs Multicellular (Fungi, Plantae, Animalia)
  • 3. Mode of nutrition: Autotrophic (Plantae) vs Heterotrophic — saprophytic (Fungi) / ingestive (Animalia) / parasitic / mixotrophic
  • 4. Reproduction: Asexual (binary fission, budding, spores) vs Sexual; role of gametes
  • 5. Phylogenetic relationship: Evolutionary lineage / common ancestry

🍄 Kingdom Fungi Deep Dive
  • Cell wall: chitin (N-acetylglucosamine). Body = mycelium (network of hyphae). Reserve food = glycogen (like animals) & oil.
  • Nutrition: Saprophytic (decomposers — absorb dissolved organic matter) / Parasitic / Symbiotic (lichen = fungus + alga; mycorrhiza = fungus + plant root).
  • Reproduction: Asexual (spores — conidia, sporangiospores, budding in yeast) & Sexual (conjugation, ascospores, basidiospores).
  • Divisions (based on spore type): Zygomycetes (Rhizopus — bread mould, zygospores), Ascomycetes (sac fungi — yeast, Penicillium, Aspergillus; ascospores), Basidiomycetes (club fungi — mushroom, Puccinia wheat rust; basidiospores), Deuteromycetes (imperfect fungi — no sexual stage; Trichophyton ringworm).
  • Useful: Yeast (bread, beer, alcohol fermentation), Penicillium notatum (penicillin antibiotic — Fleming 1928), Aspergillus niger (citric acid), mushrooms (food).
  • Harmful: Puccinia graminis (wheat rust), Rhizopus (bread mould), Trichophyton (ringworm/athlete's foot), Candida (thrush), Aflatoxin (Aspergillus flavus on groundnut — carcinogenic).
🧠 Lichen = Fungus + Alga/Cyanobacterium: Fungus provides shelter & minerals; alga photosynthesises → food. Pioneer colonisers on bare rocks (Xerarch succession). Indicator of air pollution (SO₂ sensitive).
🌱 Kingdom Plantae — Divisions Deep Dive

Plant Evolution — Key Transitions

  • Thallophyta (Algae): Thallus body (no root/stem/leaf). Aquatic. Chlorophyll. Spirogyra, Ulothrix, Chara, Kelp (brown — largest seaweeds), Volvox. Red algae (rhodophyta — floridean starch), Brown algae (phaeophyta — alginate), Green algae (chlorophyta).
  • Bryophyta: First land plants; no vascular tissue; rhizoids; need water for fertilisation (antherozoids motile); dominant gametophyte generation. Examples: Funaria, Marchantia, Sphagnum (peat moss — fuel, used as packing material).
  • Pteridophyta: First vascular plants (xylem & phloem); first true roots/stem/leaves; need water for fertilisation (multiciliated antherozoids); dominant sporophyte. Examples: Pteris, selaginella, horsetail, ferns. Used as ornamentals.
  • Gymnosperms: First seed plants (naked seeds, no fruit); vascular; needle leaves with thick cuticle (xerophytic); wind pollination; dominant sporophyte. Examples: Pinus (pine), Cycas, Ginkgo biloba (living fossil), Gnetum.
  • Angiosperms: Flowering plants; enclosed seeds in fruit; vascular; double fertilisation (one male gamete + egg → zygote; second + central cell → endosperm); ~3 lakh species; dominant sporophyte. Divided into Monocots & Dicots.

Double Fertilisation — Unique to Angiosperms

  • Pollen tube delivers 2 male gametes to embryo sac.
  • Syngamy: Male gamete 1 + Egg → Zygote (2n) → Embryo.
  • Triple fusion: Male gamete 2 + 2 polar nuclei (n+n) → Endosperm (3n) → nutritive tissue.
  • Discovered by Sergei Nawaschin (1898). Unique to angiosperms — gymnosperms have single fertilisation.

Plant Life Cycle — Alternation of Generations

  • Gametophyte (n, haploid): produces gametes by mitosis. Dominant in Bryophytes (moss plant is gametophyte).
  • Sporophyte (2n, diploid): produces spores by meiosis. Dominant in Pteridophytes, Gymnosperms, Angiosperms (the "plant" we see is sporophyte).
  • Trend: as plants evolved, sporophyte became dominant; gametophyte reduced.

Chordata — Key Sub-groups

  • Sub-phylum Urochordata (Tunicata): Notochord only in tail of larva. E.g., Ascidia, Herdmania (sea squirt). Sedentary adults.
  • Sub-phylum Cephalochordata: Notochord from head to tail, persistent. E.g., Amphioxus / Branchiostoma (lancelet). Fish-like, marine.
  • Sub-phylum Vertebrata: Notochord replaced by vertebral column (backbone). ~65,000 species. Divided into: Agnatha (jawless — lamprey, hagfish) and Gnathostomata (jawed — Pisces, Amphibia, Reptilia, Aves, Mammalia).

Bihar Fish Diversity — Aquatic Classification

  • Major carps (family Cyprinidae): Rohu (Labeo rohita), Catla (Catla catla), Mrigal (Cirrhinus mrigala) — mainstay of inland aquaculture.
  • Migratory: Hilsa (Tenualosa ilisha) — anadromous (migrates from sea to river to spawn). Declined in Ganga after Farakka barrage. State Fish of Bihar.
  • Air-breathing catfish (can survive in low-O₂ waterlogged fields): Singhi (Heteropneustes fossilis), Magur (Clarias batrachus), Koi (Anabas testudineus — climbing perch). Adapted to Bihar's flood-prone/waterlogged ponds.
  • Snakehead fish: Channa spp. (snake-like, carnivorous).
  • Bihar inland fish production: >6 lakh tonnes/year (top 5 in India). Major aquaculture districts: Madhubani, Darbhanga, Purnia, Katihar, Begusarai (Kanwar Lake — Asia's largest oxbow lake).
