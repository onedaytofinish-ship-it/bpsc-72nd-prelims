Complement System

  • Complement: ~30 plasma proteins that "complement" antibody action. Activated by: (1) Classical pathway (antigen-antibody complex), (2) Alternative pathway (microbial surfaces), (3) Lectin pathway (mannose-binding lectin).
  • Functions: Opsonisation (coat pathogens → easier phagocytosis), chemotaxis (attract WBCs), MAC (Membrane Attack Complex → lyse pathogens), inflammation.
  • C3: the central component of all three pathways. Deficiency → severe recurrent infections.

Key Vaccine Milestones

  • 1796: Edward Jenner — smallpox vaccine (cowpox → first vaccine ever)
  • 1885: Louis Pasteur — rabies vaccine (first human rabies vaccine)
  • 1921: BCG vaccine (Calmette & Guérin) — TB
  • 1955: Jonas Salk — IPV (inactivated polio vaccine)
  • 1961: Albert Sabin — OPV (oral polio vaccine, live attenuated)
  • 1986: Hepatitis B — first recombinant DNA vaccine (Saccharomyces yeast)
  • 2020: COVID-19 mRNA vaccines (Pfizer, Moderna) — fastest vaccine development ever (~11 months)
  • 2023: Nobel Prize to Karikó & Weissman for mRNA technology
🔶 Bihar Deep Dive — Disease & Vaccination Programmes
Bihar's Disease Burden & Response
  • Kala-azar Elimination: India signed the London Declaration (2012) to eliminate kala-azar by 2020 (now 2025). Bihar's 33 endemic districts. Kala-azar Elimination Response (KER): active case detection, IRS (indoor residual spraying with DDT/synthetic pyrethroids), liposomal Amphotericin B (single-dose IV, 10mg/kg). Cases: 77,000 (2007) → <1,000 (2024).
  • Japanese Encephalitis (JE): endemic in 24 Bihar districts (including Muzaffarpur, Gaya, Patna). JE vaccine (SA-14-14-2, live attenuated) included in UIP for endemic districts since 2006. Pig farming near human habitation is a risk factor (pigs = amplifier host; Culex mosquito = vector; birds = reservoir).
  • COVID-19: Bihar administered >15 crore doses. First dose coverage >90%. Rural outreach via Mobile Vaccination Teams (MVTs). Har Ghar Dastak campaign for door-to-door vaccination.
  • Measles-Rubella (MR) campaign: Bihar conducted MR campaign (2019–2022) targeting children 9 months–15 years. Goal: eliminate measles and rubella (congenital rubella syndrome) by 2025.
  • Blood banks: Bihar has ~80 blood banks. Major: AIIMS Patna, IGIMS, PMCH, RMRI. Voluntary blood donation low (~40% vs national ~70%). Shortage common during dengue/malaria seasons (platelet demand).
