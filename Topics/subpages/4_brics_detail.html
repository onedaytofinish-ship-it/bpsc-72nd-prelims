BRICS Grouping — Expansion and Summit Details

📅 BPSC PYQ Connection (70th BPSC Dec 13): Russia took over the BRICS rotating chairmanship on January 1, 2024.

1. Historical Evolution

The term **BRIC** was coined in 2001 by Goldman Sachs economist Jim O'Neill to describe the fast-growing economies of Brazil, Russia, India, and China. The first formal summit occurred in 2009 in Yekaterinburg, Russia. In 2010, South Africa joined, forming **BRICS**.

2. The Historic 2024 Expansion

During the 15th Summit in Johannesburg (2023), six countries were invited to join. Effective **January 1, 2024**, five officially became members:

  • Egypt (African Region)
  • Ethiopia (African Region)
  • Iran (Middle East Region)
  • Saudi Arabia (Middle East Region)
  • United Arab Emirates (UAE) (Middle East Region)

Note on Argentina: Argentina was the sixth invitee, but under the newly elected President Javier Milei, it officially withdrew and declined the invitation.

3. 16th BRICS Summit (Kazan, Russia — October 2024)

This was the first summit held under the expanded 10-member format.

Key Outcomes:

  • Kazan Declaration: Focused on strengthening multilateralism, integrating the new members, and exploring alternative financial systems (like a BRICS cross-border payment initiative to bypass SWIFT).
  • Bilateral Meetings: Highlighted by the formal meeting between Indian PM Narendra Modi and Chinese President Xi Jinping following the border disengagement agreement.

4. Future Chairmanships

  • 2025 Chair: Brazil (Rio de Janeiro Summit).
  • 2026 Chair: India (18th Summit).
