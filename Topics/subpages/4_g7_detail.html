Group of Seven (G7) — Detailed Profile & Summits

1. Membership & Institutional Setup

The G7 is an informal bloc of industrialized democracies. It was established in **1975** (initially as G6, Canada joined in 1976). It does not have a permanent secretariat or head office. The presidency rotates annually.

Member Countries:

  • Canada
  • France
  • Germany
  • Italy
  • Japan
  • United Kingdom
  • United States
  • Note: The European Union (EU) is a non-enumerated participant.

Note on G8: Russia joined in 1997, making it the G8. Following its annexation of Crimea in 2014, Russia was suspended indefinitely, and the group reverted to G7.

2. 50th G7 Summit (Apulia, Italy — June 2024)

Hosted by Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni. PM Modi attended as an invitee outreach guest (India is not a G7 member but is regularly invited).

Key Outcomes:

  • Apulia G7 Declaration: Focused on support for Africa (the Mattei Plan), artificial intelligence ethics (with Pope Francis attending the session), and climate action.
  • PGII Infrastructure Fund: Commitment to accelerate the Partnership for Global Infrastructure and Investment (PGII) to counter China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI).

3. 51st G7 Summit (Kananaskis, Canada — June 2025)

Marked the 50th anniversary of the grouping. Focused on economic inclusion, critical supply chain security, and safeguarding democratic processes from foreign interference.

4. 52nd G7 Summit (Évian-les-Bains, France — 2026)

Focused on digital governance, regulatory coordination for frontier AI models, and developing international supply chains for critical minerals.
