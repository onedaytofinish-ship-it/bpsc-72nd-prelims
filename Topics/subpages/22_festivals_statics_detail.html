Bihar Festivals & Culture — Core Concepts, Statics & Background
🌅 Chhath Mahaparva — Core Concepts
  • What it is: Bihar's most important festival — a four-day worship of Surya (the Sun God) and Chhathi Maiya (Usha, consort/sister aspect of the Sun in folk tradition), celebrated on Kartik Shukla Chaturthi–Saptami (six days after Diwali). Devotees (vratis) observe among the strictest fasts in the Hindu calendar — including a 36-hour nirjala (waterless) fast.
  • The four days: Nahay Khay (holy bath, first satvik meal) → Kharna/Lohanda (kheer-roti prasad at dusk; the long fast begins) → Sandhya Arghya (offering to the setting Sun at a riverbank/pond) → Usha Arghya + Paran (offering to the rising Sun; fast broken). 2025 dates: 25–28 October.
  • Distinctive features: no idol worship — direct worship of the visible Sun; thekua and rice-kheer prasad cooked in new earthen pots on mango-wood fires; soop (winnowing baskets) of sugarcane, fruits and thekua carried to the ghats; both setting and rising Sun worshipped — rare among festivals.
  • Geography of celebration: Bihar, Jharkhand, eastern UP (Purvanchal), Nepal's Terai (as Chhath Parva); carried worldwide by the Bhojpuri diaspora — Mauritius, Fiji, Suriname, Trinidad & Tobago, UAE, Netherlands — which is precisely why India filed a multinational UNESCO nomination (consultations from 15 Sep 2025; 2026–27 cycle; not inscribed as of 15 Jul 2026).
  • UNESCO ICH mechanics (background): the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity is inscribed annually by the Intergovernmental Committee. India hosted the 20th session (Red Fort, New Delhi, 8–13 Dec 2025), where Deepavali was inscribed — India's 16th element. Chhath's file targets the 2026–27 cycle.
🧠 Mnemonic — Who worships whom, when?
"Evening first, morning last" — Sandhya (evening, Day 3) precedes Usha (dawn, Day 4). Usha is also the name of the Sun's dawn-consort — so Usha Arghya = dawn = Day 4 can never be confused again.

🛕 Takht Sri Patna Sahib & the Five Takhts
  • Takht Sri Patna Sahib (Harmandir Sahib, Patna City): birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh (born 1666), the tenth Sikh Guru; built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh; one of the five Takhts (seats of temporal authority) of Sikhism. Prakash Parv draws lakhs every January — 359th edition in Jan 2026.

🗓️ Bihar Formation & Identity Statics
  • 22 March 1912: Bihar (with Orissa) carved out of the Bengal Presidency → commemorated as Bihar Diwas (114th edition on 22 Mar 2026; theme 'Unnat Bihar, Ujjwal Bihar').
  • 1936: Orissa separated from Bihar. 15 November 2000: Jharkhand carved out of Bihar (Bihar Reorganisation Act).
  • Numbers that frame culture coverage: 38 districts, 9 divisions, 243 assembly seats; Patna = capital; cultural belts — Mithila (north), Magadh (south-central), Bhojpur (south-west), Anga (east).
  • Languages & arts bank: Bhojpuri, Maithili (8th Schedule, since 2003 — 92nd Amendment), Magahi, Angika, Bajjika; folk arts — Madhubani/Mithila painting, Manjusha art (Bhagalpur region), Patna Kalam (Company-school painting, celebrated in the 2025 Biennale closing show), Sujini, Sikki, Khatwa; performing traditions — Bidesia, Jat-Jatin, Jhijhiya, Domkach.
