## 19. STATICS (evergreen facts examiners pair with this news)

**Indian civilian honours**
- Bharat Ratna & Padma awards are conferred by the **President of India**; Padma announced on **R-Day eve**, conferred at **Rashtrapati Bhavan** (Mar–Apr). Bharat Ratna instituted **1954**; first recipients: **C. Rajagopalachari, S. Radhakrishnan, C.V. Raman**; **max 3 per year**; no cash; cannot be used as a title (Art. 18(1)).
- Padma awards instituted **1954**; 3 classes — Vibhushan ("exceptional & distinguished"), Bhushan ("distinguished service of high order"), Shri ("distinguished service"); govt servants **not eligible except doctors & scientists**; Padma Awards Committee headed by **Cabinet Secretary**; nominations open to public. [ToppersNotes](https://www.toppersnotes.co/current-affairs/blog/seven-padma-awardees-from-bihar-2025-29-may-2025)
- Order: Bharat Ratna > Padma Vibhushan > Padma Bhushan > Padma Shri (civilian).

**Nobel**
- First awarded **1901** (5 prizes); **Economics added 1968** (Sveriges Riksbank). First Indian & first Asian laureate: **Rabindranath Tagore, Literature 1913** (Gitanjali). First Indian woman Nobel: **Mother Teresa, Peace 1979**. Max 3 sharers; Peace Prize awarded in **Oslo**, others in **Stockholm**; ceremony **10 Dec**. Posthumous awards barred since 1974 (unless death post-announcement). [Testbook](https://testbook.com/news/nobel-prize-2025-winners-list/), [NBCC PDF](https://nandyalbankcoachingcenter.in/UploadedFiles/NBCC%20Oct%201%20to%2015%202025%20CA%20-Final_20251028192207.pdf)

**Literature**
- Jnanpith: instituted **1961** by Bharatiya Jnanpith; first awarded **1965** to **G. Sankara Kurup** (Malayalam, *Odakkuzhal*); ₹11 lakh + bronze Vagdevi (Saraswati) statuette + citation; only living Indian citizens. [Vajiram PDF](https://vajiram-prod.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/Prelims_Knock_Part_1_f1f6a92d15.pdf)
- Sahitya Akademi: est. **1954** (inaugurated 12 Mar 1954); award since **1955**; **24 languages** (22 scheduled + English + Rajasthani); ₹1 lakh + copper plaque + shawl. [Vajiram](https://vajiramandravi.com/current-affairs/saa/)
- Booker: est. **1969**; £50,000; Indian winners — Salman Rushdie (1981, *Midnight's Children*), Arundhati Roy (1997), Kiran Desai (2006), Aravind Adiga (2008). Int'l Booker: Geetanjali Shree's *Tomb of Sand* (tr. Daisy Rockwell) 2022 — first Hindi winner. [AppX PDF](https://appx-content-v2.classx.co.in/subject/2026-01-14-0_15758455930144155.pdf)
- Pulitzer: est. **1917** (Joseph Pulitzer bequest; Columbia Univ.); Public Service winner gets gold medal; others $15,000. [Drishti PDF](https://www.drishtiias.com/images/pdf/UPPCS%20Current%20Affairs%20(August%202024-August%202025).pdf)

**Sports awards**
- Khel Ratna: instituted **1991–92**; first winner **Viswanathan Anand**; renamed **Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna in Aug 2021** (from Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna); for most outstanding performance over 4 years; **₹25 lakh** + medallion + certificate.
